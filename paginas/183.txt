Wikipedia was founded as an offshoot of Nupedia a now-abandoned project to produce a free encyclopedia. Nupedia had an elaborate system of and required highly qualified contributors, but the writing of articles was slow. During 2000, founder of Nupedia, and whom Wales had employed to work on the project, discussed ways of supplementing Nupedia with a more open, complementary project. Multiple sources suggested that a wiki might allow members of the public to contribute material, and Nupedia's first wiki went online on January10, was considerable resistance on the part of Nupedia's editors and reviewers to the idea of associating Nupedia with a website in the wiki format, so the new project was given the name "Wikipedia" and launched on its own domain, wikipedia.com, on January15 (now called by some users). The bandwidth and server (in San Diego) were donated by Wales.
