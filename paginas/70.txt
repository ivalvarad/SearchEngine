[ 19 ]   As late as Greenwich was only a fishing town, with a safe anchorage in the   [ 7 ]     Viking   [  edit  ]   the reign of the the Danish fleet anchored in the River Thames off Greenwich for over three years, with the army being encamped on the hill above. From here they attacked Kent and, in the year 1012, took the city of Canterbury making Archbishop Alphege their prisoner for seven months in their camp at Greenwich. They stoned him to death for his refusal to allow his ransom (3,000 pieces of silver) to be paid; and kept his body, until the blossoming of a stick that had been immersed in his blood. For this miracle his body was released to his followers, he achieved sainthood for his martyrdom and, in the 12th century, the parish church was dedicated to him. The present church on the site west of the town centre is Alfege's designed by in 1714 and completed in 1718. Some vestiges of the Danish camps may be traced in the names of Eastcombe and Westcombe on the borders of nearby Blackheath . It is commonly used in practice to refer to Universal (UTC) when this is viewed as a especially by bodies connected with the United Kingdom, such as the World ,   [ 40 ]   the the and others, although strictly UTC is an atomic which only approximates GMT with a tolerance of 0.9 second. It is also used to refer to (UT), which is a standard astronomical concept used in many technical fields and is referred to by the phrase . the United Kingdom grew into an advanced British mariners kept at least one chronometer on GMT in order to calculate their longitude from the which was by convention considered to have longitude zero degrees (this convention was internationally adopted in the Meridian of 1884). Note that the synchronization of the chronometer on GMT did not affect shipboard time itself, which was still solar time. But this practice, combined with mariners from other nations drawing from method of based on observations at Greenwich, eventually led to GMT being used worldwide as a reference time independent of location.
