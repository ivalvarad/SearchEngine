<p><b>Angus Stewart Deaton</b>, <a href="/wiki/British_Academy" title="British Academy">FBA</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> (born 19 October 1945) is a British economist. In 2015, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Memorial_Prize_in_Economic_Sciences" title="Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences">Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences</a> for his analysis of <a href="/wiki/Consumption_(economics)" title="Consumption (economics)">consumption</a>, poverty, and <a href="/wiki/Economic_welfare" title="Economic welfare">welfare</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Deaton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Deaton was born in Edinburgh, Scotland, and educated as a foundation scholar at <a href="/wiki/Fettes_College" title="Fettes College">Fettes College</a>. He earned his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">B.A.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts_(Oxbridge_and_Dublin)" title="Master of Arts (Oxbridge and Dublin)">M.A.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">D.Phil.</a> degrees at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>, the last with a 1975 thesis entitled <i>Models of consumer demand and their application to the United Kingdom</i>, where he was later a fellow at <a href="/wiki/Fitzwilliam_College,_Cambridge" title="Fitzwilliam College, Cambridge">Fitzwilliam College</a> and a research officer working with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Stone" title="Richard Stone">Richard Stone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Terry_Barker" title="Terry Barker">Terry Barker</a> in the Department of Applied Economics.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1976 Deaton took up post at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bristol" title="University of Bristol">University of Bristol</a> as Professor of Econometrics. During this period, he completed a significant portion of his most influential work. In 1978, he became the first ever recipient of the <a href="/wiki/Frisch_Medal" title="Frisch Medal">Frisch Medal</a>, an award given by the Econometric Society every two years. In 1980, his paper on how demand for various consumption goods depends on prices and income was published in <i><a href="/wiki/The_American_Economic_Review" title="The American Economic Review">The American Economic Review</a></i>. This paper has since been hailed as one of the twenty most influential articles published in the journal since 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-University_of_Bristol_news_website_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-University_of_Bristol_news_website-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1983, he left the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bristol" title="University of Bristol">University of Bristol</a> for <a href="/wiki/Princeton_University" title="Princeton University">Princeton University</a>, where his appointment was suggested by <a href="/wiki/John_P._Lewis" title="John P. Lewis">John P. Lewis</a>, former dean of the <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson_School_of_Public_and_International_Affairs" title="Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs">Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs</a> (WWS). He is the <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Dwight D. Eisenhower</a> Professor of International Affairs and Professor of Economics and International Affairs at WWS and the Economics Department at Princeton.<sup id="cite_ref-National_Bureau_of_Economic_Research_website_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-National_Bureau_of_Economic_Research_website-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> He holds both British and American citizenship.<sup id="cite_ref-news_Scot_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-news_Scot-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2015 it was announced that Deaton had won that year's <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Memorial_Prize_in_Economic_Sciences" title="Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences">Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences</a>. The BBC reported that Deaton was "delighted" and that he described himself as "someone who's concerned with the poor of the world and how people behave, and what gives them a good life". The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences">Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences</a> said that economic policy intended to reduce poverty could only be designed once individuals' consumption choices were understood, saying, "More than anyone else, Angus Deaton has enhanced this understanding. By linking detailed individual choices and aggregate outcomes, his research has helped transform the fields of microeconomics, macroeconomics, and development economics".<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_News_12_October_2015_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_News_12_October_2015-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> New York University economist <a href="/wiki/William_Easterly" title="William Easterly">William Easterly</a> said, "What was impressive about this Nobel is how many different fields Angus has contributed to". Easterly noted Deaton’s bravery in the face of the political aspects of his research area and the "tortuous details" involved in his work, adding: "No one accuses him of having an agenda on these questions, and there are a lot of people in this field who do have an agenda".<sup id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal_12_October_2015_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wall_Street_Journal_12_October_2015-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scholarship">Scholarship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Deaton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Scholarship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Deaton's first work to become known was the <a href="/wiki/Almost_ideal_demand_system" title="Almost ideal demand system">almost ideal demand system</a> (AIDS), which he developed with <a href="/wiki/John_Muellbauer" title="John Muellbauer">John Muellbauer</a> and published in <i><a href="/wiki/The_American_Economic_Review" title="The American Economic Review">The American Economic Review</a></i> (AER) in 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> As a consumer demand model it provides a first order approximation to any demand system which satisfies the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Axioms_of_order&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Axioms of order (page does not exist)">axioms of order</a>.</p>
<p>In 1978 Deaton became the first recipient of the <a href="/wiki/Frisch_Medal" title="Frisch Medal">Frisch Medal</a>, an award given by the <a href="/wiki/Econometric_Society" title="Econometric Society">Econometric Society</a> every two years to an applied paper published within the past five years in <i><a href="/wiki/Econometrica" title="Econometrica">Econometrica</a></i>. Deaton is a <a href="/wiki/Econometric_Society" title="Econometric Society">Fellow of the Econometric Society</a>, a <a href="/wiki/British_Academy" title="British Academy">Corresponding Fellow of the British Academy</a> (FBA),<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="American Academy of Arts and Sciences">Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences</a>. In April 2014, he was elected to the <a href="/wiki/American_Philosophical_Society" title="American Philosophical Society">American Philosophical Society</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> He holds honorary degrees from the University of Rome, Tor Vergata, University College London, and the University of St. Andrews. In 2007, he was elected president of the <a href="/wiki/American_Economic_Association" title="American Economic Association">American Economic Association</a>. He won the 2011 <a href="/wiki/BBVA_Foundation_Frontiers_of_Knowledge_Award" title="BBVA Foundation Frontiers of Knowledge Award">BBVA Foundation Frontiers of Knowledge Award</a> of Economics, Finance and Management for his fundamental contributions to the theory of consumption and savings, and the measurement of economic wellbeing.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><br />
He formulated the <i>Deaton Paradox</i> based on the observation of excess smoothness of consumption in the face of unanticipated permanent income shocks.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Deaton is also the author of "Letters from America", a popular semi-annual feature in the <i><a href="/wiki/Royal_Economic_Society" title="Royal Economic Society">Royal Economic Society</a> Newsletter</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Deaton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Deaton has two children, born in 1970 and 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-Angus_Deaton_CV_November_2014_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Angus_Deaton_CV_November_2014-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> He is married to Anne Case, a research associate at the <a href="/wiki/National_Bureau_of_Economic_Research" title="National Bureau of Economic Research">National Bureau of Economic Research</a>. The couple’s recreational activities include the opera and trout fishing.<sup id="cite_ref-National_Bureau_of_Economic_Research_website_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-National_Bureau_of_Economic_Research_website-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
