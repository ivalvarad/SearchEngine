<p><b>SABMiller plc</b> (<a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">LSE</a>:&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.londonstockexchange.com/exchange/prices-and-news/stocks/prices-search/stock-prices-search.html?nameCode=SAB">SAB</a>, <a href="/wiki/JSE_Limited" title="JSE Limited">JSE</a>: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.jse.co.za/Search?k=SAB#Instruments">SAB</a>) is a South African <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Brewing" title="Brewing">brewing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beverage" title="Beverage" class="mw-redirect">beverage</a> <a href="/wiki/Company" title="Company">company</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, England. It is the world's second-largest brewer measured by revenues (after the American-Belgian-Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch_InBev" title="Anheuser-Busch InBev">Anheuser-Busch InBev</a>) and is also a major bottler of <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-snapshot_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snapshot-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Its brands include <a href="/wiki/Foster%27s_Lager" title="Foster's Lager">Fosters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grolsch_Brewery" title="Grolsch Brewery">Grolsch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miller_Brewing_Company" title="Miller Brewing Company">Miller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peroni_Brewery" title="Peroni Brewery">Peroni</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pilsner_Urquell" title="Pilsner Urquell">Pilsner Urquell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-snapshot_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snapshot-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> It has operations in 80 countries world-wide and in 2009 sold around 21 billion <a href="/wiki/Litre" title="Litre">litres</a> of beverages.<sup id="cite_ref-snapshot_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snapshot-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span style="white-space: nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot"><span title="&#160;since April 2015">dead link</span></a></i>]</span></sup></p>
<p>SABMiller's origins date back to the foundation of South African Breweries in 1895 as to serve a growing market of <a href="/wiki/Miner" title="Miner">miners</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prospecting" title="Prospecting">prospectors</a> in and around <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>. Two years later, it became the first industrial company to list on the <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg_Stock_Exchange" title="Johannesburg Stock Exchange" class="mw-redirect">Johannesburg Stock Exchange</a>. From the early 1990s onwards, the company increasingly expanded internationally, making several acquisitions in both emerging and developed markets. In 1999, it formed a new UK-based holding company, SAB plc, and moved its primary listing to London. In May 2002, SAB plc acquired <a href="/wiki/Miller_Brewing" title="Miller Brewing" class="mw-redirect">Miller Brewing</a>, forming SABMiller plc.</p>
<p>SABMiller has a primary listing on the London Stock Exchange and is a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/FTSE_100_Index" title="FTSE 100 Index">FTSE 100 Index</a>. It had a <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalisation" title="Market capitalisation" class="mw-redirect">market capitalisation</a> of approximately £35.6&#160;billion as of 23 December 2011, the 11th-largest of any company with a primary listing on the London Stock Exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> It has a secondary listing on the Johannesburg Stock Exchange.</p>
<p>In October 2015, Anheuser-Busch InBev announced plans to acquire SABMiller for £69 billion ($104 billion).<sup id="cite_ref-inbevmerger_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inbevmerger-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The origins of the company date back to the foundation of <a href="/wiki/South_African_Breweries" title="South African Breweries">South African Breweries</a> in 1895 in South Africa. For many decades, the operations of South African Breweries were mainly limited to southern Africa, where it had established a dominant position in the market, until 1990 when it began investing in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-history_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1999, after listing on the London Stock Exchange<sup id="cite_ref-history_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> to raise capital for acquisitions, the group purchased the <a href="/wiki/Miller_Brewing_Company" title="Miller Brewing Company">Miller Brewing Company</a> in North America from the <a href="/wiki/Altria_Group" title="Altria Group" class="mw-redirect">Altria Group</a> in 2002, and changed its name to SABMiller.<sup id="cite_ref-history_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Following this, the group's next major acquisition was of a major interest in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria_Brewery_(Colombia)" title="Bavaria Brewery (Colombia)">Bavaria S. A.</a>, South America's second largest brewer and owner of the Aguila and Club Colombia brands in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-history_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The company became engaged in the hostile takeover of <a href="/wiki/Foster%27s_Group" title="Foster's Group">Fosters</a> in August 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> and in September 2011 the board of Foster's agreed to a takeover bid valuing the company at A$9.9bn (US$10.2bn; £6.5bn).<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_News_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_News-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The deal was completed by the end of 2011, but excluded the Foster's lager brand in the UK and Europe, where it is owned by <a href="/wiki/Heineken" title="Heineken">Heineken</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2011, SABMiller launched Impala Cervejas in Africa, the first commercially produced <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">cassava</a> beer, although Africans have been making cassava home brews for generations. The taste is described as "somewhat bitter, somewhat tangy, not sweet".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2014, the company announced it was divesting its 39.6% stake in casino and hotel group <a href="/wiki/Tsogo_Sun" title="Tsogo Sun">Tsogo Sun Holdings Limited</a> through institutional share placements and a partial buy-back from Tsogo Sun. SABMiller's stake at the time was valued at approximately ZAR11.7 billion (US$1.09 billion).<sup id="cite_ref-SABMillerTsogoSun_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SABMillerTsogoSun-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2014, the company made an unsuccessful attempt to acquire a controlling stake in Dutch rival <a href="/wiki/Heineken_International" title="Heineken International">Heineken International</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> a move <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Markets" title="Bloomberg Markets">Bloomberg</a> states was part of SABMiller's strategy to protect itself from a potential <a href="/wiki/Takeover" title="Takeover">takeover</a> bid from <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch_InBev" title="Anheuser-Busch InBev">Anheuser-Busch InBev</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 November 2014, it was announced that SABMiller, <a href="/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company" title="The Coca-Cola Company">The Coca-Cola Company</a> and Gutsche Family Investments had agreed to combine the bottling operations of their non-alcoholic ready-to-drink beverages businesses in Southern and East Africa. The new bottler, <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola_Beverages_Africa" title="Coca-Cola Beverages Africa">Coca-Cola Beverages Africa</a>, will serve 12 high-growth countries accounting for approximately 40 percent of all Coca-Cola beverage volumes in Africa. SABMiller will hold 57% shareholding in the proposed venture.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2015, SABMiller announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> brewery company <a href="/wiki/Meantime_Brewing" title="Meantime Brewing" class="mw-redirect">Meantime Brewing</a> for an undisclosed fee.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 16 September 2015, Anheuser-Busch InBev made another acquisition bid for SABMiller. This deal would unite the world's two biggest beermakers and control about half the industry's profit. The deal however has to go through several regulatory hurdles which would require certain operations to be spun off the group.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> A deal was announced on 13 October 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>SABMiller has grown from its original South African base into a global company with operations in both developed markets and in emerging economies such as Eastern Europe, China and India. It is one of the world's largest brewers, with brewing interests and distribution agreements across six continents. Its worldwide portfolio of brands includes premium international beers such as <a href="/wiki/Pilsner_Urquell" title="Pilsner Urquell">Pilsner Urquell</a>, Peroni Nastro Azzurro, Miller Genuine Draft and Grolsch along with leading local brands such as Aguila, Castle, Miller Lite, <a href="/wiki/Snow" title="Snow">Snow</a>, and Tyskie.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa_and_Asia_.28ex._South_Africa.29">Africa and Asia (ex. South Africa)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Africa and Asia (ex. South Africa)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>SABMiller's brewing operations in Africa span 31 countries. In China, the group's national brand in that country, Snow, is produced in partnership with <a href="/wiki/China_Resources_Enterprise" title="China Resources Enterprise">China Resources Enterprise</a> Limited, is the leading brand by volume in China. SABMiller is the second-largest brewer in India and has joint ventures in Vietnam and Australia.</p>
<p>Brands include: N'gola, 2M, <a href="/wiki/Castle_Lager" title="Castle Lager" class="mw-redirect">Castle Lager</a>, Castle Milk Stout, Eagle, Fosters, Kilimanjaro, Haywards 5000, Royal Challenge, Knock out, Indus Pride, Snow, Zorok, and <a href="/wiki/Southern_Sudan_Beverages_Limited" title="Southern Sudan Beverages Limited">White Bull</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2011, the board of <a href="/wiki/Foster%27s_Group" title="Foster's Group">Foster's Group</a> agreed to a takeover bid by SABMiller, valuing the company at A$9.9bn (US$10.2bn; £6.5bn).<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_News_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_News-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Brands include: <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Draught" title="Carlton Draught">Carlton Draught</a>, Cascade Draught (see <a href="/wiki/Cascade_Brewery" title="Cascade Brewery">Cascade Brewery</a>), <a href="/wiki/Foster%27s_Lager" title="Foster's Lager">Foster's Lager</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Bitter" title="Melbourne Bitter">Melbourne Bitter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pure_Blonde" title="Pure Blonde">Pure Blonde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Bitter" title="Victoria Bitter">Victoria Bitter</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Matilda_Bay_Brewing_Company" title="Matilda Bay Brewing Company">Matilda Bay Brewing Company</a> portfolio.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>SABMiller's entry into the European market began in 1995 with the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Dreher_Brewery" title="Dreher Brewery">Dreher</a> in Hungary. The group’s European operations are mostly centred in the fast developing consumer markets in Central and Eastern Europe (Hungary, Romania, Poland, Czech Republic and Russia), although there are operations in Italy, France, UK, Spain and Germany where it imports its international brands. On 19 November 2007, the board of Royal Grolsch NV accepted a €816&#160;million offer for the company by SABMiller. The takeover was completed with the delisting of Grolsch's shares on 20 March 2008.</p>
<p>Brands include: <a href="/wiki/Pilsner_Urquell" title="Pilsner Urquell">Pilsner Urquell</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0ari%C5%A1_Brewery" title="Šariš Brewery">Šariš Brewery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Velkopopovick%C3%BD_Kozel" title="Velkopopovický Kozel">Velkopopovický Kozel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radegast_(beer)" title="Radegast (beer)">Radegast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peroni_Brewery" title="Peroni Brewery">Nastro Azzurro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peroni_Brewery" title="Peroni Brewery">Peroni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyskie" title="Tyskie">Tyskie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Topvar" title="Topvar">Topvar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ursus_(beer)" title="Ursus (beer)" class="mw-redirect">Ursus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dreher_Brewery" title="Dreher Brewery">Dreher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grolsch" title="Grolsch" class="mw-redirect">Grolsch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lech_Brewery_Wielkopolski" title="Lech Brewery Wielkopolski" class="mw-redirect">Lech</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Latin_America">Latin America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Latin America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>SABMiller first entered the Latin American Market with the acquisition of Cerveceria Hondureña in Honduras, making the company the first international brewer to enter Central America. Since then, the group has expanded its Latin American operations into six countries, including Colombia, El Salvador, Ecuador, Panama and Peru.</p>
<p>Lager brands include: Isenbeck (Argentina), Aguila, Club Colombia, Costeña, Poker, Pilsen (Colombia), Cristal, Pilsen Callao, Pilsen Trujillo, Cusqueña, Arequipeña (Peru), Pilsener, Club (Ecuador), Pilsener, Regia, Suprema, Golden Light (El Salvador), Port Royal, Salva Vida, Imperial (Honduras), Atlas (Panama), and Balboa (Panama).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 October 2007, SABMiller and <a href="/wiki/Molson_Coors_Brewing_Company" title="Molson Coors Brewing Company">Molson Coors Brewing Company</a> announced a joint venture to be known as <a href="/wiki/MillerCoors" title="MillerCoors">MillerCoors</a>. U.S. antitrust regulators approved the joint venture on 5 June 2008. The merger was completed on 30 June 2008 and MillerCoors began operation as a combined entity on 1 July 2008. The combined venture will be headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>. Pete Coors has been named as chairman of the new company, and Molson Coors Chief Executive Leo Kiely will be the new CEO of the joint venture. Tom Long, CEO of Miller, will be appointed president and chief commercial officer. They have recently opened permanent offices in Toronto, Montreal and Vancouver, Canada.</p>
<p>Brands include: <a href="/wiki/Miller_Lite" title="Miller Lite">Miller Lite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miller_Genuine_Draft" title="Miller Genuine Draft" class="mw-redirect">Miller Genuine Draft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olde_English_800" title="Olde English 800">Olde English 800</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee%27s_Best" title="Milwaukee's Best">Milwaukee's Best</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miller_Chill" title="Miller Chill" class="mw-redirect">Miller Chill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamm%27s" title="Hamm's" class="mw-redirect">Hamm's</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Leinenkugel" title="Leinenkugel" class="mw-redirect">Leinenkugel</a>.</p>
<p>The proposed merger with Anheuser Busch InBev would give the newly combined company a near <a href="/wiki/Monopoly" title="Monopoly">monopoly</a> on beer production in the United States, whose <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a> poses the largest obstacle to the merger moving forward. Numerous brands would have to be spun off to receive regulatory approval.<sup id="cite_ref-inbevmerger_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inbevmerger-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is the group's most established market to date. Local subsidiary SAB Ltd's soft drink division is also the country's largest producer of products for <a href="/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company" title="The Coca-Cola Company">The Coca-Cola Company</a>.</p>
<p>Brands include: <a href="/wiki/Castle_Lager" title="Castle Lager" class="mw-redirect">Castle Lager</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grolsch" title="Grolsch" class="mw-redirect">Grolsch</a>, Castle Milk Stout, Hansa Marzen Gold, Hansa Pilsener, <a href="/wiki/Carling_Black_Label" title="Carling Black Label">Carling Black Label</a>, Castle Lite, Redd’s, <a href="/wiki/Peroni_Brewery" title="Peroni Brewery">Peroni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miller_Genuine_Draft" title="Miller Genuine Draft" class="mw-redirect">Miller Genuine Draft</a>, Brutal Fruit, and Sarita.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_social_responsibility">Corporate social responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Corporate social responsibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>SABMiller runs a number of sustainable development initiatives across its companies and in the countries in which it operates. They use new lightweight bottles that use 30% less glass. The lightweight bottles are designed to not only reduce the amount of waste materials but also cut down on energy used in production and distribution reducing the company's carbon emissions.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> SABMiller submits to a number of third party annual reports that review the company's environmental record. The corporation provides links to such reports on their own website.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxation">Taxation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SABMiller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Taxation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2010, the charity <a href="/wiki/ActionAid" title="ActionAid">ActionAid</a> published a report alleging that SABMiller has avoided paying a total of around £20&#160;million of corporation tax in five African countries – <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, Mozambique, <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, South Africa and <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> – and in India. SABMiller has denied these allegations.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
