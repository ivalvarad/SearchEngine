			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Dryandra</i> subg. <i>Diplophragma</i></h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b><i>Dryandra</i> subg. <i>Diplophragma</i></b> is an obsolete subgenus within the former genus <i>Dryandra</i> (now <a href="/wiki/Banksia_ser._Dryandra" title="Banksia ser. Dryandra"><i>Banksia</i> ser. <i>Dryandra</i></a>). It was first published by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Brown_(botanist)" title="Robert Brown (botanist)">Robert Brown</a> in 1830, but was discarded by <a href="/wiki/George_Bentham" title="George Bentham">George Bentham</a> in 1870. It was reinstated with a new circumscription by <a href="/wiki/Alex_George" title="Alex George">Alex George</a> in 1996, but was ultimately discarded again in 2007 when <a href="/wiki/Austin_Mast" title="Austin Mast">Austin Mast</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Thiele" title="Kevin Thiele">Kevin Thiele</a> sunk <i>Dryandra</i> into <i><a href="/wiki/Banksia" title="Banksia">Banksia</a></i>.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="According_to_Brown">According to Brown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dryandra_subg._Diplophragma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: According to Brown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Diplographma</i> was first published by Brown in his 1830 <i><a href="/wiki/Supplementum_primum_prodromi_florae_Novae_Hollandiae" title="Supplementum primum prodromi florae Novae Hollandiae" class="mw-redirect">Supplementum primum prodromi florae Novae Hollandiae</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Brown%27s_taxonomic_arrangement_of_Dryandra" title="Brown's taxonomic arrangement of Dryandra">Brown's arrangement</a> divided <i>Dryandra</i> into three groups according to what Brown perceived to be variations in the number of seed separators. He allowed for these groups to be treated at subgenus or section rank,<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_1830_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown_1830-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> but they are now treated as having been published as sections.<sup id="cite_ref-APNI_25117_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-APNI_25117-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Diplophragma</i> was defined as containing a single species, <i>D.&#160;bipinnatifida</i> (now <i><a href="/wiki/Banksia_bipinnatifida" title="Banksia bipinnatifida">Banksia bipinnatifida</a></i>), which Brown thought contained two seed separators.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_1830_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown_1830-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The epithet <i>Diplophragma</i> is from the <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <i>diplo</i> ("two") and <i>phragma</i> ("barrier"). It is not clear how Brown came to believe that this species has two seed separators, as it actually has none.<sup id="cite_ref-George_1999_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-George_1999-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The placement and circumscription of <i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Diplophragma</i> in Brown's 1830 arrangement may be summarised as follows:</p>
<dl>
<dd><i><b>Dryandra</b></i> (now <a href="/wiki/Banksia_ser._Dryandra" title="Banksia ser. Dryandra"><i>B.</i>&#160;ser. <i>Dryandra</i></a>)
<dl>
<dd><i><a href="/wiki/Dryandra_verae" title="Dryandra verae" class="mw-redirect">Dryandra verae</a></i> (18 species)</dd>
<dd><b><i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Diplophragma</i></b>
<dl>
<dd><i>D.&#160;bipinnatifida</i> (now <i><a href="/wiki/Banksia_bipinnatifida" title="Banksia bipinnatifida">B.&#160;bipinnatifida</a></i>)</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Dryandra_sect._Aphragma" title="Dryandra sect. Aphragma" class="mw-redirect"><i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Aphragma</i></a> (4 species)</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
<dd><i><a href="/wiki/Hemiclidia" title="Hemiclidia" class="mw-redirect">Hemiclidia</a></i> (1 species)</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="According_to_Meissner">According to Meissner</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dryandra_subg._Diplophragma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: According to Meissner">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1856, <a href="/wiki/Carl_Meissner" title="Carl Meissner">Carl Meissner</a> published a revision of <i>Dryandra</i>. He retained all three of Brown's sections, but added bipinnatifid leaves (that is, leaves that are divided into lobes that are themselves divided) to the defining characters of <i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Diplophragma</i>, and included in it the species <i>D.&#160;preissii</i> (now <i><a href="/wiki/Banksia_acuminata" title="Banksia acuminata">Banksia acuminata</a></i>), which had been published in 1845. The placement and circumscription of <i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Diplophragma</i> in <a href="/wiki/Meissner%27s_taxonomic_arrangement_of_Dryandra" title="Meissner's taxonomic arrangement of Dryandra">Meissner's arrangement</a> thus looks like this:<sup id="cite_ref-Meissner_1856_XLIV_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Meissner_1856_XLIV-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd><i><b>Dryandra</b></i> (now <a href="/wiki/Banksia_ser._Dryandra" title="Banksia ser. Dryandra"><i>Banksia</i> ser. <i>Dryandra</i></a>)
<dl>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Dryandra_sect._Eudryandra" title="Dryandra sect. Eudryandra" class="mw-redirect"><i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Eudryandra</i></a> (8 series, 47 species, 7 varieties)</dd>
<dd><b><i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Diplophragma</i></b>
<dl>
<dd><i>D.&#160;bipinnatifida</i> (now <i><a href="/wiki/Banksia_bipinnatifida" title="Banksia bipinnatifida">B.&#160;bipinnatifida</a></i>)</dd>
<dd><i>D.&#160;Preissii</i> (now <i><a href="/wiki/Banksia_acuminata" title="Banksia acuminata">B.&#160;acuminata</a></i>)</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Dryandra_sect._Aphragma" title="Dryandra sect. Aphragma" class="mw-redirect"><i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Aphragma</i></a> (4 species, 4 varieties)</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
<dd><i><a href="/wiki/Hemiclidia" title="Hemiclidia" class="mw-redirect">Hemiclidia</a></i> (1 species)</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="According_to_Bentham">According to Bentham</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dryandra_subg._Diplophragma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: According to Bentham">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Along with many of Meissner's infrageneric taxa, which were overly reliant on leaf characters, Bentham discarded <i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Diplophragma</i> in <a href="/wiki/Bentham%27s_taxonomic_arrangement_of_Dryandra" title="Bentham's taxonomic arrangement of Dryandra">his 1870 arrangement</a>. Despite having seen only a few seeds, he correctly observed that the characters of the fruit upon which Brown based the section were not constant:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>"[I]n the few seeds that I have been able to examine, the separation of the integument from the nucleus when not consolidated with the corresponding integument of the other seed has not appeared to me to be at all constant. The whole question requires further investigation on the part of those who may have a sufficient supply of good fruits of the several species."</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Bentham also recognised that the two species placed in <i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Diplophragma</i> by Meissner are not closely related; he placed <i>D.&#160;bipinnatifida</i> in <a href="/wiki/Dryandra_sect._Aphragma" title="Dryandra sect. Aphragma" class="mw-redirect"><i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Aphragma</i></a>, and <i>D.&#160;preissii</i> in <a href="/wiki/Dryandra_sect._Eudryandra" title="Dryandra sect. Eudryandra" class="mw-redirect"><i>D.</i>&#160;sect. <i>Eudryandra</i></a> <a href="/wiki/Dryandra_ser._Niveae" title="Dryandra ser. Niveae">ser. <i>Niveae</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bentham_1870_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bentham_1870-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="According_to_George">According to George</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dryandra_subg._Diplophragma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: According to George">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/Alex_George" title="Alex George">Alex George</a> published <a href="/wiki/George%27s_taxonomic_arrangement_of_Dryandra" title="George's taxonomic arrangement of Dryandra">his revision</a> of <i>Dryandra</i> in 1996, he recognised that <i>D.&#160;bipinatifida</i> possessed a number of very distinctive characters, especially in its fruiting structure. These include unusually large follicles and seeds; seeds that are set within the seed wings rather than along the base or one side; and unusual double wings that are attached to each side of the seed. In addition, the species has extremely long <a href="/wiki/Involucral_bract" title="Involucral bract" class="mw-redirect">involucral bracts</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_bract&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Floral bract (page does not exist)">floral bracts</a> that enlarge more than any other species. Recognising the need for this species to be placed in its own subgenus, George resurrected <i>Diplophragma</i>, promoting it to subgenus rank as <i>D.</i>&#160;subg. <i>Diplophragma</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-George_1996_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-George_1996-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The placement and circumscription of <i>D.</i>&#160;subg. <i>Diplophragma</i> in George's arrangement, as amended in 1999<sup id="cite_ref-George_1999_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-George_1999-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> and 2005,<sup id="cite_ref-George_2005_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-George_2005-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> may be summarised as follows:</p>
<dl>
<dd><i><b>Dryandra</b></i> (now <a href="/wiki/Banksia_ser._Dryandra" title="Banksia ser. Dryandra"><i>Banksia</i> ser. <i>Dryandra</i></a>)
<dl>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Dryandra_subg._Dryandra" title="Dryandra subg. Dryandra"><i>D.</i>&#160;ser. <i>Dryandra</i></a> (24 series, 91 species, 39 subspecies, 23 varieties)</dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Dryandra_subg._Hemiclidia" title="Dryandra subg. Hemiclidia"><i>D.</i>&#160;ser. <i>Hemiclidia</i></a> (2 species)</dd>
<dd><b><i>D.</i>&#160;subg. <i>Diplophragma</i></b>
<dl>
<dd><i>D.&#160;bipinnatifida</i> (now <i><a href="/wiki/Banksia_bipinnatifida" title="Banksia bipinnatifida">Banksia bipinnatifida</a></i>)</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_developments">Recent developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dryandra_subg._Diplophragma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Recent developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 1998, <a href="/wiki/Austin_Mast" title="Austin Mast">Austin Mast</a> has been publishing results of ongoing cladistic analyses of <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" title="DNA sequence" class="mw-redirect">DNA sequence</a> data for the subtribe <a href="/wiki/Banksiinae" title="Banksiinae" class="mw-redirect">Banksiinae</a>. His analyses have provided compelling evidence of the <a href="/wiki/Paraphyly" title="Paraphyly">paraphyly</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Banksia" title="Banksia">Banksia</a></i> with respect to <i><a href="/wiki/Dryandra" title="Dryandra" class="mw-redirect">Dryandra</a></i>; that is, it seems that <i>Dryandra</i> arose from within the ranks of <i>Banksia</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Mast_1998_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mast_1998-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Mast_2002_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mast_2002-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Mast_2005_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mast_2005-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Early in 2007, Mast and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Thiele" title="Kevin Thiele">Kevin Thiele</a> initiated a rearrangement of <i>Banksia</i> by sinking <i>Dryandra</i> into it as <a href="/wiki/Banksia_ser._Dryandra" title="Banksia ser. Dryandra"><i>B.</i>&#160;ser. <i>Dryandra</i></a>. This transfer necessitated the setting aside of George's infrageneric arrangement of <i>Dryandra</i>; thus <i>D.</i>&#160;subg. <i>Diplophragma</i> is no longer current. Mast and Thiele have foreshadowed publishing a full arrangement once <a href="/wiki/DNA_sampling" title="DNA sampling" class="mw-redirect">DNA sampling</a> of <i>Dryandra</i> is complete.<sup id="cite_ref-Mast_2007_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mast_2007-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
