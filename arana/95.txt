<p>The office of <b>President of Belarus</b> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be" xml:lang="be">Прэзідэнт Рэспублікі Беларусь</span>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Президент Республики Беларусь</span>) is the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Office" title="Office">office</a> was created in 1994 with the passing of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Belarus" title="Constitution of Belarus">Constitution of Belarus</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet" title="Supreme Soviet">Supreme Soviet</a>. This replaced the office of <a href="/wiki/Chairman_of_the_Supreme_Soviet" title="Chairman of the Supreme Soviet" class="mw-redirect">Chairman of the Supreme Soviet</a> as the head of state. The tasks of the president including executing <a href="/wiki/Foreign_policy" title="Foreign policy">foreign</a> and <a href="/wiki/Domestic_policy" title="Domestic policy">domestic policy</a>, defend the rights and general welfare of citizens and residents and to uphold the Constitution. The president is mandated by the Constitution to serve as a leader in the social affairs of the country and to act as its main representative abroad. The duties, responsibilities and other transitional clauses dealing with the presidency are listed in Chapter Three, Articles 79 through 89, of the Constitution.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Term_of_office" title="Term of office">term</a> for the president is five years, but due to a <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_referendum,_1996" title="Belarusian referendum, 1996">1996 referendum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_presidential_election,_2001" title="Belarusian presidential election, 2001">the election</a> that was supposed to occur in 1999 was pushed back to 2001. Under the 1994 constitution, the president could only serve for two terms as president, but due to <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_referendum,_2004" title="Belarusian referendum, 2004">a change in the constitution</a>, term limits were eliminated. During the course of the office, three elections were held in <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_presidential_election,_1994" title="Belarusian presidential election, 1994">1994</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_presidential_election,_2001" title="Belarusian presidential election, 2001">2001</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_presidential_election,_2006" title="Belarusian presidential election, 2006">2006</a>. The last election was held on December 19, 2010. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lukashenko" title="Alexander Lukashenko">Alexander Lukashenko</a> has been the only person who has served as president since the elections in 1994.</p>
<p>The Presidential office is located in the Republic Palace in <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a>, while the presidential residence is located in <a href="/wiki/Zaslawye" title="Zaslawye">Zaslawye</a> (Заслаўе), Minsk District.</p>

<p>The Republic of Belarus was formed in 1991 shortly after declaring itself independent of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. Under the government of the <a href="/wiki/Byelorussian_SSR" title="Byelorussian SSR" class="mw-redirect">Byelorussian SSR</a>, the <a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a> leader of the Soviet Republic was the first secretaries of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Byelorussia" title="Communist Party of Byelorussia">Communist Party of Byelorussia</a>, the only legal party in Soviet Belarus. From independence until passage of the Constitution in 1994, the Chairman of the Supreme Soviet was the head of state and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Belarusian_Prime_Ministers" title="List of Belarusian Prime Ministers" class="mw-redirect">Prime Minister</a> as the head of government. When the office of the presidency was created, the role of the prime minister was reduced to assisting the president and resulted in the dissolution of the Supreme Soviet, along with its Chairman, in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-byind_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-byind-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cnn96_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnn96-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the first set of elections for the office of president, the Prime Minister of Belarus, <a href="/wiki/Vyachaslaw_Kyebich" title="Vyachaslaw Kyebich" class="mw-redirect">Vyachaslaw Kyebich</a>, was defeated in a runoff vote by Alexander Lukashenko, resulting in Lukashenko becoming the first president.<sup id="cite_ref-byind_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-byind-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> In elections of 2001 and 2006, which were contested by international observers, <a href="/wiki/Western_powers" title="Western powers" class="mw-redirect">Western powers</a> and internal opposition parties due for failing to meet democratic and fair standards,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> the incumbent Lukashenko defeated the other candidates within the first ballot. As of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, he is the only person to have served as President of Belarus.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Constitutional_status">Constitutional status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Constitutional status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Article 79 of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Belarus" title="Constitution of Belarus">Constitution of Belarus</a> gives the status of <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> to the President of the Republic of Belarus. He is also considered the guardian of the Constitution and the rights and freedoms of those who claim Belarusian citizenship or residency. The President is the personification of unification of the Belarusian state when conducting foreign or internal affairs and shall be the main representative when dealing with other nations or international organizations. The President is also entrusted with the safety, prosperity and stability of the country and acts as an intermediary between the bodies of the national government.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Selection_process">Selection process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Selection process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eligibility">Eligibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Eligibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In order to be able to run for office, a candidate must be a Belarusian citizen by birth that is over thirty-five years old. The candidate must also reside within the Republic for ten years and he or she must be able to cast a ballot legally. The provisions are set down in Article 80 of the Constitution.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Election">Election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Elections_in_Belarus" title="Elections in Belarus">Elections</a> for president occur every five years by a national vote. Candidates, as soon as they are deemed eligible under Article 80 of the Constitution, are tasked with collecting signatures from eligible voters. After 100,000 signatures are collected and certified, the candidate is declared to be official by the Central Elections Committee. In the voting, the secret ballots are collected directly from eligible voters. During the first round of voting, if a candidate earns fifty percent plus one of the votes, they are declared the President-elect. If no one has achieved that number during the first round, then a <a href="/wiki/Two-round_system" title="Two-round system">run-off election</a> will occur between two candidates who won the most votes. The person who wins the most votes in the run-off is declared the President-elect.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the event the office is vacant, the election to replace the president must occur between thirty and seventy days after the vacancy occurred. During normal election cycles, the elections must occur before the last two months of the current president. In either situation, the government body that calls for elections is the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_Belarus" title="House of Representatives of Belarus">House of Representatives</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The next set of <a href="/wiki/Presidential_elections" title="Presidential elections" class="mw-redirect">presidential elections</a> <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015" title="Belarusian presidential election, 2015">will occur in 2015</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> President Lukashenko, when addressing the press in February 2007, stated his health will determine if he will run in 2011 or step down at that time.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_duties">Powers and duties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Powers and duties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Articles 84 and 85 states the official political, social and national defense duties that are rested with the president Other than the enumerated powers, Number 30 allows the president to use other powers granted to him either from national law or from other sections of the Constitution.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Part of the prerogative of the president is the right to call national <a href="/wiki/Referendum" title="Referendum">referendums</a>, and to call regular and extraordinary elections to the House of Representatives, the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Republic_of_Belarus" title="Council of the Republic of Belarus">Council of the Republic</a> and local representative bodies. He can also dissolve the chambers of the Parliament, as the Constitution permits. It is his duty to appoint the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Belarusian_Prime_Ministers" title="List of Belarusian Prime Ministers" class="mw-redirect">Prime minister of the Republic of Belarus</a> with the consent of the House of Representatives, and to decide the structure of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Belarus" title="Government of Belarus">Government of the Republic of Belarus</a>. The President signs bills, and has the right to return it, fully or in parts, with objections to the House of Representatives. He also appoints– and can dismiss– the deputy Prime ministers, the ministers and the other members of the Government, and he decides in cases of resignation of the Government, or any of its members. With the consent of the Council of the Republic, the President appoints the Chairperson of the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Belarus" title="Supreme Court of Belarus">Supreme Court</a>, and can dismiss this Chairperson and other judges. The president is supposed to deliver annual messages to the Parliament, and has the right to participate in the sessions of Parliament and its bodies. In instances of strike, the president has the right, in instances specified in the law, to defer or suspend a strike for a period not exceeding three months. In international affairs, it is the President's duty to conduct negotiations and sign international treaties, and to appoint and recall diplomatic representatives of the Republic.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Not only the president is the head of government, he is the social leader of Belarus. The president delivers messages to the citizens several times a year and can issue decrees to establish red letter days and national holidays. The president is the main authority for the granting of Belarusian citizen and can present <a href="/wiki/Orders,_decorations,_and_medals_of_Belarus" title="Orders, decorations, and medals of Belarus">state decorations</a> to honored individuals. The president also has the ability to determine the status of asylum seekers and grant <a href="/wiki/Pardon" title="Pardon">pardons</a> to convicted citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>As the Supreme <a href="/wiki/Commander-in-Chief" title="Commander-in-Chief" class="mw-redirect">Commander-in-Chief</a> of the Belarusian Armed Forces, the President has the duty to protect the Belarusian territory from internal and external forces. The president can call for a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a> in the following cases: natural disasters, a catastrophe, or unrest involving violence or the threat of violence. Regardless if the declaration affects the entire country or sections of it, the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Republic_of_Belarus" title="Council of the Republic of Belarus">Council of the Republic</a> must be notified by the President and must seek their approval within three days of notification. The same rules applies if the President issues a state of <a href="/wiki/Martial_law" title="Martial law">martial law</a> in the event of a possible military action against Belarus. The President has to form and head the Security Council of the Republic of Belarus, and can appoint and dismiss the State Secretary of the Security Council and the Supreme Command of the Armed Forces.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Oath_of_office">Oath of office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Oath of office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before any person can assume the office officially, an <a href="/wiki/Oath" title="Oath">oath</a> must be sworn within two months of the election results being finalized. The text of the oath is as follows:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;"><i>Assuming the office of President of the Republic of Belarus, I solemnly swear to faithfully serve the people of the Republic of Belarus, to respect and safeguard the rights and liberties of man and citizen, to abide by and protect the Constitution of the Republic of Belarus, and to discharge strictly and conscientiously the lofty duties that have been bestowed upon me.</i></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>During the inauguration ceremony, members of both houses of the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_the_Republic_of_Belarus" title="National Assembly of the Republic of Belarus" class="mw-redirect">National Assembly</a>, government ministers, officials and judges from the <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Court_of_Belarus" title="Constitutional Court of Belarus">Constitutional</a>, <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Belarus" title="Supreme Court of Belarus">Supreme</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economic_Court_of_Belarus" title="Economic Court of Belarus">Economic Court</a> must be present. Upon reading of the oath, any powers held by a previous president will be transferred to the president-elect. The text of the oath can be found in Article 83 of the Constitution.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Removal">Removal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Removal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Articles 87 through 89 of the Constitution deal with how the presidency can change hands in-between election cycles. The President has the ability to resign from office at anytime under Article 87. The letter of resignation is sent to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_Belarus" title="House of Representatives of Belarus">House of Representatives</a> and is accepted by them. The President has the ability to be removed from office if his physical or mental health is impaired under Article 88. In order for this to happen, a two-thirds majority must be reached in the House of Representatives and the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Republic_of_Belarus" title="Council of the Republic of Belarus">Council of the Republic</a> on the resolution to remove the President. An <a href="/wiki/Ad_hoc" title="Ad hoc">ad hoc</a> committee is formed and must make the determination about the state of health before any motion can begin. If the President has committed a grave crime, such as <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">treason</a>, one-third of the House must bring charges against the President formally. The investigation of the charges will be conducted by the Council of the Republic. In order to evict the President from office, a two-thirds majority is needed to vote in favor of conviction. The criminal case is further sent to the Supreme Court for review. The actions of either option must occur one month after the resolution is passed or the action will be considered void by the Constitution.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Privileges">Privileges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Privileges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Under Article 79 of the Constitution, the president is immune from arrest, with exception to the treason/grave crimes clauses listed in Article 88 in the same document. Also under Article 79, the honor and dignity of the president will be protected by national law.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Information, either printed in the news or reported on <a href="/wiki/Television_in_Belarus" title="Television in Belarus" class="mw-redirect">television</a>, that is considered <a href="/wiki/Defamation" title="Defamation">defamation</a> against the president is illegal under Article 5 of the Belarusian Law on Press.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The president also has an official residence in <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a> surrounded by the streets of <a href="/wiki/Karl_Marx" title="Karl Marx">Marx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Engels" title="Friedrich Engels">Engels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Kirov" title="Sergey Kirov">Kirov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Komsomol" title="Komsomol">Komsomol</a>. Like the American <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a>, the streets close to the residence are closed off to vehicular traffic and are patrolled by police forces.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbols">Symbols</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Symbols">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:117px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Presidential_Standard_of_Belarus.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Presidential_Standard_of_Belarus.svg/115px-Presidential_Standard_of_Belarus.svg.png" width="115" height="96" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Presidential_Standard_of_Belarus.svg/173px-Presidential_Standard_of_Belarus.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Presidential_Standard_of_Belarus.svg/230px-Presidential_Standard_of_Belarus.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="540" data-file-height="450" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Presidential_Standard_of_Belarus.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Presidential standard</div>

<p>Officially, the only symbol denoting the presence of the President is the standard of the office (Штандар Прэзідэнта Рэспублікі Беларусь). The standard, which has been in use since March 27, 1997, was adopted by a decree called "Concerning the Standard of the President of Republic of Belarus." signed into law by President Lukashenko.</p>
<p>The standard's design is an exact copy of the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Belarus" title="Flag of Belarus">national flag</a>, with the addition of the <a href="/wiki/National_emblem_of_Belarus" title="National emblem of Belarus">Belarusian national emblem</a> in gold. The standard's ratio of 5:6 differs from that of the national flag, making the standard almost square. The standard is bordered by a golden <a href="/wiki/Fringe_(trim)" title="Fringe (trim)">fringe</a>. There are several copies of the standard; the original is kept in the office of the President while other copies are used on buildings, residences and vehicles to denote his presence.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
