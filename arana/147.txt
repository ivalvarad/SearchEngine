<p><b>Hunan Province</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh"><a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B9%96%E5%8D%97" class="extiw" title="wikt:湖南">湖南</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin"><span class="unicode haudio" style="white-space:nowrap;"><span class="fn"><a href="/wiki/File:Zh-Hunan.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;<a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/73/Zh-Hunan.ogg" class="internal" title="Zh-Hunan.ogg">Húnán</a></span></span></span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Xiang_Chinese" title="Xiang Chinese">Hunanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shuangfeng_dialect" title="Shuangfeng dialect">Shuangfeng dialect</a>: <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[ɣəu˩˧nia˩˧]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Provinces of the People's Republic of China" class="mw-redirect">province</a> of the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China" class="mw-redirect">People's Republic of China</a>. It is located in <a href="/wiki/South_Central_China" title="South Central China">South Central China</a>, south of the middle course of the [[Yangtze River], and south of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Dongting" title="Lake Dongting" class="mw-redirect">Lake Dongting</a> (hence the name <i>Hunan</i>, which means "south of the lake").<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Hunan is sometimes called and officially abbreviated as "<a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B9%98" class="extiw" title="wikt:湘">湘</a>" (pinyin: Xiāng) for short,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> after the <a href="/wiki/Xiang_River" title="Xiang River">Xiang River</a> which runs through the province.</p>
<p>Hunan borders <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> Province in the north, <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a> Province to the east, <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> Province to the southeast, <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a> Province to the southwest, <a href="/wiki/Guizhou" title="Guizhou">Guizhou</a> Province to the west, and <a href="/wiki/Chongqing" title="Chongqing">Chongqing</a> to the northwest. The provincial <a href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)" class="mw-redirect">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Changsha" title="Changsha">Changsha</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="metadata plainlinks ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation">
<tr>
<td class="mbox-image">

<p>Hunan's primeval forests were first occupied by the ancestors of the modern <a href="/wiki/Miao_people" title="Miao people">Miao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tujia_people" title="Tujia people">Tujia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dong_people" title="Dong people" class="mw-redirect">Dong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yao_people" title="Yao people">Yao peoples</a>. It entered the written <a href="/wiki/History_of_China" title="History of China">history of China</a> around 350 BC, when under the kings of the <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Dynasty" title="Zhou Dynasty" class="mw-redirect">Zhou Dynasty</a>, it became part of the <a href="/wiki/State_of_Chu" title="State of Chu" class="mw-redirect">State of Chu</a>. After <a href="/wiki/Qin_(state)" title="Qin (state)">Qin</a> conquered the Chu heartland in 278 BC, the region came under the control of Qin, and then the <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a>. At this time, and for hundreds of years thereafter, it was a magnet for migration of <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a> from the north, who displaced or assimilated the indigenous people, cleared forests and began farming rice in the valleys and plains.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The agricultural colonization of the lowlands was carried out in part by the Han state, which managed river dikes to protect farmland from floods.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> To this day many of the small villages in Hunan are named after the Han families who settled there. Migration from the north was especially prevalent during the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Jin_Dynasty" title="Eastern Jin Dynasty" class="mw-redirect">Eastern Jin Dynasty</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_and_Northern_Dynasties" title="Southern and Northern Dynasties">Southern and Northern Dynasties</a> Periods, when nomadic invaders pushed these peoples south.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Five_Dynasties_and_Ten_Kingdoms_period" title="Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period">Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms</a> Period, Hunan was home to its own independent regime, <a href="/wiki/Chu_(Ten_Kingdoms)" title="Chu (Ten Kingdoms)">Ma Chu</a>.</p>
<p>Hunan and <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> became a part of the province of <a href="/wiki/Huguang" title="Huguang" class="mw-redirect">Huguang</a> (湖廣) until the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a>. Hunan province was created in 1664 from Huguang, renamed to its current name in 1723.</p>

<p>Hunan became an important communications center due to its position on the <a href="/wiki/Yangzi_River" title="Yangzi River" class="mw-redirect">Yangzi River</a>. It was an important centre of scholarly activity and <a href="/wiki/Confucian" title="Confucian" class="mw-redirect">Confucian</a> thought, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Yuelu_Academy" title="Yuelu Academy">Yuelu Academy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Changsha" title="Changsha">Changsha</a>. It was also on the Imperial Highway constructed between northern and southern China. The land produced grain so abundantly that it fed many parts of China with its surpluses. The population continued to climb until, by the nineteenth century, Hunan became overcrowded and prone to peasant uprisings. Some of the uprisings, such as the ten-year <a href="/wiki/Miao_Rebellion_1795-1806" title="Miao Rebellion 1795-1806" class="mw-redirect">Miao Rebellion of 1795–1806</a>, were caused by ethnic tensions. The <a href="/wiki/Taiping_Rebellion" title="Taiping Rebellion">Taiping Rebellion</a> began in the south in <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a> Province in 1850. The rebellion spread into Hunan and then further eastward along the Yangzi River valley. Ultimately, it was a Hunanese army under <a href="/wiki/Zeng_Guofan" title="Zeng Guofan">Zeng Guofan</a> who marched into <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a> to put down the uprising in 1864.</p>
<p>Hunan was relatively quiet until 1910 when there were uprisings against the crumbling Qing dynasty, which were followed by the Communist's <a href="/wiki/Autumn_Harvest_Uprising" title="Autumn Harvest Uprising">Autumn Harvest Uprising</a> of 1927. It was led by Hunanese native <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>, and established a short-lived Hunan Soviet in 1927. The Communists maintained a guerrilla army in the mountains along the Hunan-<a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a> border until 1934. Under pressure from the Nationalist <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> (KMT) forces, they began the <a href="/wiki/Long_March" title="Long March">Long March</a> to bases in <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi" title="Shaanxi">Shaanxi</a> Province. After the departure of the Communists, the KMT army fought against the Japanese in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_war" title="Second Sino-Japanese war" class="mw-redirect">second Sino-Japanese war</a>. They defended the Changsha until it fell in 1944. Japan launched <a href="/wiki/Operation_Ichigo" title="Operation Ichigo" class="mw-redirect">Operation Ichigo</a>, a plan to control the railroad from <a href="/wiki/Wuchang,_Hubei" title="Wuchang, Hubei" class="mw-redirect">Wuchang</a> to <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yuehan_Railway" title="Yuehan Railway" class="mw-redirect">Yuehan Railway</a>). Hunan was relatively unscathed by the civil war that followed the defeat of the Japanese in 1945. In 1949, the Communists returned once more as the Nationalists retreated southward.</p>
<p>As Mao Zedong's home province, Hunan supported the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a> of 1966–1976. However, it was slower than most provinces in adopting the reforms implemented by <a href="/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping" title="Deng Xiaoping">Deng Xiaoping</a> in the years that followed Mao's death in 1976.</p>
<p>Former Chinese Premier <a href="/wiki/Zhu_Rongji" title="Zhu Rongji">Zhu Rongji</a> is also Hunanese, as are the late <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="President of the People's Republic of China">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Liu_Shaoqi" title="Liu Shaoqi">Liu Shaoqi</a> and the late Marshal <a href="/wiki/Peng_Dehuai" title="Peng Dehuai">Peng Dehuai</a>.</p>

<p>Hunan is located on the south bank of the <a href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River" class="mw-redirect">Yangtze River</a>, about half way along its length, situated between 108° 47'–114° 16' east <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">longitude</a> and 24° 37'–30° 08' north <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a>. It covers an area of 211,800 square kilometres (81,800&#160;sq&#160;mi), making it the <a href="/wiki/List_of_People%27s_Republic_of_China_administrative_divisions_by_area" title="List of People's Republic of China administrative divisions by area" class="mw-redirect">10th largest</a> provincial-level division. The east, south and west sides of the province are surrounded by mountains and hills, such as the <a href="/wiki/Wuling_Mountains" title="Wuling Mountains">Wuling Mountains</a> to the northwest, the <a href="/wiki/Xuefeng_Mountains" title="Xuefeng Mountains">Xuefeng Mountains</a> to the west, the <a href="/wiki/Nanling_Mountains" title="Nanling Mountains" class="mw-redirect">Nanling Mountains</a> to the south, and the <a href="/wiki/Luoxiao_Mountains" title="Luoxiao Mountains">Luoxiao Mountains</a> to the east. The mountains and hills occupy more than 80% of the area and plains comprises less than 20% of the whole province.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Xiang_River" title="Xiang River">Xiang</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Zi_River" title="Zi River">Zi</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Yuan_River" title="Yuan River">Yuan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lishui_River" title="Lishui River">Lishui</a> Rivers converge on the Yangtze River at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Dongting" title="Lake Dongting" class="mw-redirect">Lake Dongting</a> in the north of Hunan. The center and northern parts are somewhat low and a U-shaped basin, open in the north and with Lake Dongting as its center. Most of Hunan lies in the basins of four major tributaries of the Yangtze River.</p>
<p>Lake Dongting is the largest lake in the province and the second largest freshwater lake of China. Due to the reclamation of land for agriculture, Lake Dongting has been subdivided into many smaller lakes, though there is now a trend to reverse some of the reclamation, which had damaged <a href="/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetland</a> habitats surrounding the lake.</p>
<p>Hunan's climate is <a href="/wiki/Subtropical" title="Subtropical" class="mw-redirect">subtropical</a>, and, under the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>, is classified as being <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical" title="Humid subtropical" class="mw-redirect">humid subtropical</a> (Köppen <i>Cfa</i>), with short, cool, damp winters, very hot and humid summers, and plenty of rainfall. January temperatures average 3 to 8&#160;°C (37 to 46&#160;°F) while July temperatures average around 27 to 30&#160;°C (81 to 86&#160;°F). Average annual precipitation is 1,200 to 1,700 millimetres (47 to 67&#160;in).</p>

<p>The fourteen <a href="/wiki/Prefecture_of_China" title="Prefecture of China" class="mw-redirect">prefecture</a>-level divisions of Hunan are subdivided into 122 <a href="/wiki/County_(People%27s_Republic_of_China)" title="County (People's Republic of China)" class="mw-redirect">county</a>-level divisions (35 <a href="/wiki/District_of_China" title="District of China" class="mw-redirect">districts</a>, 16 <a href="/wiki/County-level_cities" title="County-level cities" class="mw-redirect">county-level cities</a>, 64 counties, 7 <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_counties" title="Autonomous counties" class="mw-redirect">autonomous counties</a>). Those are in turn divided into 2587 <a href="/wiki/Townships_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Townships of the People's Republic of China">township</a>-level divisions (1098 <a href="/wiki/Town_of_China" title="Town of China" class="mw-redirect">towns</a>, 1158 <a href="/wiki/Townships_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Townships of the People's Republic of China">townships</a>, 98 <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_township" title="Ethnic township" class="mw-redirect">ethnic townships</a>, 225 <a href="/wiki/Subdistrict" title="Subdistrict">subdistricts</a>, and eight <a href="/wiki/District_public_office" title="District public office" class="mw-redirect">district public offices</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Hunan" title="Politics of Hunan">Politics of Hunan</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_provincial_leaders_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="List of provincial leaders of the People's Republic of China" class="mw-redirect">List of provincial leaders of the People's Republic of China</a></div>
<p>The politics of Hunan is structured in a dual party-government system like all other governing institutions in <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">mainland China</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Hunan" title="Governor of Hunan" class="mw-redirect">Governor of Hunan</a> is the highest-ranking official in the People's Government of Hunan. However, in the province's dual party-government governing system, the Governor has less power than the Hunan <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> Provincial Committee Secretary, colloquially termed the "Hunan <a href="/wiki/CPC_Party_Chief" title="CPC Party Chief" class="mw-redirect">CPC Party Chief</a>".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hunan's traditional crops are rice and cotton.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> As of the mid 19th century, Hunan exported <a href="/wiki/Rhubarb" title="Rhubarb">rhubarb</a>, musk, honey, tobacco, <a href="/wiki/Hemp" title="Hemp">hemp</a>, and birds.<sup id="cite_ref-Roberts1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roberts1-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Lake_Dongting" title="Lake Dongting" class="mw-redirect">Lake Dongting</a> area is an important center of <a href="/wiki/Ramie" title="Ramie">ramie</a> production, and Hunan is also an important center of tea cultivation. Aside from agricultural products, in recent years Hunan has grown to become an important center for steel, machinery and electronics production, especially as China's manufacturing sector moves away from coastal provinces such as <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zhejiang" title="Zhejiang">Zhejiang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lengshuijiang" title="Lengshuijiang">Lengshuijiang</a> area is noted for its <a href="/wiki/Stibnite" title="Stibnite">stibnite</a> mines, and is one of the major centers of <a href="/wiki/Antimony" title="Antimony">antimony</a> extraction in China.</p>
<p>Hunan is also well known for a few international makers of construction equipments such as <a href="/wiki/Concrete_pumps" title="Concrete pumps" class="mw-redirect">concrete pumps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crane_(machine)" title="Crane (machine)">cranes</a>, etc. These companies include <a href="/wiki/Sany_Group" title="Sany Group" class="mw-redirect">Sany Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zoomlion" title="Zoomlion">Zoomlion</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sunward_Intelligent_Equipment&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sunward Intelligent Equipment (page does not exist)">Sunward</a>. Sany is one of the major players in the world. <a href="/wiki/Liuyang" title="Liuyang">Liuyang</a> is the major maker of <a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">fireworks</a> in the world. <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Its nominal GDP for 2011 was 1.90 trillion yuan (US$300 billion). Its per capita GDP was 20,226 yuan (US$2,961).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_and_technological_development_zones">Economic and technological development zones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economic and technological development zones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Changsha" title="Changsha">Changsha</a> National Economic and Technical Development Zone</li>
</ul>
<p>The Changsha National Economic and Technology Development Zone was founded in 1992. It is located east of Changsha. The total planned area is 38.6&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (14.9&#160;sq&#160;mi) and the current area is 14&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (5.4&#160;sq&#160;mi). Near the zone is National Highways G319 and G107 as well as Jingzhu Highway. Besides that, it is very close to the downtown and the railway station. The distance between the zone and the airport is 8&#160;km (5.0&#160;mi). The major industries in the zone include high-tech industry, biology project technology and new material industry.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Changsha National New &amp; Hi-Tech Industrial Development Zone</li>
<li>Chenzhou Export Processing Zone</li>
</ul>
<p>Approved by the State Council, Chenzhou Export processing Zone (CEPZ) was established in 2005 and is the only export processing zone in Hunan province. The scheduled production area of CEPZ covers 3km2. The industrial positioning of CEPZ is to concentrate on developing export-oriented hi-tech industries, including electronic information, precision machinery, and new-type materials. The zone has good infrastructure, and the enterprises inside could enjoy the preferential policies of tax-exemption, tax-guarantee and tax-refunding. By the end of the “<a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Five-Year_Plan_(People%27s_Republic_of_China)" title="Eleventh Five-Year Plan (People's Republic of China)" class="mw-redirect">Eleventh Five-Year Plan</a>”, the CEPZ achieved a total export and import volume of over US$1 billion and provided more than 50,000 jobs. It aimed to be one of the first-class export processing zones in China.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zhuzhou" title="Zhuzhou">Zhuzhou</a> National New &amp; Hi-Tech Industrial Development Zone</li>
</ul>
<p>Zhuzhou Hi-Tech Industrial Development Zone was founded in 1992. Its total planned area is 35&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (14&#160;sq&#160;mi). It is very close to National Highway G320. The major industries in the zone include biotechnology, food processing and heavy industry. In 2007, the park signed a cooperation contract with Beijing Automobile Industry, one of the largest auto makers in China, which will set up a manufacturing base in Zhuzhou HTP.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistic">Artistic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Artistic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Hunan Province is accredited with being filled with skilled craftsmen and women who create embroidered silks, carved jade, and other skillfully hand made artistic goods of international quality.</p>

<p>As of the 2000 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>, the population of Hunan is 64,400,700 consisting of forty-one <a href="/wiki/List_of_Chinese_ethnic_groups" title="List of Chinese ethnic groups" class="mw-redirect">ethnic groups</a>. Its population grew 6.17% (3,742,700) from its 1990 levels. According to the census, 89.79% (57,540,000) identified themselves as <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han people</a>, 10.21% (6,575,300) as <a href="/wiki/Minority_group" title="Minority group">minority groups</a>. The minority groups are <a href="/wiki/Tujia_people" title="Tujia people">Tujia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hmong_people" title="Hmong people">Miao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dong_people" title="Dong people" class="mw-redirect">Dong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yao_people" title="Yao people">Yao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bai_people" title="Bai people">Bai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hui_people" title="Hui people">Hui</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zhuang_people" title="Zhuang people">Zhuang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uyghur_people" title="Uyghur people" class="mw-redirect">Uyghurs</a> and so on.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hunanese_Uyghurs">Hunanese Uyghurs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Hunanese Uyghurs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Around 5,000 Uyghurs live around <a href="/wiki/Taoyuan_County,_Hunan" title="Taoyuan County, Hunan" class="mw-redirect">Taoyuan County</a> and other parts of <a href="/wiki/Changde" title="Changde">Changde</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hui_people" title="Hui people">Hui</a> and Uyghurs have intermarried in this area.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> In addition to eating pork, the Uygurs of Changde practice other Han Chinese customs, like ancestor worship at graves. Some Uyghurs from Xinjiang visit the Hunan Uyghurs out of curiosity or interest.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Also, the Uyghurs of Hunan do not speak the Uyghur language, instead, they speak Chinese as their native language.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Religion in Hunan<sup id="cite_ref-Wang2015_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wang2015-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>note 1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<div class="legend"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color:FireBrick; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Chinese_ancestral_religion" title="Chinese ancestral religion" class="mw-redirect">Chinese ancestral religion</a> (20.19%)</div>
<div class="legend"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color:DodgerBlue; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> (0.77%)</div>
<div class="legend"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color:Honeydew; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Other religions or not religious people<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>note 2<span>]</span></a></sup> (79.04%)</div>
</div>

<p>The predominant religions in Hunan are <a href="/wiki/Chinese_folk_religion" title="Chinese folk religion">Chinese folk religions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Taoist traditions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Buddhism" title="Chinese Buddhism">Chinese Buddhism</a>. According to surveys conducted in 2007 and 2009, 20.19% of the population believes and is involved in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_ancestral_religion" title="Chinese ancestral religion" class="mw-redirect">cults of ancestors</a>, while 0.77% of the population identifies as Christian.<sup id="cite_ref-Wang2015_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wang2015-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> The reports didn't give figures for other types of religion; 79.04% of the population may be either irreligious or involved in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_folk_religion" title="Chinese folk religion">worship of nature deities</a>, Buddhism, <a href="/wiki/Confucianism" title="Confucianism">Confucianism</a>, Taoism, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_salvationist_religions" title="Chinese salvationist religions">folk religious sects</a>, and small minorities of <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Xiang_Chinese" title="Xiang Chinese">Xiang Chinese</a>(湘語) is the biggest native language to Hunan province, and it is also a group of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_dialects" title="Chinese dialects" class="mw-redirect">Chinese dialects</a> spoken in most parts of Hunan and in a few adjacent areas.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hunan_cuisine" title="Hunan cuisine">Hunanese cuisine</a> is noted for its use of <a href="/wiki/Chili_pepper" title="Chili pepper">chili peppers</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/N%C3%BC_shu" title="Nü shu" class="mw-redirect">Nü shu</a> is a writing system that was used exclusively among women in <a href="/wiki/Jiangyong_County" title="Jiangyong County">Jiangyong County</a>.</p>
<p>Hunan's culture industry generated 87 billion yuan (US$11.76 billion) in economic value in 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> a major contributor to the province's economic growth. The industry accounts for 7.5 percent of the region's GDP - 0.9 percentage points higher than the previous year.</p>
<p>In recent years, Hunan's cultural exports to the rest of China have been making a big impact. For instance, the <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Girl_(contest)" title="Super Girl (contest)" class="mw-redirect">Super Girl</a></i> contest − a Chinese version of <i><a href="/wiki/Pop_Idol" title="Pop Idol">Pop Idol</a></i> − was a significant and ground-breaking achievement for Chinese television. It included live broadcast, voting by mobile phones, and featured quirky and atypical characters. In 2005, the gross profit for the competition was 17.79 million yuan (US$2.48 million). Another television export has been the television cartoon series <i><a href="/wiki/Lan_Mao" title="Lan Mao" class="mw-redirect">Blue Cat</a></i>. As a result of these programs, Golden Eagle Broadcasting System's Hunan satellite television channel has become the most-watched regionally produced channel in China, with over 5.6 million viewers. According to Golden Eagle, its programming also airs in the US, Japan, and Europe.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Located in the south central part of the Chinese mainland, Hunan has long been known for its natural beauty. It is surrounded by mountains on the east, west, and south, and by the Yangtze River on the north. Its mixture of mountains and water makes it among the most beautiful provinces in China. For thousands of years, the region has been a major center of agriculture, growing rice, tea, and oranges. China's first all glass suspension bridge was also opened in Hunan, in Shiniuzhai National Geological Park.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
