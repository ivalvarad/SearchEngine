<p>The <b>Luzhniki Stadium</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Стадион «Лужники»</span>; <small>IPA:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Russian" title="Help:IPA for Russian">[stədʲɪˈon lʊʐnʲɪˈkʲi]</a></span>), is a sports stadium in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. Its total <a href="/wiki/Seating_capacity" title="Seating capacity">seating capacity</a> is 78,360 seats, all covered (upgraded). The stadium is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_Olympic_Complex" title="Luzhniki Olympic Complex">Luzhniki Olympic Complex</a>, and is located in <a href="/wiki/Khamovniki_District" title="Khamovniki District">Khamovniki District</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Administrative_Okrug" title="Central Administrative Okrug">Central Administrative Okrug</a> of Moscow city. The name <i>Luzhniki</i> derives from the flood meadows in the bend of <a href="/wiki/Moskva_River" title="Moskva River">Moskva River</a> where the stadium was built, translating roughly as "The Meadows".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luzhniki_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the past its field was mainly used (at various times) for <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> games played by <a href="/wiki/PFC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PFC CSKA Moscow">PFC CSKA Moscow</a>, <a href="/wiki/FC_Torpedo_Moscow" title="FC Torpedo Moscow">Torpedo Moscow</a>, who also own the sports complex, and <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">Spartak Moscow</a>, but none of these teams are based there any more. Today it is mainly used as one of the home grounds of the <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russian national football team</a>. It is one of the few major European stadia to use an artificial pitch, having installed a FIFA-approved <a href="/wiki/FieldTurf" title="FieldTurf">FieldTurf</a> pitch in 2002. The pitch is necessary because regular grass pitches cannot withstand the harsh Russian winters and must be replaced at high cost. However, a temporary natural grass pitch was installed for the <a href="/wiki/2008_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2008 UEFA Champions League Final">2008 UEFA Champions League Final</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The stadium is also used from time to time for various other sporting events and for concerts.</p>

<p>The stadium was built in 1955–56 as the Central Lenin Stadium, and was officially opened on 31 July 1956.<sup id="cite_ref-stadiumguide_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stadiumguide-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It was the <a href="/wiki/National_stadium" title="National stadium" class="mw-redirect">national stadium</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, and is now the national stadium of Russia.</p>
<p>It was the chief venue for the <a href="/wiki/1980_Summer_Olympics" title="1980 Summer Olympics">1980 Summer Olympics</a>, the spectator capacity being 103,000 at that time. The events hosted in this stadium were the <a href="/wiki/1980_Summer_Olympics_opening_ceremony" title="1980 Summer Olympics opening ceremony">Opening</a> and <a href="/wiki/1980_Summer_Olympics_closing_ceremony" title="1980 Summer Olympics closing ceremony">Closing</a> Ceremonies, <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1980_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1980 Summer Olympics">Athletics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1980_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1980 Summer Olympics">Football finals</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Equestrian_at_the_1980_Summer_Olympics" title="Equestrian at the 1980 Summer Olympics">Individual Jumping Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1992, the stadium was renamed the Luzhniki Stadium. An extensive renovation in 1996 saw the construction of a roof over the stands, and the refurbishment of the seating areas, resulting in a decrease in capacity.<sup id="cite_ref-stadiumguide_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stadiumguide-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The stadium hosted the <a href="/wiki/1999_UEFA_Cup_Final" title="1999 UEFA Cup Final">1999 UEFA Cup Final</a> in which <a href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C." class="mw-redirect">Parma</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup" class="mw-redirect">UEFA Cup</a> final to be played as a single fixture.</p>
<p>The Luzhniki Stadium was chosen by <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> to host the <a href="/wiki/2008_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2008 UEFA Champions League Final">2008 UEFA Champions League Final</a> won by <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> who beat <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> in the first all-English Champions League Final on May 21. Prior to the match some skeptics questioned the state of the <a href="/wiki/Association_football_pitch" title="Association football pitch" class="mw-redirect">pitch</a> and also the ability of Russian authorities to keep order amongst the traveling British fans; however, the match passed incident-free and a spokesman for the British Embassy in Moscow said, "The security and logistical arrangements put in place by the Russian authorities have been first-rate, as has been their cooperation with their visiting counterparts from the UK."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2013 the stadium hosted the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2013 World Championships in Athletics">World Athletics Championships</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a> has been awarded to Russia and the Luzhniki Stadium has been selected by the <a href="/wiki/Russia_2018_FIFA_World_Cup_bid" title="Russia 2018 FIFA World Cup bid">Russia 2018 FIFA World Cup bid</a> as the venue for the final which will be held on July 15, 2018. The stadium will then join Rome's <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Olimpico" title="Stadio Olimpico">Stadio Olimpico</a>, Berlin's <a href="/wiki/Olympiastadion_(Berlin)" title="Olympiastadion (Berlin)">Olympiastadion</a>, Munich's <a href="/wiki/Olympiastadion_(Munich)" title="Olympiastadion (Munich)">Olympiastadion</a> and London's <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley Stadium</a> as the only stadia to have hosted the finals of the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a>, UEFA's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">European Cup/Champions League</a> and featured as a main stadium of the <a href="/wiki/Summer_Olympic_Games" title="Summer Olympic Games">Summer Olympic Games</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_events">Other events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luzhniki_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Luzhniki Stadium hosted the final game of the 1957 <a href="/wiki/Ice_Hockey_World_Championship" title="Ice Hockey World Championship" class="mw-redirect">Ice Hockey World Championship</a> between <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, attended by a crowd of 55,000 and setting a new world record at the time. Other events staged include the <a href="/wiki/1973_Summer_Universiade" title="1973 Summer Universiade">1973 Summer Universiade</a>, the 1989 <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Music_Peace_Festival" title="Moscow Music Peace Festival">Moscow Music Peace Festival</a> and the inaugural <a href="/wiki/World_Youth_Games" title="World Youth Games" class="mw-redirect">World Youth Games</a> in 1998. The <a href="/wiki/2013_Rugby_World_Cup_Sevens" title="2013 Rugby World Cup Sevens">2013 Rugby World Cup Sevens</a> was held at the ground. It also hosts European games for other football clubs, such as <a href="/wiki/Rubin_Kazan" title="Rubin Kazan" class="mw-redirect">Rubin Kazan</a>, owing to the home stadiums of the respective clubs not meeting <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> standards.</p>
<p>Artists such as <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> (1993 <a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Tour" title="Dangerous Tour" class="mw-redirect">Dangerous Tour</a>), <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> (2006 <a href="/wiki/Confessions_Tour" title="Confessions Tour">Confessions Tour</a>), <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sick_Of_The_Studio_%2707" title="Sick Of The Studio '07" class="mw-redirect">Sick Of The Studio '07 tour</a>), <a href="/wiki/Kino_(band)" title="Kino (band)">Kino</a>, <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a> (2010 <a href="/wiki/U2_360%C2%B0_Tour" title="U2 360° Tour">U2 360° Tour</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> have all performed concerts in the stadium. It also makes an appearance in the Russian film <i><a href="/wiki/Night_Watch_(2004_film)" title="Night Watch (2004 film)">Night Watch</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Ночной дозор, <i>Nochnoy Dozor</i></span>), during the power shut-down scene when the power station goes into overload. The stadium is seen with a match taking place, and then the lights go out. <a href="/wiki/New_Japan_Pro_Wrestling" title="New Japan Pro Wrestling">New Japan Pro Wrestling</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> promotion, ran a show in 1989 as well. Most recently, <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a> played at the stadium on July 22, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Alisa" title="Alisa">Alisa</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Shabash_(album)" title="Shabash (album)">Shabash</a></i> is taken from two concerts performed here over two nights in late October 1990.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1982_Luzhniki_Disaster">1982 Luzhniki Disaster</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luzhniki_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1982 Luzhniki Disaster">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_disaster" title="Luzhniki disaster">Luzhniki disaster</a></div>
<p>On October 20, 1982, <a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_disaster" title="Luzhniki disaster">disaster struck</a> during the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup" class="mw-redirect">UEFA Cup</a> match between FC Spartak Moscow and <a href="/wiki/HFC_Haarlem" title="HFC Haarlem">HFC Haarlem</a>. 66 people died in the <a href="/wiki/Stampede" title="Stampede">stampede</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> which made it Russia's worst sporting disaster at the time.</p>
