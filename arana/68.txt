<p>The <b>R-13</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Submarine-launched_ballistic_missile" title="Submarine-launched ballistic missile">submarine-launched ballistic missile</a> (SLBM) developed by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> starting around 1955. It was assigned the <a href="/wiki/NATO_reporting_name" title="NATO reporting name">NATO reporting name</a> <b>SS-N-4 Sark</b> and carried the <a href="/wiki/GRAU" title="GRAU">GRAU</a> index <b>4K50</b>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R-13_(missile)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Development of the R-13 was authorised by the Soviet Supreme Council on 25 July 1955 for use on the <a href="/wiki/Golf-class_submarine" title="Golf-class submarine">Project 629</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hotel-class_submarine" title="Hotel-class submarine">Project 658</a> submarines. The design work was started by <a href="/wiki/S.P._Korolev_Rocket_and_Space_Corporation_Energia" title="S.P. Korolev Rocket and Space Corporation Energia">OKB-1</a> under <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Korolyov" title="Sergey Korolyov" class="mw-redirect">Sergei Korolev</a> before being transferred to CB Miasskoe engineering / <a href="/wiki/Makeyev_Rocket_Design_Bureau" title="Makeyev Rocket Design Bureau">Makeyev Rocket Design Bureau</a> (chief designer - <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Makeyev" title="Viktor Makeyev">Viktor Makeyev</a>). Final technical specifications was approved by 11 January 1956. Serial production was undertaken at Zlatoust Machinery Plant in 1959. The R-13 was a single-stage <a href="/wiki/Liquid-fuel_rocket" title="Liquid-fuel rocket" class="mw-redirect">liquid-fuel rocket</a> and entered service in 1961. This missile was somewhat similar in design to the <a href="/wiki/Scud" title="Scud">R-11FM</a> missile, which caused some confusion in Western <a href="/wiki/Intelligence_service" title="Intelligence service" class="mw-redirect">intelligence services</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>. The missiles were phased out from 1965 to 1975.</p>
<p>This missile was the first Soviet design to use a small set of <a href="/wiki/Rocket_engine" title="Rocket engine">rocket engines</a> (<a href="/wiki/Vernier_thruster" title="Vernier thruster">Vernier thrusters</a>) to perform course and trajectory alterations instead of <a href="/wiki/Aerodynamic" title="Aerodynamic" class="mw-redirect">aerodynamic</a> <a href="/wiki/Flight_control_surfaces" title="Flight control surfaces">control surfaces</a>, although a set of four <a href="/wiki/Stabilizer_(aircraft)" title="Stabilizer (aircraft)" class="mw-redirect">stabilizers</a> were used to keep the missile on-course during initial flight.</p>
<p>During initial testing before the missiles were deployed, 26 of 32 missiles (81%) were successfully launched. While the systems were deployed from 1961 to 1975, 225 of 311 launches (72%) were successful.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">Specifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R-13_(missile)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Specifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Length: 11.8 m (38&#160;ft 9 in)</li>
<li>Diameter: 1.3 m (4&#160;ft 3 in)</li>
<li>Diameter (to stabilizers): 1.9 m (6&#160;ft 3 in)</li>
<li>Launch weight: 13.7 t</li>
<li>Warhead: single nuclear: 1.2 to 2.0 Mt (perhaps as low as 1.0 Mt)</li>
<li>Propulsion: liquid-fuel rocket, single stage
<ul>
<li>Oxidizer: <a href="/wiki/Red_fuming_nitric_acid" title="Red fuming nitric acid">AK-271</a></li>
<li>Fuel: <a href="/wiki/Tonka_(fuel)" title="Tonka (fuel)">TG-02</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Range: about 600&#160;km (370&#160;mi)</li>
<li>Launching technique: surfaced</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Circular_error_probable" title="Circular error probable">CEP</a>: 1.8 to 4&#160;km (1.1 to 2.5 miles)</li>
</ul>
