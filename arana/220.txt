<p>The character first appeared as Charles Little Sky in <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a></i> #304 (June 1989) and as Portal in <i><a href="/wiki/Darkhawk" title="Darkhawk">Darkhawk</a></i> #5 (July 1991).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fictional_character_biography">Fictional character biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fictional character biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Native American <b>Charles Little Sky</b> was born in Hartsdale, <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>. As a teenager, he manifested his dimension-spanning powers during a confrontation between the <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puma_(comics)" title="Puma (comics)">Puma</a>, the superhuman protector of Little Sky's tribe. Little Sky fled the <a href="/wiki/Indian_reservation" title="Indian reservation">reservation</a> he lived on, moving to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> where he took a job as a construction worker. He was followed by Puma, who had set out in pursuit of Little Sky out of fear that the powers he'd soon manifest would prove dangerous. When Puma finally tracked him down at <a href="/wiki/Ellis_Island" title="Ellis Island">Ellis Island</a>, Little Sky's powers activated for the first time, opening a portal to the dimension where the <a href="/wiki/U-Foes" title="U-Foes">U-Foes</a> had been exiled, freeing them. The U-Foes attempted to kill Little Sky to keep him from using his powers to banish them again, and the Avengers and Puma were forced to team up to protect him. During the fight, Little Sky escaped, using his powers and began traveling the dimensions.<sup id="cite_ref-first_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-first-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Along the way he picked up a variety of weapons, including a gun that fired 'energy harpoons,' and learned to control his powers. In one dimension he encountered <a href="/w/index.php?title=Overhawk&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Overhawk (page does not exist)">Kistur</a>, the leader of an intergalactic gang of criminals who was armed with one of the android <a href="/wiki/Darkhawk" title="Darkhawk">Darkhawk</a> bodies created by <a href="/wiki/Evilhawk" title="Evilhawk">Dargin Bokk</a>. Kistur asked Little Sky to join the gang so they could use his powers to plunder other dimensions. Little Sky refused and Kistur tried to kill him, but Little Sky fought back and ended up accidentally shooting Kistur's Darkhawk amulet, the focal point of his powers, out of his chest. The loss of the amulet killed Kistur's Darkhawk body, and Little Sky removed and donned Kistur's body armor for further protection. Worried that Kistur might revive, Little Sky attempted to destroy the amulet, but when that failed he discarded it in another dimension.<sup id="cite_ref-kistur_origin_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kistur_origin-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Little Sky eventually managed to return to Earth, opening a portal to a <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museum</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> that <a href="/wiki/Darkhawk" title="Darkhawk">Chris Powell</a> and his family were touring. Powell transformed into his Darkhawk persona, and Portal, mistaking Powell for a reborn Kistur, attacked. Darkhawk managed to incapacitate Portal, who was taken in federal custody by a <a href="/wiki/Guardsman_(comics)" title="Guardsman (comics)">Guardsman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-as_portal_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-as_portal-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal had been injured during the fight and was placed in a hospital under the guard of <a href="/wiki/Captain_America" title="Captain America">Captain America</a>. The <a href="/wiki/U-Foes" title="U-Foes">U-Foes</a> wanted Portal to take them to a dimension they'd once happened upon while they were exiled from Earth, and they attacked the hospital. Captain America, Darkhawk and <a href="/wiki/Daredevil_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Daredevil (Marvel Comics)">Daredevil</a> defeated the U-Foes, but Portal revived and after explaining how he had acquired parts of his armor from an opponent resembling Darkhawk, escaped to another dimension during the fight.<sup id="cite_ref-U-Foes_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-U-Foes-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Portal surfaced again to retrieve his weapons and equipment from a federal research center, battling some Guardsmen.<sup id="cite_ref-first_brotherhood_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-first_brotherhood-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal was targeted by the <a href="/wiki/Brotherhood_of_Mutants" title="Brotherhood of Mutants">Brotherhood of Evil Mutants</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Toad_(comics)" title="Toad (comics)">Toad</a>, whose member <a href="/wiki/Sauron_(comics)" title="Sauron (comics)">Sauron</a> brainwashed Portal into assisting them.<sup id="cite_ref-second_brotherhood_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-second_brotherhood-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> He was rescued by Darkhawk, <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sleepwalker_(comics)" title="Sleepwalker (comics)">Sleepwalker</a>, after which he escaped again into another dimensional warp.<sup id="cite_ref-sleepwalker_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sleepwalker-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Portal would later return as Darkhawk's ally, protecting New York in his stead at a time when Darkhawk seemed to be dying and needed time to recuperate. During this time, Portal was targeted by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaper_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shaper (comics) (page does not exist)">Shaper</a>, a superhuman <a href="/wiki/Snuff_film" title="Snuff film">snuff</a> artist who'd targeted Darkhawk for death. Shaper ended up going after Portal instead when Portal began using Chris Powell's amulet to become the Darkhawk in Chris's place. Meanwhile, members of the <a href="/wiki/Mahari" title="Mahari" class="mw-disambig">Mahari</a> race, another alien species from the same home world as Kistur, took control of the Darkhawk ship in a plan to avenge Kistur. They reanimated Kistur in a redesigned Darkhawk android as Overhawk, and went after Portal's family. In the end, Portal and Darkhawk managed to prevent them from destroying the Earth.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (April 2009)">volume&#160;&amp;&#160;issue&#160;needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Portal is not a criminal, and cannot truly be called a villain. He is not so much evil as <a href="/wiki/Amoralism" title="Amoralism" class="mw-redirect">amoral</a>, mistrustful of others because of his being harassed by those who would exploit his mutant abilities. While he may return a favor to those who have helped him, as he did by retrieving Spider-Man when Sleepwalker had him freed from Sauron's mind control,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (January 2013)">volume&#160;&amp;&#160;issue&#160;needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and filling in for Darkhawk because of their past understanding,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (January 2013)">volume&#160;&amp;&#160;issue&#160;needed</span></a></i>]</sup> he is otherwise a loner who is not generally inclined to heroism. He will do whatever he feels he must to survive, although never more than necessary, such as when he used <a href="/wiki/Sleeping_gas" title="Sleeping gas">sleeping gas</a> to subdue the technicians studying his Darkhawk armor, not needing to kill them.</p>
<p>Charles is one of the few mutants that retained their superhuman powers after the <a href="/wiki/Decimation_(comics)" title="Decimation (comics)">M-Day</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (May 2009)">volume&#160;&amp;&#160;issue&#160;needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He is shown as the director of <a href="/wiki/A.R.M.O.R." title="A.R.M.O.R." class="mw-redirect">A.R.M.O.R.</a> and he used his powers to transport <a href="/wiki/Machine_Man" title="Machine Man">Machine Man</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jocasta_(comics)" title="Jocasta (comics)">Jocasta</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Zombies" title="Marvel Zombies">Marvel Zombies</a> universe.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> He collects Jocasta after Machine Man retrieves a sample of the still-living <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Fisk" title="Vanessa Fisk">Vanessa Fisk</a>'s tissues, but is forced to leave a badly damaged Machine Man behind.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> After fending off with the help of Jocasta, Machine Man and the 616-Earth Morbius the Zombies that invaded A.R.M.O.R., Portal discovered that some of them managed to escape. He then agreed Morbius' project to reform the <a href="/wiki/Midnight_Sons" title="Midnight Sons">Midnight Sons</a> to destroy the living dead.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers.2C_abilities.2C_and_equipment">Powers, abilities, and equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Powers, abilities, and equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Portal is a <a href="/wiki/Mutant_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Mutant (Marvel Comics)">mutant</a> capable of opening rifts in space passing through extra-dimensional warps to transport himself and others. His portals allow instantaneous travel between different vibratory-attuned planes of reality, or "dimensions". Opening a portal without preparation will give him access to another dimension completely at random.</p>
<p>He seems to possess some talent for finding dimensions he has been to before, yet he could not immediately find Earth again once he first got lost in the dimensional planes. Presumably, then, Portal has a kind of extrasensory "marking" ability, allowing him to record the space/time coordinates of a dimension while he is present in it, so that he could return to it again in the future if he chooses. He could not automatically return to Earth because he had not consciously "marked" it before leaving. Portal is capable of using his powers for teleportation, traveling instantly across about a few miles within a single dimension. Trying to transport himself more than a few miles in one jump, however, will destabilize the portal and send him off into another dimension, even if he is trying to stay anchored in one. Dimensional warps created by Portal cease to exist when he is rendered unconscious.</p>
<p>Portal has also been shown to be able to home in on other people who have gone through one of his portals, opening a new gateway to retrieve or follow them if necessary. He used this ability to rescue <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a> from the dimension he had thrown the wall-crawler into while under <a href="/wiki/Sauron_(comics)" title="Sauron (comics)">Sauron</a>'s control.</p>
<p>Portal is also armed with a wide variety of weaponry, including a huge gun that shoots 'energy <a href="/wiki/Harpoons" title="Harpoons" class="mw-redirect">harpoons</a>' (fires concussive force blasts capable of leveling an office building), a hand-weapon (capable of firing a fast-hardening adhesive substance which impedes physical movement of target), a wheel (a <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span>-inch-thick (13&#160;mm) throwing disc which can separate into components with independent guidance systems, each of which contains a burst of concussive force equal to several hand grenades), and a suit of body armor composed of alien materials that he stole from a dead <a href="/wiki/Darkhawk" title="Darkhawk">Darkhawk</a> android that has been outfitted to allow him to survive in space. Portal also carries a supply of adhesive ammunition contained in his belt, and a directional mechanism that focuses his warp power.</p>
<p>Little Sky also has a quantity of gymnastics training.<sup id="cite_ref-kistur_origin_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kistur_origin-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> He is highly skilled in the use of his own weaponry, and a skilled motorcyclist.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dimensions_and_planets">Dimensions and planets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dimensions and planets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Portal has reached a wide variety of dimensions with his powers, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>The Asta Dimension, where he encountered Kistur<sup id="cite_ref-kistur_origin_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kistur_origin-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Dormammu" title="Dormammu">Dark Dimension</a>, home of the Mindless Ones, where he banished Spider-Man under the influence of Sauron<sup id="cite_ref-second_brotherhood_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-second_brotherhood-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Zombies" title="Marvel Zombies">Marvel Zombies</a> dimension<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>The Mindscape, Sleepwalker's home dimension, which Sleepwalker once observed Portal pass through.</li>
<li>Null Space, the home of Dargin Bokk's Darkhawk ship. (It is unknown when or how Little Sky discovered the location of the Darkhawk ship, but he was already familiar with it when Darkhawk asked Little Sky to take him there.)</li>
<li>The Szell Dimension, where he acquired the device that guides his teleportation.</li>
</ul>
<p>He's also visited planets in unnamed dimensions, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>Jahraide, where he outfitted his armor with apparatus that lets him survive in space.</li>
</ul>
