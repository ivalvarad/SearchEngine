			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Wyndham Portal, 1st Viscount Portal</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Wyndham Raymond Portal, 1st Viscount Portal</b> <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">PC</a> <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Saint_Michael_and_Saint_George" title="Order of Saint Michael and Saint George" class="mw-redirect">GCMG</a> <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">DSO</a> <a href="/wiki/Royal_Victorian_Order" title="Royal Victorian Order">MVO</a> (9 April 1885 – 6 May 1949) was a British politician.</p>
<p>The eldest son of Sir William Wyndam Portal, 2nd Baronet, and Florence Elizabeth Mary Glyn CBE, daughter of Hon. St Leger Glyn, 2nd son of <a href="/wiki/George_Glyn,_1st_Baron_Wolverton" title="George Glyn, 1st Baron Wolverton">George Glyn, 1st Baron Wolverton</a>, he was educated at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christ_Church,_Oxford" title="Christ Church, Oxford">Christ Church, Oxford</a>.</p>
<p>In 1909 he married Lady Louise Rosemary Kathleen Virginia Cairns, MBE, only child of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_William_Cairns,_2nd_Earl_Cairns" title="Arthur William Cairns, 2nd Earl Cairns" class="mw-redirect">Arthur Cairns, 2nd Earl Cairns</a>.</p>
<p>He was commissioned into the <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_Yeomanry" title="Hampshire Yeomanry">Hampshire Yeomanry</a> in 1903, was promoted <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">Lieutenant</a> in 1905, and transferred to the <a href="/wiki/9th_Lancers" title="9th Lancers" class="mw-redirect">9th Lancers</a> later the same year. He transferred to the <a href="/wiki/1st_Life_Guards" title="1st Life Guards" class="mw-redirect">1st Life Guards</a> as a Second Lieutenant in 1908 and was promoted Lieutenant again later the same year, but left the Army in 1911. He rejoined the Hampshire Yeomanry in 1914 and served in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. He was promoted <a href="/wiki/Captain_(British_Army_and_Royal_Marines)" title="Captain (British Army and Royal Marines)">Captain</a> in 1914 while serving as <a href="/wiki/Adjutant" title="Adjutant">adjutant</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_1st_Devon_Yeomanry" title="Royal 1st Devon Yeomanry">Royal 1st Devon Yeomanry</a>. Transferring back to the Life Guards (<a href="/wiki/Special_Reserve_(militia)" title="Special Reserve (militia)" class="mw-redirect">Special Reserve</a>) in 1915, he was promoted <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant-Colonel" title="Lieutenant-Colonel" class="mw-redirect">Lieutenant-Colonel</a> in 1916 when he took command of the <a href="/wiki/Household_Battalion" title="Household Battalion">Household Battalion</a>. In 1917 he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">Distinguished Service Order</a> and appointed a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Victorian_Order" title="Royal Victorian Order">Member of the Royal Victorian Order</a> (MVO). He relinquished command of the battalion in 1918 and reverted to the rank of Captain, but was soon promoted <a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">Major</a> and attached to the <a href="/wiki/Machine_Gun_Corps" title="Machine Gun Corps">Machine Gun Corps</a> as a battalion commander, again with the rank of Lieutenant-Colonel. He resigned his commission in 1919.</p>
<p>Returning to civilian life, he became chairman of the Portal family's banknote paper mill company in <a href="/wiki/Laverstoke" title="Laverstoke">Laverstoke</a>, Portals Limited, in 1919<sup id="cite_ref-Oxford_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxford-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gazette_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gazette-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> which had manufactured banknote paper for the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_England" title="Bank of England">Bank of England</a> since 1724.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> and became the company's chairman when his father died in 1931.<sup id="cite_ref-Oxford_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxford-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1936, he was one of the main investors in <a href="/wiki/J._Arthur_Rank,_1st_Baron_Rank" title="J. Arthur Rank, 1st Baron Rank">J. Arthur Rank</a>'s General Cinema Finance Corporation, the company which one year later would become the British film industry's most important company, <a href="/wiki/The_Rank_Organisation" title="The Rank Organisation" class="mw-redirect">The Rank Organisation</a>. He became General Cinema Finance Corporation's chairman, and worked very closely with J. Arthur Rank for many years.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1935 he was made chairman of the Bacon Development Board, and in April 1939 he was made was regional commissioner for Wales under the Civil Defence Scheme. In 1940 he became the chairman of the Coal Production Council, and he served in government as Additional <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Secretary" title="Parliamentary Secretary">Parliamentary Secretary</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Supply" title="Ministry of Supply">Ministry of Supply</a> from 1940–1942, and as <a href="/wiki/First_Commissioner_of_Works" title="First Commissioner of Works">Minister of Works and Planning</a> from 1942–1944.<sup id="cite_ref-Oxford_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxford-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>After the war, in 1945, he became the last chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway" title="Great Western Railway">Great Western Railway</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_Hampshire" title="Lord Lieutenant of Hampshire">Lord Lieutenant of Hampshire</a> from 1947.<sup id="cite_ref-Oxford_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxford-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1935 he accepted the chairmanship of the British Olympic Association and thus led the British team in the 1936 Olympic games in Berlin. That experience made him the perfect president of the 1948 Olympic Games in London.<sup id="cite_ref-Oxford_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxford-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gazette_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gazette-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Portal succeeded to his father's baronetcy in 1931. In 1935 he was raised to the peerage as Baron Portal, of Laverstoke. He was appointed a <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">Privy Counsellor</a> in 1942, created Viscount Portal in 1945 and awarded the <a href="/wiki/GCMG" title="GCMG" class="mw-redirect">GCMG</a> in 1949.</p>
<p>He was succeeded to the Baronetcy only by his uncle, Sir Spencer John Portal, 4th Baronet.</p>
