<p><b>Kenneth Douglas "Ken" Taylor</b> <span style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Order_of_Canada" title="Order of Canada">OC</a></span> (May 10, 1934 – October 15, 2015) was a Canadian diplomat, educator and businessman, best known for his role in the 1979 <a href="/wiki/Secrecy" title="Secrecy">covert</a> operation called the "<a href="/wiki/Canadian_Caper" title="Canadian Caper">Canadian Caper</a>" when he was the <a href="/wiki/Canada%E2%80%93Iran_relations" title="Canada–Iran relations">Canadian ambassador to Iran</a>. With the cooperation of the American <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">Central Intelligence Agency</a>, Taylor helped six Americans escape from <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Iran_hostage_crisis" title="Iran hostage crisis">Iran hostage crisis</a> by procuring <a href="/wiki/Canadian_passport" title="Canadian passport">Canadian passports</a> for the Americans to deceive the Iranian Revolutionary guard, posing as a Canadian film crew scouting locations. Before the escape, the six Americans spent several weeks hiding in the homes of Taylor and another Canadian diplomat, John Sheardown.<sup id="cite_ref-FAITC_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAITC-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Taylor is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Pinsent" title="Gordon Pinsent">Gordon Pinsent</a> in the Canadian 1981 <a href="/wiki/Television_film" title="Television film">television film</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Escape_from_Iran:_The_Canadian_Caper" title="Escape from Iran: The Canadian Caper">Escape from Iran: The Canadian Caper</a></i> which dramatizes the cover story for the operation. The later 2012 American film, <i><a href="/wiki/Argo_(2012_film)" title="Argo (2012 film)">Argo</a></i>, focuses more on the CIA and Hollywood's role, with Taylor played by Canadian actor <a href="/wiki/Victor_Garber" title="Victor Garber">Victor Garber</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenneth_D._Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taylor was born in <a href="/wiki/Calgary,_Alberta" title="Calgary, Alberta" class="mw-redirect">Calgary, Alberta</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> He completed his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">BA</a> at <a href="/wiki/Victoria_University,_Toronto" title="Victoria University, Toronto">Victoria College</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> and his <a href="/wiki/MBA" title="MBA" class="mw-redirect">MBA</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> He was a brother of the <a href="/wiki/Sigma_Chi" title="Sigma Chi">Sigma Chi</a> fraternity and was later honoured with its highest award, Significant Sigma Chi.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Iran_hostage_crisis">Iran hostage crisis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenneth_D._Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Iran hostage crisis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Iran_hostage_crisis" title="Iran hostage crisis">Iran hostage crisis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Caper" title="Canadian Caper">Canadian Caper</a></div>
<p>Iranian students invaded the United States embassy in Tehran on November 4, 1979. During the riot, six Americans managed to escape. They hid for four days before reaching the Canadian embassy, where they met Taylor, who devised the plan to get them out of Iran safely. On January 20, 1981, the moment <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a> completed his 20‑minute inaugural address after being sworn in as president, the 52 American hostages were released by Iran into US custody, having spent 444 days in captivity.</p>
<p>Taylor would play a crucial role in providing intelligence on the hostage crisis to Canadian and American intelligence agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Reportedly, he helped scout out landing sites for the abortive <a href="/wiki/Delta_Force" title="Delta Force">Delta Force</a> rescue attempt, <a href="/wiki/Operation_Eagle_Claw" title="Operation Eagle Claw">Operation Eagle Claw</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Taylor did not confirm <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> that the CIA organized the rescue of the diplomats who were hiding in the Canadian embassy, a statement which echoes what former US President <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">Jimmy Carter</a> said about the operation in 1986, however Canada's historical reticence at admitting collaboration with US foreign intelligence agencies make it difficult to gauge the extent of Canadian intelligence involvement and whether Taylor acted on his own initiative or was following a planned last-resort alternative.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>A made-for-TV movie of the events, <i><a href="/wiki/Escape_from_Iran:_The_Canadian_Caper" title="Escape from Iran: The Canadian Caper">Escape from Iran: The Canadian Caper</a></i>, was produced in 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Affleck" title="Ben Affleck">Ben Affleck</a> produced and starred in a movie titled <i><a href="/wiki/Argo_(2012_film)" title="Argo (2012 film)">Argo</a></i>, which is based on the book <i>The Master of Disguise</i> by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Mendez" title="Tony Mendez">Tony Mendez</a>, a CIA agent involved in the incident. After the film was previewed at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2012 Toronto International Film Festival">2012 Toronto International Film Festival</a>, criticism arose that the film unfairly minimized the participation of the Canadian government, and Taylor, in the extraction operation. This included the addition to the film of several fictional events for dramatic reasons, as well as a postscript text indicating that the CIA let Taylor take the credit for political purposes, implying that he did not deserve the accolades he received.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Affleck noted, "Because we say it's based on a true story, rather than this is a true story, we’re allowed to take some dramatic license. There’s a spirit of truth."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> However, Affleck did respond by changing the postscript text to read, “The involvement of the CIA complemented efforts of the Canadian embassy to free the six held in Tehran. To this day the story stands as an enduring model of international co-operation between governments.”<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Historical news footage discussing Canada's role in the rescue was also added near the end of the film. Taylor himself said that the film was "fun, it’s thrilling, it’s pertinent, it’s timely,” but noted that "Canada was not merely standing around watching events take place. The CIA was a junior partner.”<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_and_death">Later life and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenneth_D._Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After returning from <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, Taylor was appointed Canadian <a href="/wiki/Consul-General" title="Consul-General" class="mw-redirect">Consul-General</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. In 1980, he was made an Officer of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Canada" title="Order of Canada">Order of Canada</a> along with his wife Pat and other Canadian personnel involved in the escape,<sup id="cite_ref-FAITC_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAITC-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> and was also awarded the United States <a href="/wiki/Congressional_Gold_Medal" title="Congressional Gold Medal">Congressional Gold Medal</a> that same year.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>He returned to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a> for several years as the <a href="/wiki/Chancellor" title="Chancellor">Chancellor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Victoria_University_at_the_University_of_Toronto" title="Victoria University at the University of Toronto" class="mw-redirect">Victoria College</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Taylor left the foreign service in 1984 and served as Senior Vice-President of <a href="/wiki/Nabisco" title="Nabisco">Nabisco</a> (RJR Nabisco after 1986) from 1984 to 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>He was the founder and chairman of public consulting firm Taylor and Ryan.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Taylor moved to the United States and lived in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> until his death at the age of 81 on October 15, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Taylor died at <a href="/wiki/NewYork%E2%80%93Presbyterian_Hospital" title="NewYork–Presbyterian Hospital">NewYork–Presbyterian Hospital</a>, where he had been treated over the past several months for Stage 4 <a href="/wiki/Colorectal_cancer" title="Colorectal cancer">colon cancer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
