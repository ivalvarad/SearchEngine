			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Portal (architecture)</h1>

<p>A <b>portal</b> is an opening in a wall of a building, gate or fortification, especially a grand entrance to an important structure.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Door" title="Door">doors</a>, metal <a href="/wiki/Gate" title="Gate">gates</a> or <a href="/wiki/Portcullis" title="Portcullis">portcullis</a> in the opening can be used to control entry or exit. The surface surrounding the opening may be made of simple building materials or decorated with <a href="/wiki/Ornament_(architecture)" title="Ornament (architecture)" class="mw-redirect">ornamentation</a>. The elements of a portal can include the <a href="/wiki/Voussoir" title="Voussoir">voussoir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tympanum_(architecture)" title="Tympanum (architecture)">tympanum</a>, an ornamented <a href="/wiki/Mullion" title="Mullion">mullion</a> or <i>trumeau</i> between doors, and <a href="/wiki/Column" title="Column">columns</a> with carvings of saints in the <a href="/wiki/Westwork" title="Westwork">westwork</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Church_(building)" title="Church (building)">church</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
