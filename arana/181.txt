This has led to speculation that these are an "artificial <a href="/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life" title="Extraterrestrial life">extraterrestrial</a> mega-structure", orbiting the star known as <a href="/wiki/KIC_8462852" title="KIC 8462852">KIC 8462852</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Cygnus" title="Cygnus" class="mw-disambig">Cygnus</a> constellation. KIC 8462852 lies just above the <a href="/wiki/Milky_Way" title="Milky Way">Milky Way</a> between the constellations Cygnus and Lyra. It first attracted the attention of astronomers in 2009 when the Kepler Space Telescope identified it as a candidate for having orbiting <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>-like planets. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/forget-water-on-mars-astronomers-may-have-just-found-giant-alien-megastructures-orbiting-a-star-near-a6693886.html">(Independent)</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://news.discovery.com/space/alien-life-exoplanets/has-kepler-discovered-an-alien-megastructure-151014.htm">(Discovery News)</a></li>
</ul>
