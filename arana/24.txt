<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Map_Projections_as_Today.27s_Featured_Picture">Map Projections as Today's Featured Picture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Talk:Main_Page&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Map Projections as Today's Featured Picture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It seems like there is one a week, what is the obsession? This feature is overly reliant on these Projection images, and there is clearly not enough variation. Can these be limited to one a year or something a little more sensible? <a href="/wiki/Special:Contributions/81.108.19.93" title="Special:Contributions/81.108.19.93">81.108.19.93</a> (<a href="/w/index.php?title=User_talk:81.108.19.93&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="User talk:81.108.19.93 (page does not exist)">talk</a>) 11:24, 16 October 2015 (UTC)</p>
<dl>
<dd>If you work on getting some more varied pictures featured, the people scheduling TFP will have more variety to choose from. <a href="/wiki/User:Grapple_X" title="User:Grapple X"><b>G<small>RAPPLE</small></b></a> <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Grapple_X" title="User talk:Grapple X"><b><small><sup>X</sup></small></b></a> 11:26, 16 October 2015 (UTC)</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>The images are shown roughly in the order they were promoted to Featured Picture (<a href="/wiki/First_in_first_out" title="First in first out" class="mw-redirect">first in first out</a>), with exceptions for relevant dates (such as anniversaries) and to spread out similar topics. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Picture_of_the_day" title="Wikipedia:Picture of the day">Wikipedia:Picture of the day</a>. In this case <a href="/wiki/User:Strebe" title="User:Strebe">User:Strebe</a> worked hard to get several map projections featured, and they're gradually being showcased on the Main Page. After a while they'll be done. If you would like to help select the range of topics for this section, please contribute at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_picture_candidates" title="Wikipedia:Featured picture candidates">Wikipedia:Featured picture candidates</a>. <a href="/wiki/User:Modest_Genius" title="User:Modest Genius"><font face="Times New Roman" color="maroon"><b>Modest Genius</b></font></a> <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Modest_Genius" title="User talk:Modest Genius"><sup>talk</sup></a> 12:37, 16 October 2015 (UTC)
<ul>
<li>You can check the POTD archives. We have 43 featured map projections, which have been running at the rate of one or two a month for a year and a half or so. As POTD runs on a more or less "first in, first out" basis, there are occasional spurts of similar subjects. Two years ago it was birds. In a year it will be banknotes and Van Gogh paintings.&#160;—&#160;<a href="/wiki/User:Crisco_1492" title="User:Crisco 1492">Chris Woodrich</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Crisco_1492" title="User talk:Crisco 1492">talk</a>) 15:52, 18 October 2015 (UTC)</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>
<dl>
<dd>As one who lends a hand at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FPC" title="Wikipedia:FPC" class="mw-redirect">WP:FPC</a>, I agree that users are encouraged to participate in the selection process. ("Outside of the nominator, all voters are expected to have been on Wikipedia for 25 days and to have made a minimum of 100 edits.") But I also agree with the IP user above that the series of map projections has become repetitive. Since we have a large backlog of approved FPs, perhaps the maps could be spread out a bit more? <a href="/wiki/User:Sca" title="User:Sca">Sca</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Sca" title="User talk:Sca">talk</a>) 16:03, 18 October 2015 (UTC)
<ul>
<li>How, exactly, is "one or two a month" not spread out enough? I'll concede that the birds last year could have been spread out a bit better (five a month was perhaps a bit much) but one or two a month is far from problematic. Even this month, we had one projection on October 2 and one on October 16; that's two weeks, not one week.
