<p><b>Nicolas Portal</b> (born April 23, 1979 in <a href="/wiki/Auch" title="Auch">Auch</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Directeur_sportif" title="Directeur sportif">directeur sportif</a> and former professional <a href="/wiki/Road_bicycle_racer" title="Road bicycle racer" class="mw-redirect">road bicycle racer</a>. He missed much of the 2009 season because of problems with <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_arrhythmia" title="Cardiac arrhythmia">cardiac arrhythmia</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> and retired as a professional cyclist following the 2010 season. He stayed with his final professional team, <a href="/wiki/Team_Sky" title="Team Sky">Team Sky</a>, as a manager from the 2011 season. Following the retirement of <a href="/wiki/Sean_Yates" title="Sean Yates">Sean Yates</a>, Portal was appointed directeur sportif of Team Sky for 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> At the 2013 Tour de France, he was the youngest directeur sportif at the race, but guided <a href="/wiki/Chris_Froome" title="Chris Froome">Chris Froome</a> to overall victory.</p>
