<p><b>Roy Kilner</b> (17 October 1890 – 5 April 1928) was an English professional <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricketer</a> who played nine <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test matches</a> for <a href="/wiki/England_cricket_team" title="England cricket team">England</a> between 1924 and 1926. An <a href="/wiki/All-rounder" title="All-rounder">all-rounder</a>, he played for <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Yorkshire County Cricket Club">Yorkshire County Cricket Club</a> between 1911 and 1927. In all <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class matches</a>, he scored 14,707 <a href="/wiki/Run_(cricket)" title="Run (cricket)">runs</a> at an <a href="/wiki/Batting_average#Cricket" title="Batting average">average</a> of 30.01 and took 1,003 <a href="/wiki/Wicket#Dismissing_a_batsman" title="Wicket">wickets</a> at an <a href="/wiki/Bowling_average" title="Bowling average">average</a> of 18.45. Kilner scored 1,000 runs in a season ten times and took 100 wickets in a season five times. On four occasions, he completed the <a href="/wiki/Double_(cricket)" title="Double (cricket)">double</a>: scoring 1,000 runs and taking 100 wickets in the same season, recognised as a sign of a quality all-rounder.</p>
<p>Kilner first played for Yorkshire as a <a href="/wiki/Batting_(cricket)" title="Batting (cricket)">batsman</a> before the First World War, establishing a regular place in the side. After being wounded in the war, he returned in 1919 to a Yorkshire side which was short of <a href="/wiki/Bowler_(cricket)" title="Bowler (cricket)" class="mw-redirect">bowlers</a>. As a result, Kilner began to practise his bowling to the point where he became highly regarded as a <a href="/wiki/Left-arm_orthodox_spin" title="Left-arm orthodox spin">slow left-arm bowler</a>. His aggressive batting and warm personality made him a popular player with both cricketers and spectators. His form brought selection by England in 1924 and a visit to Australia for the <a href="/wiki/English_cricket_team_in_Australia_in_1924%E2%80%9325" title="English cricket team in Australia in 1924–25">Ashes tour of 1924–25</a>. Although the second most successful bowler of the tour, his bowling subsequently declined in effectiveness, and did not trouble batsmen on good pitches. He was selected during the 1926 <a href="/wiki/The_Ashes" title="The Ashes">Ashes</a> but dropped for the final Test. Kilner went on several coaching trips to India during English winters, and on one of these, in 1928, he contracted an illness; on his return to England, he died aged 37. His funeral was attended by over 100,000 people and there was widespread sadness at his death.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kilner was born on 17 October 1890 in <a href="/wiki/Wombwell" title="Wombwell">Wombwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, England, the second son and one of eleven children of Seth Kilner and Mary Alice Washington.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> His brother <a href="/wiki/Norman_Kilner" title="Norman Kilner">Norman</a> also played cricket, representing Yorkshire and <a href="/wiki/Warwickshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Warwickshire County Cricket Club">Warwickshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-p4_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p4-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The Kilners attended Wombwell Parish Church and the boys were members of the <a href="/wiki/Church_Lads%27_and_Church_Girls%27_Brigade" title="Church Lads' and Church Girls' Brigade">Church Lads' Brigade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-p4_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p4-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> His father and his uncle, <a href="/wiki/Irving_Washington" title="Irving Washington">Irving Washington</a>, the former <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Yorkshire County Cricket Club">Yorkshire</a> player, encouraged him to play cricket from an early age. He showed enough ability to join the local <a href="/wiki/Colliery" title="Colliery" class="mw-redirect">colliery</a> team, Mitchell Main.<sup id="cite_ref-p4_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p4-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>At the age of 14 in 1904, Kilner reached the Mitchell Main first team.<sup id="cite_ref-p4_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p4-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Although selected regularly from 1905, Kilner was not particularly successful with the bat. He displayed aggression but often attempted difficult shots and consequently <a href="/wiki/Wicket#Dismissing_a_batsman" title="Wicket">lost his wicket</a>. His bowling was more effective but used infrequently.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> By 1909 his batting improved; he scored his first <a href="/wiki/Century_(cricket)" title="Century (cricket)">century</a> for the team and began to make consistently good scores, attracting the attention of Yorkshire.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_years_at_Yorkshire">First years at Yorkshire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First years at Yorkshire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Debut_for_Yorkshire">Debut for Yorkshire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Debut for Yorkshire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After scoring a second century for Mitchell Main at the start of the 1910 season, Kilner was chosen to play for Yorkshire Second XI. In his first season, he took three <a href="/wiki/Wicket#Dismissing_a_batsman" title="Wicket">wickets</a> and had a <a href="/wiki/Batting_average_(cricket)" title="Batting average (cricket)" class="mw-redirect">batting average</a> of 12.50 but continued to do well for Mitchell Main.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> For 1911, Yorkshire sent Kilner to play for Harrogate Cricket Club which provided a higher standard of cricket than Mitchell Main: the county had a system whereby promising young players were sent to gain experience in competitive matches for local clubs.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner began to record good batting and bowling performances. Although his form for Yorkshire Second XI was more inconsistent, he made his first-class debut for Yorkshire that season against <a href="/wiki/Somerset_County_Cricket_Club" title="Somerset County Cricket Club">Somerset</a> in the <a href="/wiki/County_Championship" title="County Championship">County Championship</a>. He scored 0 and 14 and did not bowl.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> In a further six matches for Yorkshire in 1911, his highest innings was 18 <a href="/wiki/Run_(cricket)" title="Run (cricket)">runs</a>, his average with the bat was just 6.66 in ten innings and he did not take any wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> For Harrogate, he was more successful, with 519 runs and 28 wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Yorkshire_regular">Yorkshire regular</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Yorkshire regular">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kilner made a good start to 1912 for Yorkshire Second XI, scoring centuries in two consecutive games; in the second he also took twelve wickets for 75 runs.<sup id="cite_ref-p12_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p12-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> These performances, coupled with continued good form for Harrogate, led to Kilner's recall to the Yorkshire first team in June. He replaced the injured <a href="/wiki/All-rounder" title="All-rounder">all-rounder</a> <a href="/wiki/George_Hirst" title="George Hirst">George Hirst</a> for a match against <a href="/wiki/Nottinghamshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Nottinghamshire County Cricket Club">Nottinghamshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> On the first morning, Kilner was used as the sixth bowler and proved successful; he finished with four wickets for 66 runs. Although failing to score in the first innings, he came in to bat in the second innings with Yorkshire 133 for four, needing 249 for victory. He shared a <a href="/wiki/Partnership_(cricket)" title="Partnership (cricket)">partnership</a> of 113 for the fifth wicket and scored 83 <a href="/wiki/Not_out" title="Not out">not out</a> to take Yorkshire to a five wicket win.<sup id="cite_ref-p12_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p12-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> This performance kept Kilner in the team for the remainder of the season, even after Hirst's return. He played 23 times for Yorkshire.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P14_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P14-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> He scored 570 runs at an average of 22.80 and took 16 wickets at an <a href="/wiki/Bowling_average" title="Bowling average">average</a> of 22.12, figures regarded as respectable for a first full season. Yorkshire won the County Championship that season.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P14_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P14-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Apart from one game in the <a href="/wiki/1913_English_cricket_season" title="1913 English cricket season">1913 season</a>, he ceased playing for Harrogate. In his years there, he scored 967 runs, averaging 38.68, and took 71 wickets at an average of less than ten runs per wicket.<sup id="cite_ref-P14_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P14-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the 1913 season, Kilner scored 1,586 runs at an average of 34.47, which remained his highest seasonal aggregate of runs and placed him third in the Yorkshire batting averages.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> He also took 18 wickets at 25.22.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> At the end of May and beginning of June, Kilner took part in century partnerships in four consecutive matches, culminating in his maiden first-class century against <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Leicestershire County Cricket Club">Leicestershire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fcmatches_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcmatches-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Leic1913_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leic1913-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> During his innings of 104, he hit 18 <a href="/wiki/Boundary_(cricket)#Scoring_runs" title="Boundary (cricket)">fours</a> and shared a partnership of 184 in under two hours with his close friend <a href="/wiki/Major_Booth" title="Major Booth">Major Booth</a>, rescuing Yorkshire from 58 for five and taking the score to 300.<sup id="cite_ref-P14_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P14-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Leic1913_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leic1913-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> Throughout the season, he played several valuable innings when Yorkshire were under pressure. His batting was always adventurous and attacking although he often made uncertain starts to his innings and showed impatience. The strength and variety of the Yorkshire bowling attack meant his left-arm spin was not often required.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Even so, Yorkshire dropped to second in the Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>An illness during the winter of 1913–14 took its toll on Kilner at the start of the 1914 season.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> He passed 1,000 runs for the second time but due to his uncertain form, his aggregate of runs and his average both fell. He scored 1,329 runs (average 30.90), placing him fourth in the Yorkshire batting averages.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P21_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P21-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> His bowling was rarely used and he took just one wicket.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P21_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P21-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner scored his second first-class century against <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Gloucestershire County Cricket Club">Gloucestershire</a> late in the season; he made 169 in nearly three and a half hours, hitting 28 fours.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> Yorkshire fell to fourth in the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_World_War">First World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: First World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The United Kingdom declared war on Germany on 4 August 1914, while Kilner was playing in that season's <a href="/wiki/Roses_Match" title="Roses Match">Roses Match</a> at <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford_Cricket_Ground" title="Old Trafford Cricket Ground">Old Trafford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> Initially the government requested that cricket should continue, though several cricketers with military obligations, including Yorkshire's captain, <a href="/wiki/Archibald_White_(cricketer)" title="Archibald White (cricketer)">Sir Archibald White</a>, were called up immediately. As the fighting started, and casualties began to mount, public opinion turned against the continuation of the season, and Yorkshire's match against Sussex at Hove, which concluded on 1 September, was the last County Championship match until 1919. The newspaper <i>Cricket</i> reported "The men's hearts were barely in the game, and the match was given up as a draw at tea."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> With the suspension of the championship, Kilner and Major Booth enlisted in the army together, joining the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Pals" title="Leeds Pals">Leeds and Bradford "Pals"</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Regiment" title="West Yorkshire Regiment">West Yorkshire Regiment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-P21_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P21-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner trained as a mechanic before being stationed at <a href="/wiki/Colsterdale" title="Colsterdale">Colsterdale</a> in North Yorkshire as a corporal.<sup id="cite_ref-P25_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P25-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P21-22_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P21-22-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> While on leave in November 1914, Kilner married Annie Campbelljohn—the daughter of James Campbelljohn, an engineer—at Wombwell Parish Church; Booth served as best man.<sup id="cite_ref-P21-22_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P21-22-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> During the war, Annie gave birth to the couple's first child, Roy junior.<sup id="cite_ref-P22_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P22-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner was posted with his battalion to Egypt but was forced home with an injury. When he recovered, he was sent to the <a href="/wiki/Western_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Western Front (World War I)">Western Front</a> in France.<sup id="cite_ref-P22_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P22-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Somme" title="Battle of the Somme">Battle of the Somme</a>, he was wounded shortly before his battalion engaged in the fighting, receiving a shrapnel wound in the wrist; later in the same action, Booth was killed.<sup id="cite_ref-P22_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P22-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner recovered in a military hospital near Blackpool before being assigned to Preston Garrison as a mechanic.<sup id="cite_ref-P25_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P25-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P22_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P22-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner suffered a second loss when his brother Bernard was killed at <a href="/wiki/Ypres" title="Ypres">Ypres</a> in 1917.<sup id="cite_ref-P25_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P25-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Having previously played <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> for Mitchell Main in the winter of 1912–13,<sup id="cite_ref-P14_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P14-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full_back" title="Defender (association football)">right-back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C." title="Preston North End F.C.">Preston North End F.C.</a> while posted to Preston during the war. The team won promotion from the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Second_Division" title="Football League Second Division">Second Division</a> in 1915, and took their place in the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_First_Division" title="Football League First Division">First Division</a> when league football resumed in 1919. Sometimes playing under the name of Smith to avoid detection (although it is not certain why he did so), Kilner's first certain appearance for the team was in September 1918. It is unclear how often he represented the club.<sup id="cite_ref-P25_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P25-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First-class_career_after_the_war">First-class career after the war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: First-class career after the war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_batsman_to_bowler">From batsman to bowler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: From batsman to bowler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When cricket resumed after the war in <a href="/wiki/1919_English_cricket_season" title="1919 English cricket season">1919</a>, County Championship matches were reduced from three days to two in a one-season experiment. Following the deaths of Booth in the war and <a href="/wiki/Alonzo_Drake" title="Alonzo Drake">Alonzo Drake</a> from illness, as well as the decline in the bowling of the aging George Hirst, Yorkshire lacked an effective attack. Subsequently, Kilner was asked to deliver more overs, although not to the extent of a main bowler.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P26_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P26-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> He took 45 wickets, more than his entire first-class wicket aggregate before the war, at an average of 18.12. This placed him third in the Yorkshire averages.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> With the bat he scored three centuries and reached 1,135 runs at 29.10.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> His performances earned him a place in the <a href="/wiki/Gentlemen_v_Players" title="Gentlemen v Players">Gentlemen v Players</a> match at the end of season during the <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_Festival" title="Scarborough Festival">Scarborough Festival</a>, although he neither took a wicket nor scored.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner's brother Norman also played for Yorkshire in 1919, although he was ultimately unable to secure a permanent place in the team.<sup id="cite_ref-P26_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P26-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Kilner began the <a href="/wiki/1920_English_cricket_season" title="1920 English cricket season">1920 season</a> with an innings of 206 <a href="/wiki/Not_out" title="Not out">not out</a> in his first match, against <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Derbyshire County Cricket Club">Derbyshire</a>, and the effort remained the highest score of his career. He batted for four hours, hitting 24 fours and two <a href="/wiki/Boundary_(cricket)#Scoring_runs" title="Boundary (cricket)">sixes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> He could have been caught when he had scored around fifty, but a local newspaper described the innings as brilliant, his <a href="/wiki/Drive_(cricket)" title="Drive (cricket)" class="mw-redirect">driving</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pull_(cricket)" title="Pull (cricket)" class="mw-redirect">pulling</a> being particularly effective.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner appeared in one representative match: at the end-of-season <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_Festival" title="Scarborough Festival">Scarborough Festival</a>, he played for CI Thornton's XI against the <a href="/wiki/Marylebone_Cricket_Club" title="Marylebone Cricket Club">Marylebone Cricket Club</a> (MCC) side which visited Australia in the winter—at the time, the MCC administered English cricket, and the <a href="/wiki/England_cricket_team" title="England cricket team">England team</a> toured under its name.<sup id="cite_ref-fcmatches_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcmatches-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> In total, he scored 1,316 runs at an average of 37.09 with two more centuries, placing him second in the Yorkshire averages.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> With the ball, he took 27 wickets but his average of 25.33 was relatively high.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P29_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P29-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> The county finished fourth in the Championship as Middlesex were crowned winners.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Critics believed Kilner should have bowled more frequently, an opinion shared by <a href="/wiki/Wilfred_Rhodes" title="Wilfred Rhodes">Wilfred Rhodes</a>, Yorkshire's main <a href="/wiki/Left-arm_orthodox_spin" title="Left-arm orthodox spin">slow left-arm spinner</a>. Conscious of his increasing age, the 42-year-old Rhodes wanted support with the bowling workload; he considered Kilner the best option and encouraged him to improve his left-arm spin.<sup id="cite_ref-P29_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P29-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> Consequently, during the winter, Kilner practised bowling in the yard of the Wombwell hotel where his father was landlord.<sup id="cite_ref-P29_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P29-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> Around the same time he celebrated the birth of his second son, named Major after Major Booth.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Kilner's bowling had greater success in <a href="/wiki/1921_English_cricket_season" title="1921 English cricket season">1921</a>, helping Yorkshire to rise to third in the Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Used more regularly, he took 61 wickets (average 18.80),<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> including his first <a href="/wiki/Five-wicket_haul" title="Five-wicket haul" class="mw-redirect">five-wicket hauls</a> in an innings]], against <a href="/wiki/Warwickshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Warwickshire County Cricket Club">Warwickshire</a> and Nottinghamshire.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> He scored 1,137 runs at an average of 27.73.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> He made centuries in the two matches against <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Northamptonshire County Cricket Club">Northamptonshire</a>, sharing big partnerships in both games: 276 with Rhodes in the first and 299 with <a href="/wiki/Percy_Holmes" title="Percy Holmes">Percy Holmes</a> in the second.<sup id="cite_ref-P30_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P30-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> His overall performance left him sixth in the Yorkshire batting averages, and fourth in the bowling averages.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>note 1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> Through the winter, he continued to develop his bowling.<sup id="cite_ref-P30_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P30-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Established_all-rounder">Established all-rounder</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Established all-rounder">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/1922_English_cricket_season" title="1922 English cricket season">1922</a>, Kilner's bowling had improved to the point where he passed 100 wickets in the season for the first time, taking 122 at an average of 14.72,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> helping Yorkshire to win the County Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-P32_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P32-49"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Wisden</i> recognised this was the first season when he was a front-line bowler, "[leaving] his previous form far behind."<sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> In the first five games of the campaign, he took 21 wickets for 142 runs.<sup id="cite_ref-P32_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P32-49"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> In the match against <a href="/wiki/Essex_County_Cricket_Club" title="Essex County Cricket Club">Essex</a>, he claimed ten wickets in a match for the first time, taking eleven for 51 runs.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> Against <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Hampshire County Cricket Club">Hampshire</a>, he took ten wickets for 90 runs, including six for 13 in the second innings as Hampshire were bowled out for 44.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup> At the same time, he maintained his batting form, having a similar record to the previous season. He scored 1,198 runs at 27.22 and scored two centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> This meant he completed the <a href="/wiki/Double_(cricket)" title="Double (cricket)">double</a>—regarded as the sign of a quality all-round cricketer—for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-P33_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P33-52"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner was fifth in the Yorkshire batting averages and fourth in the bowling averages.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> At the end of the season, he was again selected for CI Thornton's XI, as well as in two games at Eastbourne.<sup id="cite_ref-fcmatches_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcmatches-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> Over the English winter, Kilner accepted a job coaching for the <a href="/wiki/Bhupinder_Singh_of_Patiala" title="Bhupinder Singh of Patiala">Maharaja of Patiala</a> in India. He played in several first-class matches,<sup id="cite_ref-fcmatches_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcmatches-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P33_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P33-52"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> and enjoyed himself enough to return the following winter.<sup id="cite_ref-P33_52-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P33-52"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Statistically, Kilner's peak as a bowler came in the <a href="/wiki/1923_English_cricket_season" title="1923 English cricket season">1923 season</a>, when he took 158 wickets at an average of 12.90 and finished second in the Yorkshire averages.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup> With the bat, he failed to score a hundred for the first time since 1912,<sup id="cite_ref-P35_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P35-56"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup> but still made 1,401 runs at 34.17 and including nine scores over fifty.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> This placed him third in the Yorkshire batting averages, his contributions helping his team to retain the County Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-P35_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P35-56"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup> He made a very effective start to the season, taking 27 wickets in the first four matches for just 157 runs,<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P34_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P34-58"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup> including the best figures of his career, eight for 26 against <a href="/wiki/Glamorgan_County_Cricket_Club" title="Glamorgan County Cricket Club">Glamorgan</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup> and six for 14 against <a href="/wiki/Middlesex_County_Cricket_Club" title="Middlesex County Cricket Club">Middlesex</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup> Later that season against <a href="/wiki/Surrey_County_Cricket_Club" title="Surrey County Cricket Club">Surrey</a>, he performed what was regarded by <i>Wisden</i> as one of his best bowling feats.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> Surrey needed 184 runs to win and seemed certain winners when they had scored 127 for two. After <a href="/wiki/Emmott_Robinson" title="Emmott Robinson">Emmott Robinson</a> took a wicket, Kilner took the last five for only 15 runs in a match-winning performance. He finished with bowling figures of six for 22 and took ten wickets in the match. He had been under the impression that Surrey had only needed seven runs (rather than the 27 they required) to win at the end, and his team-mates remembered he was pale with effort and concentration.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P34_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P34-58"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup> In the same season, Kilner was picked for the <a href="/wiki/Gentlemen_v_Players" title="Gentlemen v Players">Gentlemen v Players</a> match at <a href="/wiki/Lord%27s_Cricket_Ground" title="Lord's Cricket Ground">Lord's Cricket Ground</a>, the most prestigious such fixture at the time, where he scored eight in his only innings and took three wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-P35_56-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P35-56"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-figures_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-62"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Wisden</i> commented that he seemed certain to be picked for the England <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test match</a> side shortly (there were no Tests played that summer).<sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> He took a step towards this when he was selected for a Test trial match, scoring 26 and 11 not out, but only bowling 18 overs in the match to take two wickets. He also made another appearance for the Players, this time at Scarborough.<sup id="cite_ref-P35_56-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P35-56"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup> His performances in the season earned him selection as one of <i>Wisden's</i> <a href="/wiki/Wisden_Cricketers_of_the_Year" title="Wisden Cricketers of the Year">Bowlers of the Year</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Test_match_career">Test match career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Test match career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Test_debut">Test debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Test debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After another winter coaching in India and practising his bowling,<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner took 145 first-class wickets at 13.28 during the <a href="/wiki/1924_English_cricket_season" title="1924 English cricket season">1924 English summer</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> placing him second in the national averages behind his teammate <a href="/wiki/George_Macaulay" title="George Macaulay">George Macaulay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-P40_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P40-64"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup> His batting suffered a decline; he scored 731 runs at 20.88, the lowest aggregate of his career and his first sub-1,000 total since 1912.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner took 33 wickets in the first six matches of the season, and his form earned selection for a Test trial match.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_62-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-62"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P39_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P39-65"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup> In this game, he took three for 49 in the first innings and a further wicket in the second but only scored 20 runs in two innings. After 19 wickets in his next three matches, including seven in the match against the South African touring side, he was awarded a place in the England team for the first Test.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_62-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-62"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> In his maiden Test, he scored 59 out of a large total, batting at number seven. South Africa were dismissed for 30 runs in the first innings before Kilner had an opportunity to bowl.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup> In the second innings, he delivered 22 <a href="/wiki/Over_(cricket)" title="Over (cricket)">overs</a> without taking a wicket and commentators were not impressed by his bowling. The selectors left Kilner out of the next two Tests, all-rounder <a href="/wiki/J._W._Hearne" title="J. W. Hearne">J. W. Hearne</a> taking his place,<sup id="cite_ref-P39_65-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P39-65"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67"><span>[</span>66<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68"><span>[</span>67<span>]</span></a></sup> but he played in the Gentlemen v Players match at Lord's. He scored a rapid 113—his only century of the season—for the Players. By lunch on the last day, the Gentlemen had <a href="/wiki/Follow-on" title="Follow-on">followed-on</a> but were well placed to secure a draw with the score at 112 for three. Kilner settled the match by taking quick wickets and ending the innings with figures of six for 20 as the Players won by an innings.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69"><span>[</span>68<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner was recalled for Hearne in the fourth Test, but rain prevented all play after the first day. He bowled 12 wicketless overs,<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup> meaning he had not dismissed anyone in his first two Tests and he did not play in the last Test as Hearne returned.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span>[</span>71<span>]</span></a></sup> For Yorkshire, he had a more successful time, three times taking ten wickets in a match, including twelve for 55 in the final championship match of the season, which Yorkshire had to win to have a chance of winning the title. Yorkshire's opponents, <a href="/wiki/Sussex_County_Cricket_Club" title="Sussex County Cricket Club">Sussex</a>, were bowled out twice for less than 100,<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span>[</span>72<span>]</span></a></sup> and when other results went Yorkshire's way, the club were crowned champions for the third successive year.<sup id="cite_ref-P40_64-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P40-64"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner was chosen for the Players again in an end of season match at Scarborough.<sup id="cite_ref-fcmatches_17-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcmatches-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> Although his poor batting meant his form as an all-rounder was disappointing, he had a very good season as a bowler. Nevertheless, his selection to <a href="/wiki/English_cricket_team_in_Australia_in_1924%E2%80%9325" title="English cricket team in Australia in 1924–25">tour Australia that winter</a> with the MCC team surprised commentators.<sup id="cite_ref-P40_64-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P40-64"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tour_of_Australia">Tour of Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Tour of Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kilner_bowling_1925.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A cricketer bowling" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Kilner_bowling_1925.jpg/220px-Kilner_bowling_1925.jpg" width="220" height="267" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Kilner_bowling_1925.jpg/330px-Kilner_bowling_1925.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a7/Kilner_bowling_1925.jpg 2x" data-file-width="340" data-file-height="412" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kilner_bowling_1925.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Kilner bowling in the fifth Test at Sydney, 1924–25</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Playing for MCC under the captaincy of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Gilligan" title="Arthur Gilligan">Arthur Gilligan</a>, Kilner made 103 against <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia_cricket_team" title="Western Australia cricket team">Western Australia</a> on his maiden first-class appearance in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span>[</span>73<span>]</span></a></sup> He continued to show good all-round form in the less important, non first-class matches, but in first-class matches leading up to the Tests he was less successful. Kilner passed fifty runs just once after his century and with the ball took one wicket in 81 overs during seven innings.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_62-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-62"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P40_64-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P40-64"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup> He did not play in the first two Test matches, both of which were lost by England,<sup id="cite_ref-P40_64-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P40-64"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup> but was selected for the third when the selectors altered the bowling attack. During the match, a series of injuries to colleagues left Kilner with a heavy workload. Australia batted first and scored 489, a total which looked unlikely when they lost three wickets for 22 and later six wickets for 119. England's pace bowlers, Gilligan and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Tate" title="Maurice Tate">Maurice Tate</a>, had to leave the field injured, allowing Australia to recover; later the spinner <a href="/wiki/Tich_Freeman" title="Tich Freeman">Tich Freeman</a> hurt his wrist and could not bowl.<sup id="cite_ref-3T_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3T-75"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup> The only fit front-line bowlers remaining were Kilner and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Woolley" title="Frank Woolley">Frank Woolley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tourrep_76-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tourrep-76"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup> England fielded for nearly nine hours, and Kilner bowled 56 eight-<a href="/wiki/Delivery_(cricket)" title="Delivery (cricket)">ball</a> overs to take four for 127, his first wicket in Test matches being <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Richardson_(Australian_cricketer)" title="Arthur Richardson (Australian cricketer)">Arthur Richardson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3rd_test_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3rd_test-77"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup> Given the injuries, Gilligan later said Kilner gave "a truly great performance".<sup id="cite_ref-P41_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P41-78"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup> England could not match Australia's total, scoring only 365, but when the hosts batted again, rain fell and affected the pitch, making it responsive to spin bowling. Australia lost their last seven wickets for 39 runs in an hour and Kilner took four for 14 in his final spell of bowling to end with four for 51.<sup id="cite_ref-3T_75-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3T-75"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P41_78-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P41-78"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup> Needing 375 to win, Kilner scored 24 but England fell just short to lose by 11 runs. Consequently, Australia took an unbeatable 3–0 series lead.<sup id="cite_ref-3rd_test_77-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3rd_test-77"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup> Between the third and fourth matches in the series, Kilner recorded good bowling performances, with five for 35 against <a href="/wiki/Tasmania_cricket_team" title="Tasmania cricket team">Tasmania</a> and match figures of ten for 66 against <a href="/wiki/Victoria_cricket_team" title="Victoria cricket team">Victoria</a>, and kept his place for the final two Tests.<sup id="cite_ref-fcmatches_17-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcmatches-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79"><span>[</span>78<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the fourth Test, which England won comfortably, Kilner scored 79, his highest Test score, adding 133 with <a href="/wiki/Dodger_Whysall" title="Dodger Whysall" class="mw-redirect">Dodger Whysall</a>, the largest partnership of the match. When Australia batted, Kilner took five wickets in the match and stood out as one of the best bowlers.<sup id="cite_ref-4T_81-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4T-81"><span>[</span>80<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82"><span>[</span>81<span>]</span></a></sup> In the final Test, Kilner took four for 97 in Australia's first innings, but could not prevent the home team's fourth victory of the series.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83"><span>[</span>82<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner finished the series with 129 runs and averaged 29.80, batting mainly at number eight in the order, and <i>Wisden</i> noted he scored runs when they were needed.<sup id="cite_ref-tourrep_76-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tourrep-76"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tbat_84-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbat-84"><span>[</span>83<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tpos_85-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tpos-85"><span>[</span>84<span>]</span></a></sup> In the Tests, he captured 17 wickets at an average of 23.47. <i>Wisden</i> noted that no-one but Kilner could take wickets to support Tate, the most successful bowler. Apart from these two, no other bowler could manage more than 11 wickets and the other main bowlers averaged around 50.<sup id="cite_ref-tourrep_76-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tourrep-76"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86"><span>[</span>85<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tbowl_87-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbowl-87"><span>[</span>86<span>]</span></a></sup> In all first-class matches, Kilner scored 488 runs at 24.88 and took 40 wickets at 25.17.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_seasons">Final seasons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Final seasons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Decline_as_a_bowler">Decline as a bowler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Decline as a bowler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During 1925, Kilner was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Benefit_(sports)" title="Benefit (sports)">benefit match</a> and had an improved batting record for the season but critics began to notice a decline in his bowling. He was effective on pitches which favoured spin bowlers, but not on the flatter pitches prevalent in the dry summer of 1925.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup> Cricket journals, such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cricketer" title="The Cricketer">The Cricketer</a></i>, criticised his tendency to bowl <a href="/wiki/Leg_theory" title="Leg theory">leg theory</a>—a tactic regarded as negative and overly defensive—when confronted with batting-friendly conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-P43_89-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P43-89"><span>[</span>88<span>]</span></a></sup> He took 131 wickets at 17.92, finishing second in the Yorkshire averages and helping his team to their fourth successive County Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P43_89-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P43-89"><span>[</span>88<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90"><span>[</span>89<span>]</span></a></sup> He scored 1,068 runs at an average of 30.51 with two centuries. This gave him the third double of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner's popularity with the crowds peaked in 1925,<sup id="cite_ref-P43_89-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P43-89"><span>[</span>88<span>]</span></a></sup> when over three days, 71,000 people attended his benefit match against Middlesex. It raised £4,106 (the equivalent of around £175,000 in 2008), to set a new record for benefit proceeds.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_91-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-91"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P45_92-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P45-92"><span>[</span>91<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93"><span>[</span>92<span>]</span></a></sup> There were no Test matches in 1925, but Kilner was once again selected for the Gentlemen v Players match at Lord's, scoring 59 in his only innings and taking five wickets in the match, as well as some end of season matches for the MCC's team from the previous winter.<sup id="cite_ref-fcmatches_17-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcmatches-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the winter of 1925–26, Kilner was selected for the MCC tour of the West Indies, during which no Test matches were played. It was not personally successful for him and he recorded few effective performances.<sup id="cite_ref-P45_92-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P45-92"><span>[</span>91<span>]</span></a></sup> He scored 249 runs in first-class matches at 22.63, with just one score over fifty,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> and took 34 wickets at 29.50.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Yorkshire's run of success ended in 1926 as fierce rivals <a href="/wiki/Lancashire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Lancashire County Cricket Club">Lancashire</a> relegated them into second place.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_20-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner had some successful matches in the season and achieved at a consistent level, but his performance showed signs of deteriorating.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94"><span>[</span>93<span>]</span></a></sup> He scored 1,187 runs at 37.09, the highest seasonal average of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> He completed the double for the fourth and final time by taking 107 wickets, but his bowling average of 22.52 was his worst since 1920, before he became a regular bowler.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> He finished sixth in the Yorkshire batting and third in the bowling averages.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95"><span>[</span>94<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96"><span>[</span>95<span>]</span></a></sup> He played for the Players for the final time at Lord's, scoring 72, but failed to take a wicket.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97"><span>[</span>96<span>]</span></a></sup> His final first-class century came against Middlesex, when he compiled 150 out of a total of 415, dominating the scoring while he was at the crease.<sup id="cite_ref-P45_92-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P45-92"><span>[</span>91<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98"><span>[</span>97<span>]</span></a></sup> During the summer's <a href="/wiki/Australian_cricket_team_in_England_in_1926" title="Australian cricket team in England in 1926">Ashes series</a>, he was selected for the first four Tests. He only batted twice with a highest score of 36.<sup id="cite_ref-tbat_84-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbat-84"><span>[</span>83<span>]</span></a></sup> With the ball, he took seven wickets at 39.42,<sup id="cite_ref-tbowl_87-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbowl-87"><span>[</span>86<span>]</span></a></sup> but the editor of <i>Wisden</i> criticised much of the English bowling; he described Kilner's only notable achievement as quickly ending Australia's innings in the second Test at Lord's.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99"><span>[</span>98<span>]</span></a></sup> For the final Test, Kilner was dropped in favour of 48-year-old Wilfred Rhodes. According to the chairman of selectors, <a href="/wiki/Pelham_Warner" title="Pelham Warner">Pelham Warner</a>, Rhodes was recalled because of the lack of an effective alternative left-arm spinner.<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100"><span>[</span>99<span>]</span></a></sup> Rhodes had a successful match and England defeated Australia to win <a href="/wiki/The_Ashes" title="The Ashes">the Ashes</a> for the first time since 1912, but Kilner had played his last Test. In nine Tests, he scored 233 runs at an average of 33.28 and took 24 wickets at 30.58.<sup id="cite_ref-cricinfo_101-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cricinfo-101"><span>[</span>100<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Over the winter of 1926–27, Kilner declined an invitation to tour India by the MCC.<sup id="cite_ref-P48_102-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P48-102"><span>[</span>101<span>]</span></a></sup> The 1927 season, which proved to be Kilner's last, was his least effective for several years and a disappointing summer for Yorkshire. The county fell to third in the Championship behind Lancashire and Nottinghamshire.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_20-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P48_102-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P48-102"><span>[</span>101<span>]</span></a></sup> He failed to achieve a century, scoring 1,004 runs (average 33.46), and did not reach 100 wickets for the first time since 1921, taking just 86 wickets at an average of 23.68.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_9-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_10-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Wisden</i> said his bowling had lost its effectiveness and was no longer dangerous even when the pitch was helpful to spinners.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_91-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-91"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup> In the Yorkshire averages, he finished fifth in both batting and bowling.<sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103"><span>[</span>102<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104"><span>[</span>103<span>]</span></a></sup> In Kilner's final County Championship match, he scored 91 not out and took eight wickets, including five for 21 in the second innings, helping Yorkshire to a nine wicket victory.<sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105"><span>[</span>104<span>]</span></a></sup> In his final first-class match, for Yorkshire against MCC, Kilner scored an <a href="/wiki/Not_out" title="Not out">unbeaten</a> 51 to guide Yorkshire to an eight wicket win.<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106"><span>[</span>105<span>]</span></a></sup> He ended his first-class career with 14,707 runs at an average of 30.01 and 1,003 wickets at an average of 18.45.<sup id="cite_ref-cricinfo_101-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cricinfo-101"><span>[</span>100<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Maharaja of Patiala again invited Kilner to play and coach in India in the winter of 1927–28.<sup id="cite_ref-P48_102-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P48-102"><span>[</span>101<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner's sister Mollie later said he was very reluctant to take up the invitation and hesitated before accepting.<sup id="cite_ref-P50_107-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P50-107"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup> The trip started badly when Kilner's uncle, Irving Washington, died the day after he departed, and several of Kilner's actions suggest this triggered some form of depression.<sup id="cite_ref-P50_107-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P50-107"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup> Even so, Kilner recorded several large scores, including an unbeaten 283 in one (non first-class) match.<sup id="cite_ref-P50_107-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P50-107"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup> Near the end of trip, he began to suffer from a fever. His Yorkshire team mates, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Dolphin" title="Arthur Dolphin">Arthur Dolphin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Leyland" title="Maurice Leyland">Maurice Leyland</a>, who were also in India, believed he became unwell after eating oysters, but it is not certain how or when Kilner became ill. He began to have shivering attacks and perspiration while travelling from Marseilles on the way home.<sup id="cite_ref-P50_107-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P50-107"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup> It was obvious he was seriously ill when he arrived in Southampton; a cricketing engagement was cancelled and he was confined to bed.<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108"><span>[</span>107<span>]</span></a></sup> He refused treatment in Southampton, wishing to return to his family, and asking for his wife.<sup id="cite_ref-P52_109-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P52-109"><span>[</span>108<span>]</span></a></sup> Arriving in Wombwell on 27 March 1928, he was examined by his doctor and taken home. Subsequently, his condition deteriorated and he was taken to <a href="/wiki/Kendray" title="Kendray">Kendray</a> Fever Hospital, near Barnsley. Although it was thought he had passed the worst, his condition became critical in the first few days of April. On 5 April 1928, Kilner died from <a href="/wiki/Typhoid_fever" title="Typhoid fever">enteric fever</a> in the presence of his wife.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_91-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-91"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P52_109-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P52-109"><span>[</span>108<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The town of Wombwell expressed enormous sympathy and tributes came from around the world. When Kilner's funeral took place on 10 April, the streets were packed with mourners. Many came from outside the town, and according to contemporary estimates 100,000 people were present to pay tribute to Kilner; there may have been more.<sup id="cite_ref-P54_110-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P54-110"><span>[</span>109<span>]</span></a></sup> Over a thousand people were at the cemetery for the burial, and Yorkshire cricketers carried the coffin.<sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-111"><span>[</span>110<span>]</span></a></sup> Two years later, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_cricket_team_in_England_in_1930" title="Australian cricket team in England in 1930">Australian team which toured England in 1930</a> visited Kilner's grave in Wombwell to lay a wreath.<sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112"><span>[</span>111<span>]</span></a></sup> The rector at the funeral said in his tribute: "A Yorkshire wicket has fallen and one of Yorkshire's best men is out; and we lament his loss; not merely because it is the loss of a great cricketer, but because it is the loss of such a cricketer as Roy Kilner was."<sup id="cite_ref-P54_110-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P54-110"><span>[</span>109<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_personality">Style and personality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Kilner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Style and personality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:200px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kilner_batting.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A cricketer hitting the ball" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/74/Kilner_batting.jpg" width="198" height="283" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="198" data-file-height="283" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kilner_batting.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Kilner batting in the fifth Test at Sydney, 1924–25</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Kilner first came into the Yorkshire side as a batsman. He was considered a good, reliable batsman when he began, although his style was regarded by Yorkshire critics as unorthodox to the point where they disapproved of some of his unusual, eccentric shots.<sup id="cite_ref-H92_113-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H92-113"><span>[</span>112<span>]</span></a></sup> For Yorkshire, he appeared high in the <a href="/wiki/Batting_order_(cricket)" title="Batting order (cricket)">order</a>, but batted between number seven and number nine for England.<sup id="cite_ref-tpos_85-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tpos-85"><span>[</span>84<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-114"><span>[</span>113<span>]</span></a></sup> If the circumstances of the game demanded it, he was able to play slowly and defensively and restrain his naturally aggressive style. He was particularly effective while playing the <a href="/wiki/Drive_(cricket)" title="Drive (cricket)" class="mw-redirect">drive</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pull_shot" title="Pull shot" class="mw-redirect">pull shot</a>. For most of his career, his effectiveness as a batsman was not compromised by his improvement and increasing workload as a bowler.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_91-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-91"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_115-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-115"><span>[</span>114<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In terms of bowling, Kilner was an effective performer on rain affected pitches.<sup id="cite_ref-H92_113-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H92-113"><span>[</span>112<span>]</span></a></sup> His accuracy also enabled him to bowl on good pitches without the batsmen being able to score too many runs.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_91-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-91"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1923, journalist <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Pullin" title="Alfred Pullin">Alfred Pullin</a> said Kilner spun the ball more than any other English bowler. He noted, as did <i>Wisden</i>, how Kilner often bowled <a href="/wiki/Over_the_wicket" title="Over the wicket" class="mw-redirect">over the wicket</a>, meaning he bowled from the right hand side of the wickets, unusual for a left-arm spinner in this period.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P35_56-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P35-56"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup> This established a contrast with his fellow left-arm spinner, Wilfred Rhodes, and increased their effectiveness. Furthermore, it allowed him to surprise batsmen with a different delivery, such as one <a href="/wiki/Arm_ball" title="Arm ball">which did not turn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-116" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-116"><span>[</span>115<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner's <i>Wisden</i> portrait also credited him with the gift of imagination, describing how he always tried to think of new ways to beat and dismiss batsmen.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> He would experiment with his bowling, for example altering his pace slightly, if conditions were unfavourable towards bowlers.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_91-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-91"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup> Kilner delivered <a href="/wiki/Left-arm_unorthodox_spin" title="Left-arm unorthodox spin">left-arm wrist spin</a> at times, constantly practising it in the <a href="/wiki/Cricket_nets" title="Cricket nets">cricket nets</a>. His brother Norman believed Roy was the person who coined the term "chinaman" to describe such a style, although other players have also claimed to originate the phrase.<sup id="cite_ref-P30_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P30-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>At a time when the Yorkshire team was successful but exhibited a grim and determined attitude, Kilner was very popular with spectators. They liked him for his cheerfulness and desire to entertain, an attitude not shared by all of his team-mates.<sup id="cite_ref-P35_56-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P35-56"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup> He had many friends in the game at a time when Yorkshire were not always welcome in other counties due to their on-field attitude.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_91-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-91"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-H92_113-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H92-113"><span>[</span>112<span>]</span></a></sup> When he was chosen as a Bowler of the Year in 1924, <i>Wisden</i> remarked that he was devoted to the game, possessed a cheery temperament and stood out among players for his personality,<sup id="cite_ref-coy_31-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> while his obituary described him as modest and generous.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_91-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-91"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup> His <i>Times</i> obituary described him as "not only a notable exponent of the game, but a man of rare charm. Few modern professionals commanded such a measure of esteem and kindly regard from his own immediate colleagues and his opponents in the cricket field as did Roy Kilner".<sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_115-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-115"><span>[</span>114<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Kilner was a favourite of cricket writer <a href="/wiki/Neville_Cardus" title="Neville Cardus">Neville Cardus</a>, who attributed several sayings to him in his prose. For example, Cardus described Kilner as saying about Yorkshire and Lancashire matches at the time: "What we want is no umpires and fair cheating all round," and "We say good morning and after that all we say is 'Howzat?'"<sup id="cite_ref-H93_117-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H93-117"><span>[</span>116<span>]</span></a></sup> Also, when asked about contemporary problems in the game, in particular the negative attitude of some teams, he replied the game was fine, it was "t'crowd that wants educating up to it."<sup id="cite_ref-H93_117-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H93-117"><span>[</span>116<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
