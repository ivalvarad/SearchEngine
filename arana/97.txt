<p>On 10 October 2015 at 10:04 local time (<a href="/wiki/Eastern_European_Summer_Time" title="Eastern European Summer Time">EEST</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara</a>, the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, two bombs were detonated outside <a href="/wiki/Ankara_Central_railway_station" title="Ankara Central railway station">Ankara Central railway station</a>. As of October 16, the death toll stands at 102<sup id="cite_ref-hdn102_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdn102-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> and more than 400 people were injured.<sup id="cite_ref-cnn_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-basbakanlik_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-basbakanlik-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The bombs appeared to target a "Labour, Peace and Democracy" rally organised by the <a href="/wiki/Confederation_of_Progressive_Trade_Unions_of_Turkey" title="Confederation of Progressive Trade Unions of Turkey">Confederation of Progressive Trade Unions of Turkey</a> (DİSK), the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Chambers_of_Turkish_Engineers_and_Architects" title="Union of Chambers of Turkish Engineers and Architects">Union of Chambers of Turkish Engineers and Architects</a> (TMMOB), the <a href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Democratic_Party_(Turkey)" title="Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)">Peoples' Democratic Party</a> (HDP), the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Medical_Association" title="Turkish Medical Association">Turkish Medical Association</a> (TTB) and the <a href="/wiki/Confederation_of_Public_Workers%27_Unions" title="Confederation of Public Workers' Unions">Confederation of Public Workers' Unions</a> (KESK). The rally was held to protest against the <a href="/wiki/2015_PKK_rebellion" title="2015 PKK rebellion">growing conflict</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Armed_Forces" title="Turkish Armed Forces">Turkish Armed Forces</a> and the separatist <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Workers%27_Party" title="Kurdistan Workers' Party">Kurdistan Workers' Party</a> (PKK). The incident occurred 21 days before the scheduled <a href="/wiki/Turkish_general_election,_November_2015" title="Turkish general election, November 2015">1 November general election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-aa_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aa-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The attack was the deadliest of its kind in <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Republic_of_Turkey" title="History of the Republic of Turkey">Turkey's modern history</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The governing <a href="/wiki/Justice_and_Development_Party_(Turkey)" title="Justice and Development Party (Turkey)">Justice and Development Party</a> (AKP), the main opposition <a href="/wiki/Republican_People%27s_Party_(Turkey)" title="Republican People's Party (Turkey)">Republican People's Party</a> (CHP) and the opposition <a href="/wiki/Nationalist_Movement_Party" title="Nationalist Movement Party">Nationalist Movement Party</a> (MHP) condemned the attack and called it an attempt to cause division within Turkey.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> CHP and MHP leaders heavily criticized the government for the security failure, whereas HDP directly blamed the AKP government for the bombings.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> Various political parties ended up cancelling their election campaigns while three days of national mourning were declared by the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Turkey" title="Prime Minister of Turkey">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Ahmet_Davuto%C4%9Flu" title="Ahmet Davutoğlu">Ahmet Davutoğlu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bugun.com.tr_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bugun.com.tr-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>No organisation has yet claimed responsibility for the attack. The Ankara Attorney General stated that they were investigating the possibility of two cases of <a href="/wiki/Suicide_bombing" title="Suicide bombing" class="mw-redirect">suicide bombings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> On October 19, one of the two suicide bombers was officially identified as Yunus Emre Alagöz, an ethnic Kurd from Adıyaman and the younger brother of Abdurrahman Alagöz, the perpetrator of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Suru%C3%A7_bombing" title="2015 Suruç bombing">Suruç bombing</a>; both brothers have suspected links to the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant</a> (ISIL) and the ISIL affiliated <a href="/wiki/Dokumac%C4%B1lar" title="Dokumacılar">Dokumacılar</a> group.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph11925064_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph11925064-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-alagoz_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alagoz-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following a <a href="/wiki/2015_Suru%C3%A7_bombing" title="2015 Suruç bombing">suicide bombing in Suruç</a> that killed 33 people on 20 July 2015, the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Armed_Forces" title="Turkish Armed Forces">Turkish Armed Forces</a> have been fighting both the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant</a> (ISIL) and a <a href="/wiki/2015_PKK_rebellion" title="2015 PKK rebellion">renewed PKK rebellion</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Workers%27_Party" title="Kurdistan Workers' Party">Kurdistan Workers' Party</a> (PKK). The resumption of the conflict with the PKK resulted in an end to the <a href="/wiki/Solution_process" title="Solution process">Solution process</a>, a series of peace negotiations between the government and the PKK alongside a ceasefire in place since 2012. With <a href="/wiki/Operation_Martyr_Yal%C3%A7%C4%B1n" title="Operation Martyr Yalçın">airstrikes</a> initially targeting both the PKK and ISIL, later military operations began focusing explicitly on PKK positions in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Iraq" title="Northern Iraq" class="mw-redirect">Northern Iraq</a>, prompting a surge of counter PKK-related violence in the mainly Kurdish south-east of Turkey. By 7 October, the surge in violence since July had led to the deaths of 141 soldiers and 1,740 militants, leading to several pro-government commentators to claim that the PKK was close to defeat.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> However, the large number of soldiers killed also contributed to civil unrest in other parts of the country, with attacks by <a href="/wiki/Turkish_nationalism" title="Turkish nationalism">Turkish nationalists</a> taking place against the <a href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Democratic_Party_(Turkey)" title="Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)">Peoples' Democratic Party</a> (HDP) headquarters. Many politicians and commentators alleged that the country was close to <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_situation">Political situation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political situation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The increase in violence came shortly after the governing <a href="/wiki/Justice_and_Development_Party_(Turkey)" title="Justice and Development Party (Turkey)">Justice and Development Party</a> (AKP) lost its majority in <a href="/wiki/Grand_National_Assembly_of_Turkey" title="Grand National Assembly of Turkey">Parliament</a> after 13 years of government alone in the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_general_election,_June_2015" title="Turkish general election, June 2015">June 2015 election</a>. When the attacks took place, the preceding AKP government led by <a href="/wiki/Ahmet_Davuto%C4%9Flu" title="Ahmet Davutoğlu">Ahmet Davutoğlu</a> remained in power until a new coalition government could be formed, with an <a href="/wiki/2015_interim_election_government_of_Turkey" title="2015 interim election government of Turkey">interim election government</a> also headed by Davutoğlu taking office on 28 August 2015 after President <a href="/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan" title="Recep Tayyip Erdoğan">Recep Tayyip Erdoğan</a> called for <a href="/wiki/Turkish_general_election,_November_2015" title="Turkish general election, November 2015">a new election</a>. Critics have accused the AKP of trying to regain nationalist voters back from the <a href="/wiki/Nationalist_Movement_Party" title="Nationalist Movement Party">Nationalist Movement Party</a> (MHP) by purposely ending the <a href="/wiki/Solution_process" title="Solution process">solution process</a> and also trying to reduce turnout in the HDP electoral strongholds in the south-east by creating unrest there. Concern had been raised about whether an election could be securely conducted amid the violence in the region.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Perceived_targets">Perceived targets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Perceived targets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The explosions occurred shortly before a 'Labour, Peace and Democracy' rally supported by the <a href="/wiki/Confederation_of_Progressive_Trade_Unions_of_Turkey" title="Confederation of Progressive Trade Unions of Turkey">Confederation of Progressive Trade Unions of Turkey</a> (DİSK), the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Chambers_of_Turkish_Engineers_and_Architects" title="Union of Chambers of Turkish Engineers and Architects">Union of Chambers of Turkish Engineers and Architects</a> (TMMOB), the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Medical_Association" title="Turkish Medical Association">Turkish Medical Association</a> (TTB) and the <a href="/wiki/Confederation_of_Public_Workers%27_Unions" title="Confederation of Public Workers' Unions">Confederation of Public Workers' Unions</a> (KESK) was due to take place.<sup id="cite_ref-sozcu.com.tr1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sozcu.com.tr1-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> The rally was scheduled in <a href="/wiki/S%C4%B1hhiye_Square" title="Sıhhiye Square">Sıhhiye Square</a>, the railways overpass bridge was the gathering area. It was reported that many attendants that were present in preparation for the rally were supporters of the <a href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Democratic_Party_(Turkey)" title="Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)">Peoples' Democratic Party</a> (HDP), with the rally area containing numerous HDP, <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Turkey)" title="Labour Party (Turkey)">Labour Party</a> (EMEP) and <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_of_the_Oppressed" title="Socialist Party of the Oppressed">Socialist Youth Associations Federation</a> (SGDF) flags.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bombings">Bombings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Bombings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first bomb exploded at around 10:04 local time (<a href="/wiki/Eastern_European_Summer_Time" title="Eastern European Summer Time">EEST</a>) while rally participants were singing an anthem commemorating the <a href="/wiki/Bloody_Sunday_(1969)" title="Bloody Sunday (1969)">Bloody Sunday</a> incident of 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> The second bomb exploded a few seconds later. It was also observed that the bombings were in close proximity of the <a href="/wiki/National_Intelligence_Organization_(Turkey)" title="National Intelligence Organization (Turkey)">National Intelligence Organisation</a> (MİT) headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> Shortly after the bombing, security forces cleared the area in case of a third and fourth bomb.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On the day of the blasts, the initial death toll was reported as 86, along with 186 wounded.<sup id="cite_ref-sozcu.com.tr1_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sozcu.com.tr1-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> Next day, the total number of deaths was announced as 97.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> According to the prime minister's statement on October 14, 99 people were killed in total.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> The number increased in the following days, as people in the hospitals succumbed to their wounds, to 100<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> and to 102.<sup id="cite_ref-hdn102_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdn102-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Medical_Association" title="Turkish Medical Association">Turkish Medical Association</a> (TTB) made independent claims, reporting that 97 died and over 400 people had been injured,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> which they later updated to 105,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> and 106.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Democratic_Party_(Turkey)" title="Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)">HDP</a> also independently claimed that the number of deaths was 128.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> However, after a few days they retracted the statement, and apologised for the misleading claim.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Immediate_response">Immediate response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Immediate response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Witnesses at the scene told the media that the police began using tear gas shortly after the bombs went off while stopping ambulances from getting through. Angry people tried to attack police cars after the blast, with the HDP claiming that the police attacked people carrying the injured to safety.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Shortly after the bombings, the Minister of Health <a href="/wiki/Mehmet_M%C3%BCezzino%C4%9Flu" title="Mehmet Müezzinoğlu">Mehmet Müezzinoğlu</a>, the Minister of the Interior <a href="/wiki/Selami_Alt%C4%B1nok" title="Selami Altınok">Selami Altınok</a>, and the Minister of Justice <a href="/wiki/Kenan_%C4%B0pek" title="Kenan İpek">Kenan İpek</a> visited the scene to carry out investigations. However, they were met by protestors who chanted anti-AKP slogans and were forced to leave the scene less than one minute after arriving.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> The Ankara Attorney General announced that they were investigating the possibility of two suicide bombers, while the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_State_Railways" title="Turkish State Railways">Turkish State Railways</a> (TCDD) stated that there would be delays to train services passing through the <a href="/wiki/Ankara_Central_railway_station" title="Ankara Central railway station">Ankara Central railway station</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Ahmet_Davuto%C4%9Flu" title="Ahmet Davutoğlu">Ahmet Davutoğlu</a> cancelled all of his prior engagements and stated that he would halt his election campaign for three days. He met with President <a href="/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan" title="Recep Tayyip Erdoğan">Recep Tayyip Erdoğan</a>, who also cancelled his engagements in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> following the incident. The Prime Minister later held a security summit, which was attended by Deputy Prime Ministers <a href="/wiki/Yal%C3%A7%C4%B1n_Akdo%C4%9Fan" title="Yalçın Akdoğan">Yalçın Akdoğan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Numan_Kurtulmu%C5%9F" title="Numan Kurtulmuş">Numan Kurtulmuş</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tu%C4%9Frul_T%C3%BCrke%C5%9F" title="Tuğrul Türkeş">Tuğrul Türkeş</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/National_Intelligence_Organization_(Turkey)" title="National Intelligence Organization (Turkey)">National Intelligence Organisation</a> (MİT) undersecretary <a href="/wiki/Hakan_Fidan" title="Hakan Fidan">Hakan Fidan</a>, Interior Minister <a href="/wiki/Selami_Alt%C4%B1nok" title="Selami Altınok">Selami Altınok</a>, Justice Minister <a href="/wiki/Kenan_%C4%B0pek" title="Kenan İpek">Kenan İpek</a>, the Governor of Ankara <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mehmet_K%C4%B1l%C4%B1%C3%A7lar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mehmet Kılıçlar (page does not exist)">Mehmet Kılıçlar</a>, the General Director of Security <a href="/w/index.php?title=Celalettin_Lekesiz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Celalettin Lekesiz (page does not exist)">Celalettin Lekesiz</a>, and several other senior civil servants working for the Prime Ministry and the Ministry of Health.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Radio_and_Television_Supreme_Council" title="Radio and Television Supreme Council">Radio and Television Supreme Council</a> (RTÜK) announced a temporary ban on all press coverage of the bombings following a request by the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Turkey" title="Prime Minister of Turkey">Prime Ministry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> slowed down after the attack due to heavy use.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Perpetrators">Perpetrators</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Perpetrators">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Initial_speculation">Initial speculation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Initial speculation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Ankara Attorney General stated that they were investigating the possibility of twin suicide bombings.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> It was observed that an anonymous <i><a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a></i> account had claimed that an explosion could take place in Ankara just one day before the attack actually happened.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup><sup>[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Pages_needing_translation_into_English" title="Wikipedia:Pages needing translation into English">source needs translation</a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The lack of any immediate statement from any known non-state perpetrator taking responsibility for the attack resulted in speculation over the possible perpetrators. <a href="/wiki/Nationalist_Movement_Party" title="Nationalist Movement Party">Nationalist Movement Party</a> (MHP) leader <a href="/wiki/Devlet_Bah%C3%A7eli" title="Devlet Bahçeli">Devlet Bahçeli</a> stated that the attacks bore a resemblance to the <a href="/wiki/2015_Suru%C3%A7_bombing" title="2015 Suruç bombing">explosion</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suru%C3%A7" title="Suruç">Suruç</a> in July, raising debate on whether the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant</a> (ISIL) could have been responsible.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup><sup>[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Pages_needing_translation_into_English" title="Wikipedia:Pages needing translation into English">source needs translation</a></i>]</sup> It was reported that the type of bomb used bore strong resemblance to the materials used in the Suruç bombing, signalling a potential connection between the two incidents.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup><sup>[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Pages_needing_translation_into_English" title="Wikipedia:Pages needing translation into English">source needs translation</a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Democratic_Party_(Turkey)" title="Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)">Peoples' Democratic Party</a> (HDP) openly blamed the Turkish state and the government for conducting the attack, accusing the government of collaborating with non-state actors and taking insufficient action to tackle their presence.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup> The HDP has claimed that the governing <a href="/wiki/Justice_and_Development_Party_(Turkey)" title="Justice and Development Party (Turkey)">Justice and Development Party</a> (AKP) was guilty of being "murderers with blood on their hands" and also of being the number one threat to Turkey's peace and security. The HDP's accusation was met with strong criticism by the AKP government.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Veysel_Ero%C4%9Flu" title="Veysel Eroğlu">Veysel Eroğlu</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Forest_and_Water_Management_(Turkey)" title="Ministry of Forest and Water Management (Turkey)">Minister of Forest and Water Management</a>, made a heavily criticised statement in <a href="/wiki/Afyonkarahisar" title="Afyonkarahisar">Afyonkarahisar</a> implying that the HDP had purposely organised the attack against their own supporters to raise public sympathy for their party.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> Although Eroğlu did not name the HDP or the PKK specifically, he referred to the <a href="/wiki/2015_Diyarbak%C4%B1r_rally_bombing" title="2015 Diyarbakır rally bombing" class="mw-redirect">Diyarbakır HDP rally bombing</a> in June as being an attempt to raise support for the HDP "just so that they can pass the 10% <a href="/wiki/Election_threshold" title="Election threshold">election threshold</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup><sup>[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Pages_needing_translation_into_English" title="Wikipedia:Pages needing translation into English">source needs translation</a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Investigations">Investigations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Investigations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Initially the government suggested the perpetrators could be any of the following anti-government groups:<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">Islamic State</a>/Daesh (IS), Kurdistan Workers' Party (PKK), <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_People%27s_Liberation_Party%E2%80%93Front" title="Revolutionary People's Liberation Party–Front">Revolutionary People's Liberation Party–Front</a> (DHKP-C), <a href="/wiki/Marxist%E2%80%93Leninist_Communist_Party_(Turkey/Northern_Kurdistan)" title="Marxist–Leninist Communist Party (Turkey/Northern Kurdistan)">Marxist–Leninist Communist Party</a> (MLKP)</p>
<p>A day after the bombing, Davutoğlu suggested that early investigations pointed to the involvement of IS.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup> However, opposition political parties<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words" class="mw-redirect"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (October 2015)">which?</span></a></i>]</sup> did not accept this attribution.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Yunus Emre Alagöz, an ethnic Kurd from Adıyaman and the younger brother of Abdurrahman Alagöz, the perpetrator of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Suru%C3%A7_bombing" title="2015 Suruç bombing">Suruç bombing</a>, was suspected by the government to be one of the suicide bombers.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph11925064_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph11925064-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> On October 14 media reports alleged that Yunus, and a second suspect, Ömer Deniz Dündar, both of whom are believed to have links to <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">ISIL</a>, were identified using <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> from the scene of the blast."<sup id="cite_ref-ibtimes_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ibtimes-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hdn238_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdn238-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup> On October 19, one of the two suicide bombers was officially identified as Yunus Emre Alagöz.<sup id="cite_ref-alagoz_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alagoz-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_reactions">Domestic reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Domestic reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Political">Political</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Political">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>President <a href="/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan" title="Recep Tayyip Erdoğan">Recep Tayyip Erdoğan</a> condemned the bombings and vowed that the Turkish people will stand in "unity and solidarity" following the "heinous attack". He also stated that Turkey would not give in to efforts to sow division in society.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup> He encouraged everyone to take responsibility and act with good intentions, claiming that the government was working to uncover the full details of the incident as quickly as possible.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup><sup>[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Pages_needing_translation_into_English" title="Wikipedia:Pages needing translation into English">source needs translation</a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Ahmet_Davuto%C4%9Flu" title="Ahmet Davutoğlu">Ahmet Davutoğlu</a>, the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Justice_and_Development_Party_(Turkey)" title="Justice and Development Party (Turkey)">Justice and Development Party</a> (AKP), issued a statement condemning the attack and claiming that it was an attack against democracy and against all segments of society. He said Turkey was an example of a country that had kept united despite several threats against national unity, announcing plans to meet with opposition party leaders in regards to the attack. Declaring three days of national mourning, Davutoğlu vowed to bring the perpetrators to justice regardless of who they were.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span>[</span>66<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Main opposition leader <a href="/wiki/Kemal_K%C4%B1l%C4%B1%C3%A7daro%C4%9Flu" title="Kemal Kılıçdaroğlu">Kemal Kılıçdaroğlu</a>, the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Republican_People%27s_Party_(Turkey)" title="Republican People's Party (Turkey)">Republican People's Party</a> (CHP), claimed that they were ready to fulfill any task to end such attacks in Turkey<sup id="cite_ref-cnnturk_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnnturk-67"><span>[</span>67<span>]</span></a></sup> and agreed to meet with Davutoğlu to discuss the bombing.<sup id="cite_ref-soczu_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-soczu-68"><span>[</span>68<span>]</span></a></sup> He stated that all political parties had a duty to stand together against such attacks and called on the perpetrators to identify themselves, further claiming that violence was never an answer to a difference in viewpoints.<sup id="cite_ref-cnnturk_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnnturk-67"><span>[</span>67<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Opposition leader <a href="/wiki/Devlet_Bah%C3%A7eli" title="Devlet Bahçeli">Devlet Bahçeli</a>, the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Nationalist_Movement_Party" title="Nationalist Movement Party">Nationalist Movement Party</a> (MHP), cancelled a planned electoral rally in <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0zmir" title="İzmir">İzmir</a> following the attack and issued a statement in which he claimed that Turkey was paying the price for the AKP's close relations with violent groups, refusing to meet with Davutoğlu.<sup id="cite_ref-soczu_68-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-soczu-68"><span>[</span>68<span>]</span></a></sup> Condemning the bombings as an attack on the country's unity, he also stated that the fact that such perpetrators could evade security and intelligence organisations to conduct a bombing in the country's capital city was another serious issue of concern.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Opposition leader <a href="/wiki/Selahattin_Demirta%C5%9F" title="Selahattin Demirtaş">Selahattin Demirtaş</a>, the co-leader of the <a href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Democratic_Party_(Turkey)" title="Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)">Peoples' Democratic Party</a> (HDP), drew parallels with the bombings in <a href="/wiki/2015_Suru%C3%A7_bombing" title="2015 Suruç bombing">Suruç</a> and <a href="/wiki/2015_Diyarbak%C4%B1r_rally_bombing" title="2015 Diyarbakır rally bombing" class="mw-redirect">Diyarbakır</a> earlier in the year, claiming that his party was specifically targeted. Accusing the state of conducting a "massacre" in the centre of the capital <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara</a>, he further claimed that they were facing a "mad, undignified attitude that has lost its mind". He accused the AKP government and Recep Tayyip Erdoğan of forcing violence onto the people of Turkey, denouncing them as "murderers with blood on their hands". Referring to Erdoğan as a "gang leader", he claimed that Erdoğan had been able to conduct rallies under complete security but members of the public wishing to hold a rally for peace had been "massacred". He further claimed that the AKP was the biggest threat to the country's peace and security, drawing criticism from AKP leader and Prime Minister Ahmet Davutoğlu.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Shortly after the bombing, <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Workers%27_Party" title="Kurdistan Workers' Party">PKK</a>/<a href="/wiki/Group_of_Communities_in_Kurdistan" title="Group of Communities in Kurdistan">KCK</a> declared a ceasefire in order to ensure that a peaceful election would be held on 1 November,<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span>[</span>71<span>]</span></a></sup> which was reportedly already being planned before the bombing took place.<sup id="cite_ref-bayikafp_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayikafp-72"><span>[</span>72<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span>[</span>73<span>]</span></a></sup> Nevertheless, the ceasefire did not materialize, as policemen and soldiers continued to be killed in PKK attacks everyday since the bombing.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Before closing his morning television chat show on 13 October, <a href="/wiki/TRT" title="TRT" class="mw-disambig">TRT</a> anchor Selver Gözüaçık read a <i>tweet</i> by one of his viewers that read it was not right to "lump all the victims together [because] some may be innocent." He agreed with the sentiment, saying that there may have been "police officers, cleaning staff, passersby or people trying to get to work" who were also killed in the bombings.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78"><span>[</span>78<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Former <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature" title="Nobel Prize in Literature">Nobel Prize in Literature</a> laureate <a href="/wiki/Orhan_Pamuk" title="Orhan Pamuk">Orhan Pamuk</a> criticized Erdogan for what he said was a climate of insecurity as a result of the latter's persistence on trying to achieve a parliamentary majority that has brought the country to the brink of sectarian conflict. "The electoral defeat enraged Erdogan...he didn't succeed in convincing the Kurds to give him their votes for his plan to create a presidential republic. That is why he decided to go to the polls again on November 1. But neither the government nor the army were satisfied with how things were going and they agreed to resume the war against the Kurdish movement."<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>During a <a href="/wiki/One_minute_silence" title="One minute silence" class="mw-redirect">one minute silence</a> for the victims at the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying</a> match <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying_Group_A#Turkey_v_Iceland" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying Group A">between</a> Turkey and <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, the crowd instead booed, whistled, shouted Turkish ultranationalist slogans and chanted <a href="/wiki/Allahu_akbar" title="Allahu akbar" class="mw-redirect">Allahu akbar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80"><span>[</span>80<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the attack, three days of national mourning were declared by Prime Minister Ahmet Davutoğlu.<sup id="cite_ref-bugun.com.tr_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bugun.com.tr-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Election_campaigns">Election campaigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Election campaigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the bombing occurring during the election campaigns for the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_general_election,_November_2015" title="Turkish general election, November 2015">November 2015 general election</a>, various parties abandoned their scheduled rallies. The Justice and Development Party (AKP) cancelled their planned election programme for three days after the attack.<sup id="cite_ref-123" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-123"><span>[</span>123<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-124" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-124"><span>[</span>124<span>]</span></a></sup> The Republican People's Party (CHP) also cancelled their daily programme, with party leader <a href="/wiki/Kemal_K%C4%B1l%C4%B1%C3%A7daro%C4%9Flu" title="Kemal Kılıçdaroğlu">Kemal Kılıçdaroğlu</a> having been due to spend the day campaigning in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-125" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-125"><span>[</span>125<span>]</span></a></sup> The Nationalist Movement Party (MHP) announced that their scheduled rally in <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0zmir" title="İzmir">İzmir</a> would take place at a later date.<sup id="cite_ref-126" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-126"><span>[</span>126<span>]</span></a></sup> The Peoples' Democratic Party (HDP), having claimed that they were the targets of the attack, abandoned a campaign strategy meeting due in their İstanbul headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-127" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-127"><span>[</span>127<span>]</span></a></sup> President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan also cancelled his prior engagements in Istanbul,<sup id="cite_ref-128" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-128"><span>[</span>128<span>]</span></a></sup> including a trip to Turkmenistan.<sup id="cite_ref-129" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-129"><span>[</span>129<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Resignation_calls">Resignation calls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Resignation calls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a screen grab of Justice Minister <a href="/wiki/Kenan_%C4%B0pek" title="Kenan İpek">Kenan İpek</a> showing him smiling went viral, CHP leader Kemal Kılıçdaroğlu said that they should resign in a meeting with Davutoğlu. He said: "The sight of the justice minister [smiling at the reporter's question] is startling. The  [<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>] society is going through a severe trauma and meanwhile the justice minister is smiling. The justice minister cannot remain in his office." He added that Davutoğlu would make a decision upon receipt of a report on Ipek’s conduct. It was also cricised on social media. Further, Deputy Chairman <a href="/wiki/Haluk_Ko%C3%A7" title="Haluk Koç">Haluk Koç</a> also called for the immediate resignation of Interior Minister <a href="/wiki/Selami_Alt%C4%B1nok" title="Selami Altınok">Selami Altınok</a> saying that he was unable to carry out his responsibilities.<sup id="cite_ref-130" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-130"><span>[</span>130<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_action">Industrial action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Industrial action">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Confederation_of_Public_Workers%27_Unions" title="Confederation of Public Workers' Unions">Confederation of Public Workers' Unions</a> (KESK), <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Chambers_of_Turkish_Engineers_and_Architects" title="Union of Chambers of Turkish Engineers and Architects">Union of Chambers of Turkish Engineers and Architects</a> (TMMOB), the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Medical_Association" title="Turkish Medical Association">Turkish Medical Association</a> (TTB) and the <a href="/wiki/Confederation_of_Progressive_Trade_Unions_of_Turkey" title="Confederation of Progressive Trade Unions of Turkey">Confederation of Progressive Trade Unions of Turkey</a> (DISK), which had all been organisers of the peace rally, declared that two days of industrial action would be held on Monday 12 October and Tuesday 13 October as a show of respect to the dead, as well as a protest against the "fascist massacre." The unions also stated that they would not give up on their efforts to end the conflict.<sup id="cite_ref-131" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-131"><span>[</span>131<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PKK_ceasefire">PKK ceasefire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: PKK ceasefire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shortly after the bombings, the Kurdistan Workers' Party (PKK) declared a ceasefire to allow a peaceful election to take place on 1 November. The ceasefire announcement, which was made through the executive of the <a href="/wiki/Group_of_Communities_in_Kurdistan" title="Group of Communities in Kurdistan">Group of Communities in Kurdistan</a> (KCK), declared that the PKK would not conduct any attacks unless provoked or in self-defence.<sup id="cite_ref-132" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-132"><span>[</span>132<span>]</span></a></sup> It is unclear if the ceasefire announcement, which was made approximately an hour after the Ankara bombing, was related to the incident.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anti-government_protests">Anti-government protests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Ankara_bombings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Anti-government protests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the afternoon following the attack, thousands of union members began protesting at <a href="/wiki/Taksim_Square" title="Taksim Square">Taksim Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0stanbul" title="İstanbul" class="mw-redirect">İstanbul</a> against the bombing, with many participants chanting anti-government slogans and calling on both the governing AKP and for Erdoğan to resign.<sup id="cite_ref-133" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-133"><span>[</span>133<span>]</span></a></sup> Amongst the about 10,000 people, slogans were chanted that said "the state is a killer" and "we know the murderers."<sup id="cite_ref-ptv_110-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ptv-110"><span>[</span>110<span>]</span></a></sup> Similar protests occurred in <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0zmir" title="İzmir">İzmir</a>, with tensions between protestors and riot police briefly rising in <a href="/wiki/Alsancak" title="Alsancak">Alsancak</a> before deescalating shortly after. HDP <a href="/wiki/Members_of_Parliament" title="Members of Parliament" class="mw-redirect">Members of Parliament</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_National_Assembly_of_Turkey" title="Grand National Assembly of Turkey">Grand National Assembly of Turkey</a> led a protest in <a href="/wiki/Batman,_Turkey" title="Batman, Turkey">Batman</a>, before being met by riot police using tear gas and <a href="/wiki/Water_cannon" title="Water cannon">water cannon</a>. A group of 15 masked individuals in <a href="/wiki/K%C4%B1z%C4%B1lay,_Ankara" title="Kızılay, Ankara">Kızılay, Ankara</a>, began attacking police officers with fireworks before being met by water cannon and <a href="/wiki/Pepper_spray" title="Pepper spray">pepper spray</a>. Over 2,000 protestors led demonstrations in <a href="/wiki/Diyarbak%C4%B1r" title="Diyarbakır">Diyarbakır</a> and 300 protestors participated in protests in <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Eanl%C4%B1urfa" title="Şanlıurfa">Şanlıurfa</a>, having been accompanied by HDP and <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Regions_Party" title="Democratic Regions Party">Democratic Regions Party</a> (DBP) politicians. Similar protests took place in <a href="/wiki/Van,_Turkey" title="Van, Turkey">Van</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tunceli" title="Tunceli">Tunceli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kars" title="Kars">Kars</a>, with participating politicians from the HDP and CHP as well as union members from KESK, TMMOB, TTB and DISK.<sup id="cite_ref-134" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-134"><span>[</span>134<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
