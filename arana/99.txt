<p><b>Presidential elections</b> were held in <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> on 11 October 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Long-term president <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lukashenko" title="Alexander Lukashenko">Alexander Lukashenko</a> ran for his fifth term in office, having won every presidential election since independence in 1991. He was re-elected with 83.47% of the vote. The "<a href="/wiki/None_of_the_above" title="None of the above">against all</a>" option received more votes than any opposition candidate.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the vote, six incarcerated opposition figures were pardoned by Lukashenko. The move was welcomed by the <a href="/wiki/Organization_for_Security_and_Co-operation_in_Europe" title="Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe">OSCE</a> electoral observer mission with the head of the delegation, Kent Hasted, saying: "The recent release of political prisoners and a welcoming approach to observers were positive developments. However, the hope that this gave us for broader electoral progress was largely unfulfilled."<sup id="cite_ref-React_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-React-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Candidates">Candidates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Candidates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A total of eight candidates attempted to register to run in the elections by collecting the required 100,000 signatures; incumbent President <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lukashenko" title="Alexander Lukashenko">Alexander Lukashenko</a> running as an independent, <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_Left_Party_%22A_Just_World%22" title="Belarusian Left Party &quot;A Just World&quot;">Belarusian Left Party "A Just World"</a> chairman <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Kalyakin" title="Sergey Kalyakin">Sergey Kalyakin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democratic_Party_(Belarus)" title="Liberal Democratic Party (Belarus)">Liberal Democratic Party</a> chairman <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Gaidukevich" title="Sergei Gaidukevich">Sergei Gaidukevich</a>, People's referendum member <a href="/wiki/Tatsiana_Karatkevich" title="Tatsiana Karatkevich">Tatsiana Karatkevich</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Civic_Party_of_Belarus" title="United Civic Party of Belarus">United Civic Party</a> chairman <a href="/wiki/Anatoly_Lebedko" title="Anatoly Lebedko">Anatoly Lebedko</a>, economist Viktor Tereshchenko (who ran in the <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_presidential_election,_2010" title="Belarusian presidential election, 2010">2010 elections</a>), unemployed teacher Zhanna Romanovskaya and <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_Patriotic_Party" title="Belarusian Patriotic Party">Belarusian Patriotic Party</a> chairman Nikolai Ulakhovich.</p>
<p>Although five candidates (Lukashenko, Ulakhovich, Gaidukevich, Tereshchenko and Karatkevich) obtained over 100,000 signatures,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> the vast majority of Tereshchenko's signatures were declared invalid,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> resulting in only four candidates (marked in bold) being able to contest the <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">elections</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaign">Campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The government allowed an unauthorised opposition rally in the capital, Minsk, to go ahead on the eve of the election without police intervention but Lukashenko warned that post-election protests would not be tolerated. On the same day, the winner of the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature" title="Nobel Prize in Literature">Nobel Prize in Literature</a> (announced just two days prior), <a href="/wiki/Svetlana_Alexievich" title="Svetlana Alexievich">Svetlana Alexievich</a>, warned Europe to beware of Lukashenko's government as an alleged "soft dictatorship."<sup id="cite_ref-ndv_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndv-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conduct">Conduct</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Conduct">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">CIS</a> mission included 312 accredited observers from Azerbaijan, Armenia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Moldova, Russia, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, the CIS Inter-Parliamentary Assembly, the Parliamentary Assembly of the Union of Belarus and Russia, and the CIS Executive Committee.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The OSCE/<a href="/wiki/Office_for_Democratic_Institutions_and_Human_Rights" title="Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights">ODIHR</a> long-term observation mission was led by Jacques Faure and included more than 40 observers, whilst the short-term observation mission had over 400 people.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The OSCE's <a href="/wiki/Kent_H%C3%A4rstedt" title="Kent Härstedt">Kent Härstedt</a> suggested the vote may have been undermined by "significant problems," especially during the counting of the votes. "It is clear that Belarus still has a long way to go towards fulfilling its democratic commitments."<sup id="cite_ref-ndv_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndv-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Several cases of forced early voting were recorded, usually among students of state universities and workers of state organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-viasna.dosr_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-viasna.dosr-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The "Human Rights Defenders for Free Elections" group also registered the overestimation of turnout (half of independent observers noted that the actual number of early voters didn't match with the official turnout) and unreasonable interference in observers' work.<sup id="cite_ref-viasna.dosr_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-viasna.dosr-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On election day, independent observers noted several violations during the elections. In <a href="/wiki/Barysaw" title="Barysaw">Barysaw</a>, chairman of the District Election Commission didn't announce the results before calling somewhere (presumably the higher-level electoral commission). After his call, the observer states, the number of votes for the opposition candidate Karatkevich in the final protocol decreased from 219 to 77.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Salihorsk" title="Salihorsk">Salihorsk</a> polling station 24, the number of voters was announced to be 1,190, while the independent observer counted only 808 people.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> A similar incident happened in <a href="/wiki/Babruysk" title="Babruysk">Babruysk</a>, where the official turnout on three polling stations differed from the observers' calculations by several hundred voters in each station. The observer was not allowed to watch for the counting process.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Slonim" title="Slonim">Slonim</a>, an independent observer noticed two packs of filled ballots thrown into the early voting ballot box.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the Central Election Commission, more than 36% of voters used the <a href="/wiki/Early_voting" title="Early voting">early voting</a> process, higher than in previous years.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Overall turnout was 87.22% – highest in <a href="/wiki/Vitebsk_Region" title="Vitebsk Region">Vitebsk Region</a> at 91.08% and lowest in Minsk at 74.38%.<sup id="cite_ref-result_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-result-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vybory2015_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vybory2015-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lukashenko" title="Alexander Lukashenko">Alexander Lukashenko</a> won the election with 83.47% of the vote.<sup id="cite_ref-result_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-result-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic">Domestic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Domestic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Head of the Central Election Commission, <a href="/wiki/Lidiya_Yermoshina" title="Lidiya Yermoshina" class="mw-redirect">Lidiya Yermoshina</a> is quoted to say: "I think the election campaign was civilized, cultured and calm."<sup id="cite_ref-ndv_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndv-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Opposition leaders <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Neklyaev" title="Vladimir Neklyaev" class="mw-redirect">Vladimir Neklyaev</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anatoly_Lebedko" title="Anatoly Lebedko">Anatoly Lebedko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mikola_Statkevich" title="Mikola Statkevich">Mikola Statkevich</a> said they would not recognize the results.<sup id="cite_ref-ndv_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndv-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/Flag_of_Germany.svg/23px-Flag_of_Germany.svg.png" width="23" height="14" class="thumbborder" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/Flag_of_Germany.svg/35px-Flag_of_Germany.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/Flag_of_Germany.svg/46px-Flag_of_Germany.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="600" />&#160;</span>Germany – Foreign Minister <a href="/wiki/Frank-Walter_Steinmeier" title="Frank-Walter Steinmeier">Frank-Walter Steinmeier</a> said from Luxembourg that the lack of repression against the opposition could pave the path towards easing sanctions against the country for four months. "There have been changes in Belarus, compared to the two past elections. If Belarus stays on this path, there is a willingness, and there is unanimity on this, to change the relationships with Belarus." However, he added that though there were few surprises, Belarus was changing, especially in regards to the "liberation of political prisoners" before the vote.<sup id="cite_ref-ndv_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndv-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c3/Flag_of_France.svg/23px-Flag_of_France.svg.png" width="23" height="15" class="thumbborder" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c3/Flag_of_France.svg/35px-Flag_of_France.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c3/Flag_of_France.svg/45px-Flag_of_France.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600" />&#160;</span>France – Minister for European Affairs <a href="/wiki/Harlem_D%C3%A9sir" title="Harlem Désir">Harlem Désir</a> said his country sought to encourage an opening, while also warning that backsliding on human rights could result in the re-imposition of sanctions.<sup id="cite_ref-React_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-React-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Analysts">Analysts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Analysts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An analyst at the <a href="/wiki/BelaPAN" title="BelaPAN">BelaPAN</a> Alexander Klaskovsky noticed that there were no mass protests and arrests of the opposition place this time.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A comment by <a href="/wiki/Agence_France_Presse" title="Agence France Presse" class="mw-redirect">Agence France Presse</a> suggested the changes in this election were due to Lukashenko's shrewdness in playing Western Europe against Russia and an attempt to decouple from Russia due to <a href="/wiki/International_sanctions_during_the_Ukrainian_crisis" title="International sanctions during the Ukrainian crisis">western sanctions</a> it faced.<sup id="cite_ref-ndv_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndv-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
