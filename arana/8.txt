<p><b>Sir Gerald Herbert Portal</b> <a href="/wiki/Knight_Commander_of_the_Order_of_St_Michael_and_St_George" title="Knight Commander of the Order of St Michael and St George" class="mw-redirect">KCMG</a> <a href="/wiki/Companion_of_the_Order_of_the_Bath" title="Companion of the Order of the Bath" class="mw-redirect">CB</a> (13 March 1858 – 25 January 1894) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Diplomacy" title="Diplomacy">diplomat</a> who was the <a href="/wiki/Consul_General" title="Consul General" class="mw-redirect">Consul General</a> for <a href="/wiki/British_East_Africa" title="British East Africa" class="mw-redirect">British East Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commissioner#British_and_Commonwealth_overseas_possessions" title="Commissioner">British Special Commissioner</a> to <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>, and a main figure in the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Uganda_Protectorate" title="Uganda Protectorate">Uganda Protectorate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diplomatic_career">Diplomatic career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Diplomatic career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gerald Portal was the second son of the politician <a href="/wiki/Melville_Portal" title="Melville Portal">Melville Portal</a>. He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a>, joined the diplomatic service as an <a href="/wiki/Attach%C3%A9" title="Attaché">attaché</a> in 1879, and was posted to <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> where he was promoted to third secretary of legation in 1881.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In June 1882 Portal had the good fortune to be temporarily attached to the consulate-general at <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, at a critical period in the history of British relations with <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. He was present at the <a href="/wiki/Bombardment_of_Alexandria" title="Bombardment of Alexandria">bombardment of Alexandria</a>, and for his services on that occasion received a <a href="/wiki/Egypt_Medal" title="Egypt Medal">medal</a> with clasp and the <a href="/wiki/Khedive%27s_Star" title="Khedive's Star">Khedive's Star</a>. He became a favourite with Sir Evelyn Baring (afterwards <a href="/wiki/Lord_Cromer" title="Lord Cromer" class="mw-redirect">Lord Cromer</a>), the British representative. On 1 April 1885 he was promoted second secretary.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> For some weeks in the summers of 1886 and 1887 he took charge of the residency during Lord Cromer's absence, and conducted its affairs with credit.<sup id="cite_ref-dnb_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dnb-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In October 1887 Portal was ordered to attempt a reconciliation between the king of <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Empire" title="Ethiopian Empire">Abyssinia</a> and the Italian government. To succeed in such a mission was almost impossible, but he made every effort, and showed rare judgment and coolness in travelling through a disturbed country. He returned on 31 December, without effecting his purpose, but with a considerably enhanced reputation. He was made CB, and in <i>My Mission to Abyssinia</i> (1888) he gave an account of the expedition.<sup id="cite_ref-dnb_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dnb-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Returning to his duties at the Cairo agency, Portal was <a href="/wiki/Charg%C3%A9_d%27affaires" title="Chargé d'affaires">chargé d'affaires</a> in the autumn of 1888. From 30 April to 14 November 1889 he acted as Consul-General at <a href="/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a>, and in 1891 he was appointed there as Consul-General for British East Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_to_Uganda">Mission to Uganda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mission to Uganda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1892 Portal was appointed British Special Commissioner to East Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> He was directed to visit Uganda and to report to the <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Government" title="Her Majesty's Government" class="mw-redirect">British Government</a> on the desirability of setting up a <a href="/wiki/Protectorate#British_and_Commonwealth_protectorates" title="Protectorate">British Protectorate</a> to replace the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_British_East_Africa_Company" title="Imperial British East Africa Company">Imperial British East Africa Company</a>. The British East Africa Company, which was the administrator of <a href="/wiki/British_East_Africa" title="British East Africa" class="mw-redirect">British East Africa</a> (including the territory of Uganda), was becoming increasingly ineffective in its venture as a commercial company with colonial administrative rights – amidst conflicts between rival factions, including the <a href="/wiki/Kabaka_of_Buganda" title="Kabaka of Buganda">Kabaka</a> (King) of <a href="/wiki/Buganda" title="Buganda">Buganda</a>, French <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church" class="mw-redirect">Catholic</a>, and British <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestant</a> <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionaries</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-apter_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apter-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Portal established a settlement between the French and British Missionaries in Uganda, and on 1 April 1893, Portal hauled down the flag of the British East Africa Company at <a href="/wiki/Mengo,_Uganda" title="Mengo, Uganda">Mengo</a> (the Kabaka's residence) and hoisted the <a href="/wiki/Union_Flag" title="Union Flag" class="mw-redirect">Union Jack</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> On 29 May 1893, he signed a treaty with <a href="/wiki/Mwanga_II_of_Buganda" title="Mwanga II of Buganda">Mwanga II</a>, the Kabaka.</p>
<p>Portal returned to Britain and recommended the declaration of Uganda as a protectorate. He died from <a href="/wiki/Typhoid_fever" title="Typhoid fever">typhoid fever</a> in London on 25 January 1894, at the age of 36. On 18 June 1894, Uganda was declared a British Protectorate.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The town of <a href="/wiki/Fort_Portal" title="Fort Portal">Fort Portal</a> in western Uganda, where Portal had his base, is named after him.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cricket">Cricket</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cricket">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Portal was a sound middle order batsman and a fast left arm bowler who played for the <a href="/wiki/I_Zingari" title="I Zingari">I Zingari</a> club. He played in one first class game, scoring 6 and taking 4 wickets for 55 runs.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1890 Gerald Portal married Lady Alice Bertie, daughter of the <a href="/wiki/Montagu_Bertie,_7th_Earl_of_Abingdon" title="Montagu Bertie, 7th Earl of Abingdon">Earl of Abingdon</a>. They had no children.</p>
