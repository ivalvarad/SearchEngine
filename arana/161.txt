<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_History_Portal"><big>The History Portal</big></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<p><br /></p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:1em">
<center><a href="/wiki/Library_of_Alexandria" title="Library of Alexandria">Library of Alexandria</a></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/Library_of_Alexandria" title="Library of Alexandria"><img alt="Library of Alexandria" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Ancientlibraryalex.jpg/200px-Ancientlibraryalex.jpg" width="200" height="203" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Ancientlibraryalex.jpg/300px-Ancientlibraryalex.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Ancientlibraryalex.jpg/400px-Ancientlibraryalex.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1072" data-file-height="1090" /></a><br /></p>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/History" title="History">History</a></b> is the discovery, collection, organization, analysis and presentation of information about past events. History can also mean a continuous, typically chronological, record of important or public events or of a particular trend or institution. Scholars who write about history are called <a href="/wiki/Historian" title="Historian">historians</a>. It is a field of <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a> which uses a <a href="/wiki/Narrative" title="Narrative">narrative</a> to examine and analyse the sequence of events, and it sometimes attempts to investigate objectively the patterns of cause and effect that determine events. Historians debate the nature of history and its usefulness. This includes discussing the study of the discipline as an end in itself and as a way of providing "perspective" on the problems of the present. The stories common to a particular culture, but not supported by external sources (such as the legends surrounding <a href="/wiki/King_Arthur" title="King Arthur">King Arthur</a>) are usually classified as <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">cultural heritage</a> rather than the "disinterested investigation" needed by the discipline of history. Events of the past prior to written record are considered <a href="/wiki/Prehistory" title="Prehistory">prehistory</a>.</p>
<p>Amongst scholars, the fifth century BC <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greek</a> historian <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a> is considered to be the "father of history", and, along with his contemporary <a href="/wiki/Thucydides" title="Thucydides">Thucydides</a>, forms the foundations for the modern study of history. Their influence, along with other historical traditions in other parts of their world, have spawned many different interpretations of the nature of history which has evolved over the centuries and are continuing to change. The modern study of history has many different fields including those that focus on certain regions and those which focus on certain topical or thematic elements of historical investigation. Often history is taught as part of primary and secondary education, and the academic study of history is a major discipline in university studies.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_article">Selected article</span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Fort_Snelling_Round_Tower.JPG" class="image" title="Fort Snelling played a pivotal role in Minnesota's history and in development of nearby Minneapolis and Saint Paul"><img alt="Fort Snelling played a pivotal role in Minnesota's history and in development of nearby Minneapolis and Saint Paul" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Fort_Snelling_Round_Tower.JPG/150px-Fort_Snelling_Round_Tower.JPG" width="150" height="200" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Fort_Snelling_Round_Tower.JPG/225px-Fort_Snelling_Round_Tower.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Fort_Snelling_Round_Tower.JPG/300px-Fort_Snelling_Round_Tower.JPG 2x" data-file-width="480" data-file-height="640" /></a></div>
The <b><a href="/wiki/History_of_Minnesota" title="History of Minnesota">history of the U.S. state of Minnesota</a></b> is shaped by its original <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> residents, European exploration and <a href="/wiki/European_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="European colonization of the Americas">settlement</a>, and the emergence of industries made possible by the state's natural resources. <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> achieved prominence through fur trading, logging, and farming, and later through railroads, and iron mining. While those industries remain important, the state's economy is now driven by banking, computers, and health care.
<p>The earliest known settlers followed herds of large game to the region during the <a href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">last glacial period</a>. They preceded the <a href="/wiki/Anishinaabe" title="Anishinaabe">Anishinaabe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sioux" title="Sioux">Dakota</a>, and other Native American inhabitants. <a href="/wiki/Fur_trade" title="Fur trade">Fur traders</a> from France arrived during the 17th century. Europeans, moving west during the 19th century, drove out most of the Native Americans. <a href="/wiki/Fort_Snelling" title="Fort Snelling">Fort Snelling</a>, built to protect United States territorial interests, brought early settlers to the area. Early settlers used <a href="/wiki/Saint_Anthony_Falls" title="Saint Anthony Falls">Saint Anthony Falls</a> for powering sawmills in the area that became <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis,_Minnesota" title="Minneapolis, Minnesota" class="mw-redirect">Minneapolis</a>, while others settled downriver in the area that became <a href="/wiki/Saint_Paul,_Minnesota" title="Saint Paul, Minnesota">Saint Paul</a>.</p>
<p>Minnesota became a part of the United States as <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Territory" title="Minnesota Territory">Minnesota Territory</a> in 1849, and became <a href="/wiki/List_of_U.S._states_by_date_of_statehood" title="List of U.S. states by date of statehood" class="mw-redirect">the 32nd U.S. state</a> on May 11, 1858. After the upheaval of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dakota_War_of_1862" title="Dakota War of 1862">Dakota War of 1862</a>, the state's economy started to develop when natural resources were tapped for logging and farming. Railroads attracted immigrants, established the farm economy, and brought goods to market. The power provided by Saint Anthony Falls spurred the growth of Minneapolis, and the innovative milling methods gave it the title of the "milling capital of the world."</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_biography">Selected biography</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Edward_Teach_Commonly_Call%27d_Black_Beard_(bw).jpg" class="image" title="Blackbeard (c. 1726 engraving used to illustrate Johnson's General History)"><img alt="Blackbeard (c. 1726 engraving used to illustrate Johnson's General History)" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Edward_Teach_Commonly_Call%27d_Black_Beard_%28bw%29.jpg/150px-Edward_Teach_Commonly_Call%27d_Black_Beard_%28bw%29.jpg" width="150" height="230" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Edward_Teach_Commonly_Call%27d_Black_Beard_%28bw%29.jpg/225px-Edward_Teach_Commonly_Call%27d_Black_Beard_%28bw%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Edward_Teach_Commonly_Call%27d_Black_Beard_%28bw%29.jpg/300px-Edward_Teach_Commonly_Call%27d_Black_Beard_%28bw%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3092" data-file-height="4738" /></a></div>
<b>Edward Teach</b> (c. 1680 – 22&#160;November 1718), better known as <b><a href="/wiki/Blackbeard" title="Blackbeard">Blackbeard</a></b>, was a notorious English <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">pirate</a> who operated around the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a> and the eastern coast of the <a href="/wiki/European_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="European colonization of the Americas">American colonies</a>. Although little is known about his early life, he was probably born in <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>, England. He may have been a sailor on <a href="/wiki/Privateer" title="Privateer">privateer</a> ships during <a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne%27s_War" title="Queen Anne's War">Queen Anne's War</a> before settling on the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> island of <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a>, a base for Captain <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Hornigold" title="Benjamin Hornigold">Benjamin Hornigold</a>, whose crew Teach joined sometime around 1716. Hornigold placed him in command of a sloop he had captured, and the two engaged in numerous acts of piracy. Their numbers were boosted by the addition to their fleet of two more ships, one of which was commanded by <a href="/wiki/Stede_Bonnet" title="Stede Bonnet">Stede Bonnet</a>, but toward the end of 1717 Hornigold retired from piracy, taking two vessels with him.
<p>Teach captured a French merchant vessel, renamed her <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne%27s_Revenge" title="Queen Anne's Revenge">Queen Anne's Revenge</a></i>, and equipped her with 40 guns. He became a renowned pirate, his <a href="/wiki/Cognomen" title="Cognomen">cognomen</a> derived from his thick black beard and fearsome appearance; he was reported to have tied lit fuses under his hat to frighten his enemies. He formed an alliance of pirates and blockaded the port of <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston, South Carolina</a>. After successfully ransoming its inhabitants, he ran <i>Queen Anne's Revenge</i> aground on a sandbar near Beaufort, North Carolina. He parted company with Bonnet, settling in <a href="/wiki/Bath,_North_Carolina" title="Bath, North Carolina">Bath Town</a>, where he accepted a royal pardon. But he was soon back at sea and attracted the attention of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Spotswood" title="Alexander Spotswood">Alexander Spotswood</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Virginia" title="Governor of Virginia">Governor of Virginia</a>. Spotswood arranged for a party of soldiers and sailors to try to capture the pirate, which they did on 22&#160;November 1718. During a ferocious battle, Teach and several of his crew were killed by a small force of sailors led by Lieutenant <a href="/wiki/Robert_Maynard" title="Robert Maynard">Robert Maynard</a>.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="On_this_day">On this day</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<p><b><a href="/wiki/October_20" title="October 20">October 20</a></b>: Birth of the <b><a href="/wiki/B%C3%A1b" title="Báb">Báb</a></b>, a <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar#Holy_days" title="Bahá'í calendar">holy day</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith" title="Bahá'í Faith">Bahá'í Faith</a></p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 100px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Maria_Theresa_young.jpg" class="image" title="Maria Theresa of Austria"><img alt="Maria Theresa of Austria" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Maria_Theresa_young.jpg/100px-Maria_Theresa_young.jpg" width="100" height="128" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Maria_Theresa_young.jpg/150px-Maria_Theresa_young.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Maria_Theresa_young.jpg/200px-Maria_Theresa_young.jpg 2x" data-file-width="653" data-file-height="839" /></a>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1740" title="1740">1740</a> – Under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Pragmatic_Sanction_of_1713" title="Pragmatic Sanction of 1713">Pragmatic Sanction of 1713</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/Maria_Theresa" title="Maria Theresa">Maria Theresa</a></b> <i>(pictured)</i> assumed the throne of the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg Monarchy in Austria</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1818" title="1818">1818</a> – The United Kingdom and the United States signed the <b><a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_1818" title="Treaty of 1818">Treaty of 1818</a></b>, which settled the <a href="/wiki/Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border" title="Canada–United States border">Canada–United States border</a> on the <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel</a> between the Pacific Ocean and <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_the_Woods" title="Lake of the Woods">Lake of the Woods</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1944" title="1944">1944</a> – <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>: Fulfilling <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur%27s_escape_from_the_Philippines" title="Douglas MacArthur's escape from the Philippines">a promise</a> he made two years previous, General <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte" title="Battle of Leyte">landed</a></b> on <a href="/wiki/Leyte" title="Leyte">Leyte</a> to begin the recapture of the entire Philippine Archipelago.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1961" title="1961">1961</a> – The Soviet Union performed the first armed test of a <b><a href="/wiki/Submarine-launched_ballistic_missile" title="Submarine-launched ballistic missile">submarine-launched ballistic missile</a></b>, launching an <a href="/wiki/R-13_(missile)" title="R-13 (missile)">R-13</a> from a <a href="/wiki/Golf_class_submarine" title="Golf class submarine" class="mw-redirect">Golf class submarine</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1982" title="1982">1982</a> – During a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> match between <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">FC Spartak Moscow</a> and <a href="/wiki/HFC_Haarlem" title="HFC Haarlem">HFC Haarlem</a>, a large number of attendees tried to leave the <a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_Stadium" title="Luzhniki Stadium">Grand Sports Arena of the Central Lenin Stadium</a> at the same time, resulting in <b><a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_disaster" title="Luzhniki disaster">a stampede</a></b> that caused 66 deaths.</li>
</ul>
