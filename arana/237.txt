<p>An 'intranet portal' is the gateway that unifies access to enterprise information and applications<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_Norman_Group_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_Norman_Group-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> on an <a href="/wiki/Intranet" title="Intranet">intranet</a>. It is a tool that helps a company manage its data, applications, and information more easily, and through personalized views. Some portal solutions today are able to integrate <a href="/wiki/Legacy_application" title="Legacy application" class="mw-redirect">legacy applications</a>, objects from other portals, &amp; handle thousands of user requests. In a corporate enterprise environment, it is also known as an <i><a href="/wiki/Enterprise_portal" title="Enterprise portal">enterprise portal</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intranet_portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Corporate intranets began gaining popularity during the 1990s. Intranets quickly grew more complex as the result the concept of intranet portal was born. Today, intranet portals provide value-added capabilities such as managing workflows, increasing collaboration between work groups, and allowing content creators to self-publish their information.</p>
<p>One typical example of an intranet portal is <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Sharepoint" title="Microsoft Sharepoint" class="mw-redirect">Microsoft Sharepoint</a>, which is used by 46% of organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It provides a lot of features necessary for collaboration, integration and customization.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_features">Main features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intranet_portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Integration</b> — Ability to integrate with your current tools or the possibility of adding new tools. You have your outlook calendar and email integrated within intranet.</p>
<p><b>Security</b> — Enable user or group based security to secure documents and sites throughout the intranet portal.</p>
<p><b>Customization</b> — Software that is flexible to allow for organization. Web Parts can be used to create custom modules which can make interaction easier with the site. Ability for users to customize tools and resources they use most often.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Collaboration" title="Collaboration">Collaboration</a></b> — People are now able to collaborate their work with each other. Example would be multiple people working on one document.</p>
<p><b>Communication Channels</b> — Allows corporations to promote corporate culture and present information in a more interactive way than before.</p>
<p><b>Automation</b> — Things like workflows and templates can automate specific document creation. Alerts can be created to help learn of changes and new additions to the intranet.</p>
<p><b>Applications</b> — Links to applications for associates to perform duties.</p>
<p><b>User Friendly</b> — Application must be easy to use and understand due to a wide range of technical abilities.</p>
<p><b>Remote Access</b> — Ability for users to access content away from the office.</p>
<p><b><a href="/w/index.php?title=Document_Repository&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Document Repository (page does not exist)">Document Repository</a></b> — Ability to store and retrieve document information while maintaining regular backups to prevent data loss.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">Blog</a></b> — Used as a method to provide more timely information to employees, customers, and business partners.</p>
<p><b>People Search</b> — Search enterprise wide for employee information such as contact information, specialty areas, group membership, personal interest, etc.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Enterprise_Search" title="Enterprise Search" class="mw-redirect">Enterprise Search</a></b> — search enterprise content using enterprise search</p>
<p><b>Targeted Content</b> — Business portal administrators can target content by business group area, e.g., HR, Marketing, Legal, Corporate Executives, etc.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Advantages">Advantages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intranet_portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Advantages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Intranet portal helps employees make better and more informed decisions, which result from increased knowledge. It also helps reduce costs, saves time, increases collaboration, increases productivity and effectiveness.</p>
<p>Intranet portal can help employees find information more easily and perform their jobs better, though few portal designs are optimal just out-of-the-box. In fact, especially in smaller companies, designers can realize some features found in off-the-shelf portal software through simpler (do-it-yourself) means. Most intranets have become completely unwieldy and present a highly fragmented and confusing user experience, with no consistency and little navigational support. Portals aim to correct this problem by presenting a single gateway to all corporate information and services. One benefit of creating this consistent look and feel is users need less time to learn how to use the environment. They also more easily recognize where they are in the portal and where they can go—no small feat when navigating a large information space. By integrating services and presenting personalized snippets on the initial screen, intranet portals also reduce the need for users to browse far and wide to obtain needed information, thus making it easier for them to perform their jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_Norman_Group_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_Norman_Group-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Intranet portal is a Web-based tool that allows users to create a customized site that dynamically pulls in Internet activities and desired content into a single page. By providing a contextual framework for information, portals can bring S&amp;T (Science and Technology) and organizational "knowledge" to the desktop.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Disadvantages">Disadvantages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intranet_portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Disadvantages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Intranet Portals can be a large business cost. The maintenance and management can be time consuming and expensive. Not only is it a cost to keep the portal running but a cost when the system goes offline. Most intranets are established to put all an organization's resources into one place and having that offline can force operations to be put on hold.</p>
<p>Security issues can become an ongoing problem. Unauthorized access is a concern and can result in users gaining access to sensitive information. Denial of access can cause issues for users needing access for their work.</p>
<p>Having everything in one place is only good if it's organized. Information overload can make finding information very difficult - lowering productivity.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intranet_portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Tools &amp; Resources</b> — Area for employees to link to or download necessary applications to perform work functions. Information also provided to find internal and external resources.</p>
<p><b>Associate Services</b> — .</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Business_Operations" title="Business Operations" class="mw-redirect">Business Operations</a></b> — To give users access to important business policies and manuals.</p>
<p><b>Company Calendar</b> — To give user access to important company event dates and times.</p>
<p><b>Access Point for Employees</b> — Location for employees default main company webpage to obtain all information regarding the company.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">Wiki</a></b> — can be used in the business environment for knowledge management</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Workflow" title="Workflow">Workflow Management</a></b> — Establish work flows for common business tasks such as submitting expense reports, submitting corporate HR paperwork and document approval processes.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Bulletin_Board" title="Bulletin Board" class="mw-redirect">Bulletin Board</a></b> — Manage corporate announcements.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Task_management" title="Task management">Task Management</a></b> — Create and update shared task lists throughout the corporation.</p>
