<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_portal_of_portals"><big>The portal of portals</big></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #1043b8; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #f9f9ff;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:PorcheCentralNordCathedraleChartre041130-.jpg" class="image" title="Portal"><img alt="Portal" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/PorcheCentralNordCathedraleChartre041130-.jpg/110px-PorcheCentralNordCathedraleChartre041130-.jpg" width="110" height="172" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/PorcheCentralNordCathedraleChartre041130-.jpg/165px-PorcheCentralNordCathedraleChartre041130-.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/PorcheCentralNordCathedraleChartre041130-.jpg/220px-PorcheCentralNordCathedraleChartre041130-.jpg 2x" data-file-width="751" data-file-height="1171" /></a></div>
<p>A <b><a href="/wiki/Portal_(architecture)" title="Portal (architecture)">portal</a></b> is an opening in the walls of a building, gate or fortification, and especially a grand entrance to an important structure. Doors, metal gates or <a href="/wiki/Portcullis" title="Portcullis">portcullis</a> in the opening can be used to control entry or exit. The surface surrounding the opening may be made of simple building materials or decorated with <a href="/wiki/Ornament_(architecture)" title="Ornament (architecture)" class="mw-redirect">ornamentation</a>. Elements of a portal can include the <a href="/wiki/Voussoir" title="Voussoir">voussoir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tympanum_(architecture)" title="Tympanum (architecture)">tympanum</a>, an ornamented <a href="/wiki/Mullion" title="Mullion">mullion</a> or <i>trumeau</i> between doors, and <a href="/wiki/Column" title="Column">columns</a> with carvings of saints in the <a href="/wiki/Westwork" title="Westwork">westwork</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Church_(building)" title="Church (building)">church</a>.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Portals_in_science_fiction" title="Portals in science fiction" class="mw-redirect">Portals in science fiction</a></b> allow rapid travel between distant locations, often originating from some combination of natural phenomenon and technological device. These fictional devices are required for most stories on an inter-solar scale, otherwise transit time would be excessive for <a href="/wiki/Storytelling" title="Storytelling">storytelling</a> purposes. An advantage of portal technology over a <a href="/wiki/Faster-than-light_drive" title="Faster-than-light drive" class="mw-redirect">faster-than-light drive</a> is that it can be imagined to work instantly, and can <a href="/wiki/Time_portal" title="Time portal">travel to the past or future</a>. In other forms of fiction, a portal may be <a href="/wiki/Magic_in_fiction" title="Magic in fiction">magical</a>, and connect to an <a href="/wiki/Parallel_universe_(fiction)" title="Parallel universe (fiction)">alternate universe</a> or <a href="/wiki/Plane_(cosmology)" title="Plane (cosmology)" class="mw-redirect">plane of existence</a>.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_article">Selected article</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #1043b8; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #f9f9ff;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Computer-generated_imagery" title="Computer-generated imagery">computer-generated imagery</a> and real-time <a href="/wiki/3D_computer_graphics" title="3D computer graphics">3D computer graphics</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/Portal_rendering" title="Portal rendering">portal rendering</a></b> is an algorithm for <a href="/wiki/Hidden_surface_determination" title="Hidden surface determination">visibility determination</a>.</p>
<p>A game level might contain many graphical polygons; only a few of which may be visible on screen at a given time. Visibility determination allows the renderer to decide which of those fall into that category and thus reduce rendering time.</p>
<p>For example, in a <a href="/wiki/Computer_game" title="Computer game" class="mw-redirect">computer game</a>, the game area might be divided to several <i>sectors</i>. These sectors would be then connected to each other by small openings such as doors or windows. These openings are referred as <i>portals</i>. When the sector behind a portal needs to be drawn, the only parts that are visible are the parts that can be seen through the portal. Therefore, the sector can be <a href="/wiki/Clipping_(computer_graphics)" title="Clipping (computer graphics)">clipped</a> against the portal boundaries to remove <a href="/wiki/Overdraw" title="Overdraw" class="mw-redirect">overdraw</a>.</p>
<p>The use of portals simplifies the <a href="/wiki/Game_engine" title="Game engine">game engine</a>'s task of determining visible areas and objects from any given point of view of the level, and simplifies rendering by allowing it to use each portal as a <a href="/wiki/Viewing_frustum" title="Viewing frustum">viewing frustum</a> for the area it leads to.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_biography">Selected biography</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #1043b8; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #f9f9ff;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Gerald_Herbert_Portal" title="Gerald Herbert Portal" class="mw-redirect">Gerald Herbert Portal</a></b> (1858–1894) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Diplomacy" title="Diplomacy">diplomat</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Consul_General" title="Consul General" class="mw-redirect">Consul General</a> for <a href="/wiki/British_East_Africa" title="British East Africa" class="mw-redirect">British East Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commissioner#British_and_Commonwealth_overseas_possessions" title="Commissioner">British Special Commissioner</a> to <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>. Educated at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton</a>, he joined the <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_service" title="Diplomatic service">diplomatic service</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Attach%C3%A9" title="Attaché">attaché</a> in 1879, and was posted to the Egyptian Agency the same year. In 1886 he became Acting Agent and Consul General, and the following year he was sent to <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>. In 1889 he was appointed to <a href="/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a> as the Consul General for British East Africa.</p>
<p>In 1892 Portal was appointed British Special Commissioner to Uganda to report to the <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Government" title="Her Majesty's Government" class="mw-redirect">British Government</a> on setting up a <a href="/wiki/Protectorate#British_and_Commonwealth_protectorates" title="Protectorate">British Protectorate</a> to replace the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_British_East_Africa_Company" title="Imperial British East Africa Company">East Africa Company</a>, which was becoming increasingly ineffective amidst conflicts between rival factions, including the <a href="/wiki/Kabaka_of_Buganda" title="Kabaka of Buganda">Kabaka</a> (King) of <a href="/wiki/Buganda" title="Buganda">Buganda</a>, French <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church" class="mw-redirect">Catholic</a>, and British <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestant</a> <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionaries</a>.</p>
<p>Portal established a settlement between the French and British Missionaries in Uganda, and on May 29, 1893 he signed a treaty with <a href="/wiki/Mwanga_II_of_Buganda" title="Mwanga II of Buganda">Kabaka Mwanga</a> of Buganda. On April 1, 1893, he hauled down the flag of the British East Africa Company and hoisted the <a href="/wiki/Union_Flag" title="Union Flag" class="mw-redirect">Union Jack</a>. He returned to Britain and recommended the declaration of Uganda as a protectorate.</p>
<p>Portal was made Companion of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Bath" title="Order of the Bath">Order of the Bath</a> in 1886, and Knights Commander of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_St_Michael_and_St_George" title="Order of St Michael and St George">Order of St Michael and St George</a> in 1893. He died from <a href="/wiki/Typhoid_fever" title="Typhoid fever">typhoid fever</a> in London on January 25, 1894, three months before his advice was acted upon. The town of <a href="/wiki/Fort_Portal" title="Fort Portal">Fort Portal</a> in western Uganda, where Portal had his base, is named after him.</p>

<div style="border: 1px solid #1043b8; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #f9f9ff;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:NDMap-doton-Portal.PNG" class="image" title="Location of Portal, Burke County, North Daokota"><img alt="Location of Portal, Burke County, North Daokota" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/NDMap-doton-Portal.PNG/200px-NDMap-doton-Portal.PNG" width="200" height="119" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f8/NDMap-doton-Portal.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f8/NDMap-doton-Portal.PNG 2x" data-file-width="300" data-file-height="179" /></a></div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Portal,_North_Dakota" title="Portal, North Dakota">Portal</a></b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Burke_County,_North_Dakota" title="Burke County, North Dakota">Burke County</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The population was 131 at the 2000 census. Portal was founded in <a href="/wiki/1893" title="1893">1893</a>.</p>
<p>Portal sits near the <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> border and is a major port of entry for road and rail traffic. It is one of three 24-hour ports in North Dakota (the others being <a href="/wiki/Dunseith,_North_Dakota" title="Dunseith, North Dakota">Dunseith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pembina,_North_Dakota" title="Pembina, North Dakota">Pembina</a>). The town is known for its "international" <a href="/wiki/Golf_course" title="Golf course">golf course</a>. The ninth hole is partially in the United States and partially in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.</p>
