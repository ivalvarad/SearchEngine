<p><b>Natwar Thakkar</b> (born 1932), popularly known as <b>Natwar bhai</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Gandhism" title="Gandhism">Gandhian</a> social worker from <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> who works in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_state" title="Indian state" class="mw-redirect">Indian state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nagaland" title="Nagaland">Nagaland</a>. He belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra" title="Maharashtra">Maharashtra</a> state of India but migrated to Nagaland for social work at the age of 23. He founded the Nagaland Gandhi Ashram at <a href="/wiki/Chuchuyimlang" title="Chuchuyimlang">Chuchuyimlang</a> village in the <a href="/wiki/Mokokchung_district" title="Mokokchung district">Mokokchung district</a> of Nagaland. Because of his efforts to spread Gandhian philosophy in Nagaland and his social work, he is known as "Nagaland's Gandhi".<sup id="cite_ref-telegraphindia_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraphindia-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-intoday_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intoday-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sarvodaya_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sarvodaya-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ANI_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ANI-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-jamnalalbajajfoundation1987_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jamnalalbajajfoundation1987-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natwar_Thakkar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thakkar was born in 1932 to a <a href="/wiki/Gujarati_language" title="Gujarati language">Gujarati</a>-speaking family in the coastal <a href="/wiki/Dahanu" title="Dahanu">Dahanu</a> town of the then <a href="/wiki/Bombay_Presidency" title="Bombay Presidency">Bombay Presidency</a> of <a href="/wiki/British_India" title="British India" class="mw-redirect">British India</a>. Today it is part of the <a href="/wiki/Palghar_district" title="Palghar district">Palghar district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra" title="Maharashtra">Maharashtra</a> state. Inspired by Gandhian social reformer <a href="/wiki/Kaka_Kalelkar" title="Kaka Kalelkar">Kaka Kalelkar</a> early in his life, Thakkar went to Nagaland, the North-Eastern state of India, at the age of 23 in 1955, desiring to foster "goodwill and emotional integration through voluntary social service" among the people of Nagaland, using Gandhian principles.<sup id="cite_ref-nagalandpost.com_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nagalandpost.com-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_India_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_India-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natwar_Thakkar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thakkar established the "Nagaland Gandhi Ashram" in the <a href="/wiki/Chuchuyimlang" title="Chuchuyimlang">Chuchuyimlang</a> village in Nagaland in 1955. At that time, the <a href="/wiki/Naga_nationalism" title="Naga nationalism">Naga rebels</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_army" title="Indian army" class="mw-redirect">Indian army</a> were constantly at "war" and hence the militants considered any "Indian" as a "spy" and they warned villagers not to shelter or aid Thakkar.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_India_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_India-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-telegraphindia_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraphindia-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Thakkar has assisted residents in "various development and income generating activities", including <a href="/wiki/Beekeeping" title="Beekeeping">beekeeping</a>, gur production, oil ghanis, a <a href="/wiki/Biogas" title="Biogas">biogas</a> plant, a mechanised carpentry workshop, and <a href="/wiki/Khadi" title="Khadi">Khadi</a> sales outlets.<sup id="cite_ref-nagalandpost.com_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nagalandpost.com-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Apart from popularising Khadi, he has also started a vocational training centre for school drop-outs and the physically-handicapped children.<sup id="cite_ref-BS_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BS-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>During his stay in Nagaland, he has been attacked and threatened by <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_conflict_in_Nagaland" title="Ethnic conflict in Nagaland">insurgents</a> several times to leave the state.<sup id="cite_ref-BS_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BS-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru" title="Jawaharlal Nehru">Jawaharlal Nehru</a>, the then <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_India" title="Prime Minister of India">Prime Minister of India</a>, had encouraged Thakkar to stay in the village and to continue his work; Nehru also allotted funds to promote his initiatives. Thakkar acted as an intermediary between the army and villagers and tried to strengthen bonds by personal talks and discussions. Villagers also appealed to militants not to harm Thakkar.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_India_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_India-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Due to Thakkar's efforts, an extension centre of <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Electronics_and_Information_Technology" title="National Institute of Electronics and Information Technology" class="mw-redirect">National Institute of Electronics and Information Technology</a> was started in Chuchuyimlang village in 2006. It is a government-funded institute equipped with the latest technology. Acknowledging the efforts of Thakkar, villagers donated 232 acres (94&#160;ha) of land to the "Nagaland Gandhi Ashram" for construction of the "Mahatma Gandhi centre of Social Work (MGCSW)".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ANI_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ANI-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tata_Institute_of_Social_Sciences" title="Tata Institute of Social Sciences">Tata Institute of Social Sciences</a> has shown interest in becoming associated with this institute.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_India_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_India-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natwar_Thakkar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thakkar married <a href="/wiki/Naga_people" title="Naga people">Naga Christian</a> Lentinaro Ao in 1956.<sup id="cite_ref-sarvodaya_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sarvodaya-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_India_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_India-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> They have two daughters and one son.<sup id="cite_ref-nagalandpost.com_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nagalandpost.com-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natwar_Thakkar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thakkar has been honoured with numerous awards, including:<sup id="cite_ref-nagalandpost.com_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nagalandpost.com-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Padma_Shri" title="Padma Shri">Padma Shri</a> Award (1999): India's 4th highest civilian award, awarded by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_India" title="President of India">President of India</a>.</li>
<li>Diwaliben Mehta Award (2001)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jamnalal_Bajaj_Award" title="Jamnalal Bajaj Award">Jamnalal Bajaj Award</a> (1987)<sup id="cite_ref-jamnalalbajajfoundation1987_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jamnalalbajajfoundation1987-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indira_Gandhi_Award_for_National_Integration" title="Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration">Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration</a> (1994): It is an award accorded by <a href="/wiki/Indian_National_Congress" title="Indian National Congress">Indian National Congress</a>.</li>
<li>Meghalaya State Mahatma Gandhi Award (1996)</li>
<li>Karmayogi Award (2015): It is awarded by NGO <i>My Home India</i>. It was awarded to Thakkar by <a href="/wiki/Bhartiya_Janata_Party" title="Bhartiya Janata Party" class="mw-redirect">Bhartiya Janata Party</a> President <a href="/wiki/Amit_Shah" title="Amit Shah">Amit Shah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BS_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BS-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>“Lifetime Service to Naga People” award (2009): A special award given by <a href="/wiki/Chuchuyimlang" title="Chuchuyimlang">Chuchuyimlang</a> village to Thakkar to honour his contribution for welfare of <a href="/wiki/Naga_people" title="Naga people">Naga people</a>. Thakkar donated 100,000 Indian rupees of Award money to set up Senior citizen's retreat in village.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraphindia_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraphindia-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
