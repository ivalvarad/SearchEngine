<p>The <b>Convention respecting fisheries, boundary and the restoration of slaves</b> between the United States of America and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland</a>, also known as the <b>London Convention</b>, <b>Anglo-American Convention of 1818</b>, <b>Convention of 1818</b>, or simply the <b>Treaty of 1818</b>, was a treaty signed in 1818 between the United States and the United Kingdom. Signed during the presidency of <a href="/wiki/James_Monroe" title="James Monroe">James Monroe</a>, it resolved standing boundary issues between the two nations. The treaty allowed for joint occupation and settlement of the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Country" title="Oregon Country">Oregon Country</a>, known to the British and in Canadian history as the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_District" title="Columbia District">Columbia District</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_Company" title="Hudson's Bay Company">Hudson's Bay Company</a>, and including the southern portion of its sister district <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia_(Canada)" title="New Caledonia (Canada)">New Caledonia</a>.</p>
<p>The two nations agreed to a boundary line involving the <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel north</a>, in part because a straight-line boundary would be easier to survey than the pre-existing boundaries based on watersheds. The treaty marked both the United Kingdom's last permanent major loss of territory in what is now the <a href="/wiki/Continental_United_States" title="Continental United States" class="mw-redirect">Continental United States</a> and the United States' only permanent significant cession of North American territory to a foreign power. Britain ceded all of <a href="/wiki/Rupert%27s_Land" title="Rupert's Land">Rupert's Land</a> south of the 49th parallel and west to the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>, including all of the <a href="/wiki/Red_River_Colony" title="Red River Colony">Red River Colony</a> south of that latitude, while the U.S. ceded the northernmost tip of the <a href="/wiki/Territory_of_Louisiana" title="Territory of Louisiana" class="mw-redirect">territory of Louisiana</a> above the 49th parallel.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treaty_provisions">Treaty provisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_1818&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Treaty provisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The treaty name is variously cited as <i>"Convention respecting fisheries, boundary, and the restoration of slaves"</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-US_Treaties_in_Force_2005_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-US_Treaties_in_Force_2005-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>"Convention of Commerce (Fisheries, Boundary and the Restoration of Slaves)"</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-International_Law_Reports_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-International_Law_Reports-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> and <i>"Convention of Commerce between His Majesty and the United States of America"</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Canado-American_Treaties_Text_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Canado-American_Treaties_Text-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Canado-American_Treaties_Summary_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Canado-American_Treaties_Summary-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Article I secured fishing rights along <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a> for the U.S.</li>
<li>Article II set the boundary between <a href="/wiki/British_North_America" title="British North America">British North America</a> and the United States along "a line drawn from the most <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_point_of_the_Lake_of_the_Woods" title="Northwestern point of the Lake of the Woods">northwestern point</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_the_Woods" title="Lake of the Woods">Lake of the Woods</a>, [due south, then] along the <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel</a> of north latitude..." to the "Stony Mountains"<sup id="cite_ref-Canado-American_Treaties_Text_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Canado-American_Treaties_Text-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> (now known as the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>). Britain ceded the part of <a href="/wiki/Rupert%27s_Land" title="Rupert's Land">Rupert's Land</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red_River_Colony" title="Red River Colony">Red River Colony</a> south of the 49th parallel (including the <a href="/wiki/Red_River_Basin" title="Red River Basin" class="mw-redirect">Red River Basin</a> — which now forms parts of <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Dakota" title="South Dakota">South Dakota</a> — as well as a small piece of modern-day <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a> near <a href="/wiki/Triple_Divide_Peak_(Montana)" title="Triple Divide Peak (Montana)">Triple Divide Peak</a>). The United States ceded the small portion of the <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Purchase" title="Louisiana Purchase">Louisiana Purchase</a> that lay north of the 49th parallel (namely, parts of the <a href="/wiki/Milk_River_(Alberta%E2%80%93Montana)" title="Milk River (Alberta–Montana)">Milk River</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poplar_River_(Montana%E2%80%93Saskatchewan)" title="Poplar River (Montana–Saskatchewan)">Poplar River</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Big_Muddy_Creek_(Missouri_River)" title="Big Muddy Creek (Missouri River)">Big Muddy Creek</a> watersheds in modern-day <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>).
<ul>
<li>This article settled a boundary dispute caused by ignorance of actual geography in the boundary agreed to in the 1783 <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1783)" title="Treaty of Paris (1783)">Treaty of Paris</a> that ended the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>. That earlier treaty had placed the boundary between the United States and British possessions to the north along a line going westward from the <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_the_Woods" title="Lake of the Woods">Lake of the Woods</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi River</a>. The parties had failed to realize that the river did not extend that far north, so such a line would never meet the river. In fixing this problem, the 1818 treaty inadvertently created an <a href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave" class="mw-redirect">exclave</a> of the United States, the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Angle" title="Northwest Angle">Northwest Angle</a>, which is the small section of the present state of <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> that is the only part of the United States outside Alaska north of the 49th parallel.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Article III provided for joint control of land in the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Country" title="Oregon Country">Oregon Country</a> for ten years. Both could claim land and both were guaranteed free navigation throughout.</li>
<li>Article IV confirmed the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anglo-American_Convention_of_1815&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Anglo-American Convention of 1815 (page does not exist)">Anglo-American Convention of 1815</a>, which regulated commerce between the two parties, for an additional ten years.</li>
<li>Article V agreed to refer differences over a U.S. claim arising from the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Ghent" title="Treaty of Ghent">Treaty of Ghent</a>, which ended the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>, to "some Friendly Sovereign or State to be named for that purpose." The U.S. claim was for return of, or compensation for, <a href="/wiki/Slavery_in_the_United_States" title="Slavery in the United States">slaves</a> that were in British territory or on British naval vessels when the treaty was signed. The Treaty of Ghent article in question was about handing over property, and the U.S. claimed that these slaves were the property of U.S. citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-Canado-American_Treaties_Text_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Canado-American_Treaties_Text-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Article VI established that ratification would occur within at most six months of signing the treaty.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_1818&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The treaty was negotiated for the U.S. by <a href="/wiki/Albert_Gallatin" title="Albert Gallatin">Albert Gallatin</a>, ambassador to France, and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Rush" title="Richard Rush">Richard Rush</a>, minister to the UK; and for the UK by <a href="/wiki/Frederick_John_Robinson,_1st_Viscount_Goderich" title="Frederick John Robinson, 1st Viscount Goderich" class="mw-redirect">Frederick John Robinson</a>, Treasurer of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> and member of the <a href="/wiki/Privy_council" title="Privy council">privy council</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Henry_Goulburn" title="Henry Goulburn">Henry Goulburn</a>, an undersecretary of state.<sup id="cite_ref-Canado-American_Treaties_Summary_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Canado-American_Treaties_Summary-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The treaty was signed on October 20, 1818. Ratifications were exchanged on January 30, 1819.<sup id="cite_ref-US_Treaties_in_Force_2005_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-US_Treaties_in_Force_2005-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The Convention of 1818, along with the <a href="/wiki/Rush%E2%80%93Bagot_Treaty" title="Rush–Bagot Treaty">Rush–Bagot Treaty</a> of 1817, marked the beginning of improved relations between the British Empire and its former colonies, and paved the way for more positive relations between the U.S. and Canada, notwithstanding that <a href="/wiki/Defence_Scheme_No._1" title="Defence Scheme No. 1">repelling U.S. invasion</a> was a defence priority in Canada until the Second World War.</p>
<p>After the treaty, the area east of the Oregon Territory, along the border was proposed to be organized as the Pembina Territory, but the U.S. Congress never acted on the proposal.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Instead the area became the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Territory" title="Minnesota Territory">Minnesota Territory</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dakota_Territory" title="Dakota Territory">Dakota Territory</a>.</p>
<p>Despite the relatively friendly nature of the agreement, it nevertheless resulted in a fierce struggle for control of the Oregon Country in the following two decades. The British-chartered Hudson's Bay Company, having previously established a trading network centered on <a href="/wiki/Fort_Vancouver" title="Fort Vancouver">Fort Vancouver</a> on the lower Columbia River, with other forts in what is now eastern Washington and Idaho as well as on the Oregon Coast and in Puget Sound, undertook a harsh campaign to restrict encroachment by U.S. fur traders to the area. By the 1830s, with pressure in the U.S. mounting to annex the region outright, the company undertook a deliberate policy to exterminate all fur-bearing animals from the Oregon Country, in order to both maximize its remaining profit and to delay the arrival of U.S. mountain men and settlers. The policy of discouraging settlement was undercut to some degree by the actions of <a href="/wiki/John_McLoughlin" title="John McLoughlin">John McLoughlin</a>, Chief Factor of the Hudson's Bay Company at Fort Vancouver, who regularly provided relief and welcome to U.S. immigrants who had arrived at the post over the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Trail" title="Oregon Trail">Oregon Trail</a>.</p>
<p>By the middle 1840s, the tide of U.S. immigration, as well as a U.S. political movement to claim the entire territory, led to a renegotiation of the agreement. The <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Treaty" title="Oregon Treaty">Oregon Treaty</a> in 1846 permanently established the <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel</a> as the boundary between the United States and British North America to the Pacific Ocean.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_1818&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adams%E2%80%93On%C3%ADs_Treaty" title="Adams–Onís Treaty">Adams–Onís Treaty</a> of 1819, between U.S. and Spain, resolved borders from Florida to the Pacific Ocean.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Webster%E2%80%93Ashburton_Treaty" title="Webster–Ashburton Treaty">Webster–Ashburton Treaty</a> of 1842 resolved uncertainties left by the 1818 treaty, including the Northwest Angle problem, which had been created by the use of a faulty map.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oregon_boundary_dispute" title="Oregon boundary dispute">Oregon boundary dispute</a>, concerning the joint occupation of the Oregon Country by U.S. and British settlers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Guadalupe_Hidalgo" title="Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo">Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo</a>, which established most of the southern border between the US and Mexico after the defeat and occupation of Mexico in 1848, ending the <a href="/wiki/Mexican%E2%80%93American_War" title="Mexican–American War">Mexican–American War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gadsden_Purchase" title="Gadsden Purchase">The Gadsden Purchase</a>, which completed the acquisition of the southwestern United States and completed the border between Mexico and the US in 1853 at the <a href="/wiki/32nd_parallel_north" title="32nd parallel north">32nd parallel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande" title="Rio Grande">Rio Grande</a>.</li>
</ul>
