<p><b>Forte Tenors</b> (styled as <b>FORTE</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(music)#Classical_crossover" title="Crossover (music)">classical crossover</a>–<a href="/wiki/Operatic_pop" title="Operatic pop">operatic pop</a> trio comprising <a href="/wiki/Tenor" title="Tenor">tenors</a> Josh Page, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Panikkar" title="Sean Panikkar">Sean Panikkar</a> and Hana Ryu. Forte was created specifically for <a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent_(season_8)" title="America's Got Talent (season 8)">season eight</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent" title="America's Got Talent">America's Got Talent</a></i>. Despite having met in person two days before their first performance together, and despite a personnel change ahead of their second, Forte advanced to the final round, finishing in fourth place. Following their elimination, the men signed a recording contract just offstage, where representatives of <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> were waiting for them.</p>
<p>Forte has since performed at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a> and the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a>, and has been featured at numerous charity <a href="/wiki/Festival" title="Festival">galas</a>. The group was the first finalist from season eight to headline a show in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>. Their <a href="/wiki/Eponym" title="Eponym">eponymous</a> debut <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a> peaked at <a href="/wiki/Numero_sign" title="Numero sign">No.</a> 3 on the <i>Billboard</i> Classical Albums chart. Their sophomore album, <i>The Future Classics</i>, includes their take on popular songs and music composed for <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forte_Tenors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forte_Tenors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Josh Page, a native of <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Page_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Page-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> won the "<a href="/wiki/David_Foster" title="David Foster">David Foster</a> and Friends" competition in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-Page2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Page2-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> He then gained fame when he was pulled from the audience at a <a href="/wiki/Josh_Groban" title="Josh Groban">Josh Groban</a> concert to sing a duet with Groban and the resulting video went <a href="/wiki/Viral_video" title="Viral video">viral</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-created_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-created-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sean_Panikkar" title="Sean Panikkar">Sean Panikkar</a> was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Bloomsburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Bloomsburg, Pennsylvania">Bloomsburg, Pennsylvania</a>, the second son of <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Sri_Lanka" title="Demographics of Sri Lanka">Sri Lankan</a> immigrants.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>a<span>]</span></a></sup> An established <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> performer,<sup id="cite_ref-Boca_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boca-6"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Panikkar has sung for numerous opera companies, including the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Opera" title="Pittsburgh Opera">Pittsburgh Opera</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Metropolitan Opera</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Panikkar_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Panikkar-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and at such venues as <a href="/wiki/La_Scala" title="La Scala">La Scala</a> in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, Italy.<sup id="cite_ref-CO2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CO2-7"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Hana Ryu studied at the <a href="/wiki/Korea_National_University_of_Arts" title="Korea National University of Arts">Korea National University of Arts</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, South Korea. He came to the United States on a <a href="/wiki/Visa_(document)" title="Visa (document)">student visa</a>, choosing in 2012 to pursue a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Music" title="Master of Music">Master of Music</a> degree at <a href="/wiki/Binghamton_University" title="Binghamton University">Binghamton University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryu_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ryu-8"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Former member <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Varela_(opera_singer)" title="Fernando Varela (opera singer)">Fernando Varela</a> was born in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> and raised in central <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Page_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Page-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Like Page, Varela won a David Foster competition, "Born to Sing", in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Varela1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Varela1-9"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation:_America.27s_Got_Talent">Formation: <i>America's Got Talent</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forte_Tenors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Formation: America's Got Talent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, founding members Josh Page and Fernando Varela met online, where Page suggested that they form "a new kind of tenor group" to compete on <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent_(season_8)" title="America's Got Talent (season 8)">America's Got Talent</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-created_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-created-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>b<span>]</span></a></sup> Once they found Hana Ryu, they were ready to make their debut during the audition stage in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LA_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA-12"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> only two days after the three had met in person for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-created_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-created-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Forte's performance of "<a href="/wiki/Pie_Jesu" title="Pie Jesu">Pie Jesu</a>" won praise from the judges;<sup id="cite_ref-Page_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Page-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Howard_Stern" title="Howard Stern">Howard Stern</a> said they had the element of surprise because they looked like "<a href="/wiki/List_of_English_words_of_Yiddish_origin#List_of_words" title="List of English words of Yiddish origin">schlubs</a>", but next time "we're going to know that you're not schlubs, but you're fantastic singers".<sup id="cite_ref-schlubs_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-schlubs-13"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Howie_Mandel" title="Howie Mandel">Howie Mandel</a> said the trio was like the premise to a joke: a <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans" title="Hispanic and Latino Americans">Latino</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_South_Korea" title="Demographics of South Korea">Korean</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_New_York" title="Demographics of New York">New Yorker</a> "walk into a bar, and record a giant hit record."<sup id="cite_ref-joke_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-joke-14"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Before the group could perform again, the show's producers learned about Ryu's visa status; he was told via <a href="/wiki/Email" title="Email">email</a> that he could no longer participate.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryu_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ryu-8"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Page and Varela found Sean Panikkar in time for their next performance in Las Vegas, where they were "whisked right away" to sing in front of the show's producers. Varela called it a stressful week, during which the new trio had to learn how to work together.<sup id="cite_ref-audition2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-audition2-15"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> When the men faced the judges, <a href="/wiki/Mel_B" title="Mel B">Mel B</a> prompted them to explain the change and added, "this is like a re-audition completely." After Forte sang "<a href="/wiki/The_Prayer_(Celine_Dion_and_Andrea_Bocelli_song)" title="The Prayer (Celine Dion and Andrea Bocelli song)">The Prayer</a>", Stern said to the other judges, "the new guy is the strongest singer."<sup id="cite_ref-Forte_v2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forte_v2-16"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The re-formed group continued to advance in the competition; in the rounds to follow, Forte performed such songs as "<a href="/wiki/Somewhere_(song)" title="Somewhere (song)">Somewhere</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Unchained_Melody" title="Unchained Melody">Unchained Melody</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Caruso_(song)" title="Caruso (song)">Caruso</a>" to audience ovations and judges' praise,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>c<span>]</span></a></sup> and an eventual fourth-place finish.<sup id="cite_ref-audition2_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-audition2-15"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Though they were disappointed with the outcome, Panikkar said "we were never expecting to win."<sup id="cite_ref-disappointed_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disappointed-20"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>d<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forte_Tenors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.932014:_FORTE_and_concert_appearances">2013–2014: <i>FORTE</i> and concert appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forte_Tenors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013–2014: FORTE and concert appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following Forte's elimination from <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent_(season_8)" title="America's Got Talent (season 8)">America's Got Talent</a></i>, Panikkar said, "<a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>, the largest division of <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony</a>, literally stopped us in the hallway as we were leaving the stage", and the men signed on.<sup id="cite_ref-Columbia_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Columbia-23"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Their self-titled CD was recorded within the next seven days and released in November 2013;<sup id="cite_ref-7days_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7days-24"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>FORTE</i> spent two weeks on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, peaking at No. 76.<sup id="cite_ref-BB200_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB200-25"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> The album reached No. 3 on the <i>Billboard</i> Classical Albums chart,<sup id="cite_ref-BBCA_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCA-26"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> and debuted at No. 2 on <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a>'s best sellers list and No. 1 at <a href="/wiki/Barnes_%26_Noble" title="Barnes &amp; Noble">Barnes &amp; Noble</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GoT_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoT-27"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Forte's live performances in 2013 included the Music of Hope concert at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a>, where their fans raised more than <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>3,000 for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Hat_Foundation" title="Golden Hat Foundation">Golden Hat Foundation</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GHF1_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GHF1-28"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GHF2_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GHF2-29"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Christmas_Tree_(United_States)" title="National Christmas Tree (United States)">National Christmas Tree</a> lighting ceremony at the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tree_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tree-30"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> Forte was the first act from season eight of <i>America's Got Talent</i> to appear in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, headlining a show at the <a href="/wiki/Tropicana_Las_Vegas" title="Tropicana Las Vegas">Tropicana</a> for three nights in December.<sup id="cite_ref-Tropicana_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tropicana-31"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>In March 2014, Forte was the closing act for the 8th annual Festival of the Arts Boca in <a href="/wiki/Boca_Raton,_Florida" title="Boca Raton, Florida">Boca Raton, Florida</a>, where they performed to multiple standing ovations. <i>Palm Beach ArtsPaper</i> said the men were coaxed back onto the stage for two encores, the second coming after "the thunderous applause refused to die down."<sup id="cite_ref-Boca_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boca-6"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> In April, the trio was the featured act at the <a href="/wiki/Central_City_Opera" title="Central City Opera">Central City Opera</a> Theater of Dreams Gala in <a href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" title="Denver, Colorado" class="mw-redirect">Denver, Colorado</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CCO_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCO-32"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.93present:_Ryu.27s_return_and_The_Future_Classics">2014–present: Ryu's return and <i>The Future Classics</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forte_Tenors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014–present: Ryu's return and The Future Classics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fernando Varela returned to Florida in mid-2014 to embark on a solo tour.<sup id="cite_ref-Varela2_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Varela2-33"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> Original member Hana Ryu rejoined Forte in 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-Ryu2_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ryu2-34"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> and the men began work on their second album, <i>The Future Classics</i>, which was funded by a <a href="/wiki/Crowdsourcing" title="Crowdsourcing">crowdsourcing</a> campaign though <a href="/wiki/PledgeMusic" title="PledgeMusic">PledgeMusic</a> and conceived as Forte's spin on "the popular music of today",<sup id="cite_ref-GoT_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoT-27"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> including such songs as "<a href="/wiki/Burn_(Ellie_Goulding_song)" title="Burn (Ellie Goulding song)">Burn</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Collide_(Howie_Day_song)" title="Collide (Howie Day song)">Collide</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Free_Fallin%27" title="Free Fallin'">Free Fallin'</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-updates_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-updates-35"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> The album is scheduled for release in November 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-pledge_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pledge-36"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The first single, "Game of Thrones", was inspired by the soundtrack for the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> <a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">series</a>, which Page called "the perfect subject matter to justify an epic opera performance."<sup id="cite_ref-re-tweet_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-re-tweet-37"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> Since its theme had no lyrics, Page, Panikkar and Ryu adapted <a href="/wiki/Valyrian_languages#High_Valyrian" title="Valyrian languages">High Valyrian</a> text from <i>Game of Thrones</i> and set it to the music composed by <a href="/wiki/Ramin_Djawadi" title="Ramin Djawadi">Ramin Djawadi</a>. The video for the single was <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking#Production" title="Filmmaking">shot</a> over two days' time at <a href="/wiki/Sands_Point,_New_York" title="Sands Point, New York">Sands Point</a> on <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GoT_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoT-27"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> and self-released by Forte in February 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-GoT_single_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoT_single-38"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> It was a top-five hit on YouTube's "Just-Released Music Videos" list in March.<sup id="cite_ref-GoT_YouTube_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoT_YouTube-39"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> When the video was shared by the official <i>Game of Thrones</i> <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> account, Page said "it knocked the air out of me."<sup id="cite_ref-re-tweet_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-re-tweet-37"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Forte continues to perform in concert. In August 2015, the men joined <a href="/wiki/David_Foster" title="David Foster">David Foster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Gunn" title="Nathan Gunn">Nathan Gunn</a> and <a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent_(season_9)" title="America's Got Talent (season 9)"><i>America's Got Talent</i> season 9</a> runner-up <a href="/wiki/Emily_West" title="Emily West">Emily West</a> in performance at the 11th Annual <a href="/wiki/Charles_Evans_(businessman)" title="Charles Evans (businessman)">Charles Evans</a> PCF Pro-Am Tennis Tournament Gala in <a href="/wiki/Southampton,_New_York" title="Southampton, New York">Southampton, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> Forte was the headline act on September 28 at the <a href="/wiki/Bloomsburg_Fair" title="Bloomsburg Fair">Bloomsburg Fair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bloomsburg_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomsburg-41"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>e<span>]</span></a></sup> The group joined Foster for three charity events in October:<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> the Carousel Ball in Denver, Colorado;<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> the Global Lyme Alliance Gala in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> and the Tradition of Hope Gala in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> Also in October, they are scheduled guests at the Hope On the Hill Gala at the <a href="/wiki/Van_Andel_Institute" title="Van Andel Institute">Van Andel Institute</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forte_Tenors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genre">Genre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forte_Tenors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Genre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>OperaPulse</i> listed its Top Ten Best Things to Happen to Opera In the 21st Century, and the collaboration between <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">pop culture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prime_time" title="Prime time">prime-time television</a> ranked #2. The <i><a href="/wiki/Got_Talent" title="Got Talent">Got Talent</a></i> franchise, and specifically contestants Forte and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Potts" title="Paul Potts">Paul Potts</a>, were singled out for helping the operatic genre redefine itself.<sup id="cite_ref-OperaPulse_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OperaPulse-49"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Releases">Releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forte_Tenors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

Still, ... with more time together, Forte could be the talent America’s looking for."<sup id="cite_ref-Jones_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jones-54"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
