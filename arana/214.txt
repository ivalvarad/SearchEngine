<p><a href="/wiki/Marshal_of_the_Royal_Air_Force" title="Marshal of the Royal Air Force">Marshal of the Royal Air Force</a> <b>Charles Frederick Algernon Portal, 1st Viscount Portal of Hungerford</b> <span style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Knight_of_the_Order_of_the_Garter" title="Knight of the Order of the Garter" class="mw-redirect">KG</a>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Knight_Grand_Cross_of_the_Order_of_the_Bath" title="Knight Grand Cross of the Order of the Bath" class="mw-redirect">GCB</a>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Order_of_Merit" title="Member of the Order of Merit" class="mw-redirect">OM</a>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order_and_Bar" title="Distinguished Service Order and Bar" class="mw-redirect">DSO*</a>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Military_Cross" title="Military Cross">MC</a></span> (21 May 1893 â 22 April 1971) was a senior <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> officer. In the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War" class="mw-redirect">First World War</a> he was a pilot, then a flight commander and then a squadron commander flying light bombers on the <a href="/wiki/Western_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Western Front (World War I)">Western Front</a>. In the early stages of the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War" class="mw-redirect">Second World War</a> he was commander-in-chief of <a href="/wiki/RAF_Bomber_Command" title="RAF Bomber Command">Bomber Command</a> in which role he advocated strategic <a href="/wiki/Area_bombardment" title="Area bombardment">area bombing</a> against German industrial areas, the same sort of targets that the <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a> was already targeting in the United Kingdom. He was then <a href="/wiki/Chief_of_the_Air_Staff_(United_Kingdom)" title="Chief of the Air Staff (United Kingdom)">Chief of the Air Staff</a> during the rest of the War and in that role he fended off an attempt by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> to take over <a href="/wiki/RAF_Coastal_Command" title="RAF Coastal Command">RAF Coastal Command</a> as well as an attempt by the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> to establish their own Army Air Arm. He was also an advocate of the need for a renewed <a href="/wiki/Strategic_bombing" title="Strategic bombing">strategic bombing</a> offensive. In retirement Portal was chairman of <a href="/wiki/British_Aluminium" title="British Aluminium">British Aluminium</a> and unsuccessfully fought in the "Aluminium War" against a hostile takeover bid by <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Stedeford" title="Ivan Stedeford">Sir Ivan Stedeford</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tube_Investments" title="Tube Investments" class="mw-redirect">Tube Investments</a>; he then became chairman of the <a href="/wiki/British_Aircraft_Corporation" title="British Aircraft Corporation">British Aircraft Corporation</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Portal,_1st_Viscount_Portal_of_Hungerford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Portal was born at Eddington House, <a href="/wiki/Hungerford" title="Hungerford">Hungerford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berkshire" title="Berkshire">Berkshire</a>, the son of Edward Robert Portal and his wife Ellinor Kate (nÃ©e Hill).<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> His younger brother <a href="/wiki/Admiral_(Royal_Navy)" title="Admiral (Royal Navy)">Admiral</a> Sir Reginald Portal (1894â1983) joined the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> and also had a distinguished career.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The Portals had <a href="/wiki/Huguenot" title="Huguenot">Huguenot</a> origins, having arrived in England in the 17th century.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Charles Portal, or "Peter" as he was nicknamed, was educated at <a href="/wiki/Winchester_College" title="Winchester College">Winchester College</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christ_Church,_Oxford" title="Christ Church, Oxford">Christ Church, Oxford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal had intended to become a <a href="/wiki/Barrister" title="Barrister">barrister</a> but he did not finish his degree and he left undergraduate life to enlist as a private soldier in 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-prob23_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prob23-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_World_War">First World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Portal,_1st_Viscount_Portal_of_Hungerford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">First World War</a>, Portal joined the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> and served as a <a href="/wiki/Dispatch_rider" title="Dispatch rider" class="mw-redirect">dispatch rider</a> in the motorcycle section of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Engineers" title="Royal Engineers">Royal Engineers</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Western_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Western Front (World War I)">Western Front</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal was made a <a href="/wiki/Corporal" title="Corporal">corporal</a> very soon after joining the Army and he was commissioned as a <a href="/wiki/Second_lieutenant" title="Second lieutenant">second lieutenant</a> only weeks later.<sup id="cite_ref-prob23_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prob23-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Around the same time, Portal was commended in <a href="/wiki/John_French,_1st_Earl_of_Ypres" title="John French, 1st Earl of Ypres">Sir John French</a>'s first despatch of September 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> In December 1914, Portal was given command of all riders in the <a href="/wiki/I_Corps_(United_Kingdom)" title="I Corps (United Kingdom)">1st Corps</a> Headquarters Signals Company.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In July 1915, with the need for dispatch riders reducing, Portal transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Flying_Corps" title="Royal Flying Corps">Royal Flying Corps</a> (RFC).<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> He served first as an observer and then, from November 1915, as a flying officer.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> He graduated as a pilot in April 1916, and joined <a href="/wiki/No._60_Squadron_RAF" title="No. 60 Squadron RAF">No. 60 Squadron</a> flying <a href="/wiki/Morane-Saulnier_N" title="Morane-Saulnier N">Morane biplanes</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Western_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Western Front (World War I)">Western Front</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> He became a flight commander with <a href="/wiki/No._3_Squadron_RAF" title="No. 3 Squadron RAF">No. 3 Squadron</a> flying <a href="/wiki/Royal_Aircraft_Factory_B.E.2" title="Royal Aircraft Factory B.E.2">BE2c aircraft</a> on the Western Front on 16 July 1916.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal was promoted to temporary <a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">major</a> in June 1917<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and given command of <a href="/wiki/No._16_Squadron_RAF" title="No. 16 Squadron RAF">No. 16 Squadron</a> flying <a href="/wiki/Royal_Aircraft_Factory_R.E.8" title="Royal Aircraft Factory R.E.8">RE8 aircraft</a> on the Western Front at the same time.<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> He was promoted to temporary <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant-colonel" title="Lieutenant-colonel" class="mw-redirect">lieutenant-colonel</a> on 17 June 1918<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> and given command of No. 24 (Training) Wing at <a href="/wiki/RAF_Spitalgate" title="RAF Spitalgate">RAF Grantham</a> in August 1918.<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Military_Cross" title="Military Cross">Military Cross</a> in January 1917,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">Distinguished Service Order</a> on 18 July 1917<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/Medal_bar" title="Medal bar">Bar</a> to his DSO on 18 July 1918.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inter-war_years">Inter-war years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Portal,_1st_Viscount_Portal_of_Hungerford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Inter-war years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In August 1919 Portal was appointed to a permanent commission in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> in the rank of <a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">major</a> (shortly afterwards redesignated as a <a href="/wiki/Squadron_leader" title="Squadron leader">squadron leader</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> He became a chief flying instructor at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force_College_Cranwell" title="Royal Air Force College Cranwell">Royal Air Force College Cranwell</a> in November 1919 and then attended <a href="/wiki/RAF_Staff_College,_Andover" title="RAF Staff College, Andover">RAF Staff College</a> in 1922, before joining the air staff conducting flying operations in the home sector in April 1923.<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Wing_commander_(rank)" title="Wing commander (rank)">wing commander</a> on 1 July 1925,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> he attended the senior officers' war course at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Naval_College,_Greenwich" title="Royal Naval College, Greenwich">Royal Naval College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greenwich" title="Greenwich">Greenwich</a>, in 1926 before taking over <a href="/wiki/No._7_Squadron_RAF" title="No. 7 Squadron RAF">No. 7 Squadron</a> flying <a href="/wiki/Vickers_Virginia" title="Vickers Virginia">Vickers Virginia bombers</a> from <a href="/wiki/RAF_Worthy_Down" title="RAF Worthy Down">RAF Worthy Down</a> in March 1927<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and concentrated on improving bombing accuracy.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> He attended the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Defence_Studies" title="Royal College of Defence Studies">Imperial Defence College</a> in 1929 and then became Deputy Director of Plans in the Directorate of Operations &amp; Intelligence at the <a href="/wiki/Air_Ministry" title="Air Ministry">Air Ministry</a> in December 1930.<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Group_captain" title="Group captain">group captain</a> on 1 July 1931,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> he was appointed commander of <a href="/wiki/British_Forces_Aden" title="British Forces Aden">British forces in Aden</a> in February 1934,<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> in which role he tried to control the local tribesmen by use of an air blockade.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Air_commodore" title="Air commodore">air commodore</a> on 1 January 1935,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> he joined the Directing Staff at the Imperial Defence College in January 1936.<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Air_vice_marshal" title="Air vice marshal" class="mw-redirect">air vice marshal</a> on 1 July 1937<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> before being appointed Director of Organization at the Air Ministry on 1 September 1937.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_World_War">Second World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Portal,_1st_Viscount_Portal_of_Hungerford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Second World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Air_Chief_Marshal_Sir_Charles_Portal,_Kcb,_Dso,_Mc,_Chief_of_Air_Staff_1940-1945_TR3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Air_Chief_Marshal_Sir_Charles_Portal%2C_Kcb%2C_Dso%2C_Mc%2C_Chief_of_Air_Staff_1940-1945_TR3.jpg/220px-Air_Chief_Marshal_Sir_Charles_Portal%2C_Kcb%2C_Dso%2C_Mc%2C_Chief_of_Air_Staff_1940-1945_TR3.jpg" width="220" height="282" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Air_Chief_Marshal_Sir_Charles_Portal%2C_Kcb%2C_Dso%2C_Mc%2C_Chief_of_Air_Staff_1940-1945_TR3.jpg/330px-Air_Chief_Marshal_Sir_Charles_Portal%2C_Kcb%2C_Dso%2C_Mc%2C_Chief_of_Air_Staff_1940-1945_TR3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Air_Chief_Marshal_Sir_Charles_Portal%2C_Kcb%2C_Dso%2C_Mc%2C_Chief_of_Air_Staff_1940-1945_TR3.jpg/440px-Air_Chief_Marshal_Sir_Charles_Portal%2C_Kcb%2C_Dso%2C_Mc%2C_Chief_of_Air_Staff_1940-1945_TR3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="625" data-file-height="800" /></a>

<p>Appointed a <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Bath" title="Order of the Bath">Companion of the Order of the Bath</a> in the 1939 <a href="/wiki/New_Year_Honours" title="New Year Honours">New Year Honours</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal became <a href="/wiki/Air_Member_for_Personnel" title="Air Member for Personnel">Air Member for Personnel</a> on the Air Council on 1 February 1939.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> He was promoted to the acting rank of <a href="/wiki/Air_marshal" title="Air marshal">air marshal</a> on 3 September 1939,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> appointed commander-in-chief of <a href="/wiki/RAF_Bomber_Command" title="RAF Bomber Command">Bomber Command</a> in April 1940<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and promoted to the substantive rank of air marshal on 1 July 1940.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal advocated strategic <a href="/wiki/Area_bombardment" title="Area bombardment">area bombing</a> against German industrial areas, the same sort of targets that the Luftwaffe was already targeting in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-prob24_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prob24-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> He was advanced to <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Bath" title="Order of the Bath">Knight Commander of the Order of the Bath</a> in the 1940 Birthday Honours.<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 October 1940, Portal was appointed as <a href="/wiki/Chief_of_the_Air_Staff_(United_Kingdom)" title="Chief of the Air Staff (United Kingdom)">Chief of the Air Staff</a> with the temporary rank of <a href="/wiki/Air_chief_marshal" title="Air chief marshal">air chief marshal</a><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> (made permanent in April 1942).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> He continued in this capacity for the remainder of the war.<sup id="cite_ref-air_4-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The first issue he had to resolve was an attempt by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> to take over <a href="/wiki/RAF_Coastal_Command" title="RAF Coastal Command">RAF Coastal Command</a> as well as an attempt by the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> to establish their own Army Air Arm.<sup id="cite_ref-prob25_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prob25-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal successfully persuaded both the Army and the Navy that the RAF could adequately look after their needs.<sup id="cite_ref-prob24_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prob24-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> The second issue Portal had to resolve was the need for a renewed strategic bombing offensive.<sup id="cite_ref-prob24_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prob24-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> In August 1941 he received a report on the relative inefficiency of RAF daytime raids and proposals for area bombing by night: to implement the proposals he determined that a new leader was required and replaced the chief of bomber command, Air Chief Marshal <a href="/wiki/Richard_Peirse" title="Richard Peirse">Richard Peirse</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Sir_Arthur_Harris,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Arthur Harris, 1st Baronet">Arthur Harris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prob25_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prob25-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> He was advanced to <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Bath" title="Order of the Bath">Knight Grand Cross of the Order of the Bath</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1942_Birthday_Honours" title="1942 Birthday Honours">1942 Birthday Honours</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jalta_1945.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Jalta_1945.jpg/250px-Jalta_1945.jpg" width="250" height="199" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Jalta_1945.jpg/375px-Jalta_1945.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Jalta_1945.jpg/500px-Jalta_1945.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1138" data-file-height="905" /></a>

The Yalta Conference. Portal is shown standing behind Churchill.</div>

<p>Portal accompanied Churchill to all the great conferences and made a good impression on Americans.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> In January 1943, at the <a href="/wiki/Casablanca_Conference" title="Casablanca Conference">Casablanca Conference</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Combined_Chiefs_of_Staff" title="Combined Chiefs of Staff">Combined Chiefs of Staff</a> selected him to coordinate the bomber forces of both the United States and Britain in a combined bomber offensive over Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The forces were transferred to U.S. General <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Dwight D. Eisenhower</a> for the duration of <a href="/wiki/Operation_Overlord" title="Operation Overlord">Operation <i>Overlord</i></a>;<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> but when their control reverted to the Combined Chiefs, Portal still advocated area bombing of German cities instead of specific targets, such as Axis oil production facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-spiegel_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spiegel-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> He was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Marshal_of_the_Royal_Air_Force" title="Marshal of the Royal Air Force">Marshal of the Royal Air Force</a> on 1 January 1944.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In early 1944, Portal's view of strategic bombing changed; he felt that bombers could also play a more auxiliary role in the allied offensive. (Much of what is known about Portal's thinking is based on memoranda he wrote.) He argued for the new approach on the basis of the huge increase in the size of the bomber force, which would carry out not just precision bombing but also indiscriminate <a href="/wiki/Area_bombing" title="Area bombing" class="mw-redirect">area bombing</a> by night of all German cities with populations exceeding 100,000. Portal thought that the resulting damage to the German war effort and civilian morale would lead to victory within six months. A second memorandum in 1945 made a similar argument.<sup id="cite_ref-spiegel_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spiegel-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In March 1945, <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Churchill</a> gave the final order to stop Portal's strategy of area bombing, after the firestorm of <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a> a few weeks earlier. Churchill subsequently distanced himself from the bombing writing that "the destruction of Dresden remains a serious query against the conduct of Allied Bombing".<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-war">Post-war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Portal,_1st_Viscount_Portal_of_Hungerford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1945, after the war's end, Portal retired from the RAF and on 12 October 1945 he was raised to the peerage as <a href="/wiki/Baron_Portal_of_Hungerford" title="Baron Portal of Hungerford">Baron Portal of Hungerford</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Berkshire" title="Berkshire">County of Berkshire</a>, with remainder, failing male issue of his own, to his daughters and their male heirs.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> On 8 February 1946 he was further honoured when he was made Viscount Portal of Hungerford, in the County of Berkshire, with normal remainder to his heirs male.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> He was made a member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Merit" title="Order of Merit">Order of Merit</a> on <a href="/wiki/1946_New_Year_Honours" title="1946 New Year Honours">1 January 1946</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> He was also awarded the American <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Medal_(U.S._Army)" title="Distinguished Service Medal (U.S. Army)">Distinguished Service Medal</a> on 15 March 1946<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> and appointed a Knight Grand Cross of the Dutch <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Orange-Nassau" title="Order of Orange-Nassau">Order of Orange-Nassau</a> on 18 November 1947.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> He was also appointed a Knight Grand Cross of the Belgium <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Crown_(Belgium)" title="Order of the Crown (Belgium)">Order of the Crown with Palm</a> and awarded the Belgian <a href="/wiki/Croix_de_guerre_(Belgium)" title="Croix de guerre (Belgium)">Croix de Guerre, 1940, with Palm</a> on 27 August 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>From 1946 to 1951, Portal was Controller of Production (Atomic Energy) at the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Supply" title="Ministry of Supply">Ministry of Supply</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hinton,_Baron_Hinton_of_Bankside" title="Christopher Hinton, Baron Hinton of Bankside">Christopher Hinton</a>, responsible for the production of <a href="/wiki/Fissile" title="Fissile" class="mw-redirect">fissile</a> material, said later, "I cannot remember that he ever did anything that helped us."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> He attended the funeral of <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">King George VI</a> in February 1952<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Coronation_of_Queen_Elizabeth_II" title="Coronation of Queen Elizabeth II">coronation of Queen Elizabeth II</a> in June 1953.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Portal was elected Chairman of <a href="/wiki/British_Aluminium" title="British Aluminium">British Aluminium</a> and in 1958/1959 he fought in the <a href="/wiki/City_of_London" title="City of London">City of London</a>'s "Aluminium War" against a hostile takeover bid by <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Stedeford" title="Ivan Stedeford">Sir Ivan Stedeford</a>, Chairman and Chief Executive of <a href="/wiki/TI_Group" title="TI Group">Tube Investments</a>. T.I. along with its ally <a href="/wiki/Reynolds_Group_Holdings" title="Reynolds Group Holdings">Reynolds Metals</a> of the US, won the takeover battle, and in the process, rewrote the way the City of London conducted its business in relation to shareholders and investors. Stedeford replaced Portal as Chairman of British Aluminium. In 1960 Portal was elected chairman of the <a href="/wiki/British_Aircraft_Corporation" title="British Aircraft Corporation">British Aircraft Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prob26_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prob26-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal died from <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> at his home at <a href="/wiki/Funtington" title="Funtington">West Ashling</a> near <a href="/wiki/Chichester" title="Chichester">Chichester</a> on 22 April 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Portal,_1st_Viscount_Portal_of_Hungerford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 1919, Portal married Joan Margaret Welby (1898-1996); they had a son (who died at birth) and two daughters.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The viscountcy died with him but he was succeeded in the barony according to the special remainder by his elder daughter, Rosemary Ann, who died in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
