<p><i><b>Portal</b></i> is a mix between a computerized <a href="/wiki/Novel" title="Novel">novel</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Interactive_game" title="Interactive game" class="mw-redirect">interactive game</a>. It was published for the <a href="/wiki/Amiga" title="Amiga">Amiga</a> in <a href="/wiki/1986_in_video_gaming" title="1986 in video gaming">1986</a> by <a href="/wiki/Activision" title="Activision">Activision</a>, written by Rob Swigart, produced by <a href="/wiki/Brad_Fregger" title="Brad Fregger">Brad Fregger</a>, and programmed by Nexa Corporation. Versions for the <a href="/wiki/Commodore_64" title="Commodore 64">Commodore 64</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apple_II" title="Apple II">Apple II</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DOS" title="DOS">DOS</a> were later released. Versions for the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Macintosh" title="Apple Macintosh" class="mw-redirect">Macintosh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atari_ST" title="Atari ST">Atari ST</a> were announced and developed, but never formally released. A unique game for its time, <i>Portal</i> was one part text-driven adventure (Ã  la <i><a href="/wiki/Zork" title="Zork">Zork</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Planetfall" title="Planetfall">Planetfall</a></i>) but with a graphical interface. It is unrelated to the game <i><a href="/wiki/Portal_(video_game)" title="Portal (video game)">Portal</a></i> (2007) by Valve Corporation.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Setting">Setting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(interactive_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Setting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The player, taking on the role of the unnamed <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">astronaut</a> <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">protagonist</a>, returns from a failed 100-year voyage to <a href="/wiki/61_Cygni" title="61 Cygni">61 Cygni</a> to find the Earth devoid of humans. Cars are rusted and covered with moss, the streets are completely barren and everything appears as though the entire human race had just vanished suddenly. The player happens upon a barely functioning computer terminal that is tied into a storytelling <a href="/wiki/Mainframe_computer" title="Mainframe computer">mainframe</a>, Homer. Through this interface, the player, assisted by Homer who attempts to weave the information into a coherent narrative, discovers information in order to piece together the occurrences leading to the disappearance of the human race. For instance, spending some time in the Medical Records section may unlock a piece of data in the Science section, and through these links the player can finish the game.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(interactive_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A review in <i><a href="/wiki/Computer_Gaming_World" title="Computer Gaming World">Computer Gaming World</a></i> described the story as "interesting and well-written", but felt the interface was tedious. The Amiga version, using a mouse, was considered superior to that of the C64, and only bothered the reviewer by way of slowing down the reading of the story.<sup id="cite_ref-CGW_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CGW-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Another writer in the magazine agreed that the interface was tedious and hesitated to recommend it because it was not a game, but said that the quality of the writing was very high.<sup id="cite_ref-ardai198707_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ardai198707-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Novel">Novel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(interactive_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Novel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A hardcover novel, titled <i>Portal: A Dataspace Retrieval</i> (1988) and composed mostly of the text from the interactive novel with some new additions, was written by the same author, Rob Swigart, and first published by <a href="/wiki/St._Martin%27s_Press" title="St. Martin's Press">St. Martin's Press</a>. It takes the form of a series of notes on different subjects, in an order the player would encounter them through <a href="/wiki/Homer" title="Homer">Homer</a>. A softcover edition was released by Backinprint.com in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>An eBook was released "under the <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons" title="Creative Commons">Creative Commons</a> BY-NC-ND Unported license 3.0". The author has allowed it to be uploaded to the MobileRead forum.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Crowdfunded_Remake">Crowdfunded Remake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(interactive_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Crowdfunded Remake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In April 2012, author Rob Swigart and Subliminal Games launched a <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter</a> fundraising campaign called "Rob Swigart's Portal (1986) Reborn" to recreate the world of <i>Portal</i> as a modern third-person adventure game.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Some of the features set to be included were moving backward or forward in time through a simulation of the past and changing the character's appearance for different responses and reactions by virtual non-player characters.</p>
<p>On June 5, 2012, the project creator cancelled the Kickstarter project having raised only $22,796 of the $530,000 target.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
