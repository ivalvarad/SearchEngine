<p><b>HFC Haarlem</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club from the city of <a href="/wiki/Haarlem" title="Haarlem">Haarlem</a>, established in 1889 and dissolved in 2010. The club won the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Dutch national title</a> in 1946 and reached five <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">Cup finals</a>, winning in 1902 and 1912. Haarlem reached the second round of the <a href="/wiki/1982%E2%80%9383_UEFA_Cup" title="1982–83 UEFA Cup">1982–83 UEFA Cup</a>, losing to <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">Spartak Moscow</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>.</p>
<p>Haarlem was declared bankrupt on January 25, 2010, and excluded from professional football with immediate effect. Haarlem played its last professional match on January 22, 2010, a 3-0 away loss to <a href="/wiki/SBV_Excelsior" title="SBV Excelsior" class="mw-redirect">Excelsior</a>.</p>
<p>In April 2010, three months after its exclusion from professional football, HFC Haarlem completed a fusion with amateur <a href="/wiki/Tweede_Klasse" title="Tweede Klasse">Tweede Klasse</a> club HFC Kennemerland, the new club being named <b><a href="/wiki/Haarlem_Kennemerland" title="Haarlem Kennemerland">Haarlem Kennemerland</a></b>. The team played in <a href="/wiki/Tweede_Klasse" title="Tweede Klasse">Tweede Klasse</a> A Saturday Division, West District I in its debut season.<sup id="cite_ref-fusion_1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fusion_1-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fusion_2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fusion_2-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HFC_Haarlem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded on October 1, 1889. Haarlem won the Dutch national title in 1946 and reached five <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">Dutch cup</a> finals, winning in 1902 and 1912 and losing in 1911, 1914 and 1950. Haarlem won the title in the <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Eerste Divisie</a> in 1972, 1976 and 1981. In 1982, HFC Haarlem, featuring a young <a href="/wiki/Ruud_Gullit" title="Ruud Gullit">Ruud Gullit</a>, qualified for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup" class="mw-redirect">UEFA Cup</a> football, in which they were eliminated by <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">Spartak Moscow</a> in the second round (the match hosted by Spartak is known in Russia because of the <a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_disaster" title="Luzhniki disaster">Luzhniki disaster</a> that occurred in the stadium after the game). In 1990, Haarlem was relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Eerste Divisie</a> again, in which they played until January 25, 2010.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajax-partnership">Ajax-partnership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HFC_Haarlem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ajax-partnership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 10, 2009, Haarlem and <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">AFC Ajax</a> announced a partnership.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> They decided Ajax would loan 1 to 4 players to Haarlem every season, it also meant Ajax would get a say in Haarlem-transfers, and would deploy employees to Haarlem, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cock_Jol&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cock Jol (page does not exist)">Cock Jol</a>, brother of <a href="/wiki/Martin_Jol" title="Martin Jol">Martin Jol</a> supervised the Ajax-Haarlem project.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bankruptcy">Bankruptcy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HFC_Haarlem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bankruptcy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 25, 2010, however, Haarlem was declared bankrupt and was thus according to Dutch league rules excluded from competition, with all its previous results in the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Eerste_Divisie" title="2009–10 Eerste Divisie">ongoing competition</a> expunged.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The club in its current form ceased to exist, with all its players (and staff) becoming <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agents</a>.</p>
<p>In February 2010, HFC Haarlem was reinstated as a new amateur club, who also took the naming and logo rights from the old version.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> This club then started talks for a potential merger with amateur <a href="/wiki/Tweede_Klasse" title="Tweede Klasse">Tweede Klasse</a> Haarlem-based side HFC Kennemerland,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> which was announced to have been completed on April 27; the new club will be called <b><a href="/wiki/Haarlem_Kennemerland" title="Haarlem Kennemerland">Haarlem Kennemerland</a></b>, and will play home games at <a href="/wiki/Haarlem_Stadion" title="Haarlem Stadion">Haarlem Stadion</a>, thus continuing the legacy of the old HFC Haarlem.<sup id="cite_ref-fusion_1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fusion_1-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fusion_2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fusion_2-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
