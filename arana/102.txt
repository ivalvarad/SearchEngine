<p><b>EMC Corporation</b> (stylized as <a href="/wiki/E%3Dmc%C2%B2" title="E=mc²" class="mw-redirect"><b>EMC²</b></a>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational corporation</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Hopkinton,_Massachusetts" title="Hopkinton, Massachusetts">Hopkinton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, United States.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> EMC sells <a href="/wiki/Data_storage_device" title="Data storage device">data storage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Information_security" title="Information security">information security</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virtualization" title="Virtualization">virtualization</a>, analytics, <a href="/wiki/Cloud_computing" title="Cloud computing">cloud computing</a> and other products and services that enable businesses to store, manage, protect, and analyze data. EMC's target markets include large companies and small- and medium-sized businesses across various vertical markets.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ZDNet_Jan_2011_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ZDNet_Jan_2011-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The stock was added to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> on April 6, 1986,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> and is also listed on the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" title="S&amp;P 500" class="mw-redirect">S&amp;P 500</a> index.</p>
<p>EMC has over 70,000 employees and is the world’s largest provider of data storage systems by marketshare, competing against <a href="/wiki/NetApp" title="NetApp">NetApp</a>, <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hitachi_Data_Systems" title="Hitachi Data Systems">Hitachi Data Systems</a> (arranged in descending order of external data storage marketshare).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Joseph_M._Tucci" title="Joseph M. Tucci">Joseph M. Tucci</a> has been EMC's chief executive since January 2001 and chairman of the board of directors since January 2006; he joined the company in 2000 as president and chief operating officer.<sup id="cite_ref-EMC_Tucci_bio_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EMC_Tucci_bio-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Tucci is also chairman of the board of <a href="/wiki/VMware" title="VMware">VMware</a> Corporation<sup id="cite_ref-EMC_Tucci_bio_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EMC_Tucci_bio-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> and chairman of the board of <a href="/wiki/Pivotal_Software" title="Pivotal Software">Pivotal Software</a>, which are partially owned by EMC.<sup id="cite_ref-GigaOM_May_2013_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GigaOM_May_2013-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> On October 12, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Dell_Inc." title="Dell Inc." class="mw-redirect">Dell Inc.</a> announced that it would acquire EMC in a cash-and-stock deal valued at $67 billion—the largest-ever acquisition in the technology industry.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cnn_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nyt_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EMC_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:198px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Amb._Richard_Egan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/33/Amb._Richard_Egan.jpg" width="196" height="296" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="196" data-file-height="296" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Amb._Richard_Egan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Richard Egan, co-founder of EMC Corporation</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>EMC, founded in 1979 by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Egan_(businessman)" title="Richard Egan (businessman)">Richard Egan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roger_Marino" title="Roger Marino">Roger Marino</a> (the E and M in EMC),<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> introduced its first 64-kilobyte memory boards for the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Computer" title="Prime Computer">Prime Computer</a> in 1981<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> and continued with the development of memory boards for other computer types. In the mid-1980s the company expanded beyond memory to other <a href="/wiki/Computer_data_storage" title="Computer data storage">computer data storage</a> types and networked storage platforms. EMC began shipping its flagship product, the <a href="/wiki/EMC_Symmetrix" title="EMC Symmetrix">Symmetrix</a>, in 1990. Symmetrix was the main reason for EMC's rapid growth in the 1990s, both in size and value, from a company valued in the hundreds of millions of dollars to a multi-billion company.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Ruettgers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Michael Ruettgers (page does not exist)">Michael Ruettgers</a> joined EMC in 1988 and served as CEO from 1992 until January 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_Bio_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes_Bio-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> Under Ruettgers’ leadership, EMC revenues grew from $120 million to nearly $9 billion 10 years later, and the company shifted its focus from memory boards to storage systems.<sup id="cite_ref-Gigamon_Dec_2010_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gigamon_Dec_2010-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Ruettgers was named one of <i><a href="/wiki/BusinessWeek" title="BusinessWeek" class="mw-redirect">BusinessWeek</a></i>‍ '​s "World's Top 25 Executives"; one of the "Best Chief Executive Officers in America" by <i><a href="/wiki/Worth_(magazine)" title="Worth (magazine)">Worth</a></i> magazine; and one of <i><a href="/wiki/Network_World" title="Network World">Network World</a></i>‍ '​s "25 Most Powerful People in Networking".<sup id="cite_ref-Gigamon_Dec_2010_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gigamon_Dec_2010-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, Joe Tucci was named chief executive officer of EMC.<sup id="cite_ref-Businessweek_Tucci_bio_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Businessweek_Tucci_bio-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> He has been credited with transforming EMC's business model from a focus on high-end storage platforms to a portfolio of platforms, software, and services that enable organizations to deliver IT as a service through cloud computing.<sup id="cite_ref-Businessweek_Tucci_bio_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Businessweek_Tucci_bio-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Under Tucci's leadership, EMC grew to $21.7 billion in revenues in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-MarketWatch_annual_financials_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MarketWatch_annual_financials-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, EMC and <a href="/wiki/Cisco_Systems" title="Cisco Systems">Cisco</a>, with investments from VMware and <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a>, formed a joint venture called <a href="/wiki/VCE_(company)" title="VCE (company)">VCE</a> to develop products and services for the converged infrastructure and cloud computing markets.<sup id="cite_ref-CRN_June_2011_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRN_June_2011-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>EMC announced its 450,000 square foot Center of Excellence (COE) in <a href="/wiki/Durham,_North_Carolina" title="Durham, North Carolina">Durham, North Carolina</a> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Data_Center_Knowledge_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Data_Center_Knowledge-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> The COE houses a virtualized data center and includes 130,000 square feet of global research and development labs. The company also has COEs in India, China, Egypt, Israel, Ireland, and Russia, which perform engineering, research and development, customer, and translation services for EMC business units.<sup id="cite_ref-Data_Center_Knowledge_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Data_Center_Knowledge-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, EMC launched a new company named Pivotal.<sup id="cite_ref-CRN_Pivotal_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRN_Pivotal-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> Led by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Maritz" title="Paul Maritz">Paul Maritz</a>, the organization combines technologies from VMware and EMC and is focused on delivering Platform-as-a-Service products.<sup id="cite_ref-CRN_Pivotal_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRN_Pivotal-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>EMC has been growing in part through aggressively making acquisitions of small companies, and starting to bring those products to market.<sup id="cite_ref-xconomy_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xconomy-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_by_Dell">Acquisition by Dell</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EMC_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisition by Dell">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 12, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Dell_Inc." title="Dell Inc." class="mw-redirect">Dell Inc.</a> announced its intent to acquire EMC in a cash-and-stock deal valued at $67 billion, which has been considered the largest-ever acquisition in the technology sector. Dell CEO <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dell" title="Michael Dell">Michael Dell</a> explained that the purchase was meant to "evolve the company into the most relevant areas where IT is moving", combining Dell's enterprise server, personal computer, and mobile businesses with EMC's enterprise storage business. The deal is structured so that Dell will pay $24.05 per share of EMC, and $9.05 per share of <a href="/wiki/Tracking_stock" title="Tracking stock">tracking stock</a> in VMware.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-emcdell_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-emcdell-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nyt-emcdell_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-emcdell-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc-dellemc_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-dellemc-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Big_data_projects">Big data projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EMC_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Big data projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, EMC sponsored The Human Face of Big Data,<sup id="cite_ref-Face_of_Big_Data_98-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Face_of_Big_Data-98"><span>[</span>98<span>]</span></a></sup> a globally crowd-sourced media project focusing on the ability to collect, analyze, triangulate and visualize vast amounts of data in real time. The Human Face of Big Data, produced by <a href="/wiki/Rick_Smolan" title="Rick Smolan">Rick Smolan</a> and Jennifer Erwitt, includes “a number of fascinating stories…(that) represent some of the most innovative applications of data that are shaping our future.”<sup id="cite_ref-HuffPo_Big_Data_99-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPo_Big_Data-99"><span>[</span>99<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>EMC sponsors The Digital Universe, a study by <a href="/wiki/IDC_(consulting_group)" title="IDC (consulting group)" class="mw-redirect">IDC</a> “measuring and characterizing all the digital data created, replicated, and consumed worldwide in a single year.”<sup id="cite_ref-Digital_Universe_100-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Digital_Universe-100"><span>[</span>100<span>]</span></a></sup> The Digital Universe 2012 report anticipates that "during the next eight years, the amount of digital data produced will exceed 40 zettabytes, which is the equivalent of 5,200&#160;GB of data for every man, woman and child on Earth. To hit that figure, all data is expected to double every two years through 2020."<sup id="cite_ref-Computerworld_Big_Data_101-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Computerworld_Big_Data-101"><span>[</span>101<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition_and_awards">Recognition and awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EMC_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Recognition and awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2015, EMC was ranked no. 2 on Fortune’s list of the World’s Most Admired Computer Companies for the fifth consecutive year.<sup id="cite_ref-Most_Admired_2015_102-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Most_Admired_2015-102"><span>[</span>102<span>]</span></a></sup> EMC was included on the 2012 Thompson Reuters Top 100 Global Innovators list.<sup id="cite_ref-Top_100_Global_Innovators_103-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Top_100_Global_Innovators-103"><span>[</span>103<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2013, four EMC and RSA channel executives were named CRN’s 2013 Channel Chiefs for “trying to make sure their company’s partners succeed."<sup id="cite_ref-CRN_Channel_Chiefs_104-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRN_Channel_Chiefs-104"><span>[</span>104<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>EMC has ranked on several global best places to work lists. EMC was ranked the 19th Best Multinational Workplace in Europe by Great Places to Work in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Great_Place_to_Work_2012_105-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Great_Place_to_Work_2012-105"><span>[</span>105<span>]</span></a></sup> EMC was included on Crain’s 2012 Best Places to Work in New York City,<sup id="cite_ref-Crain.27s_2012_106-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Crain.27s_2012-106"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Globe" title="The Boston Globe">The Boston Globe</a></i>’s 2012 Top Places to Work,<sup id="cite_ref-Boston_Globe_Top_Places_107-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boston_Globe_Top_Places-107"><span>[</span>107<span>]</span></a></sup> and Washington's Best Workplaces 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Puget_Sound_Biz_Journal_108-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Puget_Sound_Biz_Journal-108"><span>[</span>108<span>]</span></a></sup> EMC has also been recognized for diversity and inclusion as one of Careers &amp; the disABLED Magazine’s 2013 Top 50 employers<sup id="cite_ref-Careers_.26_disABLED_109-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Careers_.26_disABLED-109"><span>[</span>109<span>]</span></a></sup> and received a score of 100 on the Human Rights Campaign 2013 Corporate Equality Index <sup id="cite_ref-HRC_CEI_110-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRC_CEI-110"><span>[</span>110<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_responsibility_and_sustainability">Corporate responsibility and sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EMC_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Corporate responsibility and sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>EMC supports a variety of corporate responsibility initiatives around the world, and in 2012 EMC ranked 47th on Corporate Responsibility Magazine's 100 Best Corporate Citizens List.<sup id="cite_ref-Corporate_Responsibility_2012_111-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Corporate_Responsibility_2012-111"><span>[</span>111<span>]</span></a></sup> EMC was named the 43rd Greenest Company Globally on <i><a href="/wiki/Newsweek" title="Newsweek">Newsweek</a></i>’s Green Rankings 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Daily_Beast_green_companies_112-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Beast_green_companies-112"><span>[</span>112<span>]</span></a></sup> EMC sponsors programs in the United States that strive to improve education for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (<a href="/wiki/STEM_fields" title="STEM fields" class="mw-redirect">STEM</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113"><span>[</span>113<span>]</span></a></sup> More than 80,000 students have taken EMC Information Storage and Management courses since the program launched in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-EMC_Academic_Alliance_114-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EMC_Academic_Alliance-114"><span>[</span>114<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaboration_with_colleges_and_universities">Collaboration with colleges and universities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EMC_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Collaboration with colleges and universities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>EMC's Academic Alliance program, a collaboration with colleges and universities worldwide, sells curriculum-based education on technology topics such as virtualization, cloud computing, big data analytics, and information storage and management.<sup id="cite_ref-Likester_115-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Likester-115"><span>[</span>115<span>]</span></a></sup> The global program is geared towards arming students with the knowledge necessary to differentiate themselves in the job market and improve their competitive advantage.<sup id="cite_ref-STEMconnector_116-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-STEMconnector-116"><span>[</span>116<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaboration_with_NSA">Collaboration with NSA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EMC_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Collaboration with NSA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/RSA_Security" title="RSA Security">RSA Security</a>, the security division of EMC, has received widespread criticism for accepting $10 million in a secret deal with <a href="/wiki/NSA" title="NSA" class="mw-redirect">NSA</a> to make <a href="/wiki/Dual_EC_DRBG" title="Dual EC DRBG">Dual EC DRBG</a> the default <a href="/wiki/Cryptographically_secure_pseudorandom_number_generator" title="Cryptographically secure pseudorandom number generator">cryptographically secure pseudorandom number generator</a> in its prominent <a href="/wiki/BSAFE" title="BSAFE" class="mw-redirect">BSAFE</a> cryptography library, and continuing to use it until 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-117" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-117"><span>[</span>117<span>]</span></a></sup> Though not confirmed in absolute terms, since 2007 Dual_EC_DRBG 2007 has been suspected to contain a backdoor from NSA, with almost-confirmation coming in a 2013 <i>New York Times</i> article following the <a href="/wiki/Global_surveillance_disclosures_(2013%E2%80%93present)" title="Global surveillance disclosures (2013–present)">Snowden leak</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-118" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-118"><span>[</span>118<span>]</span></a></sup> The standard FIPS version of Dual_EC_DRBG (which RSA Security implemented) was theoretically and empirically shown to be insecure in 2006 and 2007 (even ignoring the backdoor), and following discoveries by Dan Shumow and <a href="/wiki/Niels_Ferguson" title="Niels Ferguson">Niels Ferguson</a> in 2007, prominent security expert <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Schneier" title="Bruce Schneier">Bruce Schneier</a> published an article in Wired titled "<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.schneier.com/essays/archives/2007/11/did_nsa_put_a_secret.html">Did NSA Put a Secret Backdoor in New Encryption Standard?</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-119" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-119"><span>[</span>119<span>]</span></a></sup> Though Dual_EC_DRBG was highly suspect after 2007, its usage in BSAFE was not widely known until the 2013 <i>New York Times</i> article brought back focus to Dual_EC_DRBG. At the 2014 <a href="/wiki/RSA_Conference" title="RSA Conference">RSA Conference</a>, RSA Security Executive Chairman Art Coviello defended RSA Security's choice to keep using Dual_EC_DRBG by saying "it became possible that concerns raised in 2007 might have merit" only after the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Standards_and_Technology" title="National Institute of Standards and Technology">National Institute of Standards and Technology</a> acknowledged the problems in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-120" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-120"><span>[</span>120<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
