<p><b>Shaoshan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">韶山</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Sháoshān</span></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/County-level_city" title="County-level city">county-level city</a> in <a href="/wiki/Xiangtan" title="Xiangtan">Xiangtan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunan_Province" title="Hunan Province" class="mw-redirect">Hunan Province</a>, noted as the birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>, founder of the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China" class="mw-redirect">People's Republic of China</a>. Shaoshan was an important base during the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Revolution_(1946%E2%88%921952)" title="Chinese Revolution (1946−1952)" class="mw-redirect">Chinese Communist Revolution</a>.</p>
<p>Shaoshan residents have capitalised on their most well-known resident such that the city is also the birthplace of Mao's Family Restaurant, a restaurant chain that has spread to many other cities. On the one hand, driven economy in many rural areas. On the other hand, spreading the Chinese nation's traditional culture and let people know more about Chinese nation's traditional culture.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaoshan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Population: city consists of 100,000 residents of which 16,000 are non-farmers. The total land area is 210 square kilometres (81&#160;sq&#160;mi).</p>
<p>Geography: in the Hunan province of Hunan in the east-central hilly region, the geographical coordinates of longitude 112 ° 23'52 "-112 ° 38'13", latitude 27 ° 51'40 "-28 ° 1'53".</p>
<p>Topography: hilly areas. The city's development over the whole territory of the formation, geological structure is complicated. Hills, gurgling streams, red soil segment is connected.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_history">Administrative history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaoshan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Administrative history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Names and administrative levels of Shaoshan:</p>
<ul>
<li>1652 (<a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty" class="mw-redirect">Qing Dynasty</a>): Qidu (<span lang="zh-hans" xml:lang="zh-hans">七都</span>), Xiangtan District (<span lang="zh-hans" xml:lang="zh-hans">縣</span>)</li>
<li>1912 (<a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China" class="mw-redirect">Republic of China</a>): The Second District (<span lang="zh-hans" xml:lang="zh-hans">第二區</span>), Xi Township (<span lang="zh-hans" xml:lang="zh-hans">西鄉</span>), Xiangtan <a href="/wiki/County" title="County">County</a></li>
<li>December 1932: The Ninth District, Xiangtan County</li>
<li>1947: Qingtian Township, Xiangtan County</li>
<li>January 1950 (<a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China" class="mw-redirect">People's Republic of China</a>): The Third District, Xiangtan County</li>
<li>1951: The Fourth District, Xiangtan County</li>
<li>August 1, 1955: Yintian District, Xiangtan County</li>
<li>1956: Yintian Town, Xiangtan County</li>
<li>September 1, 1958: Shaoshan <a href="/wiki/People%27s_commune" title="People's commune">People's Commune</a>, Xiangtan County</li>
<li>May 12, 1961: Shaoshan District, Xiangtan County</li>
<li>December 1968: county-controlled district status upgraded to regional district (<span lang="zh-hans" xml:lang="zh-hans">地級區</span>), controlled directly by the province</li>
<li>December 1981: reverted to be county-controlled</li>
<li>December 1984: upgraded to county-class district (<span lang="zh-hans" xml:lang="zh-hans">縣級區</span>), controlled by Xiangtan City</li>
<li>December 26, 1990: Upgraded to county-class city, controlled by Xiangtan City on behalf of the province.</li>
</ul>
