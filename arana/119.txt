			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Hinke Osinga</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Hinke Maria Osinga</b> is a Dutch mathematician and an expert in <a href="/wiki/Dynamical_system" title="Dynamical system">dynamical systems</a>. She works as a professor of <a href="/wiki/Applied_mathematics" title="Applied mathematics">applied mathematics</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Auckland" title="University of Auckland">University of Auckland</a> in New Zealand.<sup id="cite_ref-cv_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cv-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> As well as for her research, she is known as a creator of <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_art" title="Mathematical art" class="mw-redirect">mathematical art</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_career">Education and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hinke_Osinga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Osinga earned a master's degree in 1991 and a Ph.D. in 1996 from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Groningen" title="University of Groningen">University of Groningen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cv_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cv-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Her doctoral dissertation, jointly supervised by <a href="/wiki/Dynamical_system" title="Dynamical system">dynamical systems</a> theorist Hendrik Broer and <a href="/wiki/Computational_geometry" title="Computational geometry">computational geometer</a> Gert Vegter, was on the computation of <a href="/wiki/Invariant_manifold" title="Invariant manifold">invariant manifolds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>After postdoctoral studies at <a href="/wiki/The_Geometry_Center" title="The Geometry Center">The Geometry Center</a> and the <a href="/wiki/California_Institute_of_Technology" title="California Institute of Technology">California Institute of Technology</a>, and a short-term lecturership at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Exeter" title="University of Exeter">University of Exeter</a>, she became a lecturer at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bristol" title="University of Bristol">University of Bristol</a> in 2001, and was promoted to reader and professor there in 2005 and 2011, respectively. She moved to Auckland in 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-cv_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cv-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> becoming the first female mathematics professor at Auckland and the second in New Zealand.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mathematical_art">Mathematical art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hinke_Osinga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mathematical art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004 Osinga created a <a href="/wiki/Crochet" title="Crochet">crocheted</a> visualization of the Lorenz manifold, an invariant manifold for the <a href="/wiki/Lorenz_system" title="Lorenz system">Lorenz system</a>, and published the crochet pattern for her work with her husband Bernd Krauskopf; the resulting <a href="/wiki/Mathematics_and_fiber_arts" title="Mathematics and fiber arts">mathematical textile artwork</a> involved over 25,000 crochet stitches, and measured nearly a meter across.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Osinga and Krauskopf later collaborated with artist Benjamin Storch on a stainless steel sculpture that provides another interpretation of the same mathematical system.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honours">Awards and honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hinke_Osinga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards and honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Osinga was an <a href="/wiki/List_of_International_Congresses_of_Mathematicians_Plenary_and_Invited_Speakers" title="List of International Congresses of Mathematicians Plenary and Invited Speakers">invited speaker at the International Congress of Mathematicians</a> in 2014, speaking on "Mathematics in Science and Technology".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2015 she was elected as a <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">fellow</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Society_for_Industrial_and_Applied_Mathematics" title="Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics">Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics</a> "for contributions to theory and computational methods for dynamical systems."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
