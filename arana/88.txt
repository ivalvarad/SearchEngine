<p>The <b>Bahá'í calendar</b>, also called the <b>Badí‘ calendar</b> (<i>badí‘</i> means <i>wondrous</i> or <i>unique</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> used by <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A1bism" title="Bábism">Bábism</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith" title="Bahá'í Faith">Bahá'í Faith</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Solar_calendar" title="Solar calendar">solar calendar</a> with years composed of 19 months of 19 days each, (361 days) plus an extra period of "<a href="/wiki/Intercalation_(timekeeping)" title="Intercalation (timekeeping)">Intercalary Days</a>". Years in the calendar begin at the <a href="/wiki/Vernal_equinox" title="Vernal equinox" class="mw-redirect">vernal equinox</a>, and are counted with the <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">date notation</a> of BE (Bahá'í Era), with 21 March 1844 <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">CE</a> being the first day of the first year, the year the <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A1b" title="Báb">Báb</a> proclaimed his religion.<sup id="cite_ref-Smith-calendar_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-calendar-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Bahá'í calendar's implementation has changed over time. Initially the calendar was first implemented and used by the Bábí Faith and then adapted for use in the Bahá'í Faith, with some changes. However, the Bahá'í scriptures left a number of issues regarding the implementation of the calendar to be resolved by the <a href="/wiki/Universal_House_of_Justice" title="Universal House of Justice">Universal House of Justice</a>, the governing body of the Bahá'ís, before the calendar could be observed uniformly worldwide. Until 20 March 2015 the calendar was locked to the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> with the new year always being March 21. However, on 10 July 2014 the Universal House of Justice announced provisions that enabled the common implementation of the calendar worldwide, beginning at sunset 20 March 2015. Since 21 March 2015 the calendar is no longer locked to the Gregorian calendar and the new year will start on the day of the <a href="/wiki/Vernal_equinox" title="Vernal equinox" class="mw-redirect">vernal equinox</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-momen-intro_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-momen-intro-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The period from 21 March 2015 to 20 March 2016 is the year 172 BE.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Bahá'í calendar started from the original Badí‘ calendar, created by the <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A1b" title="Báb">Báb</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Kitabu%27l-Asm%C3%A1%27" title="Kitabu'l-Asmá'">Kitabu'l-Asmá'</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Persian_Bay%C3%A1n" title="Persian Bayán">Persian Bayán</a></i> (5:3) in the 1840s.<sup id="cite_ref-taylor_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> An early version of the calendar began to be implemented during his time.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> It used a scheme of 19 months of 19 days (19x19) for 361 days, plus intercalary days to make the calendar a <a href="/wiki/Solar_calendar" title="Solar calendar">solar calendar</a>. The first day of the early implementation of the calendar year was <a href="/wiki/Nowruz" title="Nowruz">Nowruz</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> while the intercalary days were assigned differently than the later Bahá'í implementation. The calendar contained symbolic connections to prophecies of the Báb about the next <a href="/wiki/Manifestation_of_God" title="Manifestation of God">Manifestation of God</a> termed <a href="/wiki/He_whom_God_shall_make_manifest" title="He whom God shall make manifest">He whom God shall make manifest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27u%27ll%C3%A1h" title="Bahá'u'lláh">Bahá'u'lláh</a>, the founder of the Bahá'í Faith, who claimed to be the one prophesied by the Báb, confirmed and adopted this calendar. Around 1870, he instructed <a href="/wiki/Nab%C3%ADl-i-A%60zam" title="Nabíl-i-A`zam">Nabíl-i-A`zam</a>, the author of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dawn-Breakers" title="The Dawn-Breakers">The Dawn-Breakers</a></i>, to write an overview of the Badí' calendar.<sup id="cite_ref-momen-intro_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-momen-intro-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> In the <i><a href="/wiki/Kit%C3%A1b-i-Aqdas" title="Kitáb-i-Aqdas">Kitáb-i-Aqdas</a></i> (1873) Bahá’u'lláh made <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Naw-R%C3%BAz" title="Bahá'í Naw-Rúz">Naw-Rúz</a> the first day of the year, and also clarified the position of the Intercalary days to immediately precede the last month.<sup id="cite_ref-taylor_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ka2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ka2-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Bahá'u'lláh set Naw-Rúz to the day on which the <a href="/wiki/First_Point_of_Aries" title="First Point of Aries">sun passes into the constellation Aries</a>. Bahá'ís interpret this formula as a specification of the <a href="/wiki/Vernal_equinox" title="Vernal equinox" class="mw-redirect">vernal equinox</a>, though where that should be determined was not defined.<sup id="cite_ref-ka2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ka2-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The calendar was first implemented in the West in 1907.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Bahá'í scriptures left some issues regarding the implementation of the Badi' calendar to be resolved by the <a href="/wiki/Universal_House_of_Justice" title="Universal House of Justice">Universal House of Justice</a> before the calendar can be observed uniformly worldwide. On 10 July 2014 the Universal House of Justice announced provisions that will enable the common implementation of the Badi' calendar worldwide, beginning at sunset 20 March 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-Clarifications_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clarifications-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> coinciding with the completion of the ninth cycle of the calendar (see below).<sup id="cite_ref-nakhjavani2015_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nakhjavani2015-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Before_2015">Before 2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Before 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Bahá'í calendar in western countries was synchronized to the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>, meaning that the extra day of a leap year occurs simultaneously in both calendars so there would be 4 intercalary days in most years, and 5 intercalary days during a leap year. The practice in western countries has been to start the year at sunset on March 20, regardless of when the vernal equinox technically occurs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_Naw-R.C3.BAz_2015">From Naw-Rúz 2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: From Naw-Rúz 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Universal_House_of_Justice" title="Universal House of Justice">Universal House of Justice</a> selected <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a>, the birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27u%27ll%C3%A1h" title="Bahá'u'lláh">Bahá'u'lláh</a>, as the location to which the date of the <a href="/wiki/Vernal_equinox" title="Vernal equinox" class="mw-redirect">vernal equinox</a> is to be fixed, thereby "unlocking" the Badí' calendar from the Gregorian calendar. For determining the dates, astronomical tables from reliable sources are used.<sup id="cite_ref-momen-intro_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-momen-intro-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Clarifications_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clarifications-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the same message the Universal House of Justice decided that the birthdays of the <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A1b" title="Báb">Báb</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27u%27ll%C3%A1h" title="Bahá'u'lláh">Bahá'u'lláh</a> will be celebrated on "the first and the second day following the occurrence of the eighth <a href="/wiki/New_moon" title="New moon">new moon</a> after <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Naw-R%C3%BAz" title="Bahá'í Naw-Rúz">Naw-Rúz</a>" (also with the use of astronomical tables) and fixed the dates of the Bahá'í Holy Days in the Bahá'í calendar, standardizing dates for Bahá'ís worldwide. These changes came into effect as of sunset on 20 March 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-momen-intro_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-momen-intro-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Years">Years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Years in the Bahá'í calendar are counted from Thursday, 21 March 1844, the beginning of the Bahá'í <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">Era</a> or Badí‘ Era (abbreviated <i>BE</i> or <i>B.E.</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-start_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-start-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Year 1 BE thus began at sundown 20 March 1844. Using the Bahá'í names for the weekday and month, day one of the Bahá'í Era was <i>Istijlál</i> (Majesty), 1 <i>Bahá</i> (Splendour) 1 BE. As detailed below, the names of the Bahá'í months and days reflect attributes of God.<sup id="cite_ref-bahai-us_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bahai-us-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> William Miller's polemical work against the Bahá'í Faith claims that the date the calendar was to begin was 1850 CE though most sources agree the date was with the <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A1b#Declaration_to_Mull.C3.A1_Husayn" title="Báb">Declaration of the Bab to Mullá Husayn</a>, on the evening of May 22, 1844 CE. (then the 7th of ‘Aẓamat)<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> The day is one of the Bahá'í holy days, remembered on the 8th day of ‘Aẓamat each year. The moment of the Declaration is celebrated two hours after the opening sunset (in the evening of May 22 or 23).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Months">Months</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Months">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Bahá'í calendar is composed of 19 months, each with 19 days.<sup id="cite_ref-Smith-calendar_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-calendar-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Nineteen_Day_Fast" title="Nineteen Day Fast">Nineteen Day Fast</a> is held during the final month of ‘Alá’, and is preceded by the intercalary days, known as <a href="/wiki/Ayy%C3%A1m-i-H%C3%A1" title="Ayyám-i-Há">Ayyám-i-Há</a>. The month of fasting is followed by <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Naw-R%C3%BAz" title="Bahá'í Naw-Rúz">Naw-Rúz</a>, the new year.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ayy.C3.A1m-i-H.C3.A1"><a href="/wiki/Ayy%C3%A1m-i-H%C3%A1" title="Ayyám-i-Há">Ayyám-i-Há</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ayyám-i-Há">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The introduction of <a href="/wiki/Intercalation_(timekeeping)" title="Intercalation (timekeeping)">intercalation</a> marked an important break from <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>, as under the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_calendar" title="Islamic calendar">Islamic calendar</a> the practice of intercalation had been specifically prohibited in the <a href="/wiki/Qur%27an" title="Qur'an" class="mw-redirect">Qur'an</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-taylor_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The number of the intercalary days is determined in advance to ensure that the year ends on the day before the next vernal equinox. This usually results in 4 or 5 intercalary days and is unaffected by the Gregorian leap year.</p>
<p>Before 2015, the intercalary days included 28 February and 1 March, so they automatically included the Gregorian leap day. There were four intercalary days in a regular year, and five in a leap year.<sup id="cite_ref-Smith-ayyam_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-ayyam-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Month_Names">Month Names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Month Names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The names of the months were adopted by the Báb from the <a href="/wiki/Du%27ay-i-Sahar" title="Du'ay-i-Sahar" class="mw-redirect">Du'ay-i-Sahar</a>, a Ramadan dawn prayer by <a href="/wiki/Imam" title="Imam">Imam</a> <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_al-Baqir" title="Muhammad al-Baqir">Muhammad al-Baqir</a>, the fifth Imam of <a href="/wiki/Twelver" title="Twelver">Twelver Shi'ah Islam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> These month names are described as describing attributes of God.</p>
<p>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Persian_Bayan" title="Persian Bayan" class="mw-redirect">Persian Bayan</a></i> the Báb divides the months in four groups, of three, four, six and six months respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Robin Mirshahi suggests a possible link with four realms described in <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_cosmology" title="Bahá'í cosmology">Bahá'í cosmology</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Mihrshahi_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mihrshahi-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The days of the month have the same names as the names of the month - the 9th day of the month for example is the same as the 9th month - Asmá, or "Names". In the following table, the Gregorian date indicates the first full day of the month. The month begins at sunset of the Gregorian date previous to the one listed, after which time that month's <a href="/wiki/Nineteen_Day_Feast" title="Nineteen Day Feast">Nineteen Day Feast</a> may be celebrated.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Weekdays">Weekdays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Weekdays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Bahá'í week starts on Saturday, and ends on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-vahid0_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vahid0-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> Like <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>, days begin at sunset on the previous <a href="/wiki/Solar_time" title="Solar time">solar day</a> and end at sunset of the present solar day. Bahá'í writings indicate that Friday is to be kept as a day of rest.<sup id="cite_ref-log_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-log-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> The practice of keeping Friday as a day of rest is currently not observed in all countries; for example, in the UK, the National Spiritual Assembly of the Bahá’ís confirmed it does not currently keep this practice.<sup id="cite_ref-UK_rest_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UK_rest-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Holy_days">Holy days</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Holy days">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are eleven holy days in the Bahá'í calendar; on nine of these holy days, work is suspended.<sup id="cite_ref-nsausa_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nsausa-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> There is no fixed format for any of the holy days, and Bahá’í communities organize their own commemorative meetings.<sup id="cite_ref-holy-days_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holy-days-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Festival_of_Ridv%C3%A1n" title="Festival of Ridván" class="mw-redirect">Festival of Ridván</a>, a twelve day festival that commemorates Bahá'u'lláh's announcement of prophethood, is the most holy Bahá'í festival to which Bahá'u'lláh referred as the "Most Great Festival."<sup id="cite_ref-ridvan_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ridvan-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Twin_Holy_Birthdays" title="Twin Holy Birthdays">Twin Holy Birthdays</a> are celebrated on the first and second day following the eighth <a href="/wiki/New_moon" title="New moon">new moon</a> after Naw-Rúz.<sup id="cite_ref-Clarifications_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clarifications-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-momen-intro_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-momen-intro-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> (In the Islamic lunar calendar, the births of the <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A1b" title="Báb">Báb</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27u%27ll%C3%A1h" title="Bahá'u'lláh">Bahá'u'lláh</a> fall on consecutive days; the first and second day of <a href="/wiki/Muharram" title="Muharram">Muharram</a>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-holy-days_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holy-days-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rob4_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rob4-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup>)</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cycles">Cycles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cycles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Also existing in the Bahá'í calendar system is a 19-year cycle called Váḥid and a 361-year (19x19) supercycle called Kull-i-<u>Sh</u>ay’ (literally, "All Things").<sup id="cite_ref-vahid0_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vahid0-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> Each of the 19 years in a Vahid has been given a name as shown in the table below.<sup id="cite_ref-vahid0_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vahid0-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The 10th Váḥid of the 1st Kull-i-<u>Sh</u>ay’ started on 21 March 2015, and the 11th Váḥid will begin in 2034.<sup id="cite_ref-vahid_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vahid-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The current Bahá'í year, year 172 BE (21 March 2015 - 20 March 2016), is year Alif (A) of the 10th Váḥid of the 1st Kull-i-<u>Sh</u>ay’.<sup id="cite_ref-vahid_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vahid-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> The 2nd Kull-i-<u>Sh</u>ay’ will begin in 2205.<sup id="cite_ref-vahid_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vahid-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The concept of a 19-year cycle has existed in some form since the 4th century BC. The <a href="/wiki/Metonic_cycle" title="Metonic cycle">Metonic cycle</a> represents an invented measure that approximately correlates solar and lunar markings of time and which appears in several calendar systems.</p>
