<p><b>Portal</b> is an Australian <a href="/wiki/Extreme_metal" title="Extreme metal">extreme metal</a> band known for its unorthodox <a href="/wiki/Fusion_(music)" title="Fusion (music)" class="mw-redirect">fusion</a> of <a href="/wiki/Death_metal" title="Death metal">death metal</a> with <a href="/wiki/Black_metal" title="Black metal">black metal</a>. The band's hybrid musical style is characterised by heavily <a href="/wiki/Distortion_(music)" title="Distortion (music)">distorted</a> <a href="/wiki/Guitar_riff" title="Guitar riff" class="mw-redirect">guitar riffs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Musical_tuning" title="Musical tuning">down-tuned</a> <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_section" title="Rhythm section">rhythms</a>, and vocals ranging from "menacing, echoing" <a href="/wiki/Sound_effect" title="Sound effect">sound effects</a> to <a href="/wiki/Death_growl" title="Death growl">guttural grunts</a>. Writing for <i><a href="/wiki/Popmatters" title="Popmatters" class="mw-redirect">Popmatters</a></i>, Adrien Begrand noted that "death metal always pretends to be scary, but [...] it's all rather harmless. That said, however, I make no mistake in saying that the death metal peddled by Australia’s Portal is truly friggin' terrifying".<sup id="cite_ref-Begrand_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Begrand-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Lead guitarist Horror Illogium has described Portal's intent as "to capture a cinematic horror scope".<sup id="cite_ref-Lake_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lake-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(Australian_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Portal's main musical influences were <a href="/wiki/Morbid_Angel" title="Morbid Angel">Morbid Angel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beherit_(band)" title="Beherit (band)" class="mw-redirect">Beherit</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Immolation_(band)" title="Immolation (band)">Immolation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Campbell_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campbell-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Describing the band's style, <i><a href="/wiki/Decibel_(magazine)" title="Decibel (magazine)">Decibel</a></i> magazine said: "If <a href="/wiki/Morbid_Angel" title="Morbid Angel">Morbid Angel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gorguts" title="Gorguts">Gorguts</a> had birthed a <a href="/wiki/German_Expressionism" title="German Expressionism">German Expressionist</a> child, that unholy creature would be Portal."<sup id="cite_ref-Lee_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lee-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The band has been praised for its "hellish atmosphere"<sup id="cite_ref-Gorania_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gorania-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> and "distant, obscure"<sup id="cite_ref-Gorania_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gorania-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> interpretation of extreme metal. Denise Falzon, writing for <a href="/wiki/Exclaim!" title="Exclaim!">Exclaim!</a>, described Portal's 2004 EP, <i>The Sweyy</i>, as "extremely raw [...] with heavily distorted guitar riffs that aid in the album's overall chaotic and apocalyptic atmosphere".<sup id="cite_ref-Falzon_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Falzon-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The band's style has also been described as oriented around "constant dissonance", "sporadic compositions", and "abstract themes".<sup id="cite_ref-Martin_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Martin-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> However, lead guitarist Horror Illogium has disputed the view that the band's songwriting lacks structure: "We are indeed making songs, although our structures are unusual. They are constructed with a great deal of artistic, obsessive scrutiny [...] An insane asylum is a structure; our songs can be viewed as structures housing insane elements".<sup id="cite_ref-LeeIO_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeeIO-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Asked if Portal's exploration of extreme music could eventually move beyond the scope of death metal, Horror Illogium responded that:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Portal explores depth and atmosphere in the aim of a descriptive soundscape. The term "extreme" would not really apply to us in the context that other bands would use: the fastest blastbeat or the most technical guitar work. That kind of mindframe as though it is a sport is sickening! Extreme for the sake of extreme in any regard has no place in our art. The feeling must flow, exploring different planes of darkness and atmosphere. There is no interest in taking the main core of Portal beyond Metal at all. In fact, we don't feel as though this form of Death Metal has reached its greatest potential yet.<sup id="cite_ref-LeeIO_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeeIO-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>While some critics have noted a "lack of production quality" and "unrefined, murky sound" on Portal's albums,<sup id="cite_ref-Falzon_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Falzon-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Horror Illogium countered that the band spends considerable time consciously "exploring different equipment for guitar tones with enough articulation as well as dirt".<sup id="cite_ref-LeeIO_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeeIO-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Stating that "there are only so many frequencies that can share a space," he indicated that Portal's approach involved striking "a balance to allow the instruments to spill forth and explode when necessary. This will give a more detailed soundscape and accentuate the impact and depth of certain sections of music".<sup id="cite_ref-LeeIO_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeeIO-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Member_anonymity">Member anonymity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(Australian_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Member anonymity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The band members keep their identities obscured and use <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage names</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Raggi_IV_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raggi_IV-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rivadavia_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rivadavia-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Their costumes are composed of "suits and executioners' masks save for vocalist The Curator, who wears a huge, tattered <a href="/wiki/Pointed_hat" title="Pointed hat">wizard's hat</a> that obscures his face".<sup id="cite_ref-Begrand_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Begrand-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The idea for these costumes was developed by The Curator and Horror Illogium, who were inspired by <a href="/wiki/1920s_in_fashion" title="1920s in fashion" class="mw-redirect">fashion of the 1920s</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fine_art_photography" title="Fine art photography" class="mw-redirect">imagery</a> created by actor <a href="/wiki/Lon_Chaney,_Sr." title="Lon Chaney, Sr." class="mw-redirect">Lon Chaney, Sr.</a> in a few of his roles from the <a href="/wiki/Silent_film" title="Silent film">silent film</a> era which, according to Horror Illogium, was the ideal figure that the band wanted to portray.<sup id="cite_ref-Campbell_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campbell-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Horror Illogium remarked that "anonymity has never been the modus operandi. It is the feeling we ourselves are subjected to, they [the costumes and stage names] serve as vessels for our escape".<sup id="cite_ref-Lake_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lake-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lyrical_themes_and_musical_concepts">Lyrical themes and musical concepts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(Australian_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lyrical themes and musical concepts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The band cites as inspiration for its lyrical themes the entities mentioned in <a href="/wiki/Lovecraft_Mythos" title="Lovecraft Mythos" class="mw-redirect">Lovecraft Mythos</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Nyarlathotep" title="Nyarlathotep">Nyarlathotep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yog-Sothoth" title="Yog-Sothoth">Yog-Sothoth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azathoth" title="Azathoth">Azathoth</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cthulhu" title="Cthulhu">Cthulhu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Raggi_IV_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raggi_IV-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> According to a recent interview, however, their lore is deeply set in other writings. Guitarist Horror Illogium stated that Lovecraft was barely more than a mentioning in their early days, and they have since developed their own mythos. The Curator similarly said that Portal is "not a Lovecraftian Band. We only borrowed from the Mythos in our infancy and had reference themes on several tracks."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal has defined this as the Olde Guarde, their 'sect' according to the interview, who promote ideas of the Vint-Age, which is Portal' usage of references to old technology, esoteric writings, and Victorian/Edwardian culture.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
