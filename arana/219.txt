<p><i><b>Portal</b></i> is the name given to the three <i><a href="/wiki/Magic:_The_Gathering" title="Magic: The Gathering">Magic: The Gathering</a></i> starter level sets. The original <i><b>Portal</b></i>. was released on May 1, 1997, followed by <i><b>Portal Second Age</b></i> on June 24, 1998 and <i><b>Portal Three Kingdoms</b></i> on July 6, 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-release_date_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-release_date-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The <i>Portal</i> set was inspired by Chinese mythology; <i>Three Kingdoms</i> in particular by Chinese historical novel <i><a href="/wiki/Romance_of_the_Three_Kingdoms" title="Romance of the Three Kingdoms">Romance of the Three Kingdoms</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Luo_Guanzhong" title="Luo Guanzhong">Luo Guanzhong</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MILLER_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MILLER-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MOURSUND_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MOURSUND-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Set_history">Set history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(Magic:_The_Gathering)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Set history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Portal</i> was <a href="/wiki/Wizards_of_the_Coast" title="Wizards of the Coast">Wizards of the Coast</a>'s first major attempt at a set that new players could come into the game and understand. Back before the major rules changes brought forth by <i><a href="/wiki/6th_Edition_(Magic:_The_Gathering)" title="6th Edition (Magic: The Gathering)" class="mw-redirect">6th Edition</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Magic:_The_Gathering" title="Magic: The Gathering">Magic</a></i> rules were more complicated; today, they are simplified with the addition of the stack. The three Portal sets attempted to mitigate the complexity.</p>
<p>As of October 2005<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Portal_(Magic:_The_Gathering)&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, all cards <i>Portal</i>, <i>Portal Second Age</i>, and <i>Portal Three Kingdoms</i> are legal in Vintage and Legacy <a href="/wiki/Tournaments" title="Tournaments" class="mw-redirect">tournaments</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Three_Kingdoms"><i>Three Kingdoms</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(Magic:_The_Gathering)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Three Kingdoms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Portal Three Kingdoms</i> was released mainly in Asia-Pacific markets. These cards were printed in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_character" title="Simplified Chinese character" class="mw-redirect">Simplified Chinese</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_character" title="Traditional Chinese character" class="mw-redirect">Traditional Chinese</a>. A very short English printing run was done, but these versions of the cards were released primarily in Australia and New Zealand and are relatively difficult to find, with prices amongst the highest in any set. Each card, including each basic land, was illustrated by a Chinese artist. As referenced and based on actual history, this set was also the only set not based on multiverse nor <a href="/wiki/Plane_(Magic:_The_Gathering)" title="Plane (Magic: The Gathering)" class="mw-redirect">planes</a>, the main universe in the Magic: The Gathering.</p>
<p>On October 20, 2005, the DCI legalized cards from the Portal expansions. Now, almost all cards in the Portal block are legal in Vintage and Legacy tournaments. Allowing this set to be played in tournaments had the effect of making many cards from this set very expensive, such as <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://gatherer.wizards.com/pages/card/Details.aspx?name=Zodiac+Dragon"><i>Zodiac Dragon</i></a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://gatherer.wizards.com/pages/card/Details.aspx?name=Capture+of+Jingzhou"><i>Capture of Jingzhou</i></a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://gatherer.wizards.com/pages/card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Retainers"><i>Loyal Retainers</i></a> and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://gatherer.wizards.com/pages/card/Details.aspx?name=Ravages+of+War"><i>Ravages of War</i></a>. The value of those cards now often exceeds 250USD a piece, with its most sought-after card, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://gatherer.wizards.com/pages/card/Details.aspx?name=Imperial+Seal"><i>Imperial Seal</i></a> commanding well over 600USD. Many cards from this set are now worth more than 5USD, making the sealed booster box from this set one of the most expensive along with Alpha, Beta and Unlimited. In a tournament context, <i>Three Kingdoms</i>, like all other Portal sets, can only be used in Vintage and Legacy formats where many decks function with limited or no creature base. <i>Three Kingdoms</i> is also legal in the Commander format.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mechanics">Mechanics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portal_(Magic:_The_Gathering)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mechanics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>No new mechanics were introduced with Portal, but there were several "simplifications" made to the game through things left out of the set. Instants did not exist within the set (instead, every "one-shot" spell was a sorcery; however, some sorceries could be played at particular times uncommon to sorceries but normal for instants). Most of these cards have since received errata making them Instants. All rules text was in boldface. All flavor text and reminder (italicized) text was not boldface. To separate rules text and flavor text, the cards utilized a line with a slight bulge on both top and bottom; this makes the line look like a very elongated diamond. Rather than using the words "block", "graveyard" and "library", Portal used the words "intercept", "discard pile" and "deck". Portal cards had no creature types.</p>
<p><i>Second Age</i> also did not have instants, though a number of sorceries in the set have later <span class="clarify-content" style="background-color: #fff9f9; color: DarkSlateGray; border: 1px solid #ffdcdc;">received errata making them Instants</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Clarify" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="This passage needs to be better explained. (July 2015)">clarify</span></a></i>]</sup>. It also carried over the different style for rules text. <i>Portal Second Age</i> cards dispensed with the "Summon" wording for creature spells, and were the first to word them as "Creature -- <i>type</i>" that was adopted with <i>6th Edition</i>. In <i>Second Age</i>, "block" remained as it was, replacing "intercept" from Portal. Similarly, "graveyard" re-replaced "discard pile", and "library" re-replaced "deck".</p>
<p><i>Portal Three Kingdoms</i> is the only starter level set to introduce a new keyword ability into the game: "Horsemanship", an evasion ability that works like flying in that creatures with horsemanship can be blocked only by creatures with horsemanship. Creatures with horsemanship can block creatures without horsemanship as well, but not be blocked by creatures with flying, likewise Horsemanship creatures cannot block flying creatures. As such, these creatures are effectively 'unblockable' by almost all other creatures. There was controversy when the set became tournament legal, as some players thought that cards with the horsemanship ability should be errata to have flying instead. Wizards of the Coast initially considered making this change but decided against it, stating that the creatures with horsemanship are rare and not aggressive enough to make an impact on vintage formats. Horsemanship has become an obscure ability due to the limited production and geographical release of the set, and Wizards have stated that it will be unlikely that horsemanship will return on new cards in the future. With the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Spiral_(Magic:_The_Gathering)" title="Time Spiral (Magic: The Gathering)" class="mw-redirect">Time Spiral</a></i>, Wizards returned a vast number of older pre-8th Edition keyword abilities and mechanics; horsemanship was not among the mechanics that returned. The <i>Magic the Gathering Online</i> set <i><a href="/wiki/Masters_Edition_III" title="Masters Edition III" class="mw-redirect">Masters Edition III</a></i> included a number of cards from the <i>Portal Three Kingdoms</i> set including ones with horsemanship.</p>
