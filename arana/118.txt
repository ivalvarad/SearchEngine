<p><b>British Airways Flight 2276</b> was a scheduled passenger flight which caught fire during take-off from Las Vegas-<a href="/wiki/McCarran_International_Airport" title="McCarran International Airport">McCarran International Airport</a> on 8 September 2015, prompting an <a href="/wiki/Aborted_take-off" title="Aborted take-off" class="mw-redirect">aborted take-off</a> and the evacuation of all passengers and crew. The flight, bound for <a href="/wiki/Gatwick_Airport" title="Gatwick Airport">Gatwick Airport</a> near <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, had 157 passengers and 13 crew. The aircraft had suffered an <a href="/wiki/Uncontained_engine_failure" title="Uncontained engine failure" class="mw-redirect">uncontained engine failure</a> in the left (<a href="/wiki/Aircraft_engine_position_number" title="Aircraft engine position number">#1</a>) <a href="/wiki/GE90" title="GE90" class="mw-redirect">GE90</a> engine.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incident">Incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Airways_Flight_2276&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The aircraft left Terminal 3, Gate E3, at 15:53 local time, and began its take-off from Runway 07L at 16:12 where the incident occurred.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>After noticing what the pilot later described as a "catastrophic failure of the engine"<sup id="cite_ref-phipps_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-phipps-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> well before take-off speed, the flight crew <a href="/wiki/Rejected_takeoff" title="Rejected takeoff">aborted the take-off</a> by using the aircraft's brakes and ordered an evacuation of the aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> All passengers and crew escaped, with some suffering minor injuries.</p>
<p>The airport's emergency services extinguished the fire within five minutes of the <a href="/wiki/Mayday" title="Mayday">mayday</a> call. Fourteen people were lightly injured, mostly from sliding down the escape chutes, and treated at <a href="/wiki/Sunrise_Hospital_%26_Medical_Center" title="Sunrise Hospital &amp; Medical Center">Sunrise Hospital &amp; Medical Center</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-phipps_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-phipps-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The fire caused an opening of a large hole in the cargo hold and damage to the engine.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">Federal Aviation Administration</a> (FAA) indicated the fire was caused by failure of the left <a href="/wiki/General_Electric_GE90" title="General Electric GE90">General Electric GE90</a> engine, one of two fitted on the plane.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The aircraft came to a halt upwind, causing the fire to be blown towards the fuselage; the aircraft sustained localized, but major, structural damage as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The aircraft was equipped with suppression systems, though the systems did not extinguish the fire.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The runway, one of four, was closed for four hours, and a number of inbound flights were cancelled.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aircraft">Aircraft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Airways_Flight_2276&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aircraft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The aircraft involved in the incident was a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_777-236ER" title="Boeing 777-236ER" class="mw-redirect">Boeing 777-236ER</a>, registered as G-VIIO. The aircraft itself is about 17 years old and was delivered new to British Airways on 26 January 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The aircraft is likely to be declared a <a href="/wiki/Hull_loss" title="Hull loss">constructive hull loss</a>, meaning the damage is too costly to repair.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> If written off, the insurers would have to absorb around £21.4m ($32.8m) to cover the aircraft and liabilities.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Investigation">Investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Airways_Flight_2276&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Investigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Transportation_Safety_Board" title="National Transportation Safety Board">National Transportation Safety Board</a> (NTSB), the American air accident investigative body, dispatched four investigators to the site the day after.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> As well as FAA, Boeing and General Electric involvement, the British <a href="/wiki/Air_Accidents_Investigation_Branch" title="Air Accidents Investigation Branch">Air Accidents Investigation Branch</a> has a representative and that representative has appointed "British Airways and the UK Civil Aviation Authority as technical advisors". Initial NTSB findings were that an uncontained engine failure had occurred and that the "left engine and pylon, left fuselage structure and inboard left wing airplane were substantially damaged by the fire".<sup id="cite_ref-auto_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> On 6th October 2015 the NTSB issued an update stating that the accident was traced to the failure of the "stage 8-10 spool in the high-pressure compressor section...liberating fragments that breached the engine case and cowling".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
