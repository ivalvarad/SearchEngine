			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Invariant manifold</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Dynamical_systems" title="Dynamical systems" class="mw-redirect">dynamical systems</a>, a branch of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, an <b>invariant manifold</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Topological_manifold" title="Topological manifold">topological manifold</a> that is invariant under the action of the dynamical system.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Examples include the <a href="/wiki/Slow_manifold" title="Slow manifold">slow manifold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Center_manifold" title="Center manifold">center manifold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stable_manifold" title="Stable manifold">stable manifold</a>, unstable manifold, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Subcenter_manifold&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Subcenter manifold (page does not exist)">subcenter manifold</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inertial_manifold" title="Inertial manifold">inertial manifold</a>.</p>
<p>Typically, although by no means always, invariant manifolds are constructed as a 'perturbation' of an <a href="/wiki/Invariant_subspace" title="Invariant subspace">invariant subspace</a> about an equilibrium. In dissipative systems, an invariant manifold based upon the gravest, longest lasting modes forms an effective low-dimensional, reduced, model of the dynamics. <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invariant_manifold&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Consider the <a href="/wiki/Differential_equation" title="Differential equation">differential equation</a> <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="dx/dt = f(x),\ x \in \mathbb R^n," src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/5/4/8/548d061050c25a3b5eb8cb366379b9cf.png" /> with flow <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x(t)=\phi_t(x_0)" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/b/8/1/b81292871dd21f1913bdcfa54336aad9.png" /> being the solution of the differential equation with <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x(0)=x_0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/f/3/f/f3fdf850ada5c6674dd6ef1c6345773b.png" />. A set <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="S \subset \mathbb R^n" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/c/3/a/c3a1608d2a3c060287fd44867cca13b6.png" /> is called an <i>invariant set</i> for the differential equation if, for each <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x_0 \in S" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/4/b/2/4b282c3b79352c6ac74fea59317dde80.png" />, the solution <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="t \mapsto \phi_t(x_0)" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/3/9/0/39047071862daf1f11235e421dcadce0.png" />, defined on its maximal interval of existence, has its image in <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="S" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/5/d/b/5dbc98dcc983a70728bd082d1a47546e.png" />. Alternatively, the orbit passing through each <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x_0 \in S" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/4/b/2/4b282c3b79352c6ac74fea59317dde80.png" /> lies in <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="S" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/5/d/b/5dbc98dcc983a70728bd082d1a47546e.png" />. In addition, <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="S" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/5/d/b/5dbc98dcc983a70728bd082d1a47546e.png" /> is called an <i>invariant manifold</i> if <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="S" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/5/d/b/5dbc98dcc983a70728bd082d1a47546e.png" /> is a <a href="/wiki/Manifold" title="Manifold">manifold</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invariant_manifold&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Simple_2D_dynamical_system">Simple 2D dynamical system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invariant_manifold&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Simple 2D dynamical system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For any fixed parameter <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="a" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/0/c/c/0cc175b9c0f1b6a831c399e269772661.png" />, consider the variables <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x(t),y(t)" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/3/e/6/3e6920351ebed651e55b729eb076d132.png" /> governed by the pair of coupled differential equations</p>
<dl>
<dd><img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="dx/dt=ax-xy\quad\text{and}\quad dy/dt=-y+x^2-2y^2." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/d/4/a/d4ad8ff2aef56db5395659f2e16991ea.png" /></dd>
</dl>
<p>The origin is an equilibrium. This system has two invariant manifolds of interest through the origin.</p>
<ul>
<li>The vertical line <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x=0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/e/1/1/e11729b0b65ecade3fc272548a3883fc.png" /> is invariant as when <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x=0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/e/1/1/e11729b0b65ecade3fc272548a3883fc.png" /> the <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/9/d/d/9dd4e461268c8034f5c8564e155c67a6.png" />-equation becomes <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="dx/dt=0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/a/d/2/ad2550b6103118aacd382279e0cf8d8a.png" /> which ensures <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/9/d/d/9dd4e461268c8034f5c8564e155c67a6.png" /> remains zero. This invariant manifold, <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x=0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/e/1/1/e11729b0b65ecade3fc272548a3883fc.png" />, is a <a href="/wiki/Stable_manifold" title="Stable manifold">stable manifold</a> of the origin (when <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="a\geq0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/0/1/a/01a07b0b22723e6d6fe75e73e815ecdd.png" />) as all initial conditions <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="x(0)=0,\ y(0)&gt;-1/2" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/f/8/b/f8b2b89302732519298f8b451bd171c0.png" /> lead to solutions asymptotically approaching the origin.</li>
<li>The parabola <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="y=x^2/(1+2a)" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/2/5/8/2585200dd044e7fbb7f0cd46819258ec.png" /> is invariant for all parameter <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="a" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/0/c/c/0cc175b9c0f1b6a831c399e269772661.png" />. One can see this invariance by considering the time derivative <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="d/dt[y-x^2/(1+2a)]" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/7/d/6/7d6b53129d8294d4c159ae1d31daa8da.png" /> and finding it is zero on <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="y=x^2/(1+2a)" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/2/5/8/2585200dd044e7fbb7f0cd46819258ec.png" /> as required for an invariant manifold. For <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="a&gt;0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/3/2/3/323c5f97105643bc61e288fe596194ca.png" /> this parabola is the unstable manifold of the origin. For <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="a=0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/d/e/d/ded681eaa02d11064c9a469dd1b3e04c.png" /> this parabola is a <a href="/wiki/Center_manifold" title="Center manifold">center manifold</a>, more precisely a <a href="/wiki/Slow_manifold" title="Slow manifold">slow manifold</a>, of the origin.</li>
<li>For <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="a&lt;0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/c/f/8/cf8298b0e273301afdd921e7e4cf6c2b.png" /> there is only an invariant <a href="/wiki/Stable_manifold" title="Stable manifold">stable manifold</a> about the origin, the stable manifold including all <img class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" alt="(x,y),\ y&gt;-1/2" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/5/3/1/531bffe4328fc402c793b33bbc6be747.png" />.</li>
</ul>
