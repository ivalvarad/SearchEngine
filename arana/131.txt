			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Mushroom dye</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><a href="/wiki/Mushroom" title="Mushroom">Mushrooms</a> can be used to create color <a href="/wiki/Dye#Organic_dyes" title="Dye">dyes</a> via color-extraction with a solvent (often <a href="/wiki/Ammonia" title="Ammonia">ammonia</a>) as well as particulation of raw material.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Sarcodon_imbricatus" title="Sarcodon imbricatus">shingled hedgehog mushroom</a> and related species contain blue-green pigments, which are used for dyeing wool in Norway.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The fruiting body of <i><a href="/wiki/Hydnellum_peckii" title="Hydnellum peckii">Hydnellum peckii</a></i> can be used to produce a beige color when no <a href="/wiki/Mordant" title="Mordant">mordant</a> is used, and shades of blue or green depending on the mordant added.<sup id="cite_ref-Bessette2001_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bessette2001-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Phaeolus_schweinitzii" title="Phaeolus schweinitzii">Phaeolus schweinitzii</a></i> produces green, yellow, gold, or brown colors, depending on the material dyed and the mordant used.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
