<p><b>Michel Portal</b> (born 25 November 1935 in <a href="/wiki/Bayonne" title="Bayonne">Bayonne</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saxophonist" title="Saxophonist" class="mw-redirect">saxophonist</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Clarinet" title="Clarinet">clarinetist</a>.</p>
<p>Portal studied clarinet at the <a href="/wiki/Conservatoire_de_Paris" title="Conservatoire de Paris">Conservatoire de Paris</a>. He also studied conducting with <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Dervaux" title="Pierre Dervaux">Pierre Dervaux</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>During August 1969, Portal played on several of the recordings in <a href="/wiki/Karlheinz_Stockhausen" title="Karlheinz Stockhausen">Stockhausen</a>'s cycle of intuitive works, <i><a href="/wiki/Aus_den_sieben_Tagen" title="Aus den sieben Tagen">Aus den sieben Tagen</a></i>.</p>
<p>Portal might be noted most for <a href="/wiki/Film_score" title="Film score">scoring</a> music to film, and has won the <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Award_for_Best_Music_Written_for_a_Film" title="César Award for Best Music Written for a Film">César Award for Best Music Written for a Film</a> three times. His first win was for the music to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Return_of_Martin_Guerre" title="The Return of Martin Guerre">The Return of Martin Guerre</a></i>. He plays both jazz and classical music and is considered to be one of the architects of modern European jazz.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1969, Portal co-founded the <a href="/wiki/Free_improvisation" title="Free improvisation">free improvisation</a> group <a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Phonic_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="New Phonic Art (page does not exist)">New Phonic Art</a> with <a href="/wiki/Vinko_Globokar" title="Vinko Globokar">Vinko Globokar</a>, Jean-Pierre Drouet and Carlos Roque Alsina.</p>
