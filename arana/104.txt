<p><b>Alpha Condé</b> (born 4 March 1938) is a <a href="/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinean</a> politician who has been <a href="/wiki/President_of_Guinea" title="President of Guinea" class="mw-redirect">President of Guinea</a> since December 2010. He spent decades in opposition to a succession of regimes in Guinea, unsuccessfully running against President <a href="/wiki/Lansana_Cont%C3%A9" title="Lansana Conté">Lansana Conté</a> in the 1993 and 1998 presidential elections and leading the <a href="/wiki/Rally_of_the_Guinean_People" title="Rally of the Guinean People">Rally of the Guinean People</a> (RPG), an opposition party. Standing again in the <a href="/wiki/Guinean_presidential_election,_2010" title="Guinean presidential election, 2010">2010 presidential election</a>, Condé was elected as President of Guinea in a second round of voting. When he took office that December, he became the first freely elected president in the country's history. Condé was <a href="/wiki/Guinean_presidential_election,_2015" title="Guinean presidential election, 2015">reelected in 2015</a> with almost 58% of the vote.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_personal_life">Background and personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Cond%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Condé was born on 4 March 1938 in <a href="/wiki/Bok%C3%A9" title="Boké">Boké</a> in Lower Guinea. His parents were from <a href="/wiki/Baro,_Guinea" title="Baro, Guinea">Baro</a>, a small town in <a href="/wiki/Kouroussa_Prefecture" title="Kouroussa Prefecture">Kouroussa Prefecture</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kankan_Region" title="Kankan Region">Kankan Region</a> of Upper Guinea.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Condé is married to <a href="/wiki/Djene_Kaba_Cond%C3%A9" title="Djene Kaba Condé">Djene Kaba Condé</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Condé has one son, Alpha Mohamed Condé.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Cond%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Condé left for France at the age of 15. He was active in parallel within the <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Union_of_Higher_Education&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Union of Higher Education (page does not exist)">National Union of Higher Education</a> (SNESUP) and combined the functions of charge within the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_of_Guinean_students_in_France&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Association of Guinean students in France (page does not exist)">Association of Guinean students in France</a> (AEGF), and within the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Federation_of_Black_African_Students_in_France&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Federation of Black African Students in France (page does not exist)">Federation of Black African Students in France</a> (FEANF), in which he was the Executive Coordinator of African National Groups (NG) from 1967 to 1975, overseeing the activities of the Directorate of FEANF.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Cond%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Condé won 19.6% of the vote in <a href="/wiki/Guinean_presidential_election,_1993" title="Guinean presidential election, 1993">Guinea's first multiparty presidential election</a>, held on 19 December 1993. <a href="/wiki/Lansana_Cont%C3%A9" title="Lansana Conté">Lansana Conté</a>, who had been president since a bloodless 1984 <i><a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27etat" title="Coup d'etat" class="mw-redirect">coup d'etat</a></i>, won that election with 51.7% of the vote. Condé's supporters alleged fraud in this election after the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Supreme_Court_of_Guinea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Supreme Court of Guinea (page does not exist)">Supreme Court</a> nullified results in the <a href="/wiki/Kankan" title="Kankan">Kankan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siguiri" title="Siguiri">Siguiri</a> prefectures, where Condé had received more than 90% of the vote.<sup id="cite_ref-englebert2006_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-englebert2006-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Guinean_presidential_election,_1998" title="Guinean presidential election, 1998">1998 presidential election</a>, Condé ran again and received 16.6% of the vote, placing third behind Conté (56.1%) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamadou_Boye_B%C3%A2&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mamadou Boye Bâ (page does not exist)">Mamadou Boye Bâ</a> (24.6%). On 16 December, two days after the poll, Condé was arrested and charged with trying to leave the country illegally; he was also charged with attempting to recruit forces to destabilize the government.<sup id="cite_ref-englebert2006_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-englebert2006-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Controversy during his detention focused on whether he could be represented by foreign as well as domestic lawyers, and whether defense lawyers were being given full access to him in jail. Condé's trial, initially scheduled to begin in September 1999, did not begin until April 2000. Condé, along with 47 co-defendants, were charged with hiring mercenaries, planning to assassinate President Conté, and upsetting the state's security. Defense lawyers began by calling for the judge to immediately release their clients, then quit, saying that under the circumstances they could not properly make a defense. The trial was thus delayed several times, during which time Condé refused to speak in court, and his co-defendants denied all of the charges. The trial finally continued in August, and in mid-September Condé was sentenced to jail for five years.<sup id="cite_ref-englebert2006_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-englebert2006-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>However, Condé was released in May 2001 when he was pardoned by President Conté, with the condition that he be prohibited from engaging in political activities.<sup id="cite_ref-englebert2006_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-englebert2006-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Following his release, he left Guinea for France, returning in July 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Upon his return, some reports indicated that he intended to organize the RPG for the municipal elections held in late 2005, but later stated his intention to boycott them.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Following Conté's death and the <a href="/wiki/2008_Guinean_coup_d%27etat" title="2008 Guinean coup d'etat" class="mw-redirect">23 December 2008 military coup</a>, Condé met with <a href="/wiki/Moussa_Dadis_Camara" title="Moussa Dadis Camara">Moussa Dadis Camara</a>, the President of the <a href="/wiki/National_Council_for_Democracy_and_Development" title="National Council for Democracy and Development">National Council for Democracy and Development</a> (CNDD), on 27 December 2008. After the meeting, Condé said that the members of the CNDD junta were "patriots".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Later, however, he opposed the junta<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="(July 2011)">why?</span></a></i>]</sup> and was criticised by Camara.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_election">2010 election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Cond%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010 election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Condé stood again in the <a href="/wiki/Guinean_presidential_election,_2010" title="Guinean presidential election, 2010">2010 presidential election</a>. During the first round, he received 18% of the votes, while <a href="/wiki/Cellou_Dalein_Diallo" title="Cellou Dalein Diallo">Cellou Dalein Diallo</a> came first with over 40%. 15 November 2010 Condé was declared winner of the second round with 52.5%.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> He was inaugurated in December 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Assassination_attempt">Assassination attempt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Cond%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Assassination attempt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 19 July 2011, the presidential residence was shelled, resulting in the death of a presidential guard and the injuries of two others; Condé, however, survived the assassination attempt.<sup id="cite_ref-aljaz_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aljaz-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> A former army chief and a member of the presidential guard were arrested hours after the two attacks on his house. The President later spoke to the country saying that "My house was attacked last night, but I congratulate the presidential guard who fought heroically from 3:10 until 5:00 before backup arrived." He also added that his plans for reform would not fail.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Three days later at least 38 soldiers were arrested for the assassination attempt. A government official said that many of those arrested had ties to the country's previous military rulers.</p>
<p>The United Nations reacted saying that there was a greater need for military reforms in Guinea. The UN special representative for West Africa <a href="/wiki/Said_Djinnit" title="Said Djinnit">Said Djinnit</a> said that the assault showed "weaknesses remain in Guinea's defence and security systems [and] reinforces the UN's determination to support the country's military reforms. I saw the damage...[the attack] clearly intended to kill the president."<sup id="cite_ref-aljaz_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aljaz-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_pre-election_violence">2013 pre-election violence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Cond%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013 pre-election violence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Condé received criticism during week-long protests and violence in late February/early March, after opposition supporters took to the streets of <a href="/wiki/Conakry" title="Conakry">Conakry</a> to protest against his alleged attempts to rig the May elections in 2013. The opposition coalition withdrew from the electoral process in mid-February, mainly due to Condé's insistence on using a South African firm, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Waymark_Infotech&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Waymark Infotech (page does not exist)">Waymark Infotech</a>, to draw up the registered voter list.</p>
<p>The ensuing violence resulted in at least nine deaths and hundreds injured, many from the alleged brutality of the security forces who reportedly used live fire to disperse some of the crowds.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SAL_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SAL-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_election">2015 election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Cond%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2015 election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 17 2015, Condé was <a href="/wiki/Guinean_presidential_election,_2015" title="Guinean presidential election, 2015">reelected in 2015</a> with 57.85% of the vote. However, the election has been dogged with claims of fraud and mismanagement by the opposition.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
