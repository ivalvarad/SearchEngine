<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>–<a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Border" title="Border">border</a></b>, officially known as the <b>International Boundary</b>, is the longest international border in the world, shared between <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America" class="mw-redirect">United States of America</a>, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_area" title="List of countries by area" class="mw-redirect">second and fourth largest</a> countries by area respectively. The terrestrial boundary (including small portions of maritime boundaries on the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">Arctic</a> coasts, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a>) is 8,891 kilometres (5,525&#160;mi) long, including 2,475 kilometres (1,538&#160;mi) shared with <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>. Eight <a href="/wiki/Provinces_and_territories_of_Canada" title="Provinces and territories of Canada">Canadian provinces and territories</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yukon" title="Yukon">Yukon</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>), and thirteen <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. states</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>) are located along the border.</p>

<p>The border originated with the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1783)" title="Treaty of Paris (1783)">Treaty of Paris</a> in 1783, which ended the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> between <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and the separating colonies which would form the United States. The <a href="/wiki/45th_parallel_north" title="45th parallel north">45th parallel</a> was established as the border between <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York_State" title="New York State" class="mw-redirect">New York State</a> (including what is now <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>). The <a href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River" class="mw-redirect">St. Lawrence River</a> and the Great Lakes became the boundary further west (between what is now <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> and the United States).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Jay_Treaty_.281794.29">The Jay Treaty (1794)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Jay Treaty (1794)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Jay_Treaty" title="Jay Treaty">Jay Treaty</a> of 1794 (effective 1796) created the <a href="/wiki/International_Boundary_Commission" title="International Boundary Commission" class="mw-redirect">International Boundary Commission</a>, which was charged with surveying and mapping the boundary. It also provided for removal of British military and administration from <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a> and other frontier outposts on the U.S. side. It was superseded by the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Ghent" title="Treaty of Ghent">Treaty of Ghent</a> (effective 1815) concluding the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>, which included pre-war boundaries.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_London_Convention_.281818.29">The London Convention (1818)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The London Convention (1818)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:49th_parallel_US_Canada_border.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/49th_parallel_US_Canada_border.svg/220px-49th_parallel_US_Canada_border.svg.png" width="220" height="118" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/49th_parallel_US_Canada_border.svg/330px-49th_parallel_US_Canada_border.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/49th_parallel_US_Canada_border.svg/440px-49th_parallel_US_Canada_border.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="350" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:49th_parallel_US_Canada_border.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel north</a> forms a border between the <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> provinces of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a> (to the north), and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> states of <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> (to the south).</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Westward expansion of both <a href="/wiki/British_North_America" title="British North America">British North America</a> and the United States saw the boundary extended west along the <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Angle" title="Northwest Angle">Northwest Angle</a> at <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_the_Woods" title="Lake of the Woods">Lake of the Woods</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_1818" title="Treaty of 1818">Treaty of 1818</a>. This treaty extinguished British claims south of that latitude to the <a href="/wiki/Red_River_Valley" title="Red River Valley">Red River Valley</a>, which was part of <a href="/wiki/Rupert%27s_Land" title="Rupert's Land">Rupert's Land</a>. The treaty also extinguished U.S. claims to land north of that line in the watershed of the <a href="/wiki/Missouri_River" title="Missouri River">Missouri River</a>, which was part of the <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Purchase" title="Louisiana Purchase">Louisiana Purchase</a>; this amounted to three small areas, consisting of the northern part of the drainages of the <a href="/wiki/Milk_River_(Alberta%E2%80%93Montana)" title="Milk River (Alberta–Montana)">Milk River</a> (today in southern <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> and southwestern <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Poplar_River_(Montana%E2%80%93Saskatchewan)" title="Poplar River (Montana–Saskatchewan)">Poplar River</a> (Saskatchewan), and <a href="/wiki/Big_Muddy_Creek_(Missouri_River)" title="Big Muddy Creek (Missouri River)">Big Muddy Creek</a> (Saskatchewan).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ashburton_Treaty_.281842.29">Ashburton Treaty (1842)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ashburton Treaty (1842)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Disputes over the interpretation of the border treaties, and mistakes in surveying it, required additional negotiations resulting in the <a href="/wiki/Webster%E2%80%93Ashburton_Treaty" title="Webster–Ashburton Treaty">Webster–Ashburton Treaty</a> of 1842. The treaty resolved the dispute known as the <a href="/wiki/Aroostook_War" title="Aroostook War">Aroostook War</a> over the boundary between <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a> on the one hand, and <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Canada" title="Province of Canada">Province of Canada</a> on the other. The treaty redefined the border between <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> on the one hand, and the Province of Canada on the other, resolving the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Indian_Stream" title="Republic of Indian Stream">Indian Stream</a> dispute and the <a href="/wiki/Fort_Montgomery_(Lake_Champlain)" title="Fort Montgomery (Lake Champlain)">Fort Blunder</a> dilemma at the outlet to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Champlain" title="Lake Champlain">Lake Champlain</a>. The boundary along the 45th parallel had been surveyed after the War of 1812. The US Government began to construct fortifications just south of the border at <a href="/wiki/Rouses_Point" title="Rouses Point" class="mw-redirect">Rouses Point</a> on Lake Champlain. After a significant portion of the construction was completed, measurements revealed that at that point, the actual 45th parallel was three-quarters of a mile (1.2&#160;km) south of the surveyed line; the fort, which became known as "Fort Blunder", was in Canada. This created a dilemma for the United States that was not resolved until a provision of the treaty left the border on the meandering line as surveyed. The border along the <a href="/wiki/Boundary_Waters" title="Boundary Waters">Boundary Waters</a> in present-day <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> between <a href="/wiki/Lake_Superior" title="Lake Superior">Lake Superior</a> and the Northwest Angle was also redefined.<sup id="cite_ref-WA_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WA-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lass_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lass-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Oregon_Treaty_.281846.29">The Oregon Treaty (1846)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Oregon Treaty (1846)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Oregon_boundary_dispute" title="Oregon boundary dispute">1844 boundary dispute</a> during U.S. President <a href="/wiki/James_K._Polk" title="James K. Polk">James K. Polk</a>'s administration led to a call for the northern boundary of the U.S. west of the Rockies to be latitude 54°&#160;40' north (related to the southern boundary of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Russian_America" title="Russian America">Alaska Territory</a>), but the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom</a> wanted a border that followed the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">Columbia River</a> to the Pacific Ocean. The dispute was resolved in the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Treaty" title="Oregon Treaty">Oregon Treaty</a> of 1846, which established the <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel</a> as the boundary through the Rockies.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Northwest_Boundary_Survey_.281857.E2.80.9361.29">The Northwest Boundary Survey (1857–61)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Northwest Boundary Survey (1857–61)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The Northwest Boundary Survey (1857–61) laid out the land boundary, but the water boundary was not settled for some time. After the <a href="/wiki/Pig_War" title="Pig War">Pig War</a> in 1859, arbitration in 1872 established the border between the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Islands" title="Gulf Islands">Gulf Islands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Islands" title="San Juan Islands">San Juan Islands</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_International_Boundary_Survey_.281872.E2.80.9376.29">The International Boundary Survey (1872–76)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The International Boundary Survey (1872–76)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The International Boundary Survey, called the Northern Boundary Survey in the United States, began in 1872.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Its mandate was to establish the border as agreed to in the Treaty of 1818. Archibald Campbell led the way for the United States. Donald Cameron headed the British team. This survey focused on the border from the Lake of the Woods to the summit of the Rocky Mountains.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Alaska_boundary_dispute_.281903.29">The Alaska boundary dispute (1903)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: The Alaska boundary dispute (1903)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1903 a joint United Kingdom&#160;– Canada&#160;– U.S. tribunal established the <a href="/wiki/Alaska_boundary_dispute" title="Alaska boundary dispute">boundary with Alaska</a>, much of which follows the <a href="/wiki/141st_meridian_west" title="141st meridian west">141st meridian west</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Treaty_of_1908">The Treaty of 1908</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: The Treaty of 1908">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 11, 1908, Great Britain and the United States agreed, under Article IV of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_1908&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Treaty of 1908 (page does not exist)">Treaty of 1908</a>, to survey and delimit the boundary between Canada and the United States through the St. Lawrence River and Great Lakes, in accordance with modern surveying techniques, and thus accomplished several changes to the border.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Boundary_Commission_.281925.29">International Boundary Commission (1925)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International Boundary Commission (1925)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In 1925, the International Boundary Commission was made a permanent organization responsible for surveying and mapping the boundary, maintaining boundary monuments (and <a href="/wiki/Buoy" title="Buoy">buoys</a> where applicable), as well as keeping the boundary clear of brush and vegetation for 6 metres (20&#160;ft). This "<a href="/wiki/Border_vista" title="Border vista">border vista</a>" extends for 3 metres (9.8&#160;ft) on each side of the line. Along the 49th parallel, the border vista is theoretically straight but in practice follows the 19th century surveyed border markers and varies by several hundred feet in spots.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Commission's annual budget is about US$1.4 million <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Commission is headed by two commissioners, one of whom is Canadian, the other American.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> In July 2007, the <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_George_W._Bush" title="Presidency of George W. Bush">Bush administration</a> relieved U.S. Commissioner Dennis Schornack of his post in connection with a dispute between the boundary commission and the U.S. government over private construction near the border.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Schornack rejected the dismissal, saying that the commission is an independent, international organization outside the U.S. government's jurisdiction, and that according to the 1908 treaty that created it, a vacancy can only be created by "the death, resignation or other disability" of a commissioner.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The Canadian government said that it was taking no position on the matter,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> but Peter Sullivan, the Canadian commissioner, said on July 13 that he was ready to work with David Bernhardt, a <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>-based solicitor of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_the_Interior" title="Department of the Interior" class="mw-redirect">Department of the Interior</a>, who was designated as the acting U.S. commissioner by President Bush.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Security">Security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Security">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_enforcement_approach">Law enforcement approach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Law enforcement approach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The International Boundary is commonly referred to as the world's <i>longest undefended border</i>, but this is true only in the military sense, as civilian law enforcement is present. It is illegal to pass the border outside border controls. Everyone passing the border must be checked. The relatively low level of security measures stands in contrast to that of the <a href="/wiki/Mexico%E2%80%93United_States_border" title="Mexico–United States border">United States&#160;– Mexico border</a> (one-third as long as the Canada–U.S. border), which is actively patrolled by <a href="/wiki/U.S._Customs_and_Border_Protection" title="U.S. Customs and Border Protection">U.S. customs and immigration personnel</a> to prevent <a href="/wiki/Illegal_immigration" title="Illegal immigration">illegal migration</a> and <a href="/wiki/Illegal_drug_trade" title="Illegal drug trade">drug trafficking</a>.</p>
<p>Parts of the International Boundary cross through mountainous terrain or heavily forested areas, but significant portions also cross remote prairie farmland and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lawrence_River" title="Saint Lawrence River">Saint Lawrence River</a>, in addition to the maritime components of the boundary at the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">Arctic</a> oceans. The border also runs through the middle of the <a href="/wiki/Akwesasne" title="Akwesasne">Akwesasne Nation</a> and even divides some buildings found in communities in Vermont and Quebec whose construction pre-dated the border's delineation. On the Maine-New Brunswick border, it divides the <a href="/wiki/Aroostook_Valley_Country_Club" title="Aroostook Valley Country Club">Aroostook Valley Country Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The actual number of U.S. and Canadian border security personnel is <a href="/wiki/Classified_information" title="Classified information">classified</a>; there are in excess of 17,000 <a href="/wiki/United_States_Border_Patrol" title="United States Border Patrol">United States Border Patrol</a> personnel on the Mexico–U.S. border alone.<sup id="cite_ref-cut_border_patrol_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cut_border_patrol-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11, 2001, terrorist attacks</a> in the United States, border security along the International Boundary was dramatically tightened by both nations in both populated and rural areas. Both nations are also actively involved in detailed and extensive tactical and strategic intelligence sharing.</p>
<p>As of December 2010<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Canada and the United States are negotiating an agreement titled "Beyond the Border: A Shared Vision for Perimeter Security and Competitiveness" which would give the U.S. more influence over Canada's border security and immigration controls, and more information would be shared by Canada with the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Residents of both nations who own property adjacent to the border are required to report construction of any physical border crossing on their land to their respective governments, and this is enforced by the International Boundary Commission. Where required, fences or vehicle blockades are used. All persons crossing the border are required to report to the respective customs and immigration agencies in each country. In remote areas where staffed border crossings are not available, there are hidden <a href="/wiki/Sensor" title="Sensor">sensors</a> on roads and also scattered in wooded areas near crossing points and on many trails and railways, but there are not enough border personnel on either side to verify and stop coordinated incursions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> There is no <a href="/wiki/Border_zone" title="Border zone">border zone</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> the U.S. Customs and Border Protection routinely sets up checkpoints as far as 100 miles (160&#160;km) into U.S. territory.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Smuggling">Smuggling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Smuggling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smuggling of <a href="/wiki/Alcoholic_beverage" title="Alcoholic beverage">alcoholic beverages</a> (<a href="/wiki/Rum-running" title="Rum-running">"rum running"</a>) was widespread during the 1920s, when Prohibition was in effect nationally in the <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_in_the_United_States" title="Prohibition in the United States">United States</a> and parts of <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_in_Canada" title="Prohibition in Canada">Canada</a>.</p>
<p>In more recent years, Canadian officials have complained of <a href="/wiki/Narcotics" title="Narcotics" class="mw-redirect">drug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cigarette" title="Cigarette">cigarette</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Firearm" title="Firearm">firearms</a> smuggling from the United States, while U.S. officials have complained of <a href="/wiki/Narcotics" title="Narcotics" class="mw-redirect">drug</a> smuggling from Canada.</p>
<p>In July 2005, law enforcement personnel arrested three men who had built a 360-foot (110&#160;m) tunnel under the border between <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a> that they intended to use for smuggling <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">marijuana</a>, the first such tunnel known on this border.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Cornwall,_Ontario" title="Cornwall, Ontario">Cornwall, Ontario</a>, experiences ongoing smuggling - mostly of tobacco and firearms from the United States - due to its location. The neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Mohawk_people" title="Mohawk people">Mohawk</a> territory of <a href="/wiki/Akwesasne" title="Akwesasne">Akwesasne</a> straddles the Ontario-Quebec-New York borders, and its <a href="/wiki/First_Nations" title="First Nations">First Nations</a> sovereignty prevents <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Provincial_Police" title="Ontario Provincial Police">Ontario Provincial Police</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">Royal Canadian Mounted Police</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada_Border_Services_Agency" title="Canada Border Services Agency">Canada Border Services Agency</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Coast_Guard" title="Canadian Coast Guard">Canadian Coast Guard</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Border_Patrol" title="United States Border Patrol">United States Border Patrol</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Police" title="New York State Police">New York State Police</a> from exercising jurisdiction over exchanges taking place within the territory.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_Hemisphere_Travel_Initiative_.28WHTI.29">Western Hemisphere Travel Initiative (WHTI)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Western Hemisphere Travel Initiative (WHTI)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere_Travel_Initiative" title="Western Hemisphere Travel Initiative">Western Hemisphere Travel Initiative</a></div>
<p>In late 2006, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Homeland_Security" title="United States Department of Homeland Security">United States Department of Homeland Security</a> (DHS) announced a rule regarding new identification requirements for U.S. citizens and international travelers entering the U.S. Implemented on January 23, 2007, this final rule and first phase of the WHTI specifies six forms of identification—one of which is required in order to enter the U.S. by air: a valid <a href="/wiki/Passport" title="Passport">passport</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Passport_card" title="Passport card" class="mw-redirect">passport card</a>, a state <a href="/wiki/Driver%27s_license_in_the_United_States#Enhanced_driver.27s_licenses" title="Driver's license in the United States">enhanced driver's license</a> (available in <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup>) per the Western Hemisphere Travel Initiative, or trusted traveler program card (<a href="/wiki/NEXUS_(frequent_traveler_program)" title="NEXUS (frequent traveler program)">NEXUS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Free_and_Secure_Trade" title="Free and Secure Trade">FAST</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Secure_Electronic_Network_for_Travelers_Rapid_Inspection" title="Secure Electronic Network for Travelers Rapid Inspection">SENTRI</a>); a valid Merchant Mariner Credential when traveling in conjunction with official maritime business; or a valid U.S. military identification card when traveling on official orders.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Since June 2009, every traveller arriving via a land or sea port-of-entry (including <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferries</a>) has been required to present one of the above forms of identification to cross the border.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_border_crossings_.28airports.2C_seaports.2C_rail_stations.29">Other border crossings (airports, seaports, rail stations)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Other border crossings (airports, seaports, rail stations)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>The U.S. maintains immigration offices, called <a href="/wiki/United_States_border_preclearance" title="United States border preclearance">pre-clearance facilities</a>, in eight Canadian airports with international air service to the United States (<a href="/wiki/Calgary_International_Airport" title="Calgary International Airport">Calgary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_International_Airport" title="Edmonton International Airport">Edmonton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Stanfield_International_Airport" title="Halifax Stanfield International Airport">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montr%C3%A9al%E2%80%93Pierre_Elliott_Trudeau_International_Airport" title="Montréal–Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Macdonald%E2%80%93Cartier_International_Airport" title="Ottawa Macdonald–Cartier International Airport">Ottawa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Pearson_International_Airport" title="Toronto Pearson International Airport">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_International_Airport" title="Vancouver International Airport">Vancouver</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg_James_Armstrong_Richardson_International_Airport" title="Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport">Winnipeg</a>). This expedites travel by allowing flights originating in Canada to land at a U.S. airport without being processed as an international arrival. Similar arrangements exist at major Canadian seaports that handle sealed direct import shipments into the U.S.</p>
<p>Canada does not maintain equivalent personnel at U.S. airports due to the sheer number of U.S. departure locations with Canadian-bound flights and the limited number of flights compared to the number of U.S.-bound flights that depart major Canadian airports. Additionally, at Vancouver's <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Central_Station" title="Pacific Central Station">Pacific Central Station</a>, passengers are required to pass through U.S. pre-clearance facilities and pass their baggage through an X-ray before being allowed to board the <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>-bound <a href="/wiki/Amtrak_Cascades" title="Amtrak Cascades">Amtrak <i>Cascades</i></a> train, which makes no further stops before crossing the border. Pre-clearance facilities are not available for the popular <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> (<a href="/wiki/Adirondack_(train)" title="Adirondack (train)"><i>Adirondack</i></a>) or <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> (<a href="/wiki/Maple_Leaf_(train)" title="Maple Leaf (train)"><i>Maple Leaf</i></a>) lines, as these lines have stops between Montreal or Toronto and the border. Instead, passengers must clear customs at a stop located at the actual border.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">Ferry</a> services operate between the province of <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> and the state of <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, as well as between the province of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> and the states of <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>. There are also several ferry services in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a> operating between the province of <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> and the states of <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>. The ferry between Maine and <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> ended its route in 2009, but resumed operations in 2014.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Boldt_Castle" title="Boldt Castle">Boldt Castle</a> on Heart Island in the <a href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River" class="mw-redirect">St. Lawrence River</a> has a border control point on the island, but no specific location on the Canadian side. Canadians must present identification to land on the island.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cross-border_airports">Cross-border airports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Cross-border airports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>One curiosity on the Canada–U.S. border is the presence of six airports that straddle the borderline. The airports were built prior to the <a href="/wiki/Military_history_of_the_United_States_during_World_War_II" title="Military history of the United States during World War II">U.S. entry into World War II</a> as a way to legally transfer U.S-built aircraft such as the <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Hudson" title="Lockheed Hudson">Lockheed Hudson</a> to Canada under the provisions of the <a href="/wiki/Lend-Lease" title="Lend-Lease">Lend-Lease Act</a>. In the interest of maintaining neutrality, U.S. military pilots were forbidden to deliver combat aircraft to Canada. The aircraft were flown to the border, landed, and then at night towed on their wheels over the border by tractors or horses. The next day the planes were crewed by <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Air_Force" title="Royal Canadian Air Force">RCAF</a> pilots and flown to other locations, typically airbases in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Canada" title="Eastern Canada">Eastern Canada</a>, from where they were often flown to the United Kingdom and deployed in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Atlantic" title="Battle of the Atlantic">Battle of the Atlantic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Piney_Pinecreek_Border_Airport" title="Piney Pinecreek Border Airport">Piney Pinecreek Border Airport</a> is located in <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>. The northwest/southeast-oriented runway straddles the border, and there are two ramps; one in the U.S. and one in Canada. The airport is jointly owned by the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Department_of_Transportation" title="Minnesota Department of Transportation">Minnesota Department of Transportation</a> and the local government of <a href="/wiki/Piney,_Manitoba" title="Piney, Manitoba">Piney, Manitoba</a>. It is assigned U.S. identifier 48Y and Canadian identifier JX2.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/International_Peace_Garden_Airport" title="International Peace Garden Airport">International Peace Garden Airport</a> is located in <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a> adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/International_Peace_Garden" title="International Peace Garden">International Peace Garden</a>. The runway is entirely within North Dakota, but a ramp extends across the border to allow aircraft to access Canadian customs. While not jointly owned, it is operated as an international facility for customs clearance as part of the International Peace Garden.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Coronach/Scobey_Border_Station_Airport" title="Coronach/Scobey Border Station Airport">Coronach/Scobey Border Station Airport</a> is located in <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>. The east–west runway is sited exactly on the border. The airport is jointly owned by the Canadian and U.S. governments and is assigned U.S. identifier 8U3 and Canadian identifier CKK3.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Coutts/Ross_International_Airport" title="Coutts/Ross International Airport">Coutts/Ross International Airport</a> is located in <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> and Montana. Like Coronach/Scobey, the east–west runway is sited exactly on the border. It is owned entirely by the Montana Aeronautics Division. It is assigned U.S. identifier 7S8 and Canadian identifier CEP4.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Whetstone_International_Airport" title="Whetstone International Airport">Whetstone International Airport</a> or Del Bonita/Whetstone International Airport is located in Alberta and Montana, and similarly has an east–west runway sited exactly on the border. It is assigned U.S. identifier H28 and Canadian identifier CEQ4.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Avey_Field_State_Airport" title="Avey Field State Airport">Avey Field State Airport</a> is located in <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>. The privately owned airfield is mostly in the U.S., but several hundred feet of the north-south runway extend into Canada, and both Canadian and U.S. customs are available. It is assigned U.S. identifier 69S but does not have a Canadian identifier.</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Haskell_Free_Library_and_Opera_House" title="Haskell Free Library and Opera House">Haskell Free Library and Opera House</a> straddles the border in <a href="/wiki/Derby_Line,_Vermont" title="Derby Line, Vermont">Derby Line, Vermont</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stanstead,_Quebec" title="Stanstead, Quebec">Stanstead, Quebec</a>. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boundary_divisions">Boundary divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Boundary divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practical_exclaves">Practical exclaves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Practical exclaves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>To be a true international <a href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave" class="mw-redirect">exclave</a>, <i>all</i> potential paths of travel from the exclave to the home country must cross over only the territory of a different country or countries. Like exclaves, practical exclaves are not contiguous with the <i>land</i> of the home country and have <i>land</i> access only through another country or countries. Unlike exclaves, they are not <i>entirely</i> surrounded by foreign territory. Hence, they are exclaves <i>for practical purposes</i>, without meeting the strict definition.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Practical_exclaves_of_Canada">Practical exclaves of Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Practical exclaves of Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Akwesasne" title="Akwesasne">Akwesasne</a>: Because of the St. Lawrence River to the north and New York State, USA to the south, the Quebec portion of the Akwesasne reserve is a practical <a href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave" class="mw-redirect">exclave</a> of Canada. To travel by land from Tsi:Snaine (Snye or Chenail, Quebec) or Kana:takon (<a href="/wiki/Saint_Regis,_Quebec" title="Saint Regis, Quebec" class="mw-redirect">Saint Regis, Quebec</a>) to elsewhere in Canada, one must drive through New York State.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Campobello_Island" title="Campobello Island">Campobello Island</a>, located at the entrance to <a href="/wiki/Passamaquoddy_Bay" title="Passamaquoddy Bay">Passamaquoddy Bay</a>, adjacent to the entrance to <a href="/wiki/Cobscook_Bay" title="Cobscook Bay">Cobscook Bay</a>, and within the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Fundy" title="Bay of Fundy">Bay of Fundy</a>. The island is part of <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_County,_New_Brunswick" title="Charlotte County, New Brunswick">Charlotte County</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>, but is actually physically connected by the <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Delano_Roosevelt_Bridge" title="Franklin Delano Roosevelt Bridge">Franklin Delano Roosevelt Bridge</a> with <a href="/wiki/Lubec,_Maine" title="Lubec, Maine">Lubec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a> – the easternmost tip of the continental <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Practical_exclaves_of_the_United_States">Practical exclaves of the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Practical exclaves of the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The state of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> is bounded by the Canadian province of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> and territory of <a href="/wiki/Yukon" title="Yukon">Yukon</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">Arctic Ocean</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Bering_Sea" title="Bering Sea">Bering Sea</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Point_Roberts,_Washington" title="Point Roberts, Washington">Point Roberts, Washington</a>, is bounded by the Canadian province of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Georgia" title="Strait of Georgia">Strait of Georgia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Boundary_Bay" title="Boundary Bay">Boundary Bay</a>.</li>
<li>A small unnamed peninsula in North Dakota in a lake northwest of the town of <a href="/wiki/St._John,_North_Dakota" title="St. John, North Dakota">St. John</a>, and southwest of Lena, Manitoba.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Elm_Point,_Minnesota" title="Elm Point, Minnesota">Elm Point, Minnesota</a>, and a small piece of uninhabited land just to its west are bounded by Manitoba and <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_the_Woods" title="Lake of the Woods">Lake of the Woods</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Angle" title="Northwest Angle">Northwest Angle</a>, the northernmost part of the contiguous 48 states, is bounded by Manitoba and Lake of the Woods.</li>
<li>The Alburgh Tongue in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Champlain" title="Lake Champlain">Lake Champlain</a>, location of the town of <a href="/wiki/Alburgh,_Vermont" title="Alburgh, Vermont" class="mw-redirect">Alburgh, Vermont</a> (Quebec).<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-alburgh_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alburgh-33"><span>[</span>fn 1<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lake_Champlain#Province_Point.2C_Vermont" title="Lake Champlain">Province Point</a>, the small end of a peninsula east of Alburgh, Vermont, is bounded by Quebec and Lake Champlain.</li>
</ul>
