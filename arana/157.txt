<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#f9f9ff;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Arts_Portal"><big>The Arts Portal</big></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<p><br /></p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:1em">
<center><a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Center_for_the_Performing_Arts" title="Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts">Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts</a></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/File:Lincoln_Center_Twilight.jpg" class="image" title="Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts"><img alt="Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Lincoln_Center_Twilight.jpg/200px-Lincoln_Center_Twilight.jpg" width="200" height="150" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Lincoln_Center_Twilight.jpg/300px-Lincoln_Center_Twilight.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Lincoln_Center_Twilight.jpg/400px-Lincoln_Center_Twilight.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1501" /></a><br /></p>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/The_arts" title="The arts">The arts</a></b> is a vast subdivision of <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a>, composed of many creative endeavors and disciplines. It is a broader term than "<i><a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a></i>", which, as a description of a field, usually means only the <a href="/wiki/Visual_arts" title="Visual arts">visual arts</a>. <i>The arts</i> encompass the <a href="/wiki/Visual_arts" title="Visual arts">visual arts</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Literary_arts" title="Literary arts" class="mw-redirect">literary arts</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Performing_arts" title="Performing arts">performing arts</a> – <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">theatre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dance" title="Dance">dance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a>, among others. This list is by no means comprehensive, but only meant to introduce the concept of the arts. For all intents and purposes, the history of the arts begins with the <i><a href="/wiki/History_of_art" title="History of art">history of art</a></i>. The arts might have origins in early human evolutionary prehistory.</p>
<p>Ancient Greek art saw the veneration of the animal form and the development of equivalent skills to show musculature, poise, beauty and anatomically correct proportions. <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Ancient Roman</a> art depicted gods as idealized humans, shown with characteristic distinguishing features (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Jupiter_(mythology)" title="Jupiter (mythology)">Jupiter</a>'s thunderbolt). In <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_art" title="Byzantine art">Byzantine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gothic_art" title="Gothic art">Gothic art</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, the dominance of the church insisted on the expression of biblical and not material truths. Eastern art has generally worked in a style akin to Western medieval art, namely a concentration on surface patterning and local colour (meaning the plain colour of an object, such as basic red for a red robe, rather than the modulations of that colour brought about by light, shade and reflection). A characteristic of this style is that the local colour is often defined by an outline (a contemporary equivalent is the cartoon). This is evident in, for example, the art of <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Religious <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islamic</a> art forbids iconography, and expresses religious ideas through geometry instead. The physical and rational certainties depicted by the 19th-century Enlightenment were shattered not only by new discoveries of relativity by <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Einstein</a> and of unseen psychology by <a href="/wiki/Sigmund_Freud" title="Sigmund Freud">Freud</a>, but also by unprecedented technological development. Paradoxically the expressions of new technologies were greatly influenced by the ancient tribal arts of Africa and Oceania, through the works of Paul Gauguin and the Post-Impressionists, Pablo Picasso and the Cubists, as well as the Futurists and others.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#f9f9ff;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Featured_article">Featured article</span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Hieroglyphs_from_the_tomb_of_Seti_I.jpg" class="image" title="Hieroglyphs from the tomb of Seti I"><img alt="Hieroglyphs from the tomb of Seti I" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Hieroglyphs_from_the_tomb_of_Seti_I.jpg/120px-Hieroglyphs_from_the_tomb_of_Seti_I.jpg" width="120" height="77" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Hieroglyphs_from_the_tomb_of_Seti_I.jpg/180px-Hieroglyphs_from_the_tomb_of_Seti_I.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Hieroglyphs_from_the_tomb_of_Seti_I.jpg/240px-Hieroglyphs_from_the_tomb_of_Seti_I.jpg 2x" data-file-width="589" data-file-height="379" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_literature" title="Ancient Egyptian literature">Ancient Egyptian literature</a></b> was written in the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_language" title="Egyptian language">Egyptian language</a> from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egypt</a>'s <a href="/wiki/History_of_ancient_Egypt" title="History of ancient Egypt">pharaonic period</a> until the end of <a href="/wiki/Egypt_(Roman_province)" title="Egypt (Roman province)">Roman domination</a>. Along with <a href="/wiki/Sumerian_literature" title="Sumerian literature">Sumerian literature</a>, it is considered the world's <a href="/wiki/Ancient_literature" title="Ancient literature">earliest literature</a>. <a href="/wiki/Writing_in_Ancient_Egypt" title="Writing in Ancient Egypt">Writing in Ancient Egypt</a> first appeared in the late <b>4th millennium BC</b>. By the <a href="/wiki/Old_Kingdom" title="Old Kingdom" class="mw-redirect">Old Kingdom</a>, literary works included <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_funerary_texts" title="Ancient Egyptian funerary texts">funerary texts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Epistle" title="Epistle">epistles</a> and letters, religious hymns and poems, and commemorative <a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">autobiographical</a> texts. It was not until the early <a href="/wiki/Middle_Kingdom_of_Egypt" title="Middle Kingdom of Egypt">Middle Kingdom</a> that a narrative Egyptian literature was created. <a href="/wiki/Middle_Egyptian" title="Middle Egyptian" class="mw-redirect">Middle Egyptian</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Spoken_language" title="Spoken language">spoken language</a> of the Middle Kingdom, became a <a href="/wiki/Classical_language" title="Classical language">classical language</a> during the <a href="/wiki/New_Kingdom" title="New Kingdom" class="mw-redirect">New Kingdom</a>, when the <a href="/wiki/Vernacular_language" title="Vernacular language" class="mw-redirect">vernacular language</a> known as <a href="/wiki/Late_Egyptian" title="Late Egyptian" class="mw-redirect">Late Egyptian</a> first appeared in writing. Scribes of the New Kingdom <a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Canon" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:Canon">canonized</a> and copied many literary texts written in Middle Egyptian, which remained the language used for oral readings of sacred hieroglyphic texts. Ancient Egyptian literature has been preserved on a wide variety of media, including <a href="/wiki/Papyrus" title="Papyrus">papyrus</a> scrolls and packets, limestone or ceramic <a href="/wiki/Ostracon" title="Ostracon">ostraca</a>, wooden writing boards, <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_architecture" title="Ancient Egyptian architecture">monumental stone edifices</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coffin_Texts" title="Coffin Texts">coffins</a>. Hidden caches of literature, buried for thousands of years, have been discovered in settlements on the dry desert margins of Egyptian civilization.

<p>"I did not raise my girl to be a voter": A 1915 <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">parody</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Puck_(magazine)" title="Puck (magazine)">Puck</a></i> of the <a href="/wiki/Opposition_to_World_War_I" title="Opposition to World War I">anti-World War&#160;I</a> protest song "<a href="/wiki/I_Didn%27t_Raise_My_Boy_to_Be_a_Soldier" title="I Didn't Raise My Boy to Be a Soldier">I Didn't Raise My Boy to Be a Soldier</a>" with the context altered to <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_suffrage" title="Women's suffrage">women's suffrage</a>. A conductor labeled "<a href="/wiki/Political_boss" title="Political boss">political boss</a>" leads a lone female soloist surrounded by a male chorus with various labels including "procurer", "<a href="/wiki/Child_labour" title="Child labour">child labor</a> employer", and "<a href="/wiki/Sweatshop" title="Sweatshop">sweat shop</a> owner". Arguments in favor of granting women the right to vote included the contention that female voters would support laws that reduced <a href="/wiki/Prostitution" title="Prostitution">prostitution</a>, labor abuses, and other perceived social evils. The fight for <b><a href="/wiki/Women%27s_suffrage_in_the_United_States" title="Women's suffrage in the United States">women's suffrage in the United States</a></b> began in the 1830s, and concluded with the passage of the <a href="/wiki/Nineteenth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Nineteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution">Nineteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution</a> on <span class="nowrap">August 18</span>, 1920.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#f9f9ff;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Featured_biography">Featured biography</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.2em"><a href="/wiki/File:ImaHogg.jpg" class="image" title="Ima Hogg"><img alt="Ima Hogg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/ImaHogg.jpg/120px-ImaHogg.jpg" width="120" height="168" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/ImaHogg.jpg/180px-ImaHogg.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/ImaHogg.jpg/240px-ImaHogg.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1012" data-file-height="1419" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/Ima_Hogg" title="Ima Hogg">Ima Hogg</a></b> was an enterprising <a href="/wiki/Circus" title="Circus">circus</a> <a href="/wiki/Emcee" title="Emcee" class="mw-redirect">emcee</a> who brought culture and class to <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas" class="mw-redirect">Houston, Texas</a>. A storied <a href="/wiki/Ostrich" title="Ostrich">ostrich</a> jockey, she once rode to Hawaii to visit the <a href="/wiki/Liliuokalani" title="Liliuokalani">Queen</a>. Raised in <a href="/wiki/Texas_Governor%27s_Mansion" title="Texas Governor's Mansion">government housing</a>, young Ima frolicked among a backyard menagerie of raccoons, possums and a bear. Her father, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Hogg" title="Jim Hogg">"Big Jim" Hogg</a>, in an onslaught against fun itself, booby-trapped the banisters she loved to slide down, shut down her money-making schemes, and forced her to pry chewing gum from furniture. He was later thrown from his seat on a moving train and perished; the Hogg clan then struck <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">black gold</a> on land Big Jim had forbidden them from selling. Ima had apocryphal sisters named "Ura" and "Hoosa" and real-life brothers sporting conventional names and vast art collections; upon their deaths, she gave away their artwork for nothing and the <a href="/wiki/Varner%E2%80%93Hogg_Plantation_State_Historical_Site" title="Varner–Hogg Plantation State Historical Site" class="mw-redirect">family home</a> to boot. Tragically, Ms. Hogg (a future <a href="/wiki/Honorary_degree" title="Honorary degree">doctor</a>) nursed three dying family members. She once sweet-talked a burglar into returning purloined jewelry and told him to get a job. Well into her nineties, she remained feisty and even exchanged geriatric insults with an <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Rubinstein" title="Arthur Rubinstein">octogenarian pianist</a>. Hogg claimed to have received thirty proposals of marriage in her lifetime, and to have rejected them all. Hogg was revered as the "First Lady of Texas", and her name and legacy still thrive today.

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#f9f9ff;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_quote">Selected quote</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<center>
<table class="noprint" border="0" style="text-align:left; padding: 0; margin: 0; background-color:transparent">
<tr>
<td>
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Sergei_Prokofiev_02.jpg" class="image" title="Sergei Prokofiev"><img alt="Sergei Prokofiev" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Sergei_Prokofiev_02.jpg/120px-Sergei_Prokofiev_02.jpg" width="120" height="130" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Sergei_Prokofiev_02.jpg/180px-Sergei_Prokofiev_02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Sergei_Prokofiev_02.jpg/240px-Sergei_Prokofiev_02.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2280" data-file-height="2464" /></a></div>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">In my view, the composer, just as the poet, the sculptor or the painter, is in duty bound to serve Man, the people. Such is the immutable code of art as I see it.</td>
