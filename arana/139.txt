<p><b>He Zizhen</b> or <b>Ho Tzu-chen</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">贺子珍</span>; September 1909 – April 19, 1984) was the third wife of <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a> from May 1930 to 1937.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=He_Zizhen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1928_Mao_and_third_wife_He_Jijen.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/1928_Mao_and_third_wife_He_Jijen.jpg/240px-1928_Mao_and_third_wife_He_Jijen.jpg" width="240" height="171" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/1928_Mao_and_third_wife_He_Jijen.jpg/360px-1928_Mao_and_third_wife_He_Jijen.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a8/1928_Mao_and_third_wife_He_Jijen.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="285" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1928_Mao_and_third_wife_He_Jijen.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Mao with his third wife, He Zizhen, in 1928</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>He Zizhen was born in Yunshan (云山, now <a href="/wiki/Yongxin_County" title="Yongxin County">Yongxin County</a>), <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a>, during <a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty" class="mw-redirect">Qing Dynasty</a> China and joined the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Youth_League" title="Communist Youth League" class="mw-redirect">Communist Youth League</a> in 1925. She graduated from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yongxin_Girls%27_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yongxin Girls' School (page does not exist)">Yongxin Girls' School</a> and joined the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> in 1926.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Revolutionary_Life">Revolutionary Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=He_Zizhen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Revolutionary Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He Zizhen was introduced to Mao Zedong at Jinggangshan by <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Wencai" title="Yuan Wencai">Yuan Wencai</a>, a classmate of her elder brother, in the spring of 1928. An expert in <a href="/wiki/Guerrilla_warfare" title="Guerrilla warfare">guerrilla warfare</a> and a capable fighter, He Zizhen was also an excellent shooter who earned the nickname of "Two-Gunned Girl General."<sup id="cite_ref-Karl37ff_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Karl37ff-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> When they married, Mao did not divorce <a href="/wiki/Yang_Kaihui" title="Yang Kaihui">Yang Kaihui</a> and Yang was not arrested yet.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>He Zizhen had three daughters and three sons with <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>, but except for their daughter, <a href="/wiki/Li_Min_(politician)" title="Li Min (politician)">Li Min</a>, all of them died young or were separated from the family. Their eldest daughter, who was left to a local family in <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>, was found and recognized by He Zizhen's brother in 1973, but never had the chance to meet Mao or He.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Two English researchers who retraced the entire Long March in 2002–2003, located a woman whom they believe might be a missing child left in the care of others by Mao and He in 1935.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-unbound_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unbound-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Jocelyn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ed Jocelyn (page does not exist)">Ed Jocelyn</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_McEwen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Andrew McEwen (page does not exist)">Andrew McEwen</a> are waiting for a response from family for DNA samples.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Writing_better_articles#Tone" title="Wikipedia:Writing better articles"><span title="The tone or style of material in the vicinity of this tag may not be appropriate for Wikipedia. (November 2014)">tone</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1937, she travelled to the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> to treat a wound sustained earlier in battle, later attending the Moscow East University.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>While He Zizhen was in Russia Mao met and married his fourth wife, <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Qing" title="Jiang Qing">Jiang Qing</a>. Upon her return to China in 1947 she found she could not hope to fulfil any sort of political role in Beijing. Although, she later became the chair of Zhejiang Province Women's Union. In 1984, He Zizhen died alone in Shanghai.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=He_Zizhen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, a memorial hall was opened in <a href="/wiki/Yongxin_County" title="Yongxin County">Yongxin</a> for He Zizhen with her daughter, <a href="/wiki/Li_Min_(politician)" title="Li Min (politician)">Li Min</a>, present as a guest.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
