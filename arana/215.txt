			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Portal Software</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><dl>
<dd><i>See also: Wikipedia's <a href="/wiki/Portal:Software" title="Portal:Software">Software portal</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Category:Portal_software" title="Category:Portal software">Category:Portal software</a></i></dd>
</dl>
<p><b>Portal Software</b> was founded in 1985 as Portal Information Network, one of the first <a href="/wiki/Internet_Service_Provider" title="Internet Service Provider" class="mw-redirect">ISPs</a> in the San Francisco Bay Area. It was founded by John Little. The company offered its own interface through modem access that featured Internet email. Towards the end of the 1980s, the company offered <a href="/wiki/FTP" title="FTP" class="mw-redirect">FTP</a>.</p>
<p>During this time, the company developed its own account management software. In 1992, John Little decided to focus on developing Portal's internal software for other ISPs, which he saw as a fast evolving market. Their ISP business was shut down and the accounts sold to Sprint. The company was renamed Portal Software in 1993 and Dave Labuda joined the new company as co-founder. Little and Labuda developed a scalable and flexible real-time enterprise software architecture, which they applied to the management of customers and revenue for internet and telecom service providers.</p>
<p>Portal Software developed a <a href="/wiki/Invoice" title="Invoice">billing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Telecommunications_rating" title="Telecommunications rating">revenue</a> software suite (Portal Infranet) primarily targeted at <a href="/wiki/Telecommunications" title="Telecommunications" class="mw-redirect">telecommunications</a> companies and ISPs. It was one of the largest companies in its business. Customers of Portal Software included <a href="/wiki/PSINet" title="PSINet">PSINet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Time_Warner" title="Time Warner">AOL Time Warner</a>, <a href="/wiki/China_Mobile" title="China Mobile">China Mobile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Telekom" title="Deutsche Telekom">Deutsche Telekom</a>, <a href="/wiki/France_T%C3%A9l%C3%A9com" title="France Télécom" class="mw-redirect">France Télécom</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=IG_Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="IG Brazil (page does not exist)">iG Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juno_Online_Services" title="Juno Online Services">Juno Online Services</a>, <a href="/wiki/KPN" title="KPN">KPN</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orange_SA" title="Orange SA" class="mw-redirect">Orange UK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">SIRIUS Satellite Radio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sprint_Canada" title="Sprint Canada">Sprint Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telef%C3%B3nica" title="Telefónica">Telefónica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telenor" title="Telenor">Telenor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telstra" title="Telstra">Telstra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telecom_Italia_Mobile" title="Telecom Italia Mobile" class="mw-redirect">TIM</a>, <a href="/wiki/US_Cellular" title="US Cellular" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Cellular</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vodafone" title="Vodafone">Vodafone</a>, <a href="/wiki/SaskTel" title="SaskTel">SaskTel</a> and <a href="/wiki/XM_Satellite_Radio" title="XM Satellite Radio">XM Satellite Radio</a>. In order to address the telecommunications market, Portal software acquired the <a href="/wiki/InteGrate" title="InteGrate">InteGrate</a> software from Solution42, a German company which had a history in high-performance telecommunications <a href="/wiki/Telecommunications_rating" title="Telecommunications rating">Rating</a>. This allowed a realistic performance of rating telephony usage events, something that was not feasible with the 'real-time' rating engine they had developed in-house.</p>
<p>Portal Software was bought by <a href="/wiki/Oracle_Corporation" title="Oracle Corporation">Oracle Corporation</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> in 2006 for an estimated $220 Million.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Portal Software is now a business unit of Oracle. Like other acquisition software, Portal Software will be integrated with the core products of Oracle such as Siebel (CRM), <a href="/wiki/PeopleSoft" title="PeopleSoft">PeopleSoft</a> (ERP/CRM), JD Edwards (ERP). At the time of the acquisition Bhaskar Gorti was the company CEO, Dave Labuda CTO, Bruce Grainger Vice President of Americas, Tim Porter Vice President of EMEA.</p>
