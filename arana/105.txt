<p><b>Anheuser-Busch InBev SA/NV</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈɑnɦɔi̯zər ˈbuʃ ˈɪmbɛf]</a></span>, abbreviated as <b>AB InBev</b>) is a multinational beverage and brewing company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Leuven" title="Leuven">Leuven</a>, Belgium. It is the world's largest brewer and has a 25 <a href="/wiki/Percent" title="Percent" class="mw-redirect">percent</a> global market share. AB InBev was formed through successive mergers of three international brewing groups: <a href="/wiki/Interbrew" title="Interbrew">Interbrew</a> from Belgium, <a href="/wiki/AmBev" title="AmBev">AmBev</a> from Brazil, and <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch" title="Anheuser-Busch">Anheuser-Busch</a> from the United States. It has 16 brands that individually generate over 1 billion USD per year in revenue out of a portfolio of more than 200 brands (2014).<sup id="cite_ref-CI2014_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CI2014-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> This portfolio includes global brands <a href="/wiki/Budweiser_(Anheuser-Busch)" title="Budweiser (Anheuser-Busch)" class="mw-redirect">Budweiser</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corona_(beer)" title="Corona (beer)">Corona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stella_Artois" title="Stella Artois">Stella Artois</a>, international brands <a href="/wiki/Beck%27s" title="Beck's" class="mw-redirect">Beck's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hoegaarden_Brewery" title="Hoegaarden Brewery">Hoegaarden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leffe" title="Leffe">Leffe</a> and local brands such as <a href="/wiki/Bud_Light" title="Bud Light" class="mw-redirect">Bud Light</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skol" title="Skol">Skol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brahma_beer" title="Brahma beer">Brahma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Companhia_Antarctica_Paulista" title="Companhia Antarctica Paulista">Antarctica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cerveza_Quilmes" title="Cerveza Quilmes">Quilmes</a>, Victoria, <a href="/wiki/Modelo_Especial" title="Modelo Especial" class="mw-redirect">Modelo Especial</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michelob_Ultra" title="Michelob Ultra" class="mw-redirect">Michelob Ultra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harbin_Brewery" title="Harbin Brewery">Harbin</a>, Sedrin, Klinskoye, Sibirskaya Korona, Chernigivske and <a href="/wiki/Jupiler" title="Jupiler">Jupiler</a>. Total revenue for all 200 AB InBev brands in 2014 was over 47 billion USD.<sup id="cite_ref-CI2014_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CI2014-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The company employs over 155,000 people in 25 countries. After the integration in 2008 of Anheuser-Busch, based in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Louis,_Missouri" title="Saint Louis, Missouri" class="mw-redirect">Saint Louis, Missouri</a>, the company started reporting its financial results in US dollars.</p>
<p>AB InBev has a primary listing on <a href="/wiki/Euronext_Brussels" title="Euronext Brussels" class="mw-redirect">Euronext Brussels</a> and is a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/Euro_Stoxx_50" title="Euro Stoxx 50">Euro Stoxx 50</a> index. It has a secondary listing on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>. Dividends per share increased steadily from 0.35 USD in 2008 to 3.00 USD for 2014. The payout ratio increased from 26.3% to 64.8% during the same period.<sup id="cite_ref-key_figures_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-key_figures-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> AB InBev's market share, measured as volume of beverages sold, on a country-by-country basis, was published by the company for 2014, showing it has the highest market share and market share over 40% in seven of its 10 top markets—including Belgium, United States, Canada, Mexico, Brazil, Argentina and South Korea.<sup id="cite_ref-CI2014_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CI2014-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> AB InBev operates 140 plants in its 10 top markets.</p>
<p>In October 2015, Anheuser-Busch InBev announced a successful all-cash bid to acquire multinational competitor <a href="/wiki/SABMiller" title="SABMiller">SABMiller</a> for £69 billion ($104 billion).<sup id="cite_ref-inbevmerger_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inbevmerger-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The plan must yet pass regulatory and anti-trust scrutiny.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_InBev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>AB InBev was formed following the acquisition of American brewer <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch" title="Anheuser-Busch">Anheuser-Busch</a> by Belgian-Brazilian brewer <a href="/wiki/InBev" title="InBev">InBev</a>, which is a merger of <a href="/wiki/AmBev" title="AmBev">AmBev</a> and <a href="/wiki/Interbrew" title="Interbrew">Interbrew</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Interbrew" title="Interbrew">Interbrew</a></b> was formed in 1987 from a merger of the two largest breweries in Belgium: <a href="/wiki/Stella_Artois#History" title="Stella Artois">Artois</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piedboeuf_Brewery" title="Piedboeuf Brewery">Piedboeuf</a>. The Artois brewery, previously known as <i>Den Hoorn</i>, was already established by 1466. In 1995, Interbrew purchased <a href="/wiki/Labatt_Brewing_Company" title="Labatt Brewing Company">Labatt Brewing Company</a> (founded 1847), the largest brewer in Canada. In 2002 it acquired <a href="/wiki/Beck%27s_Beer" title="Beck's Beer" class="mw-redirect">Beck's</a> (founded 1873), the maker of the world's top selling German beer.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/AmBev" title="AmBev">AmBev</a></b> (short for <i>Companhia de Bebidas das Américas</i>, or "Beverages Company of the Americas") was created in 1999 with the merger of the two biggest Brazilian brewers, <a href="/wiki/Companhia_Antarctica_Paulista" title="Companhia Antarctica Paulista">Antarctica</a> (founded in 1880) and <a href="/wiki/Brahma_beer" title="Brahma beer">Brahma</a> (founded in 1886).</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch" title="Anheuser-Busch">Anheuser-Busch</a></b> was established in 1860 in <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, United States, as <i>Anheuser &amp; Co</i>.</li>
</ul>
<p>In 2004, Interbrew and AmBev <a href="/wiki/Mergers_and_acquisitions" title="Mergers and acquisitions">merged</a>, creating the world's largest brewer, InBev.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The deal was valued at $11.5 billion and combined the 3rd largest (Interbrew) and 5th largest (Ambev) brewers into the world's No.1 beermaker. The deal consolidated the top brands from Belgium, Canada, Germany and Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, InBev acquired the Fujian Sedrin brewery in China, making InBev the No. 3 brewer in China (the world's largest beer market). In 2007, subsidiary Labatt acquired <a href="/wiki/Lakeport_Brewing_Company" title="Lakeport Brewing Company">Lakeport</a> in Canada, and InBev increased its shareholding in <a href="/wiki/Cerveza_Quilmes#QUINSA" title="Cerveza Quilmes">QUINSA</a>, strengthening the company’s foothold in Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Paraguay and Uruguay.</p>
<p>In 2008, the merger of InBev and Anheuser-Busch was completed, creating Anheuser–Busch InBev, expanding on InBev's previous status as the world's largest brewer, now creating one of the top five consumer products companies in the world. Under the terms of the merger agreement, all shares of Anheuser-Busch were acquired for 70 USD per share in cash, for an aggregate of 52 billion USD.<sup id="cite_ref-ab-inbev.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ab-inbev.com-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Interbrew">Interbrew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_InBev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Interbrew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Interbrew" title="Interbrew">Interbrew</a></div>
<p>After the merger in 1987, Interbrew acquired a number of local breweries in Belgium. By 1991, a second phase of targeted external growth began outside Belgium. The first transaction in this phase took place in Hungary, followed in 1995 by the acquisition of Labatt, in Canada, and then in 1999 by a joint venture with Sun in Russia.</p>
<p>In 2000, Interbrew acquired Bass and Whitbread in the UK, and in 2001 the company established itself in Germany, with the acquisition of Diebels. This was followed by the acquisition of Beck’s &amp; Co., the Gilde Group and Spaten. Interbrew operated as a family-owned business until December 2000. At this point it organized an Initial Public Offering, becoming a publicly owned company trading on the Euronext stock exchange (Brussels, Belgium).</p>
<p>In 2002, Interbrew strengthened its position in China, by acquiring stakes in the K.K. Brewery and the Zhujiang Brewery.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AmBev">AmBev</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_InBev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: AmBev">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/AmBev" title="AmBev">AmBev</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/AmBev" title="AmBev">AmBev</a> is a Brazilian beverages company formed by a merger in 1999 between the Brahma and Antarctica breweries. It has a dominant position in South America and the Caribbean region.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The subsidiary is listed on <a href="/wiki/BM%26F_Bovespa" title="BM&amp;F Bovespa">BM&amp;F Bovespa</a>, the São Paulo stock exchange, and on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anheuser-Busch">Anheuser-Busch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_InBev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Anheuser-Busch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch" title="Anheuser-Busch">Anheuser-Busch</a></div>
<p>Anheuser-Busch is the largest brewing company in the United States. It was the world's largest brewing company based on <a href="/wiki/Revenue" title="Revenue">revenue</a>, but third in brewing volume, before the acquisition by <a href="/wiki/InBev" title="InBev">InBev</a> announced 13 July 2008. The division operated 12 breweries in the United States and 17 others overseas.</p>
<p>Anheuser-Busch's best known beers included brands such as <a href="/wiki/Budweiser_(Anheuser-Busch)" title="Budweiser (Anheuser-Busch)" class="mw-redirect">Budweiser</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Busch_Beer" title="Busch Beer" class="mw-redirect">Busch</a> (originally known as Busch Bavarian Beer) and <a href="/wiki/Michelob" title="Michelob" class="mw-redirect">Michelob</a> families, and <a href="/wiki/Natural_Light" title="Natural Light">Natural Light</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_Ice" title="Natural Ice" class="mw-redirect">Ice</a>. The company also produced a number of smaller-volume and specialty beers, nonalcoholic brews, <a href="/wiki/Malt_liquor" title="Malt liquor">malt liquors</a> (King Cobra and the Hurricane family), and flavored <a href="/wiki/Malt_beverage" title="Malt beverage">malt beverages</a> (e.g., the Bacardi Silver family and <a href="/wiki/Tequiza" title="Tequiza" class="mw-redirect">Tequiza</a>).</p>
<p>Anheuser-Busch was also one of the largest theme park operators in the United States with ten parks throughout the United States. In October 2009, AB InBev announced the sale of its <a href="/wiki/Busch_Entertainment" title="Busch Entertainment" class="mw-redirect">Busch Entertainment</a> theme park division to <a href="/wiki/The_Blackstone_Group" title="The Blackstone Group">The Blackstone Group</a> for $2.7 billion. The company had been investigating a sale of Busch Entertainment since the merger with Inbev.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="InBev">InBev</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_InBev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: InBev">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/InBev" title="InBev">InBev</a></div>
<p>InBev was the second largest brewery company in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> While its core business is beer, the company also had a strong presence in the <a href="/wiki/Soft_drink" title="Soft drink">soft drink</a> market in Latin America. It employed about 86,000 people and was headquartered in Leuven, Belgium, where Anheuser–Busch InBev will now be based.</p>
<p>Before the merger with AmBev, Interbrew was the third largest brewing company in the world by volume, <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch" title="Anheuser-Busch">Anheuser-Busch</a> was the largest, followed by <a href="/wiki/SABMiller" title="SABMiller">SABMiller</a> in second place. <a href="/wiki/Heineken_International" title="Heineken International">Heineken International</a> was in fourth place and AmBev was the world's fifth largest brewer.</p>
<p>InBev employed close to 89,000 people, running operations in over 30 countries across the Americas, Europe and Asia Pacific. In 2007, InBev realized 14.4 billion euro of revenue.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Grupo_Modelo">Grupo Modelo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_InBev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Grupo Modelo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The company owns <a href="/wiki/Grupo_Modelo" title="Grupo Modelo">Grupo Modelo</a>, Mexico's leading brewer and owner of the <a href="/wiki/Corona_(beer)" title="Corona (beer)">Corona</a> brand as of 4 June 2013. This transaction was valued at 20.1 billion USD. To satisfy US anti-trust demands, Grupo Modelo sold its US business, including US brand rights and the Piedras Negras Brewery in Mexico, for approx. 4.75 billion USD to <a href="/wiki/Constellation_Brands" title="Constellation Brands">Constellation Brands</a>, a competitor of AB Inbev in some beverage sectors.<sup id="cite_ref-FR2014_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FR2014-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Additionally, the group has a 51% controlling stock on <a href="/wiki/Cerveceria_Nacional_Dominicana" title="Cerveceria Nacional Dominicana" class="mw-redirect">Cerveceria Nacional Dominicana</a> through the <a href="/wiki/AmBev" title="AmBev">AmBev</a> division.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oriental_Brewery">Oriental Brewery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_InBev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Oriental Brewery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 1, 2014, AB Inbev completed the re-acquisition of the <a href="/wiki/Oriental_Brewery" title="Oriental Brewery">Oriental Brewery</a> (OB), which it had sold in July 2009. OB is the largest brewer in South Korea. Its <i>CASS</i> brand is the best-selling beer in South Korea. All beers produced by OB are brewed using rice.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Analytics_Lab">Bud Analytics Lab</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_InBev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Bud Analytics Lab">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, AB Inbev opened its Bud Analytics Lab in <a href="/wiki/Research_Park,_University_of_Illinois_at_Urbana-Champaign" title="Research Park, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign">Research Park, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign</a>, which develops data research and innovation to solve problems ranging from assortment optimization, social media, and market trends to large scale data initiatives.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SABMiller">SABMiller</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_InBev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: SABMiller">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/SABMiller" title="SABMiller">SABMiller</a></div>
<p>On October 13, 2015, Anheuser-Busch InBev made a bid of $106 billion or £70 billion (£44 per share) for its largest rival, <a href="/wiki/SABMiller" title="SABMiller">SABMiller</a>, which if approved would give the company almost 30 percent of the world's beer market.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The company had previously offered £38, £40, £42.15, £43.50 per share respectively, but each of these had been turned down.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>SABMiller accepted the bid <a href="/wiki/Agreement_in_principle" title="Agreement in principle">in principle</a>, but consummation of the deal awaits <a href="/wiki/Antitrust" title="Antitrust" class="mw-redirect">antitrust</a> approval by regulatory agencies in the U.S. and possibly elsewhere. Industry leader AB InBev, based in Belgium, accounted for more than a fifth of global beer sales and 45% of the U.S. market in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-MW20150727_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MW20150727-20"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> AB InBev's brands include Budweiser, Corona, Stella Artois and Becks.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>SABMiller, whose brands include <a href="/wiki/Foster%27s_Lager" title="Foster's Lager">Fosters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pilsner_Urquell_Brewery" title="Pilsner Urquell Brewery">Pilsner Urquell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grolsch_Brewery" title="Grolsch Brewery">Grolsch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miller_Brewing_Company" title="Miller Brewing Company">Miller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coors_Brewing_Company" title="Coors Brewing Company">Coors</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Peroni_Brewery" title="Peroni Brewery">Peroni</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-snapshot_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snapshot-23"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> is the world's second-largest brewer with a global market share of nearly 10 percent. In 2014, its 58% interest in the <a href="/wiki/MillerCoors" title="MillerCoors">MillerCoors</a> joint venture represented the second-biggest share of the U.S. market at 15%.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MW20150727_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MW20150727-20"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> If the deal were to go through without requiring divestiture of some brands (seen as unlikely), the combined company would control 30% of the global market and have a dominating 60% U.S. market share.</p>
