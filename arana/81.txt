<p><br />
Year <b>1818</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCCXVIII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Common year starting on Thursday">common year starting on Thursday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Common year starting on Tuesday">common year starting on Tuesday</a> of the 12-day slower <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1818&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1818&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_1" title="January 1">January 1</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Koregaon" title="Battle of Koregaon">Battle of Koregaon</a>: Troops of the <a href="/wiki/British_East_India_Company" title="British East India Company" class="mw-redirect">British East India Company</a> score a decisive victory over the <a href="/wiki/Maratha_Empire" title="Maratha Empire">Maratha Empire</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_3" title="January 3">January 3</a> (21:52 UTC) – <a href="/wiki/Venus" title="Venus">Venus</a> <a href="/wiki/Occultation" title="Occultation">occults</a> <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a>. It is the last occultation of one planet by another before <a href="/wiki/November_22" title="November 22">November 22</a>, <a href="/wiki/2065" title="2065">2065</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_6" title="January 6">January 6</a> – Treaty of Mandeswar brings an end to the <a href="/wiki/Third_Anglo-Maratha_War" title="Third Anglo-Maratha War">Third Anglo-Maratha War</a>, ending the dominance of <a href="/wiki/Maratha" title="Maratha">Marathas</a>, and enhancing the power of the <a href="/wiki/British_East_India_Company" title="British East India Company" class="mw-redirect">British East India Company</a>, which controls territory occupied by 180 million <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_India" title="Demographics of India">Indians</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_11" title="January 11">January 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Percy_Bysshe_Shelley" title="Percy Bysshe Shelley">Percy Bysshe Shelley</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Ozymandias" title="Ozymandias">Ozymandias</a>" is published pseudonymously in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_5" title="February 5">February 5</a> – Upon his death, King <a href="/wiki/Charles_XIII_of_Sweden" title="Charles XIII of Sweden">Charles XIII of Sweden</a> (Charles II of Norway) is succeeded on both thrones by his adoptive son <a href="/wiki/Charles_XIV_John_of_Sweden" title="Charles XIV John of Sweden">Charles XIV/III John</a>, starting the Royal <a href="/wiki/House_of_Bernadotte" title="House of Bernadotte">House of Bernadotte</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_12" title="February 12">February 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Chilean Declaration of Independence">proclaims its independence</a> from Spain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_11" title="March 11">March 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mary_Shelley" title="Mary Shelley">Mary Shelley</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Frankenstein" title="Frankenstein">Frankenstein</a></i> is published anonymously in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_15" title="March 15">March 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Seminole_Wars#First_Seminole_War" title="Seminole Wars">First Seminole War</a>: General <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Jackson" title="Andrew Jackson">Andrew Jackson</a> and his American army invade <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_22" title="March 22">March 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Easter_Day" title="Easter Day" class="mw-redirect">Easter Day</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_Christianity" title="Western Christianity">Western Christianity</a> falls on its earliest possible date. In Western Christianity, it will not occur on this date again until <a href="/wiki/2285" title="2285" class="mw-redirect">2285</a>.</li>
<li>April – <a href="/wiki/John_Ross_(Arctic_explorer)" title="John Ross (Arctic explorer)">John Ross</a> sets sail in search of the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Passage" title="Northwest Passage">Northwest Passage</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_1" title="April 1">April 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Seminole_Wars#First_Seminole_War" title="Seminole Wars">First Seminole War</a>: Battle of <a href="/wiki/Miccosukee,_Florida" title="Miccosukee, Florida">Miccosukee, Florida</a>: General <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Jackson" title="Andrew Jackson">Andrew Jackson</a> defeats chief Kinhagee.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_4" title="April 4">April 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> adopts the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_States" title="Flag of the United States">flag of the United States</a> as having thirteen red and white stripes and one star for each state (twenty) with additional stars to be added whenever a new state is added to the Union.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_5" title="April 5">April 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Maip%C3%BA" title="Battle of Maipú">Battle of Maipú</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Chilean_War_of_Independence" title="Chilean War of Independence">Chilean War of Independence</a>: Patriot rebels led by <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_de_San_Mart%C3%ADn" title="José de San Martín">José de San Martín</a> decisively defeat the Spanish <a href="/wiki/Royalist_(Spanish_American_Independence)" title="Royalist (Spanish American Independence)">Royalists</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_7" title="April 7">April 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Brooks_Brothers" title="Brooks Brothers">Brooks Brothers</a>, the oldest men's clothier in the United States, opens its first store on the northeast corner of Catherine and Cherry Streets in New York City, where the later <a href="/wiki/South_Street_Seaport" title="South Street Seaport">South Street Seaport</a> stands.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_14" title="April 14">April 14</a>–<a href="/wiki/August_9" title="August 9">August 9</a> – United States Survey of the Coast operations suspended.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_11" title="May 11">May 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charles_XIV_of_Sweden" title="Charles XIV of Sweden" class="mw-redirect">Charles XIV</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sweden%E2%80%93Norway" title="Sweden–Norway" class="mw-redirect">Sweden–Norway</a> is crowned king of Sweden.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/The_Westmorland_Gazette" title="The Westmorland Gazette">The Westmorland Gazette</a></i> is first published at <a href="/wiki/Kendal" title="Kendal">Kendal</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lake_District" title="Lake District">Lake District</a> of England; in July, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_De_Quincey" title="Thomas De Quincey">Thomas De Quincey</a> will begin a 16-month term as editor.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1818&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: July–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<li>September – Sir <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Raffles" title="Stamford Raffles">Stamford Raffles</a> sets out to visit <a href="/wiki/Francis_Rawdon-Hastings,_1st_Marquess_of_Hastings" title="Francis Rawdon-Hastings, 1st Marquess of Hastings">Lord Hastings</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_India" title="Governor-General of India">Governor-General of India</a>, to gain his approval to establish a trading station at the southern tip of the <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">Malay Peninsula</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>).</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/October_20" title="October 20">October 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_1818" title="Treaty of 1818">Treaty</a> between the U.S. and the United Kingdom establishes the boundary between the U.S. and <a href="/wiki/British_North_America" title="British North America">British North America</a> as the <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_the_Woods" title="Lake of the Woods">Lake of the Woods</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>, also creating the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Angle" title="Northwest Angle">Northwest Angle</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_11" title="November 11">November 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Ying_Wa_College" title="Ying Wa College">Anglo-Chinese College</a> is founded by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Morrison_(missionary)" title="Robert Morrison (missionary)">Robert Morrison</a> in <a href="/wiki/Malacca" title="Malacca">Malacca</a> (later renamed Ying Wa College).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_16" title="November 16">November 16</a> – The Saint Louis Academy, which later becomes <a href="/wiki/Saint_Louis_University" title="Saint Louis University">Saint Louis University</a>, is founded by Reverend <a href="/wiki/Louis_William_Valentine_Dubourg" title="Louis William Valentine Dubourg">Louis William Valentine Dubourg</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_3" title="December 3">December 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a> is admitted as the 21st <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_24" title="December 24">December 24</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Christmas_carol" title="Christmas carol">Christmas carol</a> "<a href="/wiki/Silent_Night" title="Silent Night">Silent Night</a>" (<i>Stille Nacht</i>) with words by the priest <a href="/wiki/Josef_Mohr" title="Josef Mohr" class="mw-redirect">Josef Mohr</a> set to music by organist <a href="/wiki/Franz_Xaver_Gruber" title="Franz Xaver Gruber">Franz Xaver Gruber</a> is first performed at St. Nikolaus parish church in <a href="/wiki/Oberndorf_bei_Salzburg" title="Oberndorf bei Salzburg">Oberndorf bei Salzburg</a> in Austria.</li>
</ul>
