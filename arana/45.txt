<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Tomb_of_Bibi_Jawindi" title="Tomb of Bibi Jawindi">Tomb of Bibi Jawindi</a></b> is a mausoleum built in 1493 by an Iranian prince, Dilshad, for Bibi Jawindi, who was the great-granddaughter of the <a href="/wiki/Sufi" title="Sufi" class="mw-redirect">Sufi</a> saint <a href="/wiki/Jahaniyan_Jahangasht" title="Jahaniyan Jahangasht">Jahaniyan Jahangasht</a>. </tr>
<tr>
<td><a href="/wiki/File:Chamberlin_trimetric_projection_SW.jpg" class="image" title="Chamberlin trimetric projection"><img alt="Chamberlin trimetric projection" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Chamberlin_trimetric_projection_SW.jpg/320px-Chamberlin_trimetric_projection_SW.jpg" width="320" height="355" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Chamberlin_trimetric_projection_SW.jpg/480px-Chamberlin_trimetric_projection_SW.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Chamberlin_trimetric_projection_SW.jpg/640px-Chamberlin_trimetric_projection_SW.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2060" data-file-height="2284" /></a></td>
<td>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Chamberlin_trimetric_projection" title="Chamberlin trimetric projection">Chamberlin trimetric projection</a></b> is a <a href="/wiki/Map_projection" title="Map projection">map projection</a> where three points are fixed on the <a href="/wiki/Globe" title="Globe">globe</a> and the points on the <a href="/wiki/Sphere" title="Sphere">sphere</a> are mapped onto a plane by <a href="/wiki/Triangulation" title="Triangulation">triangulation</a>. It was developed in 1946 by Wellman Chamberlin for the <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Society" title="National Geographic Society">National Geographic Society</a>. It is neither <a href="/wiki/Map_projection#Conformal" title="Map projection">conformal</a> nor <a href="/wiki/Map_projection#Equal-area" title="Map projection">equal-area</a>, but rather attempts to minimize distortion of distances everywhere with the side-effect of balancing between areal equivalence and conformality.</p>

<td>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Point_Cabrillo_Light" title="Point Cabrillo Light">Point Cabrillo Light</a></b> is a <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse" title="Lighthouse">lighthouse</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, between <a href="/wiki/Point_Arena" title="Point Arena" class="mw-redirect">Point Arena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cape_Mendocino" title="Cape Mendocino">Cape Mendocino</a>, just south of the community of <a href="/wiki/Caspar,_California" title="Caspar, California">Caspar</a>. It is part of the California state park system as Point Cabrillo Light Station State Historic Park. Completed in 1909, the lighthouse was manned by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> from 1939 until it was automated in 1973. Beginning in 1996, the station was restored to the state it would have been in the 1930s.</p>

<td>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/SMS_%C3%84gir" title="SMS Ägir">SMS <i>Ägir</i></a></b> was the second and final member of the <a href="/wiki/Odin-class_coastal_defense_ship" title="Odin-class coastal defense ship"><i>Odin</i> class</a> of <a href="/wiki/Coastal_defense_ship" title="Coastal defense ship" class="mw-redirect">coastal defense ships</a> built for the <a href="/wiki/German_Imperial_Navy" title="German Imperial Navy" class="mw-redirect">German Imperial Navy</a>. <i>Ägir</i> was built by the <a href="/wiki/Kaiserliche_Werft_Danzig" title="Kaiserliche Werft Danzig">Kaiserliche Werft Danzig</a> shipyard between 1893 and 1896, and named for the <a href="/wiki/%C3%86gir" title="Ægir">eponymous Norse god</a>. She was armed with a main battery of three 24-centimeter (9.4&#160;in) guns. She served in the German fleet throughout the 1890s and was rebuilt in 1901–03. She served in the VI Battle Squadron after the outbreak of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> in August 1914, but saw no action. <i>Ägir</i> was demobilized in 1915 and used as a tender thereafter. After the war, she was rebuilt as a merchant ship and served in this capacity until December 1929, when she was wrecked on the island of <a href="/wiki/Gotland" title="Gotland">Gotland</a>.</p>

<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Day_of_Pompeii" title="The Last Day of Pompeii">The Last Day of Pompeii</a></b></i> is an oil painting on canvas completed by the Russian artist <a href="/wiki/Karl_Bryullov" title="Karl Bryullov">Karl Bryullov</a> between 1830 and 1833. <p><b><a href="/wiki/Kuchipudi" title="Kuchipudi">Kuchipudi</a></b> is a <a href="/wiki/Classical_Indian_dance" title="Classical Indian dance" class="mw-redirect">Classical Indian dance</a> from <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">Andhra Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. According to legend, an orphan named Siddhendra Yogi established the Kuchipudi dance-drama tradition in the seventh century. The performance usually begins with stage rites, after which each character comes onto the stage and introduces herself with a small composition of both song and dance. The drama then begins, and the dance is typically accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Carnatic_music" title="Carnatic music">Carnatic music</a>.</p>
