			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Portal, Tarporley</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Portal</b> is a <a href="/wiki/English_country_house" title="English country house">country house</a> standing to the northeast of the village of <a href="/wiki/Tarporley" title="Tarporley">Tarporley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a>, England. It was built in 1900–05.<sup id="cite_ref-pev_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pev-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The architect was Walter E. Tower, nephew and partner of the stained glass designer and manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Charles_Eamer_Kempe" title="Charles Eamer Kempe">C.&#160;E.&#160;Kempe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It is a <a href="/wiki/Timber-framed" title="Timber-framed" class="mw-redirect">timber-framed</a> building in Domestic Revival (<a href="/wiki/Black-and-white_Revival_architecture" title="Black-and-white Revival architecture">Black-and-white Revival</a>) style. The house is recorded in the <a href="/wiki/National_Heritage_List_for_England" title="National Heritage List for England">National Heritage List for England</a> as a designated Grade&#160;II* <a href="/wiki/Listed_building#England_and_Wales" title="Listed building">listed building</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The architectural historian <a href="/wiki/Nikolaus_Pevsner" title="Nikolaus Pevsner">Nikolaus Pevsner</a> says of it: "It is a <i>tour de force</i> in accurate but scaled-up imitation of timber-framed mansions".<sup id="cite_ref-pev_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pev-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
