<td style="color:#000;">
<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px"><b><a href="/wiki/Luo_Yixiu" title="Luo Yixiu">Luo Yixiu</a></b> (1889–1910) was the first wife of the future Chinese <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communist</a> revolutionary and political leader <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>, to whom she was married from 1908 until her death. She came from a family of impoverished <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a> landowners near <a href="/wiki/Shaoshan" title="Shaoshan">Shaoshan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>, in south central China. Most of what is known about their marriage comes from Mao and appears in the 1936 book <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Over_China" title="Red Star Over China">Red Star Over China</a></i> by the reporter <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Snow" title="Edgar Snow">Edgar Snow</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Arranged_marriage" title="Arranged marriage">marriage was arranged</a> by their fathers when Luo was eighteen and Mao was just fourteen. He later said that he was unhappy with the marriage, never consummating it and refusing to live with his wife. He moved out of the village to continue his studies elsewhere, eventually becoming a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a>. Socially disgraced, Luo lived with Mao's parents for two years until she died of <a href="/wiki/Dysentery" title="Dysentery">dysentery</a>. Various biographers have suggested that this marriage affected Mao's later views, leading him to become a critic of arranged marriage and a vocal <a href="/wiki/Feminism" title="Feminism">feminist</a>. He would marry three more times, to <a href="/wiki/Yang_Kaihui" title="Yang Kaihui">Yang Kaihui</a>, <a href="/wiki/He_Zizhen" title="He Zizhen">He Zizhen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Qing" title="Jiang Qing">Jiang Qing</a>. <div id="mp-otd">
<p><b><a href="/wiki/October_20" title="October 20">October 20</a></b>: Birth of the <b><a href="/wiki/B%C3%A1b" title="Báb">Báb</a></b>, a <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_calendar#Holy_days" title="Bahá'í calendar">holy day</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith" title="Bahá'í Faith">Bahá'í Faith</a></p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 100px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Maria_Theresa_young.jpg" class="image" title="Maria Theresa of Austria"><img alt="Maria Theresa of Austria" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Maria_Theresa_young.jpg/100px-Maria_Theresa_young.jpg" width="100" height="128" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Maria_Theresa_young.jpg/150px-Maria_Theresa_young.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Maria_Theresa_young.jpg/200px-Maria_Theresa_young.jpg 2x" data-file-width="653" data-file-height="839" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Maria Theresa of Austria</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1740" title="1740">1740</a> – Under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Pragmatic_Sanction_of_1713" title="Pragmatic Sanction of 1713">Pragmatic Sanction of 1713</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/Maria_Theresa" title="Maria Theresa">Maria Theresa</a></b> <i>(pictured)</i> assumed the throne of the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg Monarchy in Austria</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1818" title="1818">1818</a> – The United Kingdom and the United States signed the <b><a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_1818" title="Treaty of 1818">Treaty of 1818</a></b>, which settled the <a href="/wiki/Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border" title="Canada–United States border">Canada–United States border</a> on the <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel</a> between the Pacific Ocean and <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_the_Woods" title="Lake of the Woods">Lake of the Woods</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1944" title="1944">1944</a> – <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>: Fulfilling <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur%27s_escape_from_the_Philippines" title="Douglas MacArthur's escape from the Philippines">a promise</a> he made two years previous, General <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte" title="Battle of Leyte">landed</a></b> on <a href="/wiki/Leyte" title="Leyte">Leyte</a> to begin the recapture of the entire Philippine Archipelago.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1961" title="1961">1961</a> – The Soviet Union performed the first armed test of a <b><a href="/wiki/Submarine-launched_ballistic_missile" title="Submarine-launched ballistic missile">submarine-launched ballistic missile</a></b>, launching an <a href="/wiki/R-13_(missile)" title="R-13 (missile)">R-13</a> from a <a href="/wiki/Golf_class_submarine" title="Golf class submarine" class="mw-redirect">Golf class submarine</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1982" title="1982">1982</a> – During a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> match between <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">FC Spartak Moscow</a> and <a href="/wiki/HFC_Haarlem" title="HFC Haarlem">HFC Haarlem</a>, a large number of attendees tried to leave the <a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_Stadium" title="Luzhniki Stadium">Grand Sports Arena of the Central Lenin Stadium</a> at the same time, resulting in <b><a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_disaster" title="Luzhniki disaster">a stampede</a></b> that caused 66 deaths.</li>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Muammar_Gaddafi" title="Muammar Gaddafi">Muammar Gaddafi</a></b> (c. 1942&#160;– 2011) was a <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libyan</a> revolutionary and politician. Taking power in a coup d'etat, he ruled as Revolutionary Chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Libyan_Arab_Republic" title="Libyan Arab Republic" class="mw-redirect">Libyan Arab Republic</a> from 1969 to 1977 and then as the "<a href="/wiki/Brotherly_Leader_and_Guide_of_the_Revolution" title="Brotherly Leader and Guide of the Revolution">Brotherly Leader</a>" of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Socialist_People%27s_Libyan_Arab_Jamahiriya" title="Great Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya" class="mw-redirect">Great Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya</a> from 1977 to 2011, when he was ousted in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Libyan_Civil_War" title="2011 Libyan Civil War" class="mw-redirect">Libyan Civil War</a>. Initially developing his own variant of <a href="/wiki/Arab_nationalism" title="Arab nationalism">Arab nationalism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arab_socialism" title="Arab socialism">Arab socialism</a> known as the <a href="/wiki/Third_International_Theory" title="Third International Theory">Third International Theory</a>, he later embraced <a href="/wiki/Pan-Africanism" title="Pan-Africanism">Pan-Africanism</a> and served as <a href="/wiki/Chairperson_of_the_African_Union" title="Chairperson of the African Union">Chairperson</a> of the <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> from 2009 to 2010.</p>
