<p>The <b>Chairperson of the African Union</b> is the ceremonial head of the <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> elected by the <a href="/wiki/Assembly_of_the_African_Union" title="Assembly of the African Union">Assembly of Heads of State</a> for one year.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> It rotates among the continent's five <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_the_African_Union" title="Regions of the African Union">regions</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chairperson_of_the_African_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Chairperson_of_the_Organisation_of_African_Unity" title="Chairperson of the Organisation of African Unity">Chairperson of the Organisation of African Unity</a></div>
<p>In 2002, South African President <a href="/wiki/Thabo_Mbeki" title="Thabo Mbeki">Thabo Mbeki</a> served as the inaugural chairman of the union. The post rotates annually amongst the five <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_the_African_Union" title="Regions of the African Union">geographic regions</a> of Africa; and over the years it has assumed the following order: East, North, Southern, Central and West Africa.</p>
<p>In January 2007, the assembly elected Ghanaian President <a href="/wiki/John_Kufuor" title="John Kufuor">John Kufuor</a> over Sudan's President <a href="/wiki/Omar_al-Bashir" title="Omar al-Bashir">Omar al-Bashir</a> due to the ongoing <a href="/wiki/Conflict_in_Darfur" title="Conflict in Darfur" class="mw-redirect">Conflict in Darfur</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. (July 2015)">unreliable source?</span></a></i>]</sup> The government of <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> threatened to withdraw its membership if Sudan assumed the chair. Some had suggested <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> as a compromise candidate from the East African region. By consensus, Ghana was elected instead as it was celebrating its 50th independence anniversary that year.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2010, Libyan Leader <a href="/wiki/Muammar_Gaddafi" title="Muammar Gaddafi">Muammar Gaddafi</a> unsuccessfully tried to extend his tenure by an additional year,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> saying more time was needed in order to implement his vision for a <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_Africa" title="United States of Africa">United States of Africa</a> - of which he was a strong proponent. Libya was at the time one of the largest financial supporters of the AU.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The election of Equatoguinean President <a href="/wiki/Teodoro_Obiang_Nguema_Mbasogo" title="Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo">Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo</a> in January 2011 was criticized by human rights activists as it undermined the AU's commitment to democracy.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Congolese Republic President <a href="/wiki/Denis_Sassou_Nguesso" title="Denis Sassou Nguesso">Denis Sassou Nguesso</a> and Zimbabwean President <a href="/wiki/Robert_Mugabe" title="Robert Mugabe">Robert Mugabe</a> have both led the AU and its predecessor, the <a href="/wiki/Organisation_of_African_Unity" title="Organisation of African Unity">Organisation of African Unity</a> during the terms 1986-8 and 1997-1998 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chairperson_of_the_African_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The incumbent is the ceremonial head of the AU and in this capacity, chairs the biannual summits and represents the continent in various international fora such as <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_International_Conference_on_African_Development" title="Tokyo International Conference on African Development">TICAD</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forum_on_China%E2%80%93Africa_Cooperation" title="Forum on China–Africa Cooperation">FOCAC</a>, <a href="/wiki/G8_Summit" title="G8 Summit" class="mw-redirect">G8</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_G-20_summits" title="List of G-20 summits">G20</a> summits.</p>
