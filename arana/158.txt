<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Biography_Portal"><big>The Biography Portal</big></span></h2>

<div style="float:right;margin-left:1em">
<center><a href="/wiki/Foxe%27s_Book_of_Martyrs" title="Foxe's Book of Martyrs">Foxe's Book of Martyrs</a></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/File:Tyndale-martyrdom.png" class="image" title="Foxe's Book of Martyrs"><img alt="Foxe's Book of Martyrs" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Tyndale-martyrdom.png/200px-Tyndale-martyrdom.png" width="200" height="150" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Tyndale-martyrdom.png/300px-Tyndale-martyrdom.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Tyndale-martyrdom.png/400px-Tyndale-martyrdom.png 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="479" /></a><br /></p>

<p>A <b><a href="/wiki/Biography" title="Biography">biography</a></b> is a detailed description or account of someone's life. More than a list of basic facts (education, work, relationships, and death), a biography also portrays a subject's experience of these events. Unlike a profile or <a href="/wiki/Curriculum_vitae" title="Curriculum vitae">curriculum vitae</a> (<a href="/wiki/Resume" title="Resume" class="mw-redirect">résumé</a>), a biography presents a subject's life story, highlighting various aspects of his or her life, including intimate details of experience, and may include an analysis of a subject's personality.</p>
<p>Biographical works are usually non-fiction, but fiction can also be used to portray a person's life. One in-depth form of biographical coverage is called legacy writing. Biographical works in diverse media—from literature to film—form the <a href="/wiki/Genre" title="Genre">genre</a> known as biography.</p>
<p>An <b>authorized biography</b> is written with the permission, cooperation, and, at times, participation of a subject or a subject's heirs.</p>
<p>An <b><a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">autobiography</a></b> is about a life of a subject, written by that subject or sometimes with a collaborator.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Featured_biography"><i>Featured biography</i></span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Mary_Wollstonecraft_by_John_Opie_(c._1797).jpg" class="image"><img alt="Mary Wollstonecraft by John Opie (c. 1797).jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Mary_Wollstonecraft_by_John_Opie_%28c._1797%29.jpg/100px-Mary_Wollstonecraft_by_John_Opie_%28c._1797%29.jpg" width="100" height="122" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Mary_Wollstonecraft_by_John_Opie_%28c._1797%29.jpg/150px-Mary_Wollstonecraft_by_John_Opie_%28c._1797%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Mary_Wollstonecraft_by_John_Opie_%28c._1797%29.jpg/200px-Mary_Wollstonecraft_by_John_Opie_%28c._1797%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1020" data-file-height="1244" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/Mary_Wollstonecraft" title="Mary Wollstonecraft">Mary Wollstonecraft</a></b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'w' in 'wind'">w</span><span title="/ʊ/ short 'oo' in 'foot'">ʊ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ən/ 'on' in 'button'">ən</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span></span>/</a></span></span>; 27 April 1759 – 10 September 1797) was an eighteenth-century <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> writer, <a href="/wiki/Philosopher" title="Philosopher">philosopher</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Feminist" title="Feminist" class="mw-redirect">feminist</a>. During her brief career as a maid she wrote novels, treatises, a <a href="/wiki/Travel_literature" title="Travel literature">travel narrative</a>, a history of the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>, a conduct book, and a children's book. Wollstonecraft is best known for <i><a href="/wiki/A_Vindication_of_the_Rights_of_Woman" title="A Vindication of the Rights of Woman">A Vindication of the Rights of Woman</a></i> (1792), in which she argues that women are not naturally inferior to men, but appear to be only because they lack education. She suggests that both men and animals should be treated as rational beings and imagines a social order founded on reason. <b>(<a href="/wiki/Mary_Wollstonecraft" title="Mary Wollstonecraft">Read more...</a>)</b>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Lillian_Gish" title="Lillian Gish">Lillian Diana Gish</a></b> (October 14, 1893&#160;–  February 27, 1993) was an American stage, screen, and television actress, director and writer whose film acting career spanned 75 years, from 1912 to 1987. She was a prominent film star of the 1910s and 1920s, particularly associated with the films of director <a href="/wiki/D._W._Griffith" title="D. W. Griffith">D. W. Griffith</a>, including her leading role in Griffith's seminal <i><a href="/wiki/Birth_of_a_Nation" title="Birth of a Nation" class="mw-redirect">Birth of a Nation</a></i>.</p>

<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="width:100%;text-align:center;">
<p>"Know that however ugly the parts appear<br />
the whole remains beautiful...<br />
... the wholeness of life and things, the divine beauty<br />
of the universe. Love that, not man<br />
Apart from that, or else you will share man's pitiful confusions,<br />
or drown in despair when his days darken."</p>
<p>— <i><a href="/wiki/Robinson_Jeffers" title="Robinson Jeffers">Robinson Jeffers</a></i></p>
<p><small>In <i>The Answer</i>, 1936</small></p>
</div>
