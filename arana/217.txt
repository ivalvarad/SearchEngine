<p><b>Clube Atlético Portal</b>, commonly known as <b>CAP Uberlândia</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Uberl%C3%A2ndia" title="Uberlândia">Uberlândia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minas_Gerais" title="Minas Gerais">Minas Gerais state</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Megaminas_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Megaminas-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clube_Atl%C3%A9tico_Portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded in 2010 by the former footballer <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilson_Batata&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gilson Batata (page does not exist)">Gilson Batata</a>, who was also the club's first head coach and he also played a friendly game for the club. The club competed in 2011 in the Copa Amvap.<sup id="cite_ref-Copa_Amvap_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Copa_Amvap-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The club debuted in professional football competitions on August 6, 2011, in a 2011 <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Mineiro_(lower_levels)" title="Campeonato Mineiro (lower levels)">Campeonato Mineiro Third Level</a> game against <a href="/wiki/Arax%C3%A1_Esporte_Clube" title="Araxá Esporte Clube">Araxá</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_Parque_do_Sabi%C3%A1" title="Estádio Parque do Sabiá">Estádio Parque do Sabiá</a>. CAP and Araxá drew 1-1. Most of the CAP's players previously defended clubs such as <a href="/wiki/Uberl%C3%A2ndia_Esporte_Clube" title="Uberlândia Esporte Clube">Uberlândia Esporte Clube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clube_Atl%C3%A9tico_Votuporanguense" title="Clube Atlético Votuporanguense">Votuporanguense</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pl%C3%A1cido_de_Castro_Futebol_Club" title="Plácido de Castro Futebol Club">Plácido de Castro</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Correio_de_Uberl.C3.A2ndia_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Correio_de_Uberl.C3.A2ndia-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Agenda_Esportiva_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Agenda_Esportiva-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clube_Atl%C3%A9tico_Portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Clube Atlético Portal play their home games at <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_Parque_do_Sabi%C3%A1" title="Estádio Parque do Sabiá">Estádio Parque do Sabiá</a>. The stadium has a maximum capacity of 50,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-cbfstadium_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbfstadium-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
