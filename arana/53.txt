<p><b>October 21</b> is the 294th day of the year (295th in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 71 days remaining until the end of the year.<br /></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=October_21&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1096" title="1096">1096</a> – <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Crusade" title="People's Crusade">People's Crusade</a>: The Turkish army successfully fight off the People's Army of the West.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1097" title="1097">1097</a> – <a href="/wiki/First_Crusade" title="First Crusade">First Crusade</a>: Crusaders led by <a href="/wiki/Godfrey_of_Bouillon" title="Godfrey of Bouillon">Godfrey of Bouillon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bohemund_of_Taranto" title="Bohemund of Taranto" class="mw-redirect">Bohemund of Taranto</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Raymond_IV_of_Toulouse" title="Raymond IV of Toulouse" class="mw-redirect">Raymond IV of Toulouse</a>, begin the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Antioch" title="Siege of Antioch">Siege of Antioch</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1209" title="1209">1209</a> – <a href="/wiki/Otto_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Otto IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Otto IV</a> is crowned emperor of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Innocent_III" title="Pope Innocent III">Pope Innocent III</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1392" title="1392">1392</a> – <a href="/wiki/Nanboku-ch%C5%8D_period" title="Nanboku-chō period">Nanboku-chō</a>, Japan: <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Kameyama" title="Emperor Go-Kameyama">Emperor Go-Kameyama</a> abdicates in favor of rival claimant <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Komatsu" title="Emperor Go-Komatsu">Go-Komatsu</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1512" title="1512">1512</a> – <a href="/wiki/Martin_Luther" title="Martin Luther">Martin Luther</a> joins the theological faculty of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wittenberg" title="University of Wittenberg" class="mw-redirect">University of Wittenberg</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1520" title="1520">1520</a> – <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a> discovers a <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">strait</a> now known as <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a>.</li>
<li>1520 – <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_%C3%81lvares_Fagundes" title="João Álvares Fagundes">João Álvares Fagundes</a> discovers the islands of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Pierre_and_Miquelon" title="Saint Pierre and Miquelon">Saint Pierre and Miquelon</a>, bestowing them their original name of "Islands of the 11,000 Virgins".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1600" title="1600">1600</a> – <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a> defeats the leaders of rival Japanese clans in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sekigahara" title="Battle of Sekigahara">Battle of Sekigahara</a>, which marks the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a> that in effect rules Japan until the mid-nineteenth century.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1774" title="1774">1774</a> – First display of the word "Liberty" on a flag, raised by colonists in <a href="/wiki/Taunton,_Massachusetts" title="Taunton, Massachusetts">Taunton, Massachusetts</a> in defiance of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> rule in <a href="/wiki/Colonial_America" title="Colonial America" class="mw-redirect">Colonial America</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1797" title="1797">1797</a> – In <a href="/wiki/Boston_Harbor" title="Boston Harbor">Boston Harbor</a>, the 44-gun United States Navy <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigate</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Constitution" title="USS Constitution">USS&#160;<i>Constitution</i></a> is launched.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1805" title="1805">1805</a> – <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Trafalgar" title="Battle of Trafalgar">Battle of Trafalgar</a>: A British fleet led by <a href="/wiki/Admiral_(Royal_Navy)" title="Admiral (Royal Navy)">Vice Admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/Horatio_Nelson,_1st_Viscount_Nelson" title="Horatio Nelson, 1st Viscount Nelson">Lord Nelson</a> defeats a combined French and Spanish fleet off the coast of Spain under <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Charles_Villeneuve" title="Pierre-Charles Villeneuve">Admiral Villeneuve</a>, signaling almost the end of French maritime power and leaves Britain's navy unchallenged until the 20th century.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1816" title="1816">1816</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Penang_Free_School" title="Penang Free School">Penang Free School</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/George_Town,_Penang" title="George Town, Penang">George Town, Penang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, by the Rev Hutchings, the oldest English-language school in Southeast Asia.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1824" title="1824">1824</a> – <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Aspdin" title="Joseph Aspdin">Joseph Aspdin</a> <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patents</a> <a href="/wiki/Portland_cement" title="Portland cement">Portland cement</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1854" title="1854">1854</a> – <a href="/wiki/Florence_Nightingale" title="Florence Nightingale">Florence Nightingale</a> and a staff of 38 <a href="/wiki/Nurse" title="Nurse" class="mw-redirect">nurses</a> are sent to the <a href="/wiki/Crimean_War" title="Crimean War">Crimean War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1861" title="1861">1861</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ball%27s_Bluff" title="Battle of Ball's Bluff">Battle of Ball's Bluff</a> – <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">Union</a> forces under Colonel <a href="/wiki/Edward_Dickinson_Baker" title="Edward Dickinson Baker">Edward Baker</a> are defeated by <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederate</a> troops in the second major battle of the war. Baker, a close friend of <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>, is killed in the fighting.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1867" title="1867">1867</a> – <a href="/wiki/Manifest_Destiny" title="Manifest Destiny" class="mw-redirect">Manifest Destiny</a>: <a href="/wiki/Medicine_Lodge_Treaty" title="Medicine Lodge Treaty">Medicine Lodge Treaty</a> – Near <a href="/wiki/Medicine_Lodge,_Kansas" title="Medicine Lodge, Kansas">Medicine Lodge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a> a landmark treaty is signed by southern <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a> Indian leaders. The treaty requires <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> Plains tribes to relocate to a reservation in western <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1879" title="1879">1879</a> – <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Edison" title="Thomas Edison">Thomas Edison</a> invents a workable electric light bulb at his laboratory in Menlo Park, N.J. which was tested the next day and lasted 13.5 hours. This would be the invention of the first commercially practical incandescent light. Popular belief is that he invented the first light bulb, which he did not.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1888" title="1888">1888</a> – Foundation of the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Switzerland" title="Social Democratic Party of Switzerland">Swiss Social Democratic Party</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1892" title="1892">1892</a> – Opening ceremonies for the <a href="/wiki/World%27s_Columbian_Exposition" title="World's Columbian Exposition">World's Columbian Exposition</a> are held in Chicago, though because construction was behind schedule, the exposition did not open until May 1, 1893.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1895" title="1895">1895</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Formosa" title="Republic of Formosa">Republic of Formosa</a> collapses as <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japanese</a> forces invade.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1902" title="1902">1902</a> – In the United States, a five-month <a href="/wiki/Strike_action" title="Strike action">strike</a> by <a href="/wiki/United_Mine_Workers" title="United Mine Workers">United Mine Workers</a> ends.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1910" title="1910">1910</a> – <a href="/wiki/HMS_Niobe_(1897)" title="HMS Niobe (1897)">HMS&#160;<i>Niobe</i></a> arrives in <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Harbour" title="Halifax Harbour">Halifax Harbour</a> to become the first ship of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Navy" title="Royal Canadian Navy">Royal Canadian Navy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1912" title="1912">1912</a> – <a href="/wiki/First_Balkan_War" title="First Balkan War">First Balkan War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Kardzhali" title="Kardzhali">Kardzhali</a> is liberated by <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bulgaria" title="Kingdom of Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1921" title="1921">1921</a> – President <a href="/wiki/Warren_G._Harding" title="Warren G. Harding">Warren G. Harding</a> delivers the first speech by a sitting U.S. President against <a href="/wiki/Lynching" title="Lynching">lynching</a> in the deep South.</li>
<li>1921 – <a href="/wiki/George_Melford" title="George Melford">George Melford</a>'s silent film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sheik_(film)" title="The Sheik (film)">The Sheik</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Rudolph_Valentino" title="Rudolph Valentino">Rudolph Valentino</a>, premiers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1931" title="1931">1931</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Sakurakai" title="Sakurakai">Sakurakai</a>, a secret society in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a>, launches an abortive <a href="/wiki/October_Incident" title="October Incident" class="mw-redirect">coup d'état attempt</a>.</li>
