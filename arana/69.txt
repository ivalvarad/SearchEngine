<p><b>Leyte</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span></span>/</a></span></span> is an <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Visayas" title="Visayas">Visayas</a> group of the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.</p>
<p>Politically, the island is divided into two <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_Philippines" title="Provinces of the Philippines">provinces</a>: <a href="/wiki/Leyte_(province)" title="Leyte (province)">(Northern) Leyte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southern_Leyte" title="Southern Leyte">Southern Leyte</a>. Territorially, Southern Leyte Province includes the island of <a href="/wiki/Panaon_Island" title="Panaon Island">Panaon</a> to its south. <a href="/wiki/Biliran_Island" title="Biliran Island" class="mw-redirect">Biliran Island</a>, to the north of Leyte Island, was formerly a sub-province of Leyte, and is now a separate province, <a href="/wiki/Biliran_Province" title="Biliran Province" class="mw-redirect">Biliran Province</a>.</p>
<p>The major cities of Leyte are <a href="/wiki/Tacloban" title="Tacloban">Tacloban</a> City, on the eastern shore at the northwest corner of <a href="/wiki/Leyte_Gulf" title="Leyte Gulf">Leyte Gulf</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ormoc" title="Ormoc">Ormoc</a> City, on the west coast.</p>
<p>Leyte today is notable for the <a href="/wiki/Geothermal_power" title="Geothermal power" class="mw-redirect">geothermal</a> electric power plants near Ormoc.</p>
<p>However, Leyte is most famous for its role in the reconquest of the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. On 20 October 1944, General <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a> waded ashore on Leyte, saying "I have returned". However, the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> did not give up so easily, as the ensuing <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte" title="Battle of Leyte">Battle of Leyte</a> proved, and convergence of naval forces resulted in the four-day <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte_Gulf" title="Battle of Leyte Gulf">Battle of Leyte Gulf</a>, the largest naval battle in history.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island measures about 180 kilometres (110&#160;mi) north-south and about 65 kilometres (40&#160;mi) at its widest point. In the north it nearly joins the island of <a href="/wiki/Samar" title="Samar">Samar</a>, separated by the <a href="/wiki/San_Juanico_Strait" title="San Juanico Strait">San Juanico Strait</a>, which becomes as narrow as 2&#160;km (1.2&#160;mi) in some places. The island province of <a href="/wiki/Biliran" title="Biliran">Biliran</a> is also to the north of Leyte and is joined to Leyte island by a bridge across the narrow Biliran Strait. To the south Leyte is separated from <a href="/wiki/Mindanao" title="Mindanao">Mindanao</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Surigao_Strait" title="Surigao Strait">Surigao Strait</a>. To the east, Leyte is somewhat "set back" from the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Sea" title="Philippine Sea">Philippine Sea</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, Samar to the northeast and <a href="/wiki/Dinagat_Islands" title="Dinagat Islands">Dinagat Islands</a> to the southeast forming the <a href="/wiki/Leyte_Gulf" title="Leyte Gulf">Leyte Gulf</a>. To the west is the <a href="/wiki/Camotes_Sea" title="Camotes Sea">Camotes Sea</a>.</p>
<p>Leyte is mostly heavily forested and mountainous, but the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Leyte Valley (page does not exist)">Leyte Valley</a> in the northeast has much agriculture.</p>

<p>The Leyte Provincial Capitol is the seat of the provincial government where there is a historic mural depicting the First Mass of Limasawa in the Orient and the landing of Gen. <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/MacArthur_Landing_Memorial_National_Park" title="MacArthur Landing Memorial National Park">MacArthur Landing Memorial National Park</a> in Red Beach, <a href="/wiki/Palo,_Leyte" title="Palo, Leyte">Palo</a>, marks the American liberation forces landing. It also has a lagoon where the life-size statues of Gen. McArthur stands albeit the true landing General McArthur set foot takes place on <a href="/wiki/Dulag,_Leyte" title="Dulag, Leyte">Dulag, Leyte</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lake_Danao_(Leyte)" title="Lake Danao (Leyte)">Lake Danao</a> is a violin-shaped lake hemmed by cloud-capped mountain ranges.</p>
<p>Calanggaman Island in <a href="/wiki/Palompon,_Leyte" title="Palompon, Leyte">Palompon, Leyte</a> is a virgin island with pure white side. It has two long sandbars. The ecological atmosphere of the island had been preserved by the municipality.</p>
<p>The Sto. Nino Shrine and Heritage Museum boasts the painting of the fourteen station of the cross done by Filipino artists and a bas-relief of the legend of the first Filipino man and woman (Malakas and Maganda).</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/San_Juanico_Bridge" title="San Juanico Bridge">San Juanico Bridge</a> is the longest bridge in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.</p>
<p>Leyte Island is the birthplace of the <a href="/wiki/Tinikling" title="Tinikling">Tinikling</a> dance, popular throughout the Philippines.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On Friday, November 8 of 2013, <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Typhoon Haiyan">Super Typhoon Haiyan</a> destroyed 70 to 80% of the structures in its path on Leyte province. An estimated 10,000 people have died and up to 620,000 people have been displaced across the region.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Japanese film <a href="/wiki/Fires_on_the_Plain_(1959_film)" title="Fires on the Plain (1959 film)"><i>Nobi</i> ("Fires on the Plain")</a> is set on Leyte in 1945 (but was filmed in Japan).</p>
