<p><b>Presidential elections</b> were held in <a href="/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a> on 11 October 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The result was a first-round victory for incumbent President <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Cond%C3%A9" title="Alpha Condé">Alpha Condé</a>, who received 58% of the vote.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinean_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In December 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Ebola_virus_epidemic_in_West_Africa" title="Ebola virus epidemic in West Africa">Ebola virus epidemic in West Africa</a> that began in Guinea spread to neighbouring Sierra Leone and Liberia, as well as with health workers and visitors who returned to their respective countries. At the time of the elections the worst of the epidemic was over, although there were still a lesser number of prevalent cases.<sup id="cite_ref-ndtv_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndtv-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The seven opposition parties called for a postponement,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> citing alleged irregularities in the electoral roll, but their pleas were rejected by the Independent National Electoral Commission (CENI).<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cellou_Dalein_Diallo" title="Cellou Dalein Diallo">Cellou Dalein Diallo</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Democratic_Forces_of_Guinea" title="Union of Democratic Forces of Guinea">Union of Democratic Forces of Guinea</a> (UFDG) also petitioned the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Supreme_Court_of_Guinea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Supreme Court of Guinea (page does not exist)">Supreme Court of Guinea</a> to have the election postponed, but it was rejected.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electoral_system">Electoral system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinean_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Electoral system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The elections were held using the <a href="/wiki/Two-round_system" title="Two-round system">two-round system</a>, with a second round taking place if no candidate received more than 50% of the vote in the first round.<sup id="cite_ref-IFES_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IFES-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Presidents are limited to two terms in office, allowing incumbent president <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Cond%C3%A9" title="Alpha Condé">Alpha Condé</a> to run for a second term.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaign">Campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinean_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A total of eight candidates contested the elections;<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> including incumbent Condé, Diallo, who was named as the candidate of the UFDG on 25 July 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sidya_Tour%C3%A9" title="Sidya Touré">Sidya Touré</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Republican_Forces" title="Union of Republican Forces">Union of Republican Forces</a>, Papa Koly Kourouma of the Generations for Reconciliation, Union, and Prosperity party, Georges Gandhi Faraguet of the Guinean Union for Democracy and Development, Faya Millimouno of the Liberal Block, Marie Madeleine Dioubaté of the Guinea Ecologists Party and <a href="/wiki/Lansana_Kouyat%C3%A9" title="Lansana Kouyaté">Lansana Kouyaté</a> of the National Party for Hope and Development.<sup id="cite_ref-IFES_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IFES-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>During the campaign Condé told voters that he aimed to promote development and consolidate stability. Diallo promised to give priority to youth employment, as well as "access to health care, decent housing, water, electricity, safety, and justice".<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> His campaign slogan promised to deliver a "KO blow" to his opponents. He cited his record on overhauling the army and judiciary, the completion of a hydroelectric dam and reforms in making the mineral-rich country's mining contracts more transparent. He said in an interview: "Ask the people of Guinea if what we have done in five years, the others did in 50. Ask that on the streets."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> His campaign was said to benefit from improving supply of electricity in Conakry and the relative stability the country endured despite the Ebola epidemic.<sup id="cite_ref-dna_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dna-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Diallo called on voters to make their voices heard following the rejection of his plea for a delay of the "imperfect" process. "We will vote, defend our suffrage, defend our victory because there is no way we will let our victory be stolen."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Prior to the vote, all seven opponents of the incumbent said they would challenge the outcome. A joint statement read: "The seven candidates will not recognise the results which will be made following an election with anomalies and irregularities."<sup id="cite_ref-ndtv_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndtv-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conduct">Conduct</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinean_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conduct">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Violence during the election campaign, including clashes between supporters of Condé and Diallo in <a href="/wiki/Koundara" title="Koundara">Koundara</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> led to the deaths of at least three people.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> A curfew was put in place in <a href="/wiki/Nz%C3%A9r%C3%A9kor%C3%A9" title="Nzérékoré">Nzérékoré</a> after one person was killed and dozens injured during a visit by Condé on 5 October.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> United Nations Secretary-General <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a> then called for the vote to be "conducted in a peaceful and transparent manner."<sup id="cite_ref-ndtv_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndtv-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Voting centres were open from 7:00 until 18:00, while national borders were closed for the day and traffic restricted to that of electoral observers, government officials and security forces, in accordance with a presidential decree. Around 19,000 police and members of the security services were on duty.<sup id="cite_ref-ndtv_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndtv-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Condé also declared that over 90% of voter cards had been distributed.</p>
<p>Monitors included an <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> observer mission and a 72-member <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> delegation. The EU delegation cited “massive deficiencies” ahead of polling; however, in its post-election report it concluded that the process overall was “valid”.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinean_presidential_election,_2015&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After CENI head Bakary Fofana announced there would be no run-off, Diallo and Touré alleged fraud and said they would not recognise the result. Diallo said "I will invite the other candidates and all the citizens who are the real victims of this electoral hold-up to organise, conforming to the law, peaceful demonstrations to express our disapproval of this situation",<sup id="cite_ref-dna_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dna-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> and that the demonstrations would occur "at the appropriate time... to express our indignation and protest against this serious denial of democracy",<sup id="cite_ref-yahoo1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo1-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> while a spokesman for Condé criticised the notion of holding protests, saying that it "risks dragging the country into instability, chaos and violence".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> He urged supporters of Condé to "stay calm, avoid gloating and get ready to face the numerous challenges that await us".<sup id="cite_ref-yahoo1_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo1-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
