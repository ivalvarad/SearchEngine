<p><b>Furnace Run</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributary</a> of <a href="/wiki/Shamokin_Creek" title="Shamokin Creek">Shamokin Creek</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northumberland_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Northumberland County, Pennsylvania">Northumberland County, Pennsylvania</a>, in the United States. It is approximately 1.0 mile (1.6&#160;km) long and flows through <a href="/wiki/Coal_Township,_Northumberland_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Coal Township, Northumberland County, Pennsylvania">Coal Township</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shamokin,_Pennsylvania" title="Shamokin, Pennsylvania">Shamokin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nationalmap_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalmap-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The watershed of the stream has an area of 1.61 square miles (4.2&#160;km<sup>2</sup>). It is impaired by sedimentation and siltation from urban runoff and storm sewers, but is not impacted by mine drainage. Reaches of the stream have been entirely enclosed. Furnace Run is named after a furnace that was built in the area in 1825. The stream is designated as a Coldwater Fishery and a Migratory Fishery.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Course">Course</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Furnace_Run_(Shamokin_Creek)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Course">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Furnace Run begins begins in a small lake in Coal Township, in the <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fairview-Ferndale,_Pennsylvania" title="Fairview-Ferndale, Pennsylvania">Fairview-Ferndale</a>. It flows east-northeast for its entire length, almost immediately entering the census-designated place of <a href="/wiki/Edgewood,_Northumberland_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Edgewood, Northumberland County, Pennsylvania">Edgewood</a>. Several tenths of a mile further downstream, the stream enters Shamokin and reaches its confluence with Shamokin Creek.<sup id="cite_ref-nationalmap_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalmap-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Furnace Run joins Shamokin Creek 21.54 miles (34.67&#160;km) upstream of its mouth.<sup id="cite_ref-gaz_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gaz-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hydrology">Hydrology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Furnace_Run_(Shamokin_Creek)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hydrology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Furnace Run is designated as an impaired waterbody. The cause of the impairment is <a href="/wiki/Sedimentation" title="Sedimentation">sedimentation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siltation" title="Siltation">siltation</a>. The source of the impairment is <a href="/wiki/Urban_runoff" title="Urban runoff">urban runoff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Storm_sewer" title="Storm sewer" class="mw-redirect">storm sewers</a>. As of 2006, a <a href="/wiki/Total_maximum_daily_load" title="Total maximum daily load">total maximum daily load</a> is needed.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Discharges of raw <a href="/wiki/Sewage" title="Sewage">sewage</a> also have entered the stream. However, it is not seriously impacted by mine drainage; there are no <a href="/wiki/Acid_mine_drainage" title="Acid mine drainage">acid mine drainage</a> discharges in the watershed.<sup id="cite_ref-scarlift_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scarlift-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/PH" title="PH">pH</a> of Furnace Run ranges from 6.6 to 7.5, with an average of 6.9, making it one of the few headwaters tributaries of Shamokin Creek to meet the pH standards of the <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Department_of_Environmental_Protection" title="Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Protection">Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Protection</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a> concentration of the two streams averages 2.5 micrograms per liter (2.5<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>−6</sup>&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft), but can reach as high as 4.7 micrograms per liter (4.7<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>−6</sup>&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft). On average, the <a href="/wiki/Alkalinity" title="Alkalinity">alkalinity</a> concentration of the stream is 54 milligrams per liter (0.054&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-scarlift_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scarlift-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The concentration of <a href="/wiki/Dissolved_oxygen" title="Dissolved oxygen" class="mw-redirect">dissolved oxygen</a> in the stream ranged from 8.9 to 10.2 milligrams per liter (0.0089 to 0.0102&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft). The concentration of <a href="/wiki/Aluminum" title="Aluminum" class="mw-redirect">aluminum</a> ranged from 0.01 milligrams per liter (1.00<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>−5</sup>&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft) to less than 0.20 milligrams per liter (0.00020&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft), while the <a href="/wiki/Manganese" title="Manganese">manganese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a> concentrations ranged from 0.05 to 0.08 milligrams per liter (5.0<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>−5</sup> to 8.0<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>−5</sup>&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft) and 0.07 to 0.09 milligrams per liter (7.0<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>−5</sup> to 9.0<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>−5</sup>&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft), respectively. In 1999, the concentration of <a href="/wiki/Sodium" title="Sodium">sodium</a> in the stream was 33 milligrams per liter (0.033&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-usgs_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1999 and 2000, the concentration of <a href="/wiki/Sulfate" title="Sulfate">sulfate</a> in Furnace Run ranged from 20 to 24 milligrams per liter (0.020 to 0.024&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft). The concentration of <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">nitrogen</a> ranged between 1.20 and 2.0 milligrams per liter (0.00120 and 0.00200&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft). In 2000, the concentration of <a href="/wiki/Phosphorus" title="Phosphorus">phosphorus</a> was 0.039 milligrams per liter (3.9<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>−5</sup>&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft). The <a href="/wiki/Chloride" title="Chloride">chloride</a> concentration was 55 milligrams per liter (0.055&#160;oz/cu&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-usgs_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Furnace Run could experience <a href="/wiki/Discharge_(hydrology)" title="Discharge (hydrology)">flow</a> augmentation.<sup id="cite_ref-scarlift_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scarlift-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Mine <a href="/wiki/Seepage" title="Seepage" class="mw-redirect">seepage</a> has contributed flow to the stream. However, in the 1950s, it was diverted into underground mines, reducing the stream's ability to carry away waste in its <a href="/wiki/Stream_channel" title="Stream channel" class="mw-redirect">channel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-shamokindispatch_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shamokindispatch-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1999 and 2000, the flow rate of the stream ranged from 0.1 to 0.1 cubic feet per second (0.0028 to 0.0028&#160;m<sup>3</sup>/s).<sup id="cite_ref-usgs_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1999 and 2000, the water temperature of the stream ranged from 5.8&#160;°C (42.4&#160;°F) in March 2000 to 21.5&#160;°C (70.7&#160;°F) in August 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography.2C_geology.2C_and_watershed">Geography, geology, and watershed</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Furnace_Run_(Shamokin_Creek)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography, geology, and watershed">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The elevation near the <a href="/wiki/River_mouth" title="River mouth">mouth</a> of Furnace Run is 745 feet (227&#160;m) above <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gnis_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gnis-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The elevation of the stream's <a href="/wiki/River_source" title="River source">source</a> is between 840 and 860 feet (256 and 262&#160;m) above sea level.<sup id="cite_ref-nationalmap_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalmap-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>There is an <a href="/wiki/Anticline" title="Anticline">anticlinal</a> in the vicinity of Furnace Run.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bog_ore" title="Bog ore" class="mw-redirect">Bog ore</a> has historically been extracted in the vicinity of the stream.<sup id="cite_ref-history_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Some reaches of the stream have been fully enclosed.<sup id="cite_ref-shamokindispatch_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shamokindispatch-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">watershed</a> of Furnace Run has an area of 1.61 square miles (4.2&#160;km<sup>2</sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-gaz_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gaz-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The stream is entirely within the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Geological_Survey" title="United States Geological Survey">United States Geological Survey</a> <a href="/wiki/Quadrangle_(geography)" title="Quadrangle (geography)">quadrangle</a> of Shamokin.<sup id="cite_ref-gnis_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gnis-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Furnace_Run_(Shamokin_Creek)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Furnace Run was entered into the <a href="/wiki/Geographic_Names_Information_System" title="Geographic Names Information System">Geographic Names Information System</a> on August 2, 1979. Its identifier in the Geographic Names Information System is 1175361.<sup id="cite_ref-gnis_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gnis-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Coal_mining" title="Coal mining">Coal mining</a> was being occasionally done in the vicinity of Furnace Run by 1825. Henry Myers constructed an <a href="/wiki/Iron_furnace" title="Iron furnace" class="mw-redirect">iron furnace</a> on the stream in 1825 on land purchased from Solomon Dunkelberger.<sup id="cite_ref-history_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Furnace Run experienced <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biology">Biology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Furnace_Run_(Shamokin_Creek)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Biology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drainage basin of Furnace Run is designated as a Coldwater Fishery and a Migratory Fishery.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1999 and 2000, no <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> were observed at one site on the stream.<sup id="cite_ref-usgs_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The concentration of <a href="/wiki/Coliform" title="Coliform" class="mw-redirect">coliform</a> bacteria in Furnace Run was more than 3000 colonies per 100 milliliters in 1999. The concentration of <a href="/wiki/E._Coli" title="E. Coli" class="mw-redirect">E. Coli</a> in the straem was also more than 3000 colonies per 100 milliliters.<sup id="cite_ref-usgs_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
