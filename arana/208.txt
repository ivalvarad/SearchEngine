<p><b>Baron Antoine Portal</b> (January 5, 1742 – July 23, 1832) was a French anatomist, doctor, medical historian and founding president of the <a href="/wiki/Acad%C3%A9mie_Nationale_de_M%C3%A9decine" title="Académie Nationale de Médecine">Académie Nationale de Médecine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antoine_Portal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born on January 5, 1742 in <a href="/wiki/Gaillac" title="Gaillac">Gaillac</a>, he was the eldest of 12 siblings. He studied medicine in <a href="/wiki/Albi" title="Albi">Albi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toulouse" title="Toulouse">Toulouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Montpellier" title="Montpellier">Montpellier</a> and started his career as a teacher of <a href="/wiki/Anatomy" title="Anatomy">anatomy</a>.</p>
<p>In 1766, Portal moved to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> to take up a similar post, and was appointed to the prestigious position of professor of anatomy to the <a href="/wiki/Jardin_du_Roi" title="Jardin du Roi" class="mw-redirect">Jardin du Roi</a>. <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVIII" title="Louis XVIII" class="mw-redirect">Louis XVIII</a> named him the first Doctor to the King, a post he served under <a href="/wiki/Charles_X" title="Charles X" class="mw-redirect">Charles X</a> as well. His close relationship with King Louis led in 1820 to the creation of what became the <i>Académie Nationale de Médecine</i>, of which he was lifelong president.</p>
<p>In 1803 he published "Cours d'anatomie médicale", a 5-volume work on medical history. He was probably the first to describe <a href="/wiki/Amyloid" title="Amyloid">amyloid</a> in <a href="/wiki/Liver" title="Liver">liver</a> in 1789 when he noted a <a href="/wiki/Lard" title="Lard">lard</a>-like substance in an elderly woman's liver.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> He was the first to describe bleeding due to <a href="/wiki/Esophageal_varices" title="Esophageal varices">esophageal varices</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> He also published article on clinical features of <a href="/wiki/Epilepsy" title="Epilepsy">epilepsy</a>.</p>
<p>He died in 1832 at the age of 90 and was buried in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Pierre_de_Montmartre" title="Saint Pierre de Montmartre" class="mw-redirect">Saint Pierre de Montmartre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
